## Detailed Analysis

**Current debt levels (pre-Indicor).** AMETEK is a clean, conservatively financed business heading into May 2026. FY2025 totals: gross debt $2.3B, cash $458M, net debt $1.8B against EBITDA of $2.3B. That's Debt/EBITDA of 1.0x and Net Debt/EBITDA of 0.8x — well inside the "comfortable" zone (Buffett's threshold is 2x). At Q1 2026, management cited gross debt/EBITDA of 0.9x and net 0.7x, with total debt net of $2.18B at March 31, 2026 — leverage was actually trending lower before the Indicor announcement. Long-term debt as of FY2025 was $1.1B (down from $1.9B in FY2023), with the rest of the $2.3B sitting in short-term/commercial-paper buckets.

**Interest coverage and refinancing risk.** EBIT/Interest in FY2025 was 23.1x. Even if you replaced FY2024's $113M interest expense with a more punishing run rate (Indicor adding ~$200M of incremental interest at ~5% on ~$4B of new debt), the resulting ~$300M annual interest charge would still leave coverage at ~6.5x against current EBIT of ~$1.9B. That is comfortable, not stretched. The only specific 2026 maturity surfaced in search snippets is $100M of 3.83% Series K Senior Notes due September 30, 2026 — a rounding error against ~$1.7B annual FCF. Beyond that, the ladder is not transparent in public summaries; the 10-K (Feb 2025) would contain the full schedule and is worth reading directly before sizing a position. The $300M 3.73% notes already matured and were paid in Q3 2024 — that hurdle is behind them.

**The Indicor deal changes the picture.** AMETEK announced (today, 2026-05-06) the acquisition of Indicor's instrumentation portfolio for ~$5.0B in cash, with closing expected in H2 2026. Management plans to fund it through a combination of revolver draws and new debt issuance. With $1.7B in trailing FCF and $458M cash, AMETEK can carry ~$2B of the price internally over 12-18 months and will likely term out ~$3-4B in fresh senior notes. Pro-forma 2026 leverage will look approximately like this: net debt steps from ~$1.8B to ~$5.5-6B; pro-forma EBITDA (adding Indicor's ~$300-350M, applying AMETEK's typical margin levels) reaches ~$2.6-2.7B. That puts pro-forma Net Debt/EBITDA at ~2.0-2.3x at close, drifting back below 2.0x within 12-18 months as cash flow paydown occurs. This is a meaningful step up but stays inside investment-grade thresholds.

**Liquidity.** Cash + ST investments of $458M (FY2025) is light by Russell 1000 standards but irrelevant in context — AMETEK has a $2.3B commercial paper program (set up earlier, per Investing.com SEC-filing coverage) plus a committed bank revolver. Current ratio of 1.1x is tight but stable, and working capital was $177M positive at YE2025. There is no "must keep market open" dynamic — the company generates $1.7B of FCF annually that can absorb any near-term refinancing.

**Off-balance-sheet items.** The 10-K filed Feb 2025 references retirement plans and operating leases at corporate HQ in Berwyn, PA, and operating facilities globally. Pension expense actually contributed positively to net income in FY2024 (per AMETEK's own commentary on lower acquisition expenses + higher pension income), suggesting the plan is overfunded or close to it. Specific operating-lease and pension-deficit dollar amounts were not surfaced cleanly in the search snippets and would warrant a direct 10-K read. Goodwill + intangibles of $10.5B (72% of assets at YE2024) is not "debt," but it is the asset most likely to be impaired in a recession and could pressure debt covenants if a large goodwill writedown drove a GAAP equity hit.

**Stress test.** Apply Buffett's "30% revenue drop for two years" test. FY2025 revenue $7.4B → stressed $5.2B. Operating margin would compress (call it 18% in the worst-case from negative operating leverage); operating income would fall to ~$940M, EBITDA to ~$1.4B. With pro-forma Indicor debt of ~$5.5B at ~5% blended cost = ~$275M annual interest. Coverage in stressed scenario: $940M / $275M = ~3.4x. That is uncomfortable but not distress (distress is below 1.5x). Net Debt/EBITDA in the stress case: ~$5.5B / $1.4B = ~3.9x — that's the level where investment-grade ratings come under review. AMETEK survives, but rating agencies would likely place it on negative watch. Without Indicor, the stress test is trivial: 23x current coverage drops to maybe 12x in stress, no survival risk whatsoever.

**Credit rating.** Search snippets did not return AMETEK's specific Moody's/S&P ratings as of May 2026. Management's repeated statement that they will "retain an investment-grade credit rating" implies they are currently solidly investment grade (likely Mid-BBB / Baa1-Baa2 territory), and the post-Indicor capital structure is designed not to threaten that band. Worth verifying directly on Moody's/S&P public pages before taking a position.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** Pre-Indicor balance sheet is one of the cleanest in specialty industrials (1.0x leverage, 23x coverage); Indicor adds quality assets and is digested within 18 months while staying investment grade.
- **Bear case:** Indicor closes into a recession; goodwill impairment + EBITDA compression push leverage above 4x, triggering a credit downgrade that raises borrowing cost just as cash flow is needed for paydown.
- **Confidence:** Medium-High — leverage and coverage ratios are clear and conservative pre-deal; uncertainty lies in pro-forma post-Indicor capital structure and the precise debt maturity ladder, both of which require a direct 10-K/10-Q read.

## Red Flags

- ~$5.0B Indicor acquisition (announced today, 2026-05-06) will be debt-funded — first material step-up in leverage in years; requires monitoring at close (H2 2026).
- Goodwill + intangibles at $10.5B / 72% of assets — impairment in a recession could pressure debt covenants and ratings indirectly even if cash flow stays intact.
- Full debt maturity ladder beyond the Sept 2026 $100M Series K notes was not visible in search results — needs direct 10-K confirmation before treating "no refinancing risk" as a settled fact.
- Specific operating-lease and pension-obligation totals not extracted from search snippets; pension is described as a positive contributor in FY2024 but a hard dollar figure should be confirmed.

The pre-Indicor balance sheet is genuinely a 9: 1.0x leverage, 23x interest coverage, paid off the $300M 3.73% notes at maturity in 2024, only $100M due Sept 2026 — the sort of profile where a recession is a non-event. The deduction of one point reflects the just-announced ~$5.0B Indicor deal, which is funded by debt and steps pro-forma leverage to ~2.0-2.3x.
