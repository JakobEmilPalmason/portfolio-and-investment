## Detailed Analysis

**Insider ownership and skin in the game.** Zapico is a 35-year AMETEK veteran who rose from product engineer to CEO. By industrial-conglomerate CEO standards, his $80M+ stake is meaningful. By owner-operator standards, his 0.15% of the company is modest, and his Form 4 record — six open-market sells, zero open-market buys over the last five years — is consistent with a professional manager monetising vested awards rather than an owner doubling down. He was elected to Rockwell Automation's board in April 2026, which is a positive signal of industry standing but also a small distraction.

**Capital allocation track record — M&A.** AMETEK's central capital-allocation activity is bolt-on acquisitions, and the track record over Zapico's tenure is genuinely good: dozens of deals completed, very few visible duds, margin profile preserved through the integrations. The "AMETEK Growth Model" — Operational Excellence, Technology Innovation, Global & Market Expansion, Strategic Acquisitions — is a credible Roper-style playbook of buying niche leaders, applying Kaizen, and harvesting cash. Recent valuations paid have been on the high side: Paragon Medical at ~$1.9B for ~$500M revenue is roughly 4x sales; FARO at $920M for ~$340M revenue is ~2.7x sales (and a 40% premium to its prior closing price). Both are defensible because the targets fit cleanly into existing platforms and AMETEK has historically pulled 200-400bps of margin out of acquired businesses post-integration. The tape on these recent deals is not fully written yet.

**Buybacks and dividends.** Buybacks are opportunistic — heavy in 2022 and 2025, light in 2023 (when Paragon consumed firepower). The new $1.25B authorisation indicates ongoing intent. Dividends have grown roughly in line with earnings; the 11% 2025 hike to $1.24 annualised keeps the payout ratio under 20%, leaving most cash for the M&A engine. Share count has been roughly flat over the past several years (229M outstanding in 2025), with buybacks roughly offsetting equity-comp issuance — the pie is essentially the same size, not shrinking, but not diluting either. For a company spending $1B+/year on M&A, that's a fair outcome.

**Compensation and dilution.** SBC of ~$48M annually on $7.4B revenue (0.65% of sales) is a positive surprise for a US large-cap industrial. The company is not handing out equity in volumes that would meaningfully change its cap table. Compensation design (per the 2025 proxy summary) is structured around organic sales growth, operating income, EPS, and ROTC — a sensible mix that includes per-share metrics rather than headline revenue or EBITDA only. I did not verify the exact metric weights line-by-line.

**Candor and consistency.** Earnings call transcripts read steadily on-message: organic vs acquired growth is broken out clearly, segment commentary is consistent, and guidance has generally been hit or beat over the past five years. The company has not had a major restatement, accounting issue, or governance incident in recent memory. Communication style is professional but not Buffett-style folksy — closer to a Roper or Danaher tone than a Berkshire tone.

**Governance.** Single class of stock, no founder voting control, no related-party red flags surfaced in the search. The board is independent in the standard NYSE sense. Nothing structurally concerning. Zapico holding both Chairman and CEO roles is a minor governance point — a separate independent chair is the better-practice setup, but it is not unusual at US large-cap industrials and does not look problematic given the rest of the picture.

**The simple tell.** Two readable signals: (a) SBC at 0.65% of sales is quietly excellent; many "compounder" peers run 2-3x that; (b) M&A multiples paid in 2023-2025 have crept up — Paragon at 15x EBITDA is on the rich end of historic AMETEK deals, and FARO was a competitive auction. This is not a deal-breaker, but the cost of staying on the M&A treadmill at scale is rising, and management's discipline will be tested if a recession brings cheaper assets they can't pay up for.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** A 35-year insider CEO running a disciplined Roper-style playbook with low SBC, opportunistic buybacks, a growing dividend, and a deep M&A pipeline — the kind of management you let compound for a decade.
- **Bear case:** Recent acquisition multiples have crept higher (Paragon ~15x EBITDA, FARO at a 40% premium), and the CEO's open-market trading record is sells-only — execution-good, owner-operator-grade ownership stake is missing.
- **Confidence:** Medium-High — track record is multi-year, financials confirm the discipline, but the proxy was not parsed line-by-line so compensation-metric details are at a summary level only.

## Red Flags

- CEO Form 4 history shows six open-market sells and zero open-market buys over the past five years.
- Acquisition multiples paid in 2023-2025 (Paragon ~15x EBITDA, FARO at a 40% premium) are on the rich end of AMETEK's historic range.
- Combined CEO/Chairman role; no separate independent chair.
- ROIC at 12-13% is good but not exceptional; if M&A multiples keep creeping up, this will drift.

## Score: 8 / 10

Disciplined, long-tenured insider CEO; clean proxy mechanics; very low SBC; disciplined M&A engine. Held back from a 9 by recent deal pricing and a CEO ownership profile that is professional rather than owner-operator.
