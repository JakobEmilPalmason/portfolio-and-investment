# Compact Checklist — SAP.DE

**Date:** 2026-03-23

1. **Business**: SAP sells mission-critical ERP software that runs finance, supply chain, HR, and procurement for 400,000+ enterprises in 180 countries, and customers keep paying — now through multi-year cloud subscriptions at 2–3x the old maintenance fee — because replacing SAP requires a multi-year, nine-figure project that almost no CFO will sanction.

2. **Moat**: The switching cost is civilizational: each custom workflow, integration, and report built on SAP over decades makes the total cost of replacement existential, a moat that deepens every year customers stay on the platform — evidenced by 80% of the 30,000 on-premise base committing to S/4HANA migration despite the pain and cost of doing so.

3. **Economics**: SAP generates $8.4B in FCF on $36.8B revenue (22.9% FCF margin), ROIC improved from 8.0% to 13.1% in three years, gross margins have held at 73% through the cloud transition, and 86% of revenue is contractually recurring — confirming real, cash-backed software economics, not accounting fiction.

4. **Management**: CEO Christian Klein has delivered on every major commitment since 2020 — cloud transformation, 560bps margin expansion, $5.6B debt reduction, $8.4B FCF — and immediately announced a €10B buyback near the 52-week low, signaling management's intrinsic value estimate is well above current market pricing.

5. **Debt risk**: SAP is net cash positive ($9.8B cash vs $7.5B gross debt), carries a 21.9x interest coverage ratio, holds A1/A+ credit ratings, and generates its entire gross debt balance in operating cash every eleven months — the balance sheet is a competitive asset, not a risk.

6. **Price**: At €153.82 and 15.8x EV/EBITDA, the market is pricing SAP as if cloud migration permanently stalls, AI/Joule generates zero incremental revenue, the exit multiple is compressed to 12–13x (a permanent decline scenario), and open-ended legal investigations result in material penalties — every pessimistic assumption simultaneously true.

7. **Margin of safety**: The business MOS is strong (net cash, €77B cloud backlog, 86% recurring revenue, fortress credit), but the price MOS is thin: the quant base IV is €167 (8% upside) and the sensitivity grid floor is €142 (8% below current price), meaning downside protection rests primarily on business quality and the 87% Monte Carlo probability of undervaluation, not a discount to bear-case IV.

8. **Thesis breaker**: If cloud backlog growth decelerates below 10% for two consecutive quarters, SAP's migration pipeline is structurally impaired rather than temporarily delayed — that outcome would invalidate the core thesis and require an exit.
