## Detailed Analysis

**Owner Earnings Estimation.** Waste Connections reported FY2025 net income of $1.08B, D&A of $1.2B, and CapEx of $1.2B, yielding owner earnings of approximately $1.1B (net income + D&A - maintenance CapEx, adjusting for growth CapEx being roughly 20% of total). SBC of $79M is a real cost that slightly dilutes this figure. On a per-share basis, owner earnings are roughly $4.30. The company guides 2026 net income to $1.22-1.24B and adjusted FCF to $1.40-1.45B, reflecting significant improvement as Chiquita Canyon remediation costs wind down ($200M cash impact in 2025, expected $100-150M in 2026) and EBITDA margins expand to roughly 33.2-33.4%.

**Scenario Analysis — Bear Case (Year 5 Owner Earnings).** Assume revenue grows at 3% CAGR (macro slowdown, volume declines persist, C&D remains weak), EBITDA margins compress 50bps to 32.5% from commodity headwinds and integration friction, and maintenance CapEx stays elevated. Year 5 (2030) revenue: ~$11.0B, EBITDA: ~$3.6B, owner earnings: ~$1.4B. Apply a 14x EV/EBITDA exit multiple (discount to current), enterprise value of $50.4B, subtract $8.8B net debt, equity value $41.6B, or ~$163/share. Discounted back at 10%: ~$101/share.

**Scenario Analysis — Base Case.** Revenue grows at 5% CAGR (in line with guidance: pricing at 5-5.5%, flat volumes, plus tuck-in acquisitions), EBITDA margins expand to 34% as operating leverage continues. Year 5 revenue: ~$12.1B, EBITDA: ~$4.1B, owner earnings: ~$1.7B. Apply 16x EV/EBITDA (modest discount to historical average). Enterprise value $65.6B, equity value $56.8B, or ~$222/share. Discounted back at 9%: ~$144/share.

**Scenario Analysis — Bull Case.** Revenue grows at 7% CAGR (acquisition-driven, recycling commodity recovery, RNG ramp), EBITDA margins reach 35.5% as automation and route density improve. Year 5 revenue: ~$13.3B, EBITDA: ~$4.7B, owner earnings: ~$2.0B. Apply 18x EV/EBITDA (premium sustained for best-in-class operator). Enterprise value $84.6B, equity value $75.8B, or ~$297/share. Discounted back at 9%: ~$193/share.

**Multiples in Context.** WCN currently trades at 17.2x EV/EBITDA, which is a premium to Waste Management (14.6x) and Republic Services (15.5x), but below its own 5-year average of approximately 19-20x. The premium has historically been justified by WCN's superior EBITDA margin trajectory (33% and rising vs peers at ~31%), its secondary-market focus reducing competitive intensity, and its acquisition integration track record. The forward P/E of 27x looks expensive on a standalone basis but is supported by 30%+ EPS growth guided for 2026 as one-time Chiquita costs normalize.

**Reverse-Engineered Implied Growth.** At $167.24 with owner earnings of ~$1.1B (FY2025), the market is paying a 2.6% owner earnings yield. For this to deliver a 9% annual return, owner earnings need to compound at roughly 6.4% annually — achievable given the company's pricing power and acquisition flywheel, but not a bargain. If we use 2026 guided FCF of $1.4B, the implied FCF yield improves to 3.3%, requiring ~5.7% growth for a 9% return — more reasonable but still not offering a wide margin of safety.

**Multiple DCF cross-checks.** Simply Wall St's DCF model estimates intrinsic value at approximately $236-239/share, suggesting roughly 30-40% upside. However, these models typically assume continuation of above-average growth without margin-of-safety haircuts. A more conservative approach using a 10% discount rate and conservative terminal assumptions lands closer to $140-160/share, suggesting the stock is roughly fairly valued to modestly overvalued at current levels.

## Signal Summary
- **Bull case:** Best-in-class waste operator with 33%+ EBITDA margins, strong pricing power, and a proven acquisition machine trading 17% below 52-week highs. 2026 is a reset year with Chiquita headwinds fading, FCF inflecting 15%+, and insider buying signaling management confidence. At $167, the forward FCF yield of 3.3% on guided numbers is the cheapest WCN has been in years.
- **Bear case:** Even after the pullback, WCN trades at a significant premium to peers (17x vs 14-15x EV/EBITDA) and to the broader market. Owner earnings yield of 2.6% is thin. Volume trends are negative (Q4 down 2.7%), C&D is 15% below 2023 levels, and commodity revenues face further headwinds. The stock requires near-perfect execution to justify current multiples.
- **Confidence:** Medium — The business quality is high and predictable, but the valuation leaves limited room for error. The pullback helps but doesn't create a clear bargain.

## Red Flags
- Owner earnings yield of 2.6% is below the 10-year Treasury yield, requiring significant growth to justify
- Trading at 17%+ premium to larger peers WM and RSG on EV/EBITDA
- Volume declines in key segments (C&D down 5% FY2025) suggest cyclical softness not fully priced
- Chiquita Canyon tail risk: $100-150M additional costs in 2026, with "challenging regulatory and political environment in California"

## Score: 5 / 10
WCN is roughly fairly valued at $167 — the pullback from $200+ removes the worst of the overvaluation but does not create a compelling discount to conservative intrinsic value estimates, with the base-case IV of ~$144-193 straddling the current price.
