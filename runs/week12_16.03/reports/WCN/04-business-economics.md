## Detailed Analysis

**Returns on Capital: The Goodwill Problem.** WCN's reported ROIC of 8.4% (FY2025) is mediocre at face value, but the picture is distorted by the capital-heavy acquisition model. The balance sheet carries enormous goodwill and intangible assets from decades of serial acquisitions — total assets of $21.1B include roughly $12B+ in goodwill/intangibles versus $8.2B in stockholders' equity. On a tangible-capital or pre-goodwill basis, returns are significantly higher (mid-teens ROIC ex-goodwill per industry analyses). ROE tells a similar story: 13.4% in FY2025, recovering from a Chiquita Canyon landfill closure-impacted 7.9% in FY2024. These are respectable but not exceptional returns, reflecting the reality that waste management is a capital-intensive, acquisition-driven business rather than an asset-light compounder.

**Margin Structure: Steady Expansion.** The margin trajectory is the strongest element of WCN's business economics. Gross margins have expanded from 39.9% (FY2022) to 42.4% (FY2025). Operating margins improved from 17.5% to 19.2% over the same period. Adjusted EBITDA margin reached 33.0% for FY2025, up 100 basis points year-over-year and industry-leading among waste peers. Management guided FY2026 adjusted EBITDA margin of 33.3-33.4%, implying continued expansion. This margin improvement reflects pricing discipline, route density optimization, and the shedding of lower-margin contracts — hallmarks of WCN's operating culture focused on margin over volume.

**Cash Generation: Reliable but Capital-Hungry.** Operating cash flow has grown steadily: $2.0B (FY2022) to $2.2B (FY2024) to $2.4B (FY2025). FCF has been remarkably stable at approximately $1.2B per year across all four years, though this flatness reflects high and growing capital expenditures ($1.2B in FY2025, with $1.25B guided for FY2026 including $100M for RNG and recycling). The company guided FY2026 adjusted FCF of $1.4-1.45B, which would represent meaningful acceleration. FCF conversion relative to net income has been volatile (113% to 190%) largely due to swings in net income from the Chiquita Canyon charges, but on an adjusted basis conversion is consistently around 50% of adjusted EBITDA — a standard benchmark for the waste industry.

**Revenue Quality: The Core Strength.** WCN's revenue stream is among the most predictable in the industrial landscape. The company operates primarily in secondary and rural markets with exclusive municipal franchise agreements, creating local monopoly-like dynamics. Revenue growth has been consistent: $7.2B (FY2022) to $9.5B (FY2025), a 32% cumulative increase driven by a mix of organic pricing (mid-single-digits annually), volume, and acquisitions ($330M of acquired annualized revenue in FY2025 alone). Core pricing consistently exceeds cost inflation, and contract structures often include CPI-linked escalators. This is as close to recurring, subscription-like revenue as an industrial company can achieve.

**Capital Intensity and Allocation.** The business is inherently capital-intensive: capex/revenue runs around 12-13%, and the company requires ongoing investment in trucks, containers, landfill cells, and transfer stations. However, the split between maintenance and growth capex is favorable — a significant portion funds landfill cell development and fleet replacement (maintenance), while incremental growth capex goes toward RNG projects and tuck-in acquisition integration. Shareholder returns totaled a record $839M in FY2025 through buybacks and dividends, demonstrating that FCF, while not explosive, comfortably funds both reinvestment and capital return. SBC of $79.4M (FY2025) is modest relative to revenue.

**Operating Leverage.** The waste business exhibits meaningful operating leverage once route density is optimized — adding a stop on an existing route is nearly 100% incremental margin. WCN's focus on exclusive franchise markets amplifies this effect. The 100 bps EBITDA margin expansion in FY2025 on 6% organic revenue growth demonstrates solid operating leverage at work. As the company integrates recent acquisitions and grows RNG contributions, further margin tailwind is plausible.

## Signal Summary
- **Bull case:** Highly recurring revenue with pricing power, expanding margins, growing cash flow, and a disciplined acquisition machine that should compound mid-to-high single digits annually with improving returns.
- **Bear case:** Reported ROIC is sub-10%, capital intensity limits FCF growth, and the acquisition-dependent model means returns on incremental capital depend heavily on deal discipline continuing under future management.
- **Confidence:** Medium-High — Financial data is comprehensive from public filings and recent earnings. The economic model is well-understood and stable. Minor uncertainty around true maintenance vs growth capex split.

## Red Flags
- Reported ROIC of 8-9% is below the 15% threshold typically associated with wide-moat compounders
- FCF has been flat at ~$1.2B for four years despite 32% revenue growth — capital intensity absorbs much of the operating improvement
- Chiquita Canyon landfill closure created a $200M+ cash flow headwind in FY2025 and depressed FY2024 net income to $618M — one-off but a reminder of environmental liability tail risk
- Negative working capital (-$813M) is structural for the industry but limits financial flexibility in a stress scenario

## Score: 6 / 10
WCN delivers highly predictable, recurring revenue with expanding margins and reliable cash generation, but reported ROIC in the 8-9% range, flat FCF despite strong revenue growth, and high capital intensity place it squarely in the "solid but not exceptional" category for business economics — a good business, not a great one by Buffett standards.
