# Compact Checklist — JPM

**Date:** 2026-03-22

1. **Business**: JPM collects the spread between $1T+ in cheap deposits and higher-rate loans, plus fees on every capital markets, payments, and wealth management transaction across 82M+ households and global institutional clients — customers stay because switching treasury and custody integrations takes months and carries operational risk.
2. **Moat**: A $20B/year technology budget, #1 positions in US deposits, credit cards, and investment banking, and 20% ROTCE vs. 12-14% for peers create a scale and switching-cost advantage that no competitor or fintech can replicate across all three segments simultaneously.
3. **Economics**: 20% ROTCE in FY2025 is roughly double the 10-12% cost of equity, the efficiency ratio of 52-53% is best-in-class among universal banks, and tangible book value per share has compounded at ~10% annually since 2006.
4. **Management**: Dimon's 21-year tenure produced ~10% annual book value compounding, a perfectly timed First Republic acquisition, and $34.6B in buybacks in FY2025 alone — but he sold 11%+ of his shares in early 2025 and his exit within 2-3 years is the biggest open question.
5. **Debt risk**: CET1 of 14.5% sits 300 bps above the regulatory minimum, the Fed just cut JPM's stress capital buffer to 2.5%, and $57B in pre-provision earnings can absorb a doubling of credit losses before capital is even threatened.
6. **Price**: At 2.66x tangible book (vs. 10-year median of 1.57x), the stock requires JPM to sustain 18-20% ROTCE indefinitely, execute a flawless CEO succession, and avoid any meaningful credit cycle — all simultaneously.
7. **Margin of safety**: There is none — the price exceeds our base IV of $260 by 10%, with 32% downside to the bear case ($195) versus only 15% upside to the bull case ($330).
8. **Thesis breaker**: ROTCE falling below 12% for two consecutive quarters, a new CEO making a large dilutive acquisition, or Basel III endgame rules materially increasing capital requirements would each break the investment case.
