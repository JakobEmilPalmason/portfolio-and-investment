## Detailed Analysis

JPMorgan Chase's business economics are exceptional for a bank of its size and complexity. The single most important metric for a bank is whether it earns above its cost of equity on a sustained basis, and JPM clears this bar decisively. With a 2025 ROTCE of 20% — against an estimated cost of equity in the 10-12% range — JPM generates roughly 800-1000 basis points of economic profit spread. This is not a one-year phenomenon: ROE has tracked between 12.9% and 17.4% over 2022-2025, and ROTCE has been consistently higher, reflecting management's focus on tangible capital returns. The firm's through-the-cycle ROTCE target of 17% implies even under stress conditions, JPM expects to earn well above its cost of equity.

Net interest income is the core earnings engine and exhibits remarkable stability. At roughly $95.5B ex-Markets in 2025, JPM's NII franchise dwarfs all peers. The 2026 guidance of ~$95B ex-Markets (and ~$103B total including Markets NII) suggests the firm has navigated the rate normalization cycle without material NII erosion. Average deposit growth of 6% YoY in 2025 — described as notable in a lower-rate environment that typically sees deposit flight — demonstrates the stickiness and franchise value of JPM's deposit base. This combination of rate-resilient NII and growing deposits is the hallmark of a dominant banking franchise.

Fee income diversification is where JPM separates from nearly all peers. The three-segment structure — CCB (consumer banking, cards, payments), CIB (investment banking, markets, wholesale payments), and AWM (asset and wealth management) — creates multiple independent profit engines. In Q4 2025, AWM produced a 44% ROE on record $6.5B revenue, while CCB earned a 25% ROE on $19.4B revenue. CIB revenue rose 10% with strong markets performance. This diversification means JPM is not hostage to any single rate environment, credit cycle, or capital markets regime. When NII compresses, trading and advisory fees often rise, and vice versa. This natural hedge is extremely valuable.

The efficiency ratio of approximately 52-53% is best-in-class for a universal bank. For context, most large US banks run efficiency ratios in the 55-65% range. JPM's scale advantages — in technology investment (over $17B annually), branch network, and processing infrastructure — create operating leverage that smaller competitors cannot replicate. Noninterest expense is the main risk: the firm reported rising expenses in 2025 from technology investment, compensation, and the First Republic integration. However, management has consistently demonstrated the ability to grow revenue faster than expenses, maintaining or improving the efficiency ratio over time.

Credit costs are well-contained relative to the firm's earnings power. The full-year 2025 provision of $4.7B — which includes a non-recurring $2.2B reserve build for the Apple Card portfolio acquisition — represents a modest charge against $57B of net income. Excluding the Apple Card reserve, normalized provisions were roughly $2.5B, suggesting net charge-offs remain low across the consumer and wholesale books. The expected 2026 Card net charge-off rate of ~3.4% is consistent with a normalized credit environment rather than stress. JPM's pre-provision net revenue of approximately $57B+ provides massive loss-absorbing capacity before capital is impaired.

Net margins of 31.4% in 2025 (34.5% in 2024) are exceptionally high for a diversified bank, though the YoY decline reflects the Apple Card reserve impact and higher expenses. The EPS trajectory — from $12.09 in 2022 to $20.02 in 2025 — combined with aggressive buybacks ($34.6B in 2025 alone) demonstrates a capital return machine that compounds tangible book value per share (up 11.8% YoY to $107.56) while still investing heavily in the franchise.

## Signal Summary
- **Bull case:** JPM earns 800+ bps above cost of equity with the most diversified revenue mix in banking, creating a compounding machine that grows TBVPS at ~10% annually through cycles.
- **Bear case:** Scale-driven returns could compress if regulatory capital requirements tighten (Basel III endgame), NII faces sustained rate pressure, or credit losses normalize higher than provisioned.
- **Confidence:** High — JPM's business economics are observable across multiple cycles, supported by audited financials, and validated by consistent ROTCE well above peers and cost of equity.

## Red Flags
- Apple Card portfolio acquisition adds ~$2.2B in reserves and introduces consumer credit risk from a new, less-seasoned book
- Noninterest expense growth requires vigilance — technology and compensation inflation could erode efficiency gains
- 2025 net income declined slightly from 2024 peak ($57.0B vs $58.5B), suggesting the cyclical earnings peak may have passed
- Concentration of returns in markets/trading revenue can be volatile quarter to quarter

## Score: 9 / 10
JPM delivers 20% ROTCE on a sustained basis, roughly double its cost of equity, with best-in-class efficiency, exceptional fee diversification, and a rate-resilient NII franchise — this is among the strongest business economics profiles in global banking, with the only deduction for cyclical earnings normalization risk and rising expense base.
