## Detailed Analysis

**Why the DCF model fails for banks.** The quant DCF model produced a base intrinsic value of $142 and a bull case of $226. These figures are deeply misleading. Banks earn revenue from the net interest margin spread between assets (loans, securities) and liabilities (deposits, borrowings), not from operating margins on product sales. JPM's operating income, EBITDA, and CapEx fields all show "n/a" in financial data precisely because these traditional metrics are meaningless for a bank. Cash flow from operations swings wildly — FCF margin ranged from -81% to +84% over four years — driven by loan origination cycles, not business quality. The DCF's 15% operating margin assumption has no anchor in bank economics. We discard these outputs entirely.

**Bank-appropriate valuation: Price-to-Tangible Book.** JPM reported tangible book value per share of $107.56 at Q4 2025, growing at ~12% annually over the past two years (from ~$85 in 2023). At $286.56, the stock trades at 2.66x TBVPS. Over the past decade, JPM's P/B ratio has ranged from 0.94x (COVID trough) to 2.62x (recent highs). The 10-year median is 1.57x. The current multiple is at the absolute top of the historical range. Even accounting for JPM's vastly improved profitability (ROTCE expanding from ~13% to 20%), this is a premium valuation. A fair P/TBV for a bank earning 17% ROTCE with a ~10% cost of equity might be 1.7-2.2x on a Gordon Growth framework. At 2.66x, the market is pricing in sustained ROTCE well above 17%.

**Normalized earnings power approach.** JPM earned $20.02 EPS in FY2025, with consensus at $23.49 for 2026. However, FY2025 included elevated trading revenue and favorable credit conditions. Management's own 17% ROTCE target for 2026 implies normalized earnings closer to $18-19 on current tangible equity, which would suggest EPS in the $18-20 range in a mid-cycle environment. At a 12-14x normalized P/E (appropriate for a large-cap diversified bank), this yields a fair value range of $216-$280. The forward consensus of $23.49 at 12.2x gives $286 — essentially where it trades today, meaning consensus earnings are fully priced.

**Residual income / excess return model.** If JPM sustainably earns 17% ROTCE against a 10% cost of equity, the 7% excess return on ~$108 of tangible book generates ~$7.50/share in annual economic profit. Capitalizing this at a 10% discount rate adds ~$75 to tangible book, yielding an intrinsic value of ~$183. To justify $286, the market must believe JPM will sustain 20%+ ROTCE indefinitely — possible given its scale advantages, but aggressive. Adjusting for TBVPS growth of 8-10% annually via retained earnings and buybacks, a 3-year forward TBVPS of ~$135-140 at a 2.0x multiple yields $270-280, which frames a reasonable bull case.

**Peer comparison context.** JPM commands the highest valuation multiple among diversified US banks. Bank of America trades at ~1.3x P/B with lower ROTCE (~12-13%), Wells Fargo at ~1.6x with improving but still constrained returns, and Goldman Sachs at ~2.6x P/B as a capital-markets-heavy franchise. JPM's premium is earned — it has the best ROTCE, most diversified revenue, and strongest capital position — but the spread to peers is wider than historical norms, suggesting some "Dimon premium" that may compress post-succession.

**Capital return and floor dynamics.** JPM has authorized $50B in share buybacks (effective July 2025) and raised the quarterly dividend to $1.50 ($6.00/yr, ~2.1% yield). With CET1 at 14.5% vs. the 11.5% requirement, JPM has ~$30-40B in excess capital. This creates a soft floor under the stock — management will aggressively buy back shares on any meaningful dip — but it does not create upside from current levels. The market has already capitalized the buyback machine into the price.

## Signal Summary
- **Bull case:** JPM's dominant franchise, 17-20% ROTCE, and massive capital return justify a sustained 2.0-2.5x TBVPS; forward TBVPS growth to ~$135+ by 2028 supports $270-$340 range.
- **Bear case:** Current 2.66x P/TBV is at the all-time high; ROTCE normalization to 15-17%, credit cycle turn, or succession discount could compress multiples to 1.5-1.8x on $110-115 TBVPS, implying $165-$207.
- **Confidence:** Medium — JPM is the highest-quality large bank globally, but valuation is stretched vs. history and peers; the range of outcomes is wide depending on ROTCE sustainability.

## Red Flags
- Trading at the very top of historical P/TBV range (2.66x vs. 10-year median of 1.57x)
- FY2025 ROTCE of 20% may be cyclically inflated; management itself guides to 17% for 2026
- Quant DCF model completely unreliable for bank valuation — any decision based on those outputs would be deeply flawed
- Consensus forward EPS of $23.49 appears optimistic relative to management's own ROTCE guidance

## Score: 5 / 10
JPM is a world-class franchise fairly valued to modestly overvalued at current prices; the stock prices in near-perfect execution and sustained above-trend profitability, leaving limited margin of safety for a Buffett-style investor.
