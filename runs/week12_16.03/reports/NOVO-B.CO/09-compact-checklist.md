# Compact Checklist — NOVO-B

**Date:** 2026-03-22

1. **Business**: Novo Nordisk sells GLP-1 injectable and oral drugs for diabetes (40M+ patients) and obesity to chronic-disease patients who cannot stop treatment without clinical relapse, generating DKK 309B revenue at 81% gross margins.
2. **Moat**: Peptide manufacturing scale built over 100 years, regulatory barriers requiring 8-12 years and billions to replicate, and patient switching costs on titrated chronic therapy — though the shift from monopoly to duopoly with Lilly has permanently narrowed pricing power.
3. **Economics**: ROIC of 36% on a vastly expanded capital base, DKK 119B operating cash flow, and DKK 102B owner earnings confirm elite unit economics, but reported FCF collapsed to DKK 29B because DKK 68B of growth capex is funding a once-in-a-generation manufacturing buildout that peaks in 2025.
4. **Management**: The Novo Nordisk Foundation forced out the CEO, board chair, and six directors in 2025 — new CEO Doustdar has moved fast with 9,000 layoffs saving DKK 8B annually and unusual candor about the road ahead, but the wholesale leadership turnover during the hardest competitive transition in company history is untested.
5. **Debt risk**: At 0.6x net debt/EBITDA with 32x interest coverage, AA/Aa3 credit ratings, and DKK 119B operating cash flow that could retire all DKK 131B of debt in 13 months, Novo survives a 30% revenue decline for two years without issuing equity.
6. **Price**: At 10.3x trailing earnings, the market requires you to believe net income permanently halves from DKK 102B to DKK 50B — equivalent to semaglutide revenue being cut in half with zero pipeline value from CagriSema, oral Wegovy, or amycretin.
7. **Margin of safety**: Even the stress-tested bear case (DKK 325) sits 37% above today's DKK 237 price, an essential DKK 60-70B insulin franchise provides a hard business floor, and the 6.7% dividend yield pays you to wait.
8. **Thesis breaker**: Sell if the FDA issues a class-wide GLP-1 safety signal (black box warning or prescribing restriction on semaglutide), GLP-1 market share drops below 20% for two consecutive quarters, or the Novo Nordisk Foundation begins selling its controlling stake.
