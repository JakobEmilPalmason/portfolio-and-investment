# Compact Checklist — CBOE

**Date:** 2026-03-18

1. **Business**: CBOE runs the only exchange where SPX and VIX options trade, earning a per-contract toll on 3.9 million daily contracts at 67% operating margins with near-zero marginal cost.
2. **Moat**: Exclusive SPX licensing through 2032 and outright VIX ownership, reinforced by a self-compounding liquidity network effect that no competitor has ever successfully replicated in electronic derivatives.
3. **Economics**: ROIC of 17.3%, FCF conversion of 153% on just $71M capex, and EBITDA margins expanding to 68% confirm a capital-light toll bridge that converts almost every incremental revenue dollar to cash.
4. **Management**: New CEO Donohue is executing a disciplined realignment — exiting failed Australian, Canadian, and digital-asset expansions — and has retired 22% of shares since 2011, though insider ownership at 0.51% is too low.
5. **Debt risk**: With $2.3B cash against $1.6B debt, 31x interest coverage, and the ability to remain profitable even under a 50% revenue decline, financial distress is virtually impossible.
6. **Price**: At $287 (22.5x forward earnings), the stock requires 6-7% annual earnings growth just to deliver 10% returns — essentially pricing in the base case with no room for disappointment.
7. **Margin of safety**: The business itself is a fortress, but the stock trades 85% above bear-case IV ($155) and 30% above base-case IV ($222), offering zero price protection for a new buyer.
8. **Thesis breaker**: Loss of the exclusive SPX license at the 2032 renewal, or SEC restrictions on 0DTE options that impair the 59%-of-volume growth engine, would break the thesis.
