## Detailed Analysis

### Owner Earnings Estimate

Starting with FY2025 reported figures: net income $1.1B + D&A $122M - maintenance capex (estimated at ~$50M, roughly 70% of total capex of $71M given CBOE is primarily a technology platform requiring steady infrastructure investment) = approximately $1.17B in owner earnings. This aligns closely with the $1.2B owner earnings figure from yfinance. Stock-based compensation of $50M is modest at ~4% of owner earnings and does not materially distort the picture. On a per-share basis, owner earnings are approximately $11.20, placing the stock at roughly 25.6x owner earnings.

Looking forward 3-5 years, if we assume mid-single-digit revenue growth (5-7% annually) with stable-to-slightly-expanding margins (CBOE has demonstrated operating leverage), owner earnings could grow to $1.4-1.7B by 2030, or $13.50-$16.50 per share assuming modest buyback activity reducing the share count by 1-2% annually.

### Scenario Analysis

**Bear Case (Growth Stalls, Margins Compress):** Assume derivatives volumes normalize after 2025's record levels, 0DTE regulatory headwinds emerge, and revenue growth drops to 2-3% annually. Margins compress 200bps from opex growth. Year 5 owner earnings: ~$1.25B (~$12.50/share). Apply a 20x multiple (compression from current 25x due to lower growth): fair value ~$250. Discounted back at 10%: ~$155.

**Base Case (Reasonable Continuation):** Mid-single-digit organic revenue growth (5%), stable margins, modest buybacks. Year 5 owner earnings: ~$1.55B (~$15.50/share). Apply a 23x multiple (slight premium for quality/moat): fair value ~$357. Discounted back at 10%: ~$222.

**Bull Case (Derivatives Secular Trend Accelerates):** Options volumes continue secular growth at 8-10% CAGR, Data Vantage grows high single digits, new products (prediction markets, 24x5 trading, global expansion) add incremental revenue. Year 5 owner earnings: ~$1.85B (~$19.00/share). Apply a 25x multiple: fair value ~$475. Discounted back at 10%: ~$295.

### Multiples in Context

CBOE's forward P/E of 22.5x compares to CME at ~24x trailing (CME's lower growth justifies a lower forward multiple), ICE at ~22-24x, and Nasdaq at ~25-28x. CBOE's EV/EBITDA of 18.4x is below CME's ~19-22x range and below the financial exchanges sector median. The P/FCF of 23.7x reflects genuine free cash flow generation without heavy capital requirements. Historically, CBOE has traded in a 18-28x forward P/E range over the past five years; the current 22.5x sits in the lower-to-middle portion of that range.

### What Must Be True for Today's Price

At $287, investors are paying ~25.6x owner earnings. For this to generate a 10% annual return over 5 years, CBOE needs to grow owner earnings at roughly 6-7% annually while maintaining its current multiple — essentially the base case scenario. The price assumes no multiple expansion, no major regulatory disruption to 0DTE or index options, and continued dominance in proprietary index derivatives. This is plausible but leaves limited margin for error.

### Implied Expectations vs Reality

The market is pricing in roughly the company's own mid-single-digit guidance — no heroics required. The risk is that 2025's blowout year creates a tough comparison, and any volume normalization could make 2026 look like deceleration even if the underlying business is healthy. The upside case rests on the secular shift toward options as an asset class, which appears durable but is now well-recognized by the market.

## Signal Summary
- **Bull case:** Secular growth in derivatives volumes, proprietary VIX/SPX moat, and capital-light model could drive 8-10% earnings growth and multiple expansion toward $350+.
- **Bear case:** 2025 was a cyclical peak in derivatives activity; normalization, regulatory action on 0DTE, or index competition could compress both earnings and multiples toward $200.
- **Confidence:** Medium — the business quality is high but the stock appears to be pricing in the good news; limited margin of safety at current levels.

## Red Flags
- Analyst consensus target ($288) essentially matches the current price, suggesting limited near-term upside conviction from the Street
- Trailing P/E of 27.5x is inflated by a blowout year that may not repeat
- 0DTE regulatory risk is real but unquantifiable — any restriction would directly hit the highest-growth revenue stream

## Score: 5 / 10
CBOE is a high-quality business trading at approximately fair value on a base case; the bear case discounted value of ~$155 is well below the current price, while the bull case of ~$295 barely exceeds it, suggesting the risk-reward is balanced but not compelling for a value-oriented buyer.
