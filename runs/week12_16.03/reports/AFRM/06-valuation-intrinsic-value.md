## Detailed Analysis

**Owner Earnings Estimate.** Affirm's FY2025 owner earnings (net income + D&A - maintenance capex) come to approximately $85M. However, this figure is deeply misleading in isolation. Stock-based compensation was $321M in FY2025 — roughly 6x net income — meaning real economic earnings to shareholders are effectively negative when you account for dilution. FCF of $602M looks strong, but that includes the $321M SBC benefit (a non-cash add-back). True free cash flow to equity holders, adjusted for SBC, is closer to $280M. The company also initiated $250M in buybacks, which roughly offsets ongoing dilution but doesn't create incremental value. Over the next 3-5 years, if Affirm can scale revenue to $6-8B while maintaining 65%+ gross margins and reducing SBC as a percentage of revenue to 5-6% (from ~10% currently), owner earnings could reach $500M-$800M. That is the bull case.

**Scenario Analysis.** For the bear case, I assume revenue growth decelerates to 15% as BNPL competition intensifies (Klarna IPO, PayPal, Apple Pay Later), credit losses rise in a downturn, and margins compress. By FY2030, revenue reaches ~$5.5B with 5% net margins = ~$275M net income. Applying a 15x multiple = $4.1B market cap, discounted back at 12% = ~$2.3B today, or roughly **$8 per share**. For the base case, revenue grows 20% annually to ~$7.5B by FY2030, net margins reach 10% = $750M net income. At 20x = $15B, discounted back = ~$8.5B, or roughly **$29 per share**. For the bull case, revenue grows 25%+ to ~$10B, net margins hit 15% = $1.5B net income. At 25x = $37.5B, discounted back = ~$21.3B, or roughly **$73 per share**. Note: these are fully diluted estimates assuming ~310M shares by FY2030.

**Multiples in Context.** At 4.3x trailing P/S, Affirm trades at a premium to Klarna (pre-IPO implied ~3-4x) and well above traditional financial services (1-2x). The 34.7x P/FCF is high but less extreme when you consider the FCF growth trajectory. The forward P/E of 12.5x looks cheap, but it embeds an earnings estimate ($3.82 EPS) that requires near-perfect execution. EV/EBITDA of 44.6x is expensive by any financial services standard. Historical context: AFRM traded at 20x+ sales at its 2021 peak and as low as 2x sales during the 2022 trough. Current 4.3x is mid-range but above what a rational long-term buyer should pay for an unproven earnings stream.

**What Must Be True for Today's Price.** At $47.69 and ~$15.9B market cap, the market is pricing in roughly $600-800M in sustainable annual net income within 3-5 years. This requires: (1) revenue reaching $7B+ with no major merchant losses, (2) net margins expanding to 8-12% from the current 1.6%, (3) credit losses staying contained even through a recession, (4) SBC declining as a share of revenue, and (5) no disruptive regulatory intervention in BNPL. All five of these must go right. Any one failing — especially credit losses in a downturn — could crater the stock.

**Implied Expectations vs. Reality.** The gap between trailing earnings ($52M) and forward consensus ($1.1B+) is enormous. Q2 FY2026 showed $130M net income — annualizing to ~$520M. Even that more realistic run-rate gives a 30x P/E at current prices. The market is betting Affirm is at an inflection point where operating leverage kicks in dramatically. That could happen, but BNPL is a cyclically sensitive, competitively intense, and increasingly regulated business. The Walmart loss to Klarna is a concrete reminder that merchant relationships are fragile. The current price embeds a lot of optimism with limited margin for error.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** Affirm scales to $10B+ revenue as the dominant US BNPL network, achieves 15% net margins, and justifies a $70+ stock price within 3-4 years.
- **Bear case:** A recession spikes credit losses, merchant churn accelerates, regulatory costs rise, and the stock revisits $10-15 as profitability proves ephemeral.
- **Confidence:** Medium — The growth trajectory is real and Q2 FY2026 was strong, but the distance between current earnings and what the price implies is unusually large.

## Red Flags

- SBC of $321M vs. net income of $52M in FY2025 — shareholders are being diluted faster than the company earns
- Interest coverage of just 1.1x — thin margin if funding costs rise or credit losses spike
- Walmart merchant loss to Klarna demonstrates fragility of enterprise relationships
- 72.8% debt-to-capital ratio (even accounting for securitized loan funding, leverage is high)
- Beta of 3.73 means this stock will amplify every macro scare — painful for long-term holders
- Forward EPS consensus requires a ~20x earnings jump from FY2025 — any miss will be punished severely

## Score: 4 / 10

Current price embeds aggressive growth and margin assumptions with minimal margin for error; owner earnings are negligible today, SBC dwarfs net income, and the path to justifying the valuation requires near-perfect execution in a competitive, cyclical, and increasingly regulated market.
