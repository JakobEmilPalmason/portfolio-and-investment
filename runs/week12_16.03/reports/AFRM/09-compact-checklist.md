# Compact Checklist — AFRM

**Date:** 2026-03-17

1. **Business**: Affirm lends money to shoppers at checkout, earning merchant fees and consumer interest on installment loans, with 96% of transactions coming from repeat users who split purchases across 478K active merchants including Amazon (locked through 2031).
2. **Moat**: Proprietary per-transaction underwriting models (producing 2.7% delinquency rates vs 8.8% for credit cards) and the fast-growing Affirm Card (3.7M users, 159% GMV growth) are real advantages, but merchants can and do switch providers — as Walmart's defection to Klarna proved.
3. **Economics**: ROIC is a meager 2.8% after years of losses, gross margins are expanding (67.5%), but SBC of $321M dwarfs net income of $52M, and the $8.8B loan book requires continuous capital market access to fund — this is a leveraged lender, not an asset-light platform.
4. **Management**: Levchin is a credible founder-operator with modest $538K cash compensation and SBC is declining as a share of revenue (28% to 10%), but his 44.4% voting control through dual-class shares and $113M in recent stock sales with zero purchases weaken the alignment story.
5. **Debt risk**: Interest coverage of 1.1x and $7.9B in securitized debt that requires constant warehouse facility renewals mean a credit market freeze or consumer downturn would simultaneously hit revenue, increase losses, and restrict funding — a survivable but painful triple threat.
6. **Price**: At $47.69 (base-case IV of $29, bear-case IV of $8), the market must believe Affirm will reach $600-800M in sustainable net income within 3-5 years — requiring 20x the current earnings with no credit cycle disruption.
7. **Margin of safety**: There is none — the stock trades 64% above base-case IV with inverted risk-reward (83% downside to bear case vs 53% upside to bull case), and a rational entry point is below $20.
8. **Thesis breaker**: 30+ day delinquency rates exceeding 4% for two consecutive quarters, or the loss of the Amazon partnership, would confirm the credit risk and merchant fragility that the current price ignores.
