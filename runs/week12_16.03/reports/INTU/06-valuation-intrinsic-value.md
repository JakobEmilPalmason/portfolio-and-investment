## Detailed Analysis

### Owner Earnings Calculation

Starting from FY2025 (ending July 2025) reported figures:
- Net Income: $3.9B
- Add back D&A: $809M
- Less maintenance CapEx (estimated at ~$124M, since most CapEx is maintenance for a software company): -$124M
- **Reported Owner Earnings: ~$4.6B**
- Less SBC: -$2.0B
- **SBC-Adjusted Owner Earnings: ~$2.6B**

This distinction matters enormously. At $447/share and 276.6M shares ($123.7B market cap), the reported owner earnings yield is 3.7%, but the SBC-adjusted yield is only 2.1%. For a Buffett-style analysis, the SBC-adjusted figure is the honest one — SBC dilutes shareholders and is a real economic cost of running the business. SBC represents roughly 10.6% of revenue, which is high even by software standards.

### Three-Scenario DCF (5-Year Horizon)

**Assumptions common to all scenarios:**
- Starting SBC-adjusted owner earnings: $2.6B (FY2025)
- Discount rate: 10% (opportunity cost)
- Terminal multiple applied to Year 5 SBC-adjusted owner earnings
- Shares: 276.6M (assume modest net dilution offset by buybacks)

**Bear Case (Slow Growth + Multiple Compression)**
- Owner earnings growth: 8% CAGR (AI disruption pressures consumer segment, SBC stays elevated)
- Year 5 SBC-adjusted OE: $2.6B x 1.08^5 = $3.82B
- Terminal multiple: 18x (mature software, growth slowing)
- Terminal value: $68.8B
- PV of terminal: $68.8B / 1.10^5 = $42.7B
- PV of interim cash flows (Years 1-5, discounted): ~$11.6B
- Enterprise value: $54.3B. Less net debt $3.1B = $51.2B equity
- **Per share: $185**

**Base Case (Steady Execution)**
- Owner earnings growth: 12% CAGR (in line with revenue guidance, some SBC leverage)
- Year 5 SBC-adjusted OE: $2.6B x 1.12^5 = $4.58B
- Terminal multiple: 22x (quality compounder)
- Terminal value: $100.8B
- PV of terminal: $100.8B / 1.10^5 = $62.6B
- PV of interim cash flows: ~$13.7B
- Enterprise value: $76.3B. Less net debt $3.1B = $73.2B equity
- **Per share: $265**

**Bull Case (AI Monetization + SBC Discipline)**
- Owner earnings growth: 16% CAGR (AI drives upsell, SBC/revenue drops to 8%)
- Year 5 SBC-adjusted OE: $2.6B x 1.16^5 = $5.46B
- Terminal multiple: 25x (premium compounder with AI optionality)
- Terminal value: $136.5B
- PV of terminal: $136.5B / 1.10^5 = $84.7B
- PV of interim cash flows: ~$15.9B
- Enterprise value: $100.6B. Less net debt $3.1B = $97.5B equity
- **Per share: $353**

### What The Current Price Implies

At $447/share ($123.7B market cap), the market is pricing in substantially more than even our bull case on an SBC-adjusted basis. Reverse-engineering: to justify $447 at a 10% discount rate and 22x terminal multiple, you need SBC-adjusted owner earnings to compound at ~18% annually for 5 years, reaching ~$5.9B. That requires either (a) revenue growing 15%+ while SBC grows much slower, or (b) a structurally higher terminal multiple of 28-30x. Both are possible but represent optimistic assumptions for a company whose organic growth rate is decelerating (Q3 guidance at 10% vs Q2 at 17%).

If we use reported (non-SBC-adjusted) owner earnings of $4.6B as the starting point, the picture looks much more favorable — a base case of $540-600/share. This is roughly where sell-side consensus sits ($610 mean target), suggesting most analysts are using non-GAAP metrics and ignoring SBC dilution.

## Signal Summary
- **Bull case:** AI monetization through Intuit Assist drives meaningful upsell and retention, SBC as a percentage of revenue declines, IRS Direct File is dead, and INTU re-rates to software-peer multiples — $600+ fair value on a non-GAAP basis.
- **Bear case:** SBC remains elevated (10%+ of revenue), AI commoditizes tax prep reducing pricing power, growth decelerates to high-single-digits, and the stock de-rates to a market multiple on GAAP earnings — $200-250 fair value.
- **Confidence:** Medium — The valuation answer depends entirely on how you treat SBC. Non-GAAP metrics say cheap; SBC-adjusted metrics say fairly valued to expensive.

## Red Flags
- SBC of $2.0B is 10.6% of revenue and 51% of net income — this is a massive real cost that non-GAAP metrics hide
- Revenue growth decelerating (Q3 guide 10% vs Q2 actual 17%)
- ROIC of 15.8% is respectable but not exceptional for a software company with $6.6B in debt
- Share count has been roughly stable, meaning SBC dilution is being offset by buybacks funded from operating cash flow — the buybacks are effectively compensating employees, not returning capital to shareholders

## Score: 6 / 10
At $447, INTU is fairly valued to modestly undervalued on conventional (non-GAAP) metrics, but when SBC is properly accounted for as a real expense, the margin of safety is thin. The stock is no longer expensive after a 45% drawdown from highs, but it is not clearly cheap either. The honest SBC-adjusted owner earnings yield of 2.1% demands faith in sustained double-digit growth for a decade. Score of 6 reflects a stock that has moved from overvalued to roughly fair value — not yet in bargain territory for a disciplined value investor.
