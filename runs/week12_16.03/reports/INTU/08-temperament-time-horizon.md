## Detailed Analysis

### Holding Period Suitability

Intuit is structurally a 10+ year holding. Tax filing is not going away, SMBs will always need accounting software, and Intuit's data advantages compound over time as more customers use the platform. The AI strategy (Intuit Assist, agentic AI agents for tax, payments, and accounting) could extend the growth runway well beyond what a simple tax-software narrative implies.

However, the holding period question is inseparable from entry price. At $447, a 10-year hold with 12% annual owner earnings growth (base case) and a terminal multiple of 22x on SBC-adjusted earnings would yield roughly a 6-7% annualized return — below the 10% hurdle rate a disciplined investor should demand. You would need to hold through significant volatility for a below-market return. A more attractive entry around $250-300 would shift the 10-year return into the 12-15% range, making the holding period thesis much more compelling.

### Volatility Tolerance Required

INTU demands high volatility tolerance:
- Beta: 1.28 — amplifies market moves by nearly 30%
- Current drawdown from 52-week high: -45% ($814 to $447)
- Historical worst drawdowns: -72% (Dot-Com), -49% (inflation shock), -38% (GFC), -36% (Covid)
- Average drawdown across 15 systemic shocks: -16%

An investor buying at $447 should be psychologically prepared for a further 30-40% decline to the $270-310 range in a recession scenario. This is not a utility stock. The recurring revenue base provides business stability, but the market treats INTU as a growth stock and prices it accordingly — when growth sentiment turns, the drawdowns are severe.

### Catalysts

**Near-term (0-12 months):**
- Q3 FY2026 earnings (May 2026) — tax season results will reveal whether TurboTax AI features are driving ARPU growth
- FY2026 guidance update — any acceleration or deceleration in the 12-13% revenue growth target
- Broader market sentiment on software/AI stocks — INTU trades with the sector

**Medium-term (1-3 years):**
- Intuit Assist monetization — if agentic AI features drive meaningful upsell, this could re-rate the stock
- SBC discipline — any trend toward SBC/revenue declining below 10% would be a positive signal
- Credit Karma cross-sell — financial services distribution to 44M users is an underappreciated asset
- International expansion — QuickBooks international penetration remains low relative to the opportunity

**Negative catalysts:**
- Q3 tax season miss or guidance cut
- Renewed IRS Direct File initiative under a different political environment
- Major tech platform entering tax prep or SMB accounting
- Recession causing SMB churn spike

### Position Sizing Logic

Given the negative margin of safety on SBC-adjusted metrics and the high-beta profile, position sizing should be conservative:

- **At current price ($447):** Watchlist only. No position warranted until price declines to a level offering genuine margin of safety. A 2% tracking position is the maximum for investors who want exposure to the name while waiting.
- **At $300-350:** Starter position (2-3% of portfolio). This brings the MOS closer to neutral on SBC-adjusted metrics and offers a more compelling risk/reward.
- **At $200-250:** Full conviction position (4-5% of portfolio). At this level, even SBC-adjusted metrics show meaningful upside.

### Correlation and Portfolio Overlap

INTU occupies a unique niche: financial software for consumers and SMBs. It has low correlation with:
- Traditional financials (banks, insurers) — INTU is a software company, not a financial company
- Industrials, healthcare, consumer staples — different business drivers
- Enterprise SaaS (Salesforce, ServiceNow) — different customer base (consumer/SMB vs enterprise)

Moderate correlation with:
- Other consumer-facing tech (Google, Meta) — ad-driven Credit Karma has some overlap
- Other tax/financial services (H&R Block, Block/Square) — direct competitive overlap
- Broad software/tech sector — trades with growth sentiment

INTU adds diversification to a portfolio heavy in industrials, healthcare, or financials. It overlaps with a portfolio already exposed to consumer tech or SMB software.

### Process Discipline

**Entry plan:** Do not buy at current prices. Set alerts at $300, $275, $250. Re-evaluate SBC trends and growth trajectory at each level. Only enter when SBC-adjusted owner earnings yield exceeds 4% (roughly $250/share at current earnings).

**Sell rules:**
- Sell if SBC/revenue rises above 13% with no plan to moderate
- Sell if revenue growth drops below 5% for two consecutive quarters
- Sell if a major competitive threat (government free filing, tech platform entry) materializes and TurboTax ARPU declines
- Trim if position exceeds 5% of portfolio due to appreciation

**Review cadence:** Quarterly after earnings. Annual deep review of SBC trends, competitive positioning, and AI monetization progress.

## Signal Summary
- **Bull case:** INTU is a generational compounder bought during a 45% drawdown, AI investments pay off, and patient holders earn 15%+ annual returns over a decade as the business compounds through tax, accounting, and financial services.
- **Bear case:** Entry at $447 is still too expensive on honest metrics, volatility is high (beta 1.28, historical drawdowns 38-72%), and there is no near-term catalyst — the patient investor waits for a better price while the impatient investor suffers drawdowns and mediocre returns.
- **Confidence:** Medium — The business quality and long-term runway are high-conviction. The entry price is the problem. This is a great business at a fair-to-expensive price, not yet a great investment.

## Red Flags
- 45% drawdown from highs sounds like a bargain, but the stock was extremely overvalued at $814 — the decline merely normalizes valuation, it does not create deep value
- Beta of 1.28 means this stock will amplify any further market decline
- No near-term catalyst to close the valuation gap before Q3 earnings in May
- Management has not signaled any plan to moderate SBC — until they do, real per-share economics will grow slower than headline numbers suggest
- The "AI will save everything" narrative is consensus — if Intuit Assist disappoints, there is no backup thesis at this price

## Score: 6 / 10
Intuit is a high-quality business with a long runway that merits a permanent spot on the watchlist. The 45% drawdown has made the stock more interesting but not yet compelling on SBC-adjusted metrics. A score of 6 reflects a business that fits well in a quality-focused portfolio — but only at the right price. The temperament required (high volatility tolerance, 5-10 year horizon, discipline to wait for a better entry) is demanding. At $250-300, this would be a 7-8. At current prices, patience is the correct posture.
