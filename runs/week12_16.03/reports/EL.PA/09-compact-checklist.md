# Compact Checklist — EL.PA

**Date:** 2026-03-23

1. **Business**: EssilorLuxottica sells prescription lenses, branded eyewear frames, and smart glasses through 18,000+ owned stores and wholesale channels, driven by non-discretionary vision correction demand that grows with aging demographics and rising global myopia.
2. **Moat**: Vertical integration across lenses, frames, and retail at a scale no competitor can replicate, combined with optician switching costs on Essilor lens technology and the world's strongest eyewear brand portfolio (Ray-Ban, Oakley, 20+ luxury licenses), produces 60%+ gross margins and ~27% global market share.
3. **Economics**: Tangible ROIC is 15-20% once you strip EUR 30B of merger goodwill, FCF conversion runs above 140% of net income, and capex at 5.4% of revenue confirms asset-light compounding — though gross margin fell 280bps in FY2025 from smart glasses mix dilution.
4. **Management**: CEO Milleri has executed well operationally (11.2% constant-currency growth, margin expansion roadmap to 19-20%), but he holds minimal equity, serves as dual Chairman/CEO, and the Delfin controlling shareholder has been paralyzed by a three-year succession dispute among the founder's eight heirs.
5. **Debt risk**: Net debt/EBITDA of 1.1x, A2/A credit ratings, EUR 3.5B cash on hand, and EUR 3.8B annual FCF mean EssilorLuxottica would remain FCF-positive even in a 30% revenue decline scenario — this balance sheet is a competitive weapon, not a liability.
6. **Price**: At EUR 195 and a 22.5x forward P/E (versus a 5-year average of 30-35x), the market is pricing in an immediate growth collapse to low-single-digits and permanent multiple compression, which contradicts record 11.2% organic growth and Q4 acceleration to 18.4%.
7. **Margin of safety**: The business quality moat is fortress-grade and Monte Carlo shows 99.9% probability of undervaluation, but the price sits 10% above the quant bear IV of EUR 176, so a truly conservative buyer would prefer to enter closer to EUR 175 for maximum downside protection.
8. **Thesis breaker**: Sell if adjusted ROIC (ex-goodwill) falls below 8% for two consecutive years, signaling the vertically integrated moat is eroding rather than compounding.
