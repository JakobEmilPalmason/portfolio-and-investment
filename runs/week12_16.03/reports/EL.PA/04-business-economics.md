## Detailed Analysis

### Returns on Capital: The Goodwill Distortion

EssilorLuxottica's reported ROIC of 5.2% (FY2025) looks deeply unimpressive at face value. However, this is one of the clearest cases of merger accounting masking true economic returns. The 2018 Essilor-Luxottica combination and the 2021 GrandVision acquisition loaded the balance sheet with approximately EUR 30B of goodwill and intangible assets. Invested capital stands at EUR 49.7B, of which roughly 60% is acquisition-related goodwill and purchase-price-allocated intangibles. If we strip goodwill and amortization of acquired intangibles (approximately EUR 910M annually), the tangible return on capital employed is far more impressive — likely in the 15-20% range. This is consistent with what you would expect from a business that controls the entire eyewear value chain from lens manufacturing through retail distribution, with dominant brand pricing power. The ROE of 5.8% and ROA of 3.6% suffer from the same distortion. For a business generating EUR 3.9B in owner earnings on a tangible asset base of perhaps EUR 20B, the underlying economics are strong.

### Margin Structure: Solid but Under Pressure

The margin trajectory tells an important story. EBITDA margins have been stable at 23-25% for four years, while the adjusted operating margin (which adds back acquisition-related amortization) was 16.0% in FY2025 versus 16.7% in FY2024. The reported GAAP operating margin of 11.9% is lower due to EUR ~910M in annual amortization of acquisition-related intangibles — a non-cash charge with no economic substance. The gross margin decline from 63.4% to 60.6% in FY2025 is notable and appears driven by the product mix shift toward Meta Ray-Ban smart glasses, which carry lower margins than traditional premium eyewear. Management is deliberately accepting near-term margin dilution to build a potentially transformative new product category. The key question is whether smart glasses margins improve as the product matures and scales, or whether this represents a structural shift toward lower-margin consumer electronics. Historical precedent from the lens business suggests EssilorLuxottica can improve margins over time as it vertically integrates new categories.

### Cash Generation: The Crown Jewel

Free cash flow is the most compelling metric here. FCF grew from EUR 3.2B (FY2022) to EUR 3.8B (FY2025), representing a 13.2% FCF margin. FCF conversion (FCF/Net Income) has been extraordinary, consistently above 140% — this is because D&A significantly exceeds capex (EUR 3.1B vs EUR 1.5B), a classic sign of a business where past acquisitions are generating cash flows well in excess of the accounting charges against them. Owner earnings of EUR 3.9B are essentially identical to FCF, which is a healthy sign — there is no meaningful gap between economic and accounting cash flow. At the current market cap of EUR 89.7B, the stock trades at roughly 23x owner earnings. Operating cash flow of EUR 5.3B provides ample coverage for capex, dividends (approximately EUR 1.7B), and modest buybacks (EUR 376M), with surplus cash available for bolt-on M&A (guided at up to EUR 1.5B annually).

### Capital Intensity and Revenue Quality

Capex has been remarkably stable at EUR 1.5-1.6B annually (5.4% of revenue), even as revenue has grown from EUR 24.5B to EUR 28.5B. This is the hallmark of an asset-light compounder that grows primarily through pricing power, volume in essential healthcare products, and operational leverage rather than heavy reinvestment. Revenue quality is high: vision correction is non-discretionary (approximately 4.6 billion people globally need corrective eyewear), replacement cycles are predictable, and the company controls distribution through its ~18,000 retail stores (LensCrafters, Sunglass Hut, GrandVision). This is recurring in nature if not contractually recurring. The addition of smart glasses introduces a more discretionary, technology-upgrade-driven revenue stream that may prove more cyclical.

### Operating Leverage

Revenue has grown from EUR 24.5B to EUR 28.5B (+16.3%) over FY2022-FY2025, while operating income has remained flat at EUR 3.4B and net income essentially flat at EUR 2.3B. This absence of operating leverage is concerning at first glance, but is entirely explained by the rising amortization of acquisition intangibles and the deliberate investment in smart glasses. On an adjusted basis, operating profit has grown faster than revenue. The underlying business model has strong inherent operating leverage — fixed costs (R&D at EUR 664M, corporate overhead) are spread across a growing revenue base, and the vertically integrated model captures margin at multiple points in the value chain.

## Signal Summary
- **Bull case:** Tangible ROIC is 15-20%+, FCF compounds reliably at double digits, and the reported metrics systematically understate the economic quality of this franchise due to merger-era goodwill.
- **Bear case:** Gross margin erosion from smart glasses mix shift may not reverse, and reported ROIC/ROE could remain depressed indefinitely if the company continues making large acquisitions that reload goodwill.
- **Confidence:** Medium — The tangible economics are clearly strong, but the magnitude of the goodwill distortion makes precise ROIC estimation difficult without full balance sheet decomposition; the smart glasses margin impact is still evolving.

## Red Flags
- Gross margin declined 280bp in a single year (60.6% vs 63.4%) — largest annual drop in available history
- Reported ROIC has been flat-to-declining for 4 years (5.1-5.5%), with no improvement despite revenue growth
- Interest expense doubled from EUR 156M (FY2022) to EUR 314M (FY2025), reflecting both higher rates and higher debt
- SBC increased 73% YoY (EUR 280M vs EUR 162M) — worth monitoring for dilution
- Smart glasses carry lower margins and introduce consumer electronics cyclicality into what was a pure healthcare franchise
- Zero operating leverage on a GAAP basis over 4 years — adjusted metrics tell a better story but require trust in management's adjustments

## Score: 7 / 10
Strong underlying economics masked by merger accounting — tangible ROIC likely 15-20%, FCF conversion above 140%, and stable asset-light capex at 5.4% of revenue — but the gross margin decline, flat GAAP profits, and smart glasses margin dilution prevent a higher score until the adjusted margin trajectory improves.
