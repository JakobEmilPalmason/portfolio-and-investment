## Detailed Analysis

### Returns on Capital: Acquisition-Driven Dilution, Not Deterioration

The headline ROIC decline from 9.2% (FY2022) to 5.9% (FY2025) looks alarming but requires careful decomposition. Invested capital ballooned from $18.7B to $36.3B, driven overwhelmingly by the $13.45B AssuredPartners acquisition that closed in August 2025. This deal added enormous goodwill and intangible assets to the balance sheet overnight. The denominator effect is the primary culprit — AJG's underlying brokerage operations did not suddenly become less efficient. Pre-acquisition ROIC was tracking 8–9%, consistent with the asset-light brokerage model. ROE followed a similar path, falling from 12.2% to 6.9%, reflecting both the equity raise ($8.5B in December 2024 plus overallotment) and the goodwill-heavy acquisition. The critical question is whether AssuredPartners will earn returns above cost of capital as synergies materialize. Management targets $160M in annual run-rate synergies by end of 2026 and $260–280M by early 2028. If achieved, this should lift ROIC back toward 7–8% by 2027–2028 as the acquired book matures and integration costs fade. The transaction is projected to be 10–12% accretive to EPS on a full-run-rate basis, which at current EPS of ~$10.69 adjusted implies meaningful compounding ahead.

### Margin Structure: Stable Gross, Expanding Adjusted

AJG's margin profile reflects an asset-light intermediary model. Gross margins have been remarkably stable at 42.1–42.6% for four consecutive fiscal years, a hallmark of businesses with pricing power and predictable commission economics. GAAP operating margins of 18–20% are compressed by $1.1B in D&A, the vast majority of which is amortization of acquired intangibles — economically a different item from depreciation of physical assets. The adjusted EBITDAC margin, which adds back acquisition-related amortization and is the standard metric for public brokers, reached 35% for the combined brokerage and risk management segments in full-year 2025 — approximately 70 basis points higher year-on-year on a comparable underlying basis. The Brokerage segment hit 32.2% adjusted EBITDAC margin in Q4 2025, with management guiding for 40–60 bps of underlying margin expansion in 2026. For context, Aon's GAAP operating margin runs ~24%; AJG's 35% adjusted EBITDAC compares favorably once both are put on a comparable basis. Net margins of 10–13% will remain depressed by intangible amortization for years, which investors must accept as the cost of AJG's acquisition-driven growth model.

### Cash Generation: The Crown Jewel

This is where AJG's economics shine most clearly. FY2025 FCF of $1.8B on only $145M capex confirms the extraordinarily low capital intensity of insurance brokerage — CapEx/revenue is barely 1.0%. Owner earnings (NI + D&A − CapEx) reached $2.5B in FY2025, substantially above GAAP net income of $1.5B and far more representative of the business's true earning power. FCF conversion averaged ~140% of net income over FY2024–2025. FY2025 operating CF of $1.9B was lower than FY2024's $2.6B, partly because FY2024 was inflated by the pre-close cash staging for AssuredPartners (the $15.0B cash balance at year-end FY2024 reflects equity offering proceeds held ahead of the August 2025 close). FY2025 represents a more normalized operating cash flow run rate. As integration costs fade and a full 12 months of AssuredPartners cash flows are captured in FY2026, management's FCF trajectory should approach $2.5B+ annually — a number that makes the $12.1B long-term debt look progressively more manageable.

### Capital Intensity and Revenue Quality

Insurance brokerage is among the most capital-light business models in financial services. AJG's maintenance capex of $145M is 1.0% of revenue — the business requires almost no physical reinvestment to maintain earnings power. Growth capital goes to M&A (people and relationships), not plant or equipment. Revenue quality is exceptionally high: commissions and fees are overwhelmingly recurring from policy renewals, with client retention rates that typically exceed 90% in commercial brokerage. Growth comes from three overlapping drivers: net new business wins, insurance rate increases (which drove outperformance in 2022–2024), and exposure/economic growth. In the current soft-to-moderating commercial lines environment, rate tailwinds that drove 7–9% organic growth in 2023–2024 have faded, producing full-year 2025 organic growth of approximately 6% — still healthy, but down from the elevated recent years. Management guided 5.5% brokerage segment organic growth for 2026, with a range of 5–9%. D&A of $1.1B (FY2025) is almost entirely amortization of acquired intangibles — this confirms physical asset intensity is minimal.

### Peer Context and Operating Leverage

Among the large public brokers, AJG stood out in organic growth cadence during 2022–2024 but converged toward the peer average in 2025. In Q3 2025, AJG posted 4.8% organic growth in combined segments vs. Aon's 7% and MMC's 4%, suggesting the moderation is partly industry-wide. Full-year 2025 organic growth of ~6% is consistent with AJG's long-run 5–7% organic rate. The 23rd consecutive quarter of double-digit adjusted EBITDA growth underscores the compounding power of the acquisition strategy layered on an organic growth base. Operating leverage is embedded in the model: as revenue from new acquisitions moves through Gallagher's platform, duplicate costs are eliminated and incremental margin flows at 40–50% contribution rates. This leverage is why management can guide margin expansion even while integrating a transformational deal, and why analysts continue to model EPS of $14.82 on a forward basis (14.5x forward P/E) versus trailing $5.74 GAAP EPS.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** An asset-light, highly recurring commission business with 42% stable gross margins, $2.5B in owner earnings, and 23 consecutive quarters of double-digit adjusted EBITDA growth; AssuredPartners integration drives ROIC recovery and 10–12% EPS accretion through 2027–2028.
- **Bear case:** Organic growth deceleration toward 5% in a softening commercial insurance rate environment, combined with integration execution risk on a $13.45B acquisition, leaves AJG earning below its cost of capital for longer than expected with limited capital allocation flexibility.
- **Confidence:** High — the operating economics of insurance brokerage are well-understood and deeply recurring; four years of reported financials are internally consistent with the commission-based model thesis, and peer data corroborates the organic growth trajectory.

## Red Flags

- ROIC at 5.9% is below most estimates of cost of capital (~7–8%); even if temporary, AJG must demonstrate tangible AssuredPartners synergy realization within 18–24 months
- Organic growth decelerated from 9.5% (Q1 2025) to ~5% (Q4 2025) — partly rate cycle softening, partly harder comparables, but requires monitoring as a potential structural shift
- Operating cash flow declined from $2.6B (FY2024) to $1.9B (FY2025) — partly timing from pre-close cash staging, but FY2026 will be the first clean read on normalized FCF including a full year of AssuredPartners
- GAAP EPS of $5.74 vs. adjusted EPS of $10.69 — the $5 per share gap is almost entirely intangible amortization; investors must accept that AJG's reported earnings will structurally understate economic earnings for years

## Score: 7 / 10

AJG operates an inherently excellent business model — highly recurring revenue, 42% stable gross margins, $2.5B in owner earnings, and 1% capex intensity — but the massive AssuredPartners acquisition has temporarily diluted reported ROIC below cost of capital, and the 2026 integration year will be the critical test of whether AJG's acquisition machine continues to create value at scale.
