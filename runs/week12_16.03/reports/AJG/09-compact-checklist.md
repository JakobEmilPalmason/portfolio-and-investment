# Compact Checklist — AJG

**Date:** 2026-03-18

1. **Business**: AJG earns recurring commissions and fees by placing insurance for middle-market companies that must renew annually, bearing no underwriting risk itself.
2. **Moat**: Deep client switching costs (brokers hold years of institutional risk knowledge), a 600-acquisition data flywheel powering AI analytics, and dominant scale in the fragmented 100-2,500-employee segment where Marsh and Aon do not compete.
3. **Economics**: FCF of $1.8B on just $145M capex confirms exceptional capital efficiency, though ROIC has temporarily fallen to 5.9% — below cost of capital — because the $13.5B AssuredPartners deal doubled the invested capital denominator overnight.
4. **Management**: Pat Gallagher's 31-year track record of 600+ successful tuck-in acquisitions is outstanding, but the transformative AssuredPartners bet diluted shareholders 20%, pushed leverage to 3.7x, and he is 73 with no disclosed succession plan.
5. **Debt risk**: AJG would survive a severe downturn — all $13.5B of debt is fixed-rate with laddered maturities (nothing large due before 2035), $2.5B undrawn revolver, and the sticky renewal-based revenue model declined only 5% even in 2008-2009.
6. **Price**: At 14x forward earnings, the market must believe AssuredPartners synergies will largely fail, organic growth will stall at 3-4%, and AI will structurally disintermediate commercial insurance brokerage within five years.
7. **Margin of safety**: The 40% drawdown on a capital-light business generating $2.5B in owner earnings creates 2.5:1 upside/downside asymmetry, with downside floored by non-discretionary insurance spending and $3.9B of available liquidity.
8. **Thesis breaker**: Sell if organic growth falls below 3% for two consecutive quarters while AssuredPartners synergies run below $100M by end of 2026, confirming that the acquisition destroyed value rather than compounded it.
