## Detailed Analysis

Arthur J. Gallagher & Co. is a global insurance brokerage and risk management services firm. The core product is simple: AJG sits between a business that needs insurance coverage and the insurance carriers that provide it. AJG does not underwrite policies — it earns a commission (typically 10–15% of premium) or a negotiated fee for placing coverage, advising the client, and servicing the relationship year-over-year. This intermediary model means AJG never absorbs insurance losses. It is an information and relationship business, not a risk-bearing one.

The company operates two reportable segments. The **Brokerage segment** (~86% of revenue) encompasses property & casualty brokerage, employee benefits consulting, and retirement plan services for corporate clients. Brokerage reported Q4 2025 revenue growth of 38% year-over-year with adjusted EBITDAC margin of approximately 32%. The **Risk Management segment** (~14% of revenue) provides third-party claims administration — primarily under the Gallagher Bassett brand — and risk management consulting to large corporations and public entities that self-insure portions of their risk. Risk Management reported Q4 organic growth of 6–7%. Both segments generate highly recurring revenue: commercial insurance renews annually and switching costs are material, while claims administration contracts are multi-year.

Revenue growth has been exceptional and consistent. Total revenue compounded from $8.6B in FY2022 to $13.9B in FY2025, a CAGR of approximately 17%. Growth is driven by two identifiable engines: organic growth of 5–7% annually (from insurance premium rate increases, new client wins, and cross-selling) and bolt-on plus larger M&A. The company has completed 776+ acquisitions since 2002, primarily absorbing regional and specialty brokers in fragmented markets. The August 2025 close of AssuredPartners — acquired for approximately $13.8B — was the single largest deal in company history, adding roughly $2.5B in annualized revenue and catapulting AJG into the definitive top-three global brokers.

AJG's focus on the **middle market** is a key driver that is straightforward to evaluate. Marsh McLennan and Aon dominate the Fortune 500 and global multinationals, where client relationships are institutionalized and the selling proposition is global reach and actuarial sophistication. AJG competes most intensely for businesses with $10M–$500M in revenue — a segment large enough to be valuable, fragmented enough that no single broker dominates, and where personal relationships and local expertise matter most. This focus has been articulated consistently by management for over a decade and explains why AJG can continue growing organically without needing to displace the giants, while also providing a vast pipeline of independent regional agencies to acquire.

The revenue model is easy to verify through the income statement: gross margins are stable at ~42% for four consecutive years, operating margins are 18–20%, and free cash flow conversion has averaged approximately 130% of net income over the same period. Capital expenditures at just ~1% of revenue confirm that growth is funded through relationships and people, not physical capital. Stock-based compensation is commendably low at $49M (~0.4% of revenue). This is a business a non-specialist can fully model and verify.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** A capital-light intermediary with sticky client relationships, proven organic growth, and a repeatable M&A engine operating in the large and highly fragmented global insurance distribution market — with $13.9B in scale now backing the platform.
- **Bear case:** The AssuredPartners acquisition has loaded the balance sheet with $13.5B in total debt; any integration failure or prolonged soft market cycle could pressure both margins and cash generation during the digestion period.
- **Confidence:** High — AJG is a textbook two-segment intermediary with a clear revenue model, identifiable key drivers, and nearly 100 years of operating history. The business is entirely understandable.

## Red Flags

- None from a business clarity standpoint. The only complexity worth flagging is separating organic from acquired revenue growth in reported figures — a common issue with active acquirers.

## Score: 9 / 10

AJG is a highly understandable insurance intermediary — commissions and fees, two clean segments, recurring renewal-driven revenue, stable margins, and an identifiable two-engine growth model; one point deducted only because the pace of M&A adds complexity to tracking true organic performance.
