## Detailed Analysis

**Owner Earnings Baseline.** Halma generated approximately £357M in owner earnings in FY2025 (net income £296M + D&A £120M - estimated maintenance capex of ~£59M). The business is remarkably asset-light: total capex of £59M against £2.2B revenue means roughly 2.7% capex intensity. This is a genuine free cash flow machine with FCF conversion of 146% of net income. For our scenario analysis, we use £360M as the normalised owner earnings starting point.

**Scenario Analysis — Three Cases.**

*Bear Case (5% annual OE growth, 22x terminal multiple):* This assumes organic growth slows to mid-single digits as Halma's niche markets mature, acquisition returns normalise, and margin expansion stalls. Owner earnings in Year 5: ~£460M. Applying a 22x multiple (in line with Spirax Group and lower-growth UK industrials) gives a terminal value of ~£10.1B. Discounted at 10% back to today: ~£6.3B equity value, or approximately **1,660 GBp per share**.

*Base Case (10% annual OE growth, 28x terminal multiple):* Halma sustains double-digit growth through its proven combination of organic growth (mid-to-high single digits) plus bolt-on acquisitions (3-5% contribution). Margin nudges higher toward 23%. Owner earnings in Year 5: ~£580M. At 28x: terminal value ~£16.2B. Discounted at 10%: ~£10.1B, or approximately **2,670 GBp per share**.

*Bull Case (14% annual OE growth, 33x terminal multiple):* Halma maintains its exceptional HY2026 momentum, photonics and environmental markets deliver structural tailwinds, and the acquisition machine continues firing on all cylinders. Owner earnings in Year 5: ~£695M. At 33x: terminal value ~£22.9B. Discounted at 10%: ~£14.2B, or approximately **3,770 GBp per share**.

**Multiples in Context.** Halma's trailing P/E of ~42x and forward P/E of ~31x sit at a persistent premium. Its 10-year average P/E is approximately 35x. Key peer comparisons: Roper Technologies trades at a forward P/E of ~25x with EV/EBITDA of ~22.5x; Danaher at a forward P/E of ~24x. Spirax Group (another UK compounder) trades at ~26x forward earnings. Halma's premium of 20-30% over these serial acquirers is partly justified by its higher organic growth rate and more fragmented, lower-risk acquisition targets, but it is not fully explained by fundamentals alone — sentiment and scarcity premium (few listed pure-play safety/environmental compounders) play a role.

**Reverse-Engineering the Implied Growth.** At GBp 3,834 (market cap ~£14.5B) with owner earnings of ~£360M, the market is paying ~40x OE. For this to be justified assuming a 10% discount rate and a 25x exit multiple in Year 10, Halma needs to compound owner earnings at approximately 12-13% annually. This is within historical range (Halma has delivered ~10% revenue CAGR and faster earnings growth), but it requires essentially zero execution stumbles for a decade. The margin of error is thin.

**Perfection vs. Pessimism.** The market is pricing in something close to perfection. HY2026 results (16.7% organic revenue growth, 22.3% EBIT margin) are outstanding and justify current enthusiasm, but these are peak-cycle metrics. Currency headwinds (£74M revenue, £17M profit impact expected in FY2026), potential acquisition fatigue, and any slowdown in the photonics tailwind could compress the multiple. The stock trades 43% above its 52-week low but only 9% below its all-time high — this is a momentum-driven premium on top of an already expensive base.

## Signal Summary
- **Bull case:** Halma is a rare, proven compounder with structural tailwinds in safety regulation, environmental monitoring, and healthcare — mid-teens growth is sustainable and the premium multiple is deserved for a business of this quality.
- **Bear case:** At 40x+ owner earnings, the stock prices in a decade of flawless execution. Any normalisation toward peer multiples (25-28x) would mean 20-30% downside even with continued growth. You are paying a full price for a wonderful business.
- **Confidence:** Medium-High. The business quality is clear; the valuation math is straightforward. The key uncertainty is whether the premium multiple persists or mean-reverts.

## Red Flags
- Forward P/E of 31x is 20-30% above closest peers (Roper, Danaher) without proportionate earnings growth superiority
- Current price sits above both Bear and Base intrinsic value estimates — you are paying for the Bull case
- Sterling strength creating FX headwinds (£17M profit impact in FY2026)
- 10-year mean P/E of ~35x suggests current ~42x trailing is elevated even by Halma's own standards

## Score: 4 / 10
Halma is a genuinely wonderful business trading at a price that offers little margin of safety — the stock is modestly overvalued relative to conservative and base-case intrinsic value estimates, with current pricing requiring sustained Bull-case execution.
