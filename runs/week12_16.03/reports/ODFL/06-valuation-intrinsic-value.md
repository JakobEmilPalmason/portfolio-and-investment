## Detailed Analysis

**Owner Earnings Estimate.** Starting from FY2025 reported figures: net income of $1.0B plus depreciation of ~$450M minus maintenance capex (estimated at ~$475M, roughly 60% of total capex of $790M, given ODFL's heavy investment in service centers and equipment) yields owner earnings of approximately $975M. This aligns closely with the auto-fetched figure of $973M. On 208.4M diluted shares, that is roughly $4.67 per share of owner earnings. At a $182.44 share price, the market is paying 39x current owner earnings — a steep price even for a business of this quality.

**Scenario Analysis.** I model three scenarios for Year 5 (FY2030) owner earnings, then apply a terminal multiple and discount back at 10%:

- **Bear case:** The freight recession lingers into 2027, followed by a slow recovery. Revenue grows at 3% annually to ~$6.4B by 2030. Operating margins stabilize at 24% (current depressed level) rather than returning to prior peaks. Owner earnings reach ~$1.15B, or ~$5.90/share (assuming continued buybacks reduce share count to ~195M). Apply a 22x multiple (below the 10-year average P/E, reflecting slower growth) = $130/share target, discounted back 5 years at 10% = ~$81/share present value.

- **Base case:** Freight cycle inflects in H2 2026 as expected. Revenue recovers to $7.0B by 2030 (~5% CAGR from trough). Operating margins return to 27% as volumes normalize and pricing power reasserts. Owner earnings reach ~$1.45B, or ~$7.45/share. Apply a 27x multiple (10-year average) = $201/share, discounted at 10% = ~$125/share present value.

- **Bull case:** Strong freight recovery combined with market share gains from Yellow's exit (continued tailwind) and pricing discipline. Revenue reaches $7.8B by 2030 (~7% CAGR). Margins return to 29%+. Owner earnings hit ~$1.75B, or ~$9.25/share. Apply a 32x multiple (5-year average, reflecting premium quality) = $296/share, discounted at 10% = ~$184/share present value.

**What Must Be True for Today's Price.** At $182.44, the market is effectively pricing in the bull case — near-full margin recovery, above-GDP revenue growth, and a continued premium multiple. To generate even a 10% annualized return from here, ODFL would need to reach roughly $294/share by 2030, which requires both $7.5B+ revenue and a sustained 30x+ earnings multiple. That is achievable but leaves no room for disappointment.

**Multiples in Context.** ODFL's trailing P/E of 37.8x is above both its 5-year average (~32x) and 10-year average (~27x). Compared to LTL peers: XPO trades at roughly 20–22x forward earnings, SAIA at roughly 25–28x. ODFL deserves a premium for superior operating ratio and capital returns, but the gap has widened. The current EV/EBITDA of 22x is similarly rich relative to history and peers.

**Implied Expectations vs Reality.** The market is pricing ODFL as if the freight recession is a temporary blip and the path back to peak earnings is clear. But FY2025 daily LTL tons declined 4.8% year-over-year in February 2026, suggesting the recovery is not yet underway. There is a real possibility that "mid-cycle" earnings power has shifted lower due to structural changes in freight patterns (nearshoring effects, e-commerce mix shifts). If normalized EPS is closer to $5.50 than $6.50, the stock is meaningfully overvalued.
