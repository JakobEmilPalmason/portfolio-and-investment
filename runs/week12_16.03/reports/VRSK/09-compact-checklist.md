# Compact Checklist — VRSK

**Date:** 2026-03-18

1. **Business**: Verisk sells subscription data, standardized forms, and catastrophe models to ~90% of U.S. P&C insurers, who keep buying because the data is embedded in their regulatory filings, underwriting workflows, and claims processes.
2. **Moat**: Verisk is the legally designated statistical agent in all U.S. states with a 30B+ record contributory data flywheel and switching costs so high that migration would require insurers to rebuild years of regulatory filings, retrain staff, and risk compliance failures.
3. **Economics**: ROIC has averaged ~24% over four years, EBITDA margins hit 56%, and $1.19B in FCF was generated on just $244M of capex — a 4.9x FCF-to-capex ratio with 131% FCF conversion.
4. **Management**: CEO Shavel divested $3.6B of non-core businesses to focus the franchise, reduced the share count 15% in five years with minimal SBC dilution ($54M), and commits 75%+ of FCF to shareholder returns.
5. **Debt risk**: Pro forma leverage is 1.9x EBITDA with all fixed-rate debt, $1.2B annual FCF covers interest 7x over, and even a 30% revenue decline still produces positive FCF — the business survives any plausible recession.
6. **Price**: At $204 and 24x forward earnings, the market must believe Verisk grows like a utility at 6-7% with zero margin expansion — any re-rating toward its historical 30x+ multiple on consensus 20% EPS growth delivers 30%+ upside.
7. **Margin of safety**: The business itself is the margin of safety — 85% recurring revenue, 92% retention, and a quasi-regulatory monopoly that has never experienced a meaningful revenue disruption in 55 years — though the price offers no discount to the $195 bear-case IV.
8. **Thesis breaker**: Organic revenue growth below 4% for two consecutive quarters or customer retention falling below 85% would signal the moat is eroding and the compounding thesis is broken.
