## Detailed Analysis

### Owner Earnings Estimate

Verisk's FY2025 owner earnings (net income + D&A - capex) came in at $991M. Adjusting for the one-time effects of the elevated debt raise and normalizing the tax rate, sustainable owner earnings power sits around $950M-$1.0B currently. With 7% revenue growth, expanding margins (operating margin has climbed from 42% to 44%), and disciplined SBC, owner earnings should compound at 10-13% annually over the next 3-5 years. By FY2030, owner earnings could reach $1.5B-$1.8B under reasonable assumptions.

### Three Scenario Valuation (DCF-Lite)

**Bear Case:** Revenue growth decelerates to 5% as insurance industry digitization slows. Operating margins compress to 40% due to competitive pressure from emerging InsurTech platforms. Year 5 owner earnings: ~$1.25B. Apply a 22x terminal multiple (reflecting lower growth), discount at 10%. IV = ~$195 per share.

**Base Case:** Revenue grows 7% annually (consistent with the last 3 years), operating margins expand modestly to 45%. Owner earnings reach ~$1.55B by Year 5. Apply a 25x terminal multiple (monopolistic data business deserves a premium), discount at 10%. IV = ~$270 per share.

**Bull Case:** Revenue growth accelerates to 9% via pricing power, international expansion, and analytics cross-sell. Margins reach 47%. Owner earnings hit ~$1.85B by Year 5. Apply a 28x terminal multiple, discount at 10%. IV = ~$360 per share.

### Multiples in Context

VRSK's trailing P/E of 31.5x looks elevated in isolation, but the forward P/E of 23.8x is well below its 5-year average of ~32x forward. The EV/EBITDA of 20.8x is similarly compressed from historical levels above 25x. Compared to data analytics peers (S&P Global at ~30x forward P/E, MSCI at ~35x, ICE at ~22x), Verisk is trading at the lower end of its peer group for the first time in years. The P/FCF of 28.1x is reasonable for a business generating nearly 39% FCF margins with 20%+ ROIC.

### What Must Be True for Today's Price

At $204.39, the market is pricing in roughly 6-7% revenue growth with no margin expansion — essentially a continuation of current trends with zero upside surprise. This is a "just the facts" valuation that gives no credit for Verisk's monopoly-like position in insurance data, its pricing power, or the potential for AI-driven analytics upsell. The implied forward P/E of 23.8x on consensus $8.60 EPS suggests the market expects Verisk to grow like a utility, not like a data monopoly.

### Implied Expectations vs Likely Reality

The disconnect between implied expectations (low-single-digit earnings growth embedded in the price) and likely reality (mid-teens EPS growth from revenue growth + margin expansion + buybacks) creates an interesting setup. The $1.5B accelerated buyback at depressed prices should be meaningfully accretive. If Verisk delivers even base-case results, multiple re-expansion from 24x to 28-30x forward P/E alone would drive 20-30% upside from here.

## Signal Summary
- **Bull case:** Market is pricing Verisk as a slow-growth utility when it is actually a data monopoly with expanding margins, accelerating FCF, and aggressive buybacks at depressed prices — multiple re-expansion plus earnings growth could drive 30-40% upside over 2 years.
- **Bear case:** Elevated debt (2.9x Debt/EBITDA), insurance industry cyclicality, and potential competitive disruption from AI-native platforms could keep the multiple compressed, limiting upside to low single digits.
- **Confidence:** Medium-High — Verisk's financial predictability is among the highest in its peer group; the main uncertainty is whether the multiple re-rates, not whether the business delivers.

## Red Flags
- Total debt nearly doubled from $3.2B to $4.9B in one year; interest coverage dropped from 10.9x to 7.9x
- Net income declined 5% YoY despite 7% revenue growth — need to understand the non-recurring items
- P/B of 91.5x reflects minimal tangible book value (equity has been cannibalized by buybacks) — makes balance sheet analysis less meaningful
- The $1.5B accelerated buyback at recently depressed prices is smart capital allocation but adds financial leverage risk
- Revenue growth has been steady at 7% but never meaningfully above — the business may simply be a high-quality mid-single-digit grower

## Score: 7 / 10
VRSK is modestly undervalued at $204 relative to a conservative intrinsic value of ~$195 and a base case of ~$270, offering meaningful upside if the business executes and the multiple normalizes, though the margin of safety is thin on the bear case.
