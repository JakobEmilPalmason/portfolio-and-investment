## Detailed Analysis

**Owner Earnings and Normalization.** Apple's FY2025 owner earnings (net income + D&A - capex) came in at $111.0B, up 16% from $95.7B in FY2024. However, this jump was partly driven by a lower effective tax rate in FY2025 (15.6% vs 24.1% in FY2024, when Apple booked a one-time $10B EU tax settlement). Normalizing for a ~16.5% effective tax rate going forward, sustainable owner earnings are closer to $108-112B. Maintenance capex is difficult to isolate from growth capex, but Apple's asset-light model means D&A of $11.7B likely overstates true maintenance needs. A conservative maintenance capex estimate of $6-7B (roughly half of total capex) would put normalized owner earnings at $117-120B.

**Scenario Analysis.** I model three scenarios for Year 5 (FY2030) owner earnings, applying an exit multiple and discounting back at 10% (my required return):

- **Bear case:** Revenue grows 4% annually (services slows, iPhone saturates, tariff headwinds bite). Operating margins compress 100bp to 31% from regulatory pressure on App Store. Owner earnings reach ~$130B by FY2030. Apply 22x multiple (de-rating toward hardware peer average) = $2.86T enterprise value. Subtract net debt, divide by ~13.2B shares (continued buybacks at reduced pace) = ~$199 per share. Discounted back 5 years at 10% = ~$124 per share today.

- **Base case:** Revenue grows 7% annually (AI-driven iPhone upgrade cycle through FY2027, services maintains 12-14% growth). Margins stable at 32%. Owner earnings reach ~$155B by FY2030. Apply 27x multiple (in-line with 5-year average) = $4.19T enterprise value. ~13.0B shares = ~$307 per share. Discounted back = ~$191 per share today.

- **Bull case:** Revenue grows 10% annually (successful AI monetization, new product categories like AR glasses contribute, India/emerging market expansion). Margins expand to 34% via services mix shift. Owner earnings reach ~$195B by FY2030. Apply 30x multiple (premium sustained) = $5.85T enterprise value. ~12.5B shares = ~$445 per share. Discounted back = ~$276 per share today.

**Multiples in Context.** At 31.4x trailing earnings, Apple trades at a ~15% premium to the S&P 500 (27x) and roughly in line with mega-cap tech peers like Microsoft (32x) but well above Alphabet (22x) and Meta (24x). The premium over Alphabet and Meta is justified by Apple's ecosystem stickiness, hardware + services flywheel, and capital return program. However, Apple's revenue growth rate (6.4% in FY2025) lags behind these peers meaningfully. The EV/EBITDA of 24.0x compares to Apple's own 5-year range of roughly 19-28x, placing it in the upper half. P/FCF at 34.3x is near the top of the 5-year range, partly because FY2025 FCF dipped on higher capex.

**What Must Be True.** For $248 to be justified at a 10% discount rate over 5 years, Apple needs to generate cumulative owner earnings worth roughly $248 x 14.7B shares = $3.65T in present value terms. Working backward, the market is pricing in roughly 10-11% annual owner earnings growth and a terminal multiple of 28-30x — essentially the bull case with no margin compression. This means the market is giving Apple very little credit for risk of regulatory headwinds (DOJ antitrust trial in 2028, EU DMA enforcement), tariff impacts ($3.3B annual cost from new Section 122 tariffs), or AI strategy execution risk. If any of these materialize significantly, the stock is overpriced.

**Buyback Floor.** Apple's $90.7B annual buyback program provides a meaningful return floor. Even if the business merely treads water, ~2% annual share count reduction plus the 0.4% dividend yield gives shareholders roughly 2.5% annual return from capital returns alone. This is real but insufficient to justify the current price if growth disappoints.

## Signal Summary
- **Bull case:** AI-driven iPhone supercycle (FY2026-2028) plus services growth acceleration to 15%+ drives earnings above consensus, justifying current multiples. New product categories (AR, health) open fresh TAMs. Buybacks compound aggressively at lower share prices.
- **Bear case:** iPhone upgrade cycle disappoints, services margins erode under DMA/antitrust pressure, tariffs add $3B+ annual cost, and China competition intensifies. Multiple de-rates from 31x toward 22-25x, resulting in 30-40% downside.
- **Confidence:** Medium — Apple's business quality is beyond question, but the current price leaves almost no room for disappointment. The gap between conservative IV ($124-$191) and the market price ($248) is concerning.

## Red Flags
- FCF declined 9% YoY despite 6.4% revenue growth — capex is rising and may continue as AI infrastructure investment scales
- FY2024 net income was depressed by $10B EU tax settlement, making FY2025 YoY growth look artificially strong; normalized earnings growth is closer to 8-10%
- P/B of 41.3x reflects negative tangible book value reality — this company has been financially engineered through debt-funded buybacks, which works beautifully in good times but reduces balance sheet cushion
- Market is pricing in bull-case growth with no discount for regulatory, tariff, or competitive risks

## Score: 4 / 10
Apple is a world-class business trading at a price that demands near-perfect execution and sustained double-digit growth — conservative intrinsic value estimates ($124-$191) sit 23-50% below the current $248 price, leaving negative margin of safety under base and bear scenarios.
