# Compact Checklist — EW

**Date:** 2026-03-22

1. **Business**: Edwards sells transcatheter heart valves (74% TAVR, ~$30K per procedure) to hospitals, with revenue driven by countable procedure volumes in an aging population where only ~50% of eligible patients currently receive treatment.
2. **Moat**: Physician switching costs (retraining risks patient lives), FDA PMA barriers requiring $100M+ multi-year trials, and 60%+ US market share held for over a decade against well-funded Medtronic limit competitive entry to three approved platforms.
3. **Economics**: Gross margins of 78% and FY2025 FCF of $1.3B (22% margin, 124% conversion) confirm strong cash generation, but ROIC has declined from 21% to 13% over four years and operating margins compressed 750bps to 27%.
4. **Management**: The team returned $4.7B in buybacks over four years and executed a disciplined $4.2B Critical Care divestiture, but the FTC-blocked $945M JenaValve deal and removal of FCF from incentive metrics are capital allocation blemishes.
5. **Debt risk**: With $3.5B net cash, 0.5x debt/EBITDA, and 63x interest coverage, Edwards would generate positive free cash flow even with a 30% revenue decline sustained for two years.
6. **Price**: At $82.50 (25x forward earnings), the market requires Edwards to deliver 8-10% revenue growth with margin recovery to 29%+ -- roughly management's guidance executed without disruption from Medtronic's improving Evolut durability data.
7. **Margin of safety**: The business quality (dominant share, fortress balance sheet, essential products) provides a strong floor, but the price offers zero cushion -- only 5 of 25 sensitivity grid cells produce IV above $82.50, and Monte Carlo gives just 43% probability of being undervalued.
8. **Thesis breaker**: Sell if TAVR US market share drops below 50% on a sustained basis, signaling that Medtronic's superior durability data is converting physician preference and eroding the switching-cost moat.
