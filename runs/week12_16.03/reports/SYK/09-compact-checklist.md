# Compact Checklist — SYK

**Date:** 2026-03-17

1. **Business**: Stryker sells surgical implants, instruments, and Mako robotic systems to hospitals, with 70-75% of revenue effectively recurring through procedure-driven consumables and high surgeon switching costs.
2. **Moat**: Surgeons train for years on Stryker's instruments and Mako robots, hospitals invest $1M+ per installation, and the platform exclusively uses Stryker implants — creating a razor/blade lock-in with 2M+ cumulative procedures.
3. **Economics**: FCF of $4.3B converts at 132% of net income with 64% gross margins, but ROIC of 10% (down from 12.2%) shows the serial M&A model dilutes capital efficiency through goodwill accumulation.
4. **Management**: CEO Kevin Lobo's 14-year tenure produced 11% revenue CAGR and the transformative MAKO acquisition, but insider ownership below 0.5% means this is professional management, not owner-operator stewardship.
5. **Debt risk**: Net debt/EBITDA of 1.9x with $7.1B in liquidity and well-laddered maturities through 2036 means Stryker survives a 30% revenue decline while still covering $607M in annual interest 3x over.
6. **Price**: At $354 and 42x trailing earnings, the market requires Stryker to compound owner earnings at 8.5% annually for five years with no integration stumbles or multiple compression — achievable but not conservative.
7. **Margin of safety**: The stock trades 20% above conservative IV of $295 with only 9% discount to base case IV of $387, offering no price cushion despite strong business durability and near-zero permanent loss probability.
8. **Thesis breaker**: ROIC falling below 8% for two consecutive years would prove the acquisition machine is destroying capital, or demonstrated Mako market share losses to Zimmer ROSA or J&J Velys would invalidate the ecosystem lock-in thesis.
