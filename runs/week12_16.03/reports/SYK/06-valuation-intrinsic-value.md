## Detailed Analysis

**Current Owner Earnings and Earnings Power.** Stryker generated $3.7B in owner earnings (net income + D&A - capex) in FY2025, or $9.67 per share. Free cash flow was even stronger at $4.3B ($11.24/share) due to favorable working capital dynamics. The gap between trailing GAAP EPS ($8.40) and forward EPS ($16.73) is striking and reflects the heavy amortization burden from Stryker's acquisition-heavy strategy. On an adjusted basis stripping out acquisition-related intangible amortization, Stryker's true earnings power is substantially higher than reported GAAP figures. This is a well-understood dynamic in medtech, but investors must track whether acquired businesses actually deliver the synergies embedded in forward estimates.

**Three-Scenario DCF Framework.** For all scenarios, I use owner earnings as the starting base ($9.67/share in FY2025) and discount at 10% (required return). Stryker's terminal multiple is benchmarked against its own 5-year history (18-24x owner earnings) and peer set.

*Bear Case:* Revenue growth decelerates to 5-6% as procedure volumes normalize post-COVID tailwinds, robotic surgery competition intensifies (Intuitive Surgical, Medtronic Hugo), and Inari Medical integration proves slower than expected. Operating margins compress 100bps to ~19% from pricing pressure and R&D reinvestment needs. Year 5 owner earnings reach ~$12.50/share. Applying a 17x multiple (compression toward Medtronic territory) yields a Year 5 value of $212.50, discounted back at 10% to ~$132 per share today. This is an extreme scenario but illustrates fragility at current multiples.

More realistically, a moderate bear yields Year 5 owner earnings of ~$14.00/share at 18x = $252, discounted to ~$295 today.

*Base Case:* Revenue grows 8-9% annually (organic ~6-7% plus bolt-on M&A), consistent with Stryker's decade-long track record. Margins expand modestly to ~21-22% operating as scale benefits and Mako robotic platform penetration continue. Year 5 owner earnings reach ~$16.50/share. Applying a 20x multiple (in-line with current forward valuation) yields Year 5 value of $330, discounted back to ~$387 today.

*Bull Case:* Revenue growth sustains 10-12% via continued share gains in orthopedics (Mako), neurovascular expansion (Inari), and international penetration. Margins expand to 23%+ as the high-margin robotic ecosystem drives recurring instrument revenue. Year 5 owner earnings reach ~$20.00/share. At 22x multiple (premium compounder), Year 5 value of $440, discounted to ~$516 today.

**What the Current Price Implies.** At $354.06, the market is pricing Stryker between the moderate bear and base scenarios. Reverse-engineering: at a 10% discount rate and 20x terminal multiple, the current price implies Year 5 owner earnings of roughly $14.50/share, representing ~8.5% annual growth in owner earnings. This is achievable but not conservative -- it essentially assumes Stryker continues executing at its historical pace with no meaningful hiccups.

**Peer Valuation Context.** Stryker has historically commanded a premium to medtech peers due to superior organic growth and execution. Medtronic trades at ~14x EV/EBITDA (slower growth, turnaround story), Zimmer Biomet at ~12x (narrower portfolio), Boston Scientific at ~19x (high growth but different mix), and Edwards Lifesciences at ~25x (structural heart growth). Stryker's 21x EV/EBITDA reflects its position as perhaps the most consistent large-cap medtech compounder, but the premium is near its upper historical bound.

## Signal Summary
- **Bull case:** Stryker's Mako ecosystem, diversified medtech portfolio, and proven M&A machine can compound owner earnings at 12%+ annually, making today's price a reasonable entry for a $500+ stock in five years.
- **Bear case:** At 42x trailing earnings and 21x EV/EBITDA, the stock prices in near-perfect execution; any growth deceleration, integration stumble, or multiple compression could drive 15-25% downside.
- **Confidence:** Medium — Stryker's business quality is high and well-understood, but the valuation leaves thin room for error, making the intrinsic value call sensitive to growth assumptions.

## Red Flags
- Trailing P/E of 42.2x is the highest in Stryker's recent history, reflecting both acquisition distortions and elevated expectations
- Total debt of $15.9B (up from $13.0B in FY2022) driven by M&A financing; debt/EBITDA at 2.5x is manageable but trending higher
- ROIC of 10.0% in FY2025 is below the 12.2% in FY2024, suggesting recent acquisitions have diluted capital efficiency
- Interest expense nearly doubled from $363M (FY2023) to $607M (FY2025), reflecting higher debt load in a higher-rate environment

## Score: 5 / 10
Stryker is a high-quality business fairly valued at best; at $354, the stock prices in continued strong execution with limited margin of safety, placing it squarely in "fair value" territory rather than the clear undervaluation a Buffett-style investor demands.
