# Compact Checklist — WST

**Date:** 2026-03-22

1. **Business**: West sells elastomeric stoppers, seals, and plungers that go into nearly every injectable drug vial and syringe worldwide, with revenue recurring automatically as long as drugs stay in production.
2. **Moat**: FDA Drug Master File lock-in means switching closure suppliers takes 2-3 years and costs millions, giving West ~70% market share with near-zero customer churn.
3. **Economics**: ROIC of 16.2% comfortably exceeds the 10.8% WACC, FCF conversion hit 95% in FY2025, and the HVP mix shift (now 48% of sales, growing 20% YoY) structurally lifts margins over time.
4. **Management**: A decade of disciplined capital return ($1.16B in buybacks that actually shrank shares from 76M to 72.1M, 33 consecutive dividend increases), but CEO Eric Green's retirement in H2 2026 with no successor named is the top near-term risk.
5. **Debt risk**: Net cash of $470M, debt/EBITDA of 0.4x, and interest coverage of 1,203x mean WST would survive a 30% revenue shock for two years while actually growing its cash balance.
6. **Price**: At $237 (35x earnings), the stock exceeds every cell in the quant sensitivity grid and the Monte Carlo gives only a 1.4% probability that intrinsic value exceeds the current price.
7. **Margin of safety**: The business itself is nearly indestructible (essential products, regulatory lock-in, fortress balance sheet), but at 82% above bear-case IV of $130 and 26% above base-case IV of $175, there is zero price margin of safety.
8. **Thesis breaker**: Sell if organic revenue growth stalls below 4% for two consecutive quarters, a major FDA quality enforcement action occurs, or oral biologics begin displacing injectable delivery in a top-5 drug category.
