# Compact Checklist — OR.PA

**Date:** 2026-03-22

1. **Business**: L'Oreal sells branded beauty consumables (skincare, haircare, cosmetics, fragrances) across 35+ brands from mass to luxury, generating EUR 44B revenue with 74% gross margins from repeat purchases that 150+ countries of consumers replenish routinely.
2. **Moat**: A EUR 14.2B annual advertising budget (32% of sales), EUR 1.4B R&D spend producing 500+ patents per year, and 35+ brands spanning every price tier create a scale and brand wall that no competitor or startup can replicate — confirmed by Morningstar's Wide Moat rating and 74.3% gross margins stable over four years.
3. **Economics**: FCF conversion of 117% on EUR 6.2B net income, gross margins expanding from 72.4% to 74.3% over four years, and CapEx at just 3.4% of revenue prove this is a capital-light cash machine, though ROIC declined from 18.8% to 15.1% due to EUR 6.5B in acquisition-inflated invested capital.
4. **Management**: The Bettencourt Meyers family's 35% stake (held for 117 years) ensures decade-long thinking, and CEO Hieronimus has delivered consistent margin expansion and 20+ years of consecutive dividend growth, though EUR 6.5B in acquisitions (Aesop + Kering Beauty) doubled debt and remain unproven at scale.
5. **Debt risk**: Near-zero net debt (EUR 253M) despite EUR 11.9B gross debt, 24x interest coverage, EUR 9.9B cash, and a product category that declined only 6% during COVID make bankruptcy risk effectively zero even in a severe recession.
6. **Price**: At EUR 347.70 (27x forward earnings, 16% above base IV of EUR 299), the stock requires sustained 7%+ organic growth to justify itself — well above the 4% like-for-like growth L'Oreal actually delivered in 2025.
7. **Margin of safety**: Business quality (74% gross margins, #1 global share, 117-year track record) provides a durable earnings floor, but zero price-based margin of safety exists — the Monte Carlo simulation gives only a 20.6% chance the stock is undervalued, with 2:1 downside-to-upside asymmetry.
8. **Thesis breaker**: Sell if gross margins fall below 70% for two consecutive years, ROIC continues declining below 12% (signaling acquisition-driven moat erosion rather than temporary dilution), or management pursues another transformative deal that pushes net debt above 2x EBITDA.
