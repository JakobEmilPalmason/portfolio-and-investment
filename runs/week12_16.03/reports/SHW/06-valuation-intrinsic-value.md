## Detailed Analysis

**Owner Earnings Estimation.** Sherwin-Williams reported FY2025 net income of approximately $2.6B, D&A of $677M, and capex of $798M. Stripping out growth capex (estimated maintenance capex at ~$500M given ~$24B revenue base and asset-light distribution model), owner earnings approximate $2.4B or ~$9.70 per share on 247M diluted shares. Stock-based compensation of $124M is a real economic cost that further reduces true owner earnings to roughly $9.20/share. At the current $312 price, the stock trades at 34x true owner earnings — a premium multiple for a business guiding to low-single-digit top-line growth and sub-5% EPS growth.

**Bear / Base / Bull Scenarios.** In the bear case, housing remains depressed through 2027 with mortgage rates above 6.5%, raw material tariffs compress margins, and Suvinil integration consumes management attention. Normalized EPS settles at $10.50 and the market re-rates SHW to 22x (closer to its 10-year trough multiple), yielding a bear IV of $231. In the base case, housing activity gradually recovers in late 2026/2027, price increases offset tariff-driven input costs, Suvinil contributes incrementally, and EPS reaches $12.50 by 2027. A 25x multiple (modest premium to peers, below historical average) yields a base IV of $313. In the bull case, pent-up housing demand drives a sharp rebound in paint volumes, Suvinil exceeds expectations, and continued share buybacks accelerate EPS to $14.50 by 2027. At a 28x multiple (below the 5-year average of 34x), the bull IV is $406.

**Multiples in Context.** SHW's 5-year average trailing P/E is 34.3x and its 13-year median EV/EBITDA is 21.8x. The current trailing P/E of 30.4x and EV/EBITDA of 20.7x are modestly below these averages, suggesting the stock is not egregiously expensive by its own history. However, compared to peers — PPG at ~13x forward earnings, RPM at ~16x forward — SHW commands a 50-80% premium. This premium reflects SHW's superior store network, higher ROIC (19.9% vs PPG's ~12%), and stronger pricing power through its Paint Stores Group. The question is whether the premium is justified in a prolonged demand downturn.

**Implied Expectations.** At $312 and a 23x forward P/E on $13.50 consensus, the market is pricing in the consensus case almost exactly. However, the company's own guidance of $11.50-$11.90 (adjusted) is well below the $13.50 Street forward estimate. If $13.50 assumes a housing recovery that management explicitly says they do not see, forward estimates carry downside risk. For the current price to be justified on a 5-year DCF basis (10% discount rate, 3% terminal growth), SHW needs to compound FCF/share at ~8% annually — achievable through buybacks and modest organic growth, but not guaranteed in a flat demand environment.

**Price Context.** The stock sits near its 52-week low of $308.84, down 14.5% in the past month alone. This pullback has compressed the valuation from what was 36-38x trailing earnings earlier in 2026 to a more reasonable 30x. While this improves the setup, it does not create a clear margin of safety given the demand headwinds. Analyst targets cluster around $388-400 (mean $388, median $400), implying 24-28% upside — but these targets typically embed a recovery assumption.

**Valuation Verdict.** SHW is a best-in-class coatings franchise trading at a fair-to-slightly-expensive price relative to near-term fundamentals. The conservative IV ($231) reflects genuine downside risk in a prolonged housing slump, while the base case ($313) suggests the stock is approximately fairly valued today. The bull case ($406) requires a demand recovery that management has not signaled. At $312, the stock offers limited margin of safety on conservative assumptions.

## Signal Summary
- **Bull case:** Housing recovery in H2 2026 drives volume rebound; Suvinil accretive faster than expected; buybacks accelerate EPS to $14+ by 2027; re-rating to 28x+ forward = $400+
- **Bear case:** Third year of flat/declining volumes; tariff-driven margin squeeze; forward estimates cut to $11-12 range; multiple compresses to 22x = $240-265
- **Confidence:** Medium — SHW is a high-quality franchise but near-term earnings visibility is low given housing uncertainty and the gap between Street estimates and company guidance

## Red Flags
- Company guidance ($11.50-$11.90) significantly below Street forward EPS ($13.50) — potential for estimate cuts
- Third consecutive year of "softer-for-longer" demand environment per CEO
- New residential construction expected down mid-single digits in 2026
- Raw material costs rising low-single-digits from tariffs; price increase execution is key
- Debt/EBITDA at 2.9x elevated for a cyclically-exposed business in a downturn
- FCF yield of only 2.5% provides thin cash return cushion

## Score: 5 / 10
SHW is approximately fairly valued at $312 on base-case assumptions, with limited margin of safety and meaningful downside if housing demand does not recover; the premium multiple requires growth that management's own guidance does not support.
