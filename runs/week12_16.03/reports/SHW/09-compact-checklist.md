# Compact Checklist — SHW

**Date:** 2026-03-18

1. **Business**: Sherwin-Williams manufactures paint and sells it through 4,900+ company-owned stores to professional contractors who visit multiple times per week for a consumable product driven by non-discretionary repaint cycles.
2. **Moat**: The captive store network is 6x larger than the nearest competitor (PPG at ~800 stores), creating behavioral switching costs through embedded credit lines, color-matching systems, and workflow habits that lock in fragmented professional painters.
3. **Economics**: ROIC has held at ~20% for four consecutive years, gross margins expanded 670bps to 48.8%, and FCF conversion hit 103% in FY2025 — real cash generation from a structurally advantaged distribution model.
4. **Management**: Capital allocation is disciplined — share count reduced 28% over 16 years through consistent buybacks, Valspar synergies exceeded $320M, and the 47-year dividend streak was raised 10.5% in 2025.
5. **Debt risk**: Total debt of $12.9B (2.9x EBITDA) is elevated but serviceable at 8.2x interest coverage, with a $2.5B undrawn revolver and no near-term maturity wall — survivable in a recession but leaves no margin for a simultaneous housing crash and raw material spike.
6. **Price**: At $312 and 30x trailing earnings, the market must believe housing volumes recover by 2027 and EPS reaches $13.50 — company guidance of $11.50-$11.90 does not support that assumption.
7. **Margin of safety**: The business itself is a fortress (48.8% gross margins, recurring repaint demand, dominant distribution), but the price offers zero discount to base-case IV of $313 and sits 35% above conservative IV of $231.
8. **Thesis breaker**: Operating margins sustained below 14% for two consecutive quarters, signaling permanent loss of pricing power or a raw material cost structure the captive store model can no longer absorb.
