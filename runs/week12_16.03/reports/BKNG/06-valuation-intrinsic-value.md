## Detailed Analysis

**Owner Earnings Estimate.** Booking Holdings generated $5.4B in net income plus $768M in D&A less an estimated $350M in maintenance capex (slightly above reported $322M CapEx to account for intangible maintenance), yielding current owner earnings of approximately $5.8B. FCF at $9.1B is significantly higher due to working capital dynamics and the timing of merchant payments. For valuation purposes, I anchor to owner earnings of $5.8B as the more conservative and repeatable figure. Projected forward, assuming 10-12% earnings growth over the next 3-5 years (driven by continued room night growth, Connected Trip platform expansion, and share buybacks), owner earnings could reach $8.5-9.5B by 2030-2031.

**Scenario Analysis.** In the bear case, I assume revenue growth decelerates to 5-6% annually as EU regulatory headwinds (DMA gatekeeper status, dismantled price parity clauses) erode take rates, AI-driven booking disruption gains traction, and global travel demand normalizes. Operating margins compress to 30% from 34.5%. This yields Year 5 owner earnings of ~$6.5B. Applying a 16x multiple (below historical median, reflecting lower growth) gives an enterprise value of $104B. Discounting back at 10% produces a present value of ~$64.6B, or roughly $2,040 per share. In the base case, revenue grows 8-10% annually with stable margins around 33-34%. Owner earnings reach ~$8.5B in Year 5. A 20x multiple gives EV of $170B, discounting back at 10% yields ~$105.6B, or approximately $3,330 per share. In the bull case, revenue growth continues at 12%+ driven by Connected Trip adoption, alternative accommodations growth, and emerging market penetration. Margins expand to 36% as AI-driven efficiency gains and merchant revenue mix improve. Owner earnings hit ~$10.5B. A 23x multiple on those earnings gives EV of $241.5B, discounting back at 10% yields ~$150B, or approximately $4,730 per share.

**Multiples in Context.** The trailing P/E of 26.7x and forward P/E of 14.1x present a striking gap, suggesting analysts expect a significant earnings jump (forward EPS of $313 vs trailing $166). Against peers, BKNG's P/E premium over Expedia (~19x) is justified by superior margins (34.5% vs 14.7%) and higher ROIC (57% vs ~20%). Airbnb trades at a higher P/S but lower profitability. The current EV/EBITDA of 14.1x is near the bottom of the 10-year range (7.5-64x, median 18.8x), historically a zone where BKNG has offered attractive forward returns. The P/FCF of 21.8x on $9.1B FCF is reasonable for a business compounding free cash flow at 10%+.

**Reverse-Engineering Current Price.** At $4,430 per share ($142.8B market cap, $142.9B EV), the market implies owner earnings growth of roughly 6-7% annually over the next 5 years with a terminal multiple of ~18x. This is conservative relative to both BKNG's historical growth rate and the online travel industry's projected 5-10% CAGR. The market appears to be pricing in meaningful headwinds from EU regulation and potential AI disruption to the OTA model.

**Implied Expectations vs Reality.** The gap between forward PE (14.1x) and trailing PE (26.7x) suggests the market expects a near-doubling of EPS. The forward EPS estimate of $313 implies net income of ~$9.9B on a ~31.7M share base — an 83% increase from FY2025's $5.4B. This is aggressive and likely reflects both operational leverage and continued aggressive buybacks (BKNG retired $6.4B in shares in FY2025). If even half of this earnings expansion materializes, the stock appears undervalued at current levels. The key question is whether EU regulatory costs and potential take-rate compression will prevent this earnings trajectory from playing out.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** Market is over-discounting regulatory headwinds; at 14x forward earnings, BKNG offers a rare chance to buy a dominant platform business at a mid-teens multiple with 10%+ FCF growth.
- **Bear case:** EU DMA enforcement erodes pricing power, AI agents disintermediate OTA platforms, and the forward EPS estimates prove overly optimistic, leaving the stock fairly valued at best.
- **Confidence:** Medium — The financial data is clear and the business quality is exceptional, but the magnitude of the forward EPS jump creates uncertainty, and regulatory outcomes in Europe remain genuinely unpredictable.

## Red Flags

- Net income declined YoY (FY2025 $5.4B vs FY2024 $5.9B) despite 13.5% revenue growth — investigate whether this reflects one-time charges or structural cost pressure
- $19.3B in total debt is high in absolute terms, though manageable at 2.1x EBITDA
- Share-based compensation of $617M partially dilutes the benefit of buybacks
- Forward EPS estimates imply an 83% earnings jump that may prove aggressive

## Score: 7 / 10

BKNG is modestly undervalued on a base-case basis, with the current price sitting between the bear and base intrinsic value estimates and well below the bull case; the market is pricing in below-historical-average growth that appears overly conservative for a business of this quality and cash generation profile.
