# Compact Checklist — BKNG

**Date:** 2026-03-18

1. **Business**: Booking Holdings is an asset-light global toll booth on travel, earning commissions and merchant fees on 1.2 billion room nights annually across 220+ countries, with $26.9B revenue growing 13% YoY driven by the shift to its higher-margin merchant payments platform.
2. **Moat**: A two-sided network effect with 29 million property listings and ~40% OTA market share (2x Expedia) creates a self-reinforcing flywheel that has driven ROIC from 26% to 57% over three years, though $7-8B in annual Google marketing spend reveals the demand-side moat is thinner than the supply side.
3. **Economics**: 57% ROIC, 34% FCF margins on just 1.2% CapEx intensity, and $9.1B in free cash flow converting at 168% of net income confirm this is one of the best economic engines among large-cap internet businesses.
4. **Management**: CEO Fogel has returned $23.5B to shareholders via buybacks over four years while avoiding empire-building acquisitions, consistently under-promising and over-delivering with 97% equity-linked compensation ensuring alignment.
5. **Debt risk**: Net debt of only $1.5B against $9.1B annual FCF and $17.2B cash means the company could survive a 30% revenue decline for two years while still covering all debt obligations, though declining interest coverage (6.6x to 5.2x) from $19.3B gross debt warrants monitoring.
6. **Price**: At $4,430, the market prices in only 6-7% annual earnings growth — well below demonstrated rates — but the stock trades 33% above base-case IV of $3,330 and 117% above bear-case IV of $2,040, meaning you need the bull case to be right.
7. **Margin of safety**: The business itself provides a margin of safety through 57% ROIC, $9.1B FCF, and an asset-light model that survived COVID, but the price offers no margin of safety — downside of 40-57% in a recession/regulatory scenario versus 7-30% upside to bull IV.
8. **Thesis breaker**: Sell if EU regulation permanently compresses European take rates below 12% (currently ~15%), or if AI booking agents capture more than 10% of accommodation booking volume, as either would structurally impair the intermediary value Booking provides.
