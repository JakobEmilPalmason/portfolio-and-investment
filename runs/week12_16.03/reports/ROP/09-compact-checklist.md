# Compact Checklist — ROP

**Date:** 2026-03-23

1. **Business**: Roper owns ~28 niche vertical software businesses (legal, healthcare, freight, government) that serve as the operational backbone of their customers' daily workflows, producing 80%+ recurring revenue with 95%+ gross retention.
2. **Moat**: Switching costs are enormous because replacing system-of-record software (court management, healthcare compliance, freight logistics) requires 6-12 months of disruption, data migration, and retraining — confirmed by 95%+ retention and stable 69% gross margins for four consecutive years.
3. **Economics**: FCF of $2.4B on $7.9B revenue (31% margin) with only $105M capex (1.3% of sales) and 158% FCF-to-net-income conversion makes this one of the most capital-efficient large-cap businesses in the S&P 500.
4. **Management**: A 20-year track record of deploying ~$25B in acquisitions that transformed a cyclical industrial into an 80% recurring-revenue software portfolio, with 96% at-risk CEO compensation, though insider ownership at ~0.4-3% is disappointingly low.
5. **Debt risk**: Debt/EBITDA of 3.0x with $9.3B total debt is serviceable given $2.4B annual FCF and well-laddered maturities, but interest coverage has deteriorated from 11.6x to 7.0x in two years due to acquisition-funded borrowing.
6. **Price**: At $354 (15x forward earnings vs 5-year average of 32x), the market is pricing in permanent 2-3% organic growth and zero accretive M&A — a structural impairment thesis that contradicts the 80% recurring revenue base and $6B deployment capacity.
7. **Margin of safety**: The stock trades 14% below the quant bear-case IV of $411 and below every cell in the 25-scenario sensitivity grid (floor $583), with a 4:1 upside/downside ratio and near-zero permanent capital loss risk across 28 diversified software businesses.
8. **Thesis breaker**: Sell if net revenue retention drops below 100% for two consecutive years, signaling that AI-native competitors are successfully unbundling Roper's vertical software moats and eroding the switching-cost architecture that underpins the entire thesis.
