# Compact Checklist — CPRT

**Date:** 2026-03-22

1. **Business**: Copart runs online auctions for totaled vehicles on behalf of insurance companies, earning fees on both sides of a transaction that recurs because cars will always crash and modern vehicles are increasingly too expensive to repair.
2. **Moat**: A duopoly with IAA controlling ~80% of US salvage auctions, protected by 250+ owned yards that take decades of zoning and permitting to replicate, plus network effects where 1M+ global buyers drive ASPs 3x higher than competitors.
3. **Economics**: Adjusted ROIC on operating assets is 29-32% (headline 16.6% is depressed by $4.8B idle cash), the consignment model requires no inventory risk, and owner earnings hit $1.6B in FY2025 on 45% gross margins.
4. **Management**: Founder Willis Johnson holds ~$2B in stock as Chairman, CEO Liaw's equity is locked up for 10 years, and SBC at 0.8% of revenue is among the lowest in corporate America — though the $4.8B cash pile earns below cost of equity.
5. **Debt risk**: With $5.1B net cash, zero meaningful debt, and an 8.4x current ratio, Copart would generate positive free cash flow even through a 30% revenue decline sustained for two years.
6. **Price**: At 21x trailing earnings versus a 5-year average of 35x, the market is pricing in 6% perpetual growth — roughly half Copart's 10% historical revenue CAGR — meaning you need only base-case execution for the stock to work.
7. **Margin of safety**: The business cannot go to zero (duopoly, $5.1B cash, $1.2B annual FCF), but there is no price MOS against the bear case IV of $23, so the protection is business quality rather than cheap entry.
8. **Thesis breaker**: Sell if the DOJ investigation results in structural restrictions on international buyer access, or if ROIC on operating assets falls below 15% for two consecutive years signaling the duopoly's pricing power is broken.
