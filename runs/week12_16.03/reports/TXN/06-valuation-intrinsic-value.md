## Detailed Analysis

**Current vs Normalized Owner Earnings.** TXN's FY2025 owner earnings of ~$2.5B ($2.75/share) are deeply suppressed by the elevated capex cycle ($4.5B) and a revenue base ($17.7B) still well below the FY2022 peak of $20B. The company has now guided 2026 capex down to $2-3B (likely trending toward the low end), which instantly frees $1.5-2.5B of cash flow. At guided >$8 FCF/share on ~910M shares, that implies ~$7.3B of FCF in 2026 — a dramatic inflection. Looking further out to FY2027-2028, if revenue recovers to $20-22B (in line with the analog cycle turn and TXN's data center growth), gross margins expand back toward 62-65% as 300mm capacity ramps, and capex normalizes at $2-2.5B, normalized owner earnings could reach $7.5-9.0B ($8.20-9.90/share). This is the core thesis: TXN is transitioning from a heavy investment phase to a harvest phase.

**Scenario Analysis.** I model three 5-year scenarios discounted at 10% (required return for a high-quality compounder):

*Bear Case:* Analog recovery stalls; FY2028 revenue reaches only $19B. Gross margins recover to 60%. Capex stays at $2.5B. Year 5 owner earnings ~$6.5B ($7.15/share). Apply 20x multiple = $143/share. Discounted back at 10% = ~$112. This scenario assumes the analog cycle disappoints and competitive pressures limit margin recovery.

*Base Case:* Revenue recovers to $21.5B by FY2028, in line with mid-single-digit CAGR from trough. Gross margins reach 63%. Capex at $2.2B. Year 5 owner earnings ~$8.5B ($9.35/share). Apply 23x multiple = $215/share. Discounted back at 10% = ~$168.

*Bull Case:* Revenue reaches $24B+ by FY2028-2029, driven by analog recovery plus data center/AI power management (70% YoY growth segment). Gross margins hit 66% on full 300mm utilization. Capex at $2B. Year 5 owner earnings ~$10.5B ($11.55/share). Apply 25x multiple = $289/share. Discounted back at 10% = ~$226.

**Multiples in Context.** TXN's trailing P/E of 35.6x sits well above its 10-year median of ~23x and its 10-year average of ~24-28x. However, the trailing earnings are cyclically depressed. On a forward P/E of 24.5x (consensus $7.91 EPS), it looks more reasonable but still above median. Historically, TXN has commanded a PE range of 15-41x over the past decade. Compared to ADI at ~58x trailing and ~28x forward, TXN screens cheaper. Compared to the broader S&P 500 at ~21x forward, TXN demands a premium justified by its capital-light (post-cycle) analog moat, high ROIC potential, and secular tailwinds.

**What Must Be True.** For $194 to be fair value, TXN needs to deliver roughly $8-9 in EPS within 2-3 years at a 22-24x multiple. This requires: (1) revenue recovery to $20B+, (2) gross margin expansion back to 60%+, and (3) capex normalization to ~$2-2.5B. The company has explicitly guided toward all three of these, and the analog cycle turn provides a macro tailwind. The risk is timing — if recovery takes longer than expected, the stock could stagnate at these multiples.

**Implied Expectations vs Likely Reality.** At $194 and a forward PE of 24.5x on $7.91 consensus EPS, the market is pricing in a solid recovery but not a full return to FY2022 peak earnings ($9.41 EPS). Given the 300mm cost advantage, data center growth, and cycle turn signals, achieving $8+ EPS in FY2026-2027 seems plausible. The market's expectations appear reasonable but leave limited margin of safety at today's price — much of the recovery is already priced in.

## Signal Summary
- **Bull case:** Capex normalization + analog cycle recovery + data center tailwind drive FCF to $8-10/share by 2027, with 300mm cost advantage widening margins beyond historical peaks.
- **Bear case:** Analog recovery disappoints, inventory corrections persist, and $194 price already embeds the harvest year thesis with little room for error.
- **Confidence:** Medium — the capex inflection is management-guided and highly visible, but revenue recovery timing and margin trajectory depend on the broader analog cycle.

## Red Flags
- Trailing P/E of 35.6x is expensive on current earnings; any delay in recovery punishes the stock disproportionately
- Gross margins have compressed from 68.8% (FY2022) to 57.0% (FY2025) — the 300mm ramp must deliver as promised
- Net debt of $10.8B is meaningfully higher than pre-capex-cycle levels
- The $8 FCF/share target is a management aspiration, not a guarantee — it requires revenue recovery
- FY2025 ROIC of 17.7% vs 38.9% in FY2022 shows the business is currently earning well below its potential

## Score: 6 / 10
TXN is a world-class analog franchise entering a highly visible capex normalization, but at $194 the stock prices in much of the recovery — base-case IV of $168 suggests limited upside, and only the bull case offers meaningful margin of safety above today's price.
