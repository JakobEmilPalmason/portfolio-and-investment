## Detailed Analysis

**Returns on Capital — Still Elite, But Resetting to a New Plateau.** The FY2025 ROIC of 36.0% is down sharply from the 70.5% peak of FY2023, and the headline reason is the denominator: invested capital rose from DKK 128B to DKK 316B in two years as the company bought three Catalent fill-finish sites for roughly DKK 75B, started a USD 4.1B build in Clayton NC, expanded Kalundborg (~DKK 42B over multiple years), and upgraded Chartres in France (~DKK 16B). ROE fell from 88.1% to 60.7%; ROA from 26.6% to 18.9%. None of these returns are bad — they are still top-decile pharma — but the level matters for valuation. Two years ago Novo Nordisk looked like a 60%+ ROIC business; today it looks like a 30-40% ROIC business that may settle at 25-30% once the MFN-priced revenue runs through a higher asset base. That is still excellent in absolute terms, but it is no longer the freakishly capital-light compounder it was in 2022-2023. Q1 2026 results suggest the operating engine has not deteriorated — the depressed FCF is a capex-cycle artifact, not a return-on-capital problem.

**Margin Structure — Compression Is Real but Less Severe Than Feared.** Gross margins fell 370 bps to 81.0% in FY2025 from 84.7% in FY2024, a meaningful step-down attributable to (a) the Catalent sites operating at sub-scale utilization during integration, (b) early Wegovy pill production at higher unit cost, and (c) early MFN price effects in the late-2025 quarters. Operating margin compressed 290 bps to 41.3%. The Q1 2026 print showed adjusted operating profit margin near 34% on an adjusted basis (DKK 32.9B on adjusted sales ~DKK 97B), well below the FY2024 peak but stabilizing. R&D rose to DKK 52B (FY2025), up 60% in two years, driven by CagriSema (next-gen weekly obesity injection), the Akero MASH program, oral GLP-1 line extensions, and several earlier-stage cardiovascular and Alzheimer's readouts. This is real investment, not cost bloat, and it expands the post-semaglutide-exclusivity option set. The risk is that R&D stays at 16-17% of revenue while revenue declines, structurally compressing margins for 2-3 years before pipeline contribution shows up.

**Cash Generation — Reported FCF Misleads; Owner Earnings Is the Right Lens.** Reported FY2025 FCF of DKK 29B and FCF conversion of 28% looks terrible. It is not, because DKK 90B of capex includes roughly DKK 68B of growth capex (Clayton, Kalundborg, Chartres, Catalent capacity ramp). The quant model's owner-earnings calculation, which strips out growth capex and keeps maintenance at ~DKK 22B, lands at DKK 102B (essentially flat with FY2024). This is the right way to look at the business. Q1 2026 already produced DKK 12.8B of reported FCF — annualizing naively to ~DKK 50B — and capex guidance of approximately DKK 55B for 2026 implies reported FCF should approach DKK 60-65B as the capex wave subsides. The reported buyback of just DKK 1.4B in FY2025 has already been replaced by a DKK 15B program for 2026 (DKK 3.8B first tranche completed in May 2026), confirming management views the capex peak as behind. Q1 2026 cash returns to shareholders (dividends + buyback) totaled DKK 37.7B with full-year expectation above DKK 60B — a strong tell.

**Capital Intensity — Permanently Higher, Now Plateauing.** Novo Nordisk used to be one of the most asset-light pharmaceutical businesses in the world (capex/sales of 8% in FY2022). FY2025 capex was DKK 90B on revenue of DKK 309B, a ratio of 29%. The 2026 guide at DKK 55B brings the ratio back near 18% — still triple the 2022 level. The Catalent sites (Anagni Italy, Bloomington US, Brussels Belgium) are operational but ramping; Clayton NC comes online over 2026-2027; Kalundborg API expansion is a multi-year program. Even at steady state, maintenance capex on a DKK 300B+ tangible asset base is likely DKK 25-30B annually, double the pre-2022 levels. This is the central structural change in the business model — Novo is no longer Coca-Cola, it is a vertically integrated biologic manufacturer. The trade-off is supply security and oral-formulation control (critical for Wegovy pill economics), which the pre-Catalent business arguably could not deliver. Whether that trade-off creates value depends entirely on whether GLP-1 volumes at new lower prices fill the capacity.

**Revenue Quality — Recurring, But Per-Patient Economics Are Permanently Lower.** Diabetes and obesity remain chronic conditions; once a patient starts GLP-1 therapy, adherence is sticky. The volume dynamic in Q1 2026 was overwhelmingly positive: Wegovy pill volume launch is the largest in US GLP-1 history, and overall Novo branded GLP-1 share now sits around 60-65% of new US prescriptions. However, the MFN pricing agreement permanently reset list prices: injectable Wegovy/Ozempic at $245/month for Medicare and $350/month direct-to-consumer on TrumpRx (down from $1,000-1,350); oral GLP-1 initiation doses at $149. This is roughly a 65-75% reduction in US list price for the affected channels. Net revenue per patient also drops, though less severely than list because the 340B and Medicaid rebate burden is partially alleviated under the new framework. The Q1 2026 result — adjusted revenue down only 4% despite the price reset — implies volume growth is offsetting much (not all) of the price headwind. The structural conclusion: revenue is still recurring, but per-patient revenue is permanently 30-50% lower than the 2024 peak, with volume growth required to close the gap.

**Operating Leverage — Negative Through 2026, Restoring Thereafter.** FY2025 revenue grew 6% but operating income fell 0.5% (negative operating leverage) as commercial and R&D costs scaled faster than sales. The 2026 outlook (-4% to -12% adjusted sales decline) implies operating leverage remains negative in the short term. However, two forces should restore positive leverage from 2027 onwards: (a) capex steps down from DKK 90B to DKK 55B and then likely toward DKK 35-40B, freeing cash flow; and (b) Wegovy pill economics improve as Catalent sites reach scale and oral semaglutide displaces some of the more expensive injectable supply chain. The longer the MFN price regime holds without further cuts, the more reliable this leverage restoration becomes.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** Q1 2026 confirms the operating engine survived the MFN reset with adjusted sales down only 4%; Wegovy pill is the fastest GLP-1 launch in US history; capex peak is behind; FCF recovery to DKK 60B+ in 2026 underwrites a resumed DKK 15B buyback and continued double-digit dividend.
- **Bear case:** Even after the better-than-feared Q1, full-year 2026 still guides to revenue decline; ROIC has structurally reset from ~60% to 30-35%; gross margins are 370 bps lower than two years ago and may not fully recover; pipeline (CagriSema, MASH) must deliver to justify the DKK 52B R&D run-rate.
- **Confidence:** Medium-High — The Q1 2026 print materially de-risks the near-term scenario and confirms the engine is intact; remaining uncertainty is the steady-state margin and ROIC level once Catalent fully integrates and MFN pricing flows through a full year.

## Red Flags

- Gross margin compression of 370 bps in FY2025 is real and not fully a one-off; Catalent integration drag plus MFN pricing means 80-82% gross margins, not 84-85%, are the new normal
- R&D at 16.8% of revenue is the highest in company history; if pipeline assets (CagriSema, MASH) disappoint, this is a permanent margin headwind
- Reported FY2025 FCF conversion of 28% would be a crisis at most companies; here it is a capex-cycle artifact, but it does mean the business is harder to value on a backward FCF multiple
- Operating margin compression of 290 bps in FY2025 + Q1 2026 adjusted operating margin around 34% (vs 44% FY2024 peak) confirm a structural reset, not a one-quarter blip
- The narrowed 2026 guide is still a revenue decline — the first in over a decade — and the magnitude of decline (4-12%) leaves wide uncertainty on full-year operating profit

## Score: 7 / 10

Novo Nordisk remains an elite economic engine (36% ROIC, 81% gross margin, DKK 102B owner earnings, DKK 60B+ planned shareholder returns), and Q1 2026 confirmed the post-MFN business is structurally healthier than the early-2026 guide implied; the score holds at 7 because the structural reset is real — gross margins, operating margins, and ROIC have all stepped down to new plateaus from 2023-2024 peaks — and the elevated R&D and capital base mean normalized economics will be materially lower than the recent peak, even in the bull case.
