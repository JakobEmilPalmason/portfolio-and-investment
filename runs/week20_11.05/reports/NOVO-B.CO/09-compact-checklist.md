# Compact Checklist — NOVO-B.CO

**Date:** 2026-05-11

1. **Business**: Novo Nordisk sells semaglutide-based GLP-1 drugs (Ozempic, Wegovy injectable and oral) plus a legacy insulin franchise to chronic diabetes and obesity patients globally, with Q1 2026 revenue of DKK 96.8B (+32% CER) and oral Wegovy already at 65% of US new prescriptions in its first full quarter.
2. **Moat**: A peptide manufacturing footprint of 14 sites built with DKK 90B of FY2025 capex (Catalent, Kalundborg, Clayton, Chartres) plus 154 granted semaglutide patents extending to 2042 — a barrier that would cost a competitor $15–25B and 5–7 years to replicate, even though Eli Lilly's tirzepatide currently holds ~60% of US total GLP-1 share.
3. **Economics**: FY2025 produced 81% gross margin, 41% operating margin, 36% ROIC and DKK 102B of owner earnings (reported FCF of DKK 29B is a capex-cycle artifact, not an earnings problem) — elite by any pharma standard, even after a structural reset from the freakish 70% ROIC of FY2023.
4. **Management**: New CEO Maziar Mike Doustdar (in role since August 2025) cut 9,000 jobs for DKK 8B in run-rate savings, restarted a DKK 15B buyback, and delivered a Q1 2026 beat plus guidance raise — all under the permanent stewardship of the Novo Nordisk Foundation, which controls 28% of equity and 77% of votes on a multi-century time horizon.
5. **Debt risk**: With Net Debt/EBITDA of 0.6x, interest coverage of 32x, Aa3/AA credit ratings (among the highest in global pharma), and DKK 119B of annual operating cash flow, Novo would survive a 30% revenue decline with coverage still above 12x — financial distress is not a realistic scenario.
6. **Price**: At DKK 300 the market is pricing roughly the "decline scenario" (IV ~DKK 240 — flat-to-negative revenue for 5 years, margins compressing to 30%, exit multiple below 10x EV/EBITDA), so today's price only makes sense if you believe Lilly's retatrutide takes another 10+ points of share AND US MFN pricing keeps ratcheting down AND oral generics arrive before 2030.
7. **Margin of safety**: 35% discount to my conservative IV of DKK 460 (43% to my probability-weighted DKK 525) plus a 5.5% dividend yield that pays you to wait, on top of a business with 81% gross margins and a balance sheet that can't be broken — even a re-test to the 52-week low of DKK 220 leaves an upside-to-downside ratio of roughly 2.8x.
8. **Thesis breaker**: Sell if Lilly's retatrutide ships with clearly best-in-class efficacy (>26% weight loss with clean safety) AND Novo's amycretin disappoints in Phase 3 (2027–2028), OR if semaglutide faces a class-wide safety signal forcing label restriction, OR if ROIC falls below 15% for two consecutive years signaling permanent capex impairment.
