# Compact Checklist — MSFT

**Date:** 2026-05-11

1. **Business**: Microsoft rents productivity software (M365, Dynamics, LinkedIn) and cloud infrastructure (Azure) to enterprises on recurring subscription and consumption contracts, plus sells Windows and gaming to consumers — FY25 revenue was $281.7B with the commercial cloud now exceeding $54B per quarter and Azure growing 40% YoY.
2. **Moat**: Extreme M365 switching costs (identity, email, documents, Teams, and tenant-trained Copilot data are embedded in 64% of the Fortune 500), reinforced by hyperscaler scale economics no challenger can match at $190B/yr capex, LinkedIn/GitHub network effects, and a 30%+ ROIC sustained for four consecutive years.
3. **Economics**: ROIC has held at 30%+ for four straight years, operating margin expanded to 45.6% in FY25, and gross margin stayed in the 68-70% band despite Azure mix — though FCF conversion has fallen from 89.6% (FY22) to 70.3% (FY25) as $64.6B of capex (heading to ~$190B in CY26) absorbs cash that the underlying engine still earns.
4. **Management**: Nadella and Hood have a twelve-year track record of 26% annual stock compounding, best-in-class compensation design (100% performance equity, >95% performance-based, no employment contracts, robust clawback), disciplined buybacks (more when cheap, less at $400+), and a successful M&A book (LinkedIn, GitHub, Activision) with no Ballmer-style writedowns.
5. **Debt risk**: Fortress balance sheet — $60.6B total debt against $160.2B EBITDA (0.4x leverage), 52.8x interest coverage, AAA credit rating, $94.6B cash, and long-term debt actually shrinking even as capex tripled, so survival under any plausible downturn is not a question.
6. **Price**: At $409.58 the market is pricing roughly 12% revenue growth for five years fading to 3% terminal at a ~10.4% WACC, with stable 45% operating margins and the $190B AI capex bet earning at least its cost of capital — essentially the adjusted Base IV of $420.
7. **Margin of safety**: There is no price MOS — current price is -46% versus adjusted Bear IV ($280) and only +2.5% versus Base IV ($420); the saving grace is the business margin of safety from the AAA balance sheet, recurring revenue mix, multi-franchise diversification, and Monte Carlo P(IV > Price) of 58%.
8. **Thesis breaker**: Sell if Azure growth falls below 20% for two consecutive quarters with no offsetting mix story, or if operating margin compresses below 38%, or if management materially walks back AI monetization claims, or if a single acquisition exceeds 10% of market cap — these signal that the $190B capex bet is failing on returns.
