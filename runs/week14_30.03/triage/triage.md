## Deep Dive Shortlist (8)

All 8 picks are un-analyzed names with quality_score >= 7 and genuine conviction. Ranked by conviction.

### 1. VRTX — Vertex Pharmaceuticals | quality: 9 | valuation: 6 | balance sheet: 9

**Why:** Monopoly on cystic fibrosis treatment (Trikafta/Kaftrio) with no generic threat until 2037+. $10B+ revenue, net cash balance sheet, 70%+ gross margins. Pain pipeline (suzetrigine approved) adds a second franchise leg. At $447 vs $554 analyst consensus, the valuation setup is reasonable for a near-monopoly. Single-franchise concentration is the key risk to assess.

### 2. REGN — Regeneron Pharmaceuticals | quality: 8 | valuation: 7 | balance sheet: 9

**Why:** Dupixent is a $15B+ franchise expanding into new indications globally. Pipeline breadth (Dupixent extensions, oncology, obesity) is unusual for biotech. Net cash balance sheet, strong FCF generation. At $757 vs ~$800 analyst targets, modestly undervalued. Needs proper assessment of Dupixent biosimilar timeline and pipeline probability-weighting.

### 3. NFLX — Netflix | quality: 8 | valuation: 6 | balance sheet: 7

**Why:** Global streaming scale with 300M+ subscribers and proven pricing power (multiple price hikes absorbed without churn). Ad tier scaling rapidly. P/E compressed from 47x to ~30x — a genuine multiple de-rating while fundamentals strengthened. Content library is an irreplaceable asset. Needs valuation work to determine if the compressed multiple is justified or an opportunity.

### 4. GE — GE Aerospace | quality: 8 | valuation: 6 | balance sheet: 7

**Why:** Duopoly position in aero engines (LEAP, GEnx, GE9X) with 20+ year aftermarket revenue streams. $8B+ guided FCF for 2026. Post-separation pure-play with cleaner financials. At $273 vs $329 consensus target, reasonable entry. Supply chain constraints and FAA scrutiny are the risks to size. Classic Buffett-style franchise with long-duration recurring revenue.

### 5. AMGN — Amgen | quality: 7 | valuation: 6 | balance sheet: 5

**Why:** $33B revenue base with diversified portfolio, but the real attraction is MariTide — a potential blockbuster in the obesity space competing with LLY/NVO. The Horizon acquisition added leverage (~4x debt/EBITDA) that needs careful assessment. If MariTide data is strong, the optionality is enormous. Balance sheet risk is the key differentiator vs other biotech picks.

### 6. GE — Deere & Co. | quality: 7 | valuation: 6 | balance sheet: 6

**Why:** Dominant ag equipment franchise with precision agriculture software creating a recurring revenue moat that is deepening with every connected machine sold. Cyclical downturn in ag may be creating an entry point. Dealer network and aftermarket parts are durable competitive advantages. Needs assessment of cyclical trough valuation vs normalized earnings power.

### 7. PANW — Palo Alto Networks | quality: 7 | valuation: 5 | balance sheet: 7

**Why:** Cybersecurity platform consolidation leader riding the secular tailwind of increasing threat complexity. Platformization strategy (free firewalls to win full-stack customers) is bold and potentially moat-widening. $4B+ ARR growing rapidly. Competitive dynamics with CrowdStrike and the transition from billings to ARR metrics need proper analysis. Valuation likely stretched but the secular tailwind is real.

### 8. BX — Blackstone | quality: 8 | valuation: 5 | balance sheet: 7

**Why:** Largest alternative asset manager with $1T+ AUM and a fee-related earnings base that provides recurring, management-fee-driven income independent of performance fees. Retail capital channel (BREIT, BXPE) is a structural growth engine. The AUM flywheel is genuinely durable. Needs assessment of performance fee volatility, private credit crowding risk, and whether the current multiple prices in the growth.
