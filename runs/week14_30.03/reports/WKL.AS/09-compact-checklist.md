# Compact Checklist — WKL.AS

**Date:** 2026-04-07

1. **Business**: Wolters Kluwer sells subscription compliance software and clinical decision tools to doctors, accountants, lawyers, and banks — 83% recurring revenue from products professionals cannot operate without.
2. **Moat**: Deeply embedded workflows with 90%+ retention and high switching costs protect pricing power, though Morningstar downgraded to Narrow in 2026 because generative AI could erode the ~30-40% of revenue tied to curated information products like UpToDate.
3. **Economics**: ROIC expanded from 18.1% to 24.6% over four years, gross margins hit 73.5%, and FCF conversion exceeds 100% of net income every year — this is an elite asset-light compounder.
4. **Management**: McKinstry delivered a 10x market cap over 23 years through disciplined bolt-on M&A, EUR 1.1B annual buybacks with only EUR 26M in SBC, and a print-to-digital transformation, but she retired in February 2026 and successor Caywood is untested.
5. **Debt risk**: Net debt/EBITDA of 1.7x with 15x interest coverage and an S&P A- rating means even a 50% EBIT decline would leave coverage above 7x — financial distress is nearly impossible.
6. **Price**: At 9.7x EV/EBITDA (half its 5-year average and below every cell in the 25-point sensitivity grid), the market must believe either margins permanently collapse to ~15% or revenues structurally decline — neither is supported by 6% organic growth and expanding margins in FY2025.
7. **Margin of safety**: The stock trades 22% below bear-case IV of EUR 86 with a 10% owner earnings yield, 3.86% dividend, and 100% Monte Carlo probability of undervaluation across 10,000 simulations.
8. **Thesis breaker**: Organic revenue growth turns negative for two consecutive years, signaling AI-driven commoditization has overcome switching costs and regulatory tailwinds.
