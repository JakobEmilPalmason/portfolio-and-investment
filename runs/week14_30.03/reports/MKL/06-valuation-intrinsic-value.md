## Detailed Analysis

Markel trades at $1,899, or roughly 1.3x book value of $1,477 per share. For a company generating 14% ROE in 2025 with a five-year compound annual growth rate of 15% in intrinsic value per share, this is a modest multiple. Berkshire Hathaway, the closest comparable, has historically traded between 1.4-1.6x book in similar ROE environments. The P/E of 11.2x trailing earnings ($169 EPS in FY2025) appears cheap, though forward estimates of ~$126 reflect a genuine earnings air pocket in 2026 as the reinsurance exit removes roughly $2B in gross written premium and the Hagerty partnership shifts to fronting.

The quant DCF model anchors base IV at $2,754 per share, using an 8.1% WACC and 15x exit multiple on owner earnings of $2.2B. This implies roughly 45% upside from current levels. The bear case ($1,680) assumes revenue contraction of ~5.6% and a higher 10.1% discount rate -- even here, the stock only falls to roughly $1,999 at the P5 Monte Carlo percentile, suggesting the model's true floor is somewhat above the mechanical bear IV. The bull case of $4,015 captures the scenario where Markel's specialty insurance refocusing drives combined ratios below 93% while Markel Ventures and the equity portfolio compound at healthy rates.

The sensitivity grid is instructive. At the base WACC of 8.1%, the stock needs revenue growth to decline by more than 5.6% annually to push IV below current price. Even at a punitive 10.1% WACC, base-case growth still yields IV of $2,394 -- 26% above the current price. The grid only produces values below $1,899 in the most extreme corner (high WACC + deep revenue decline), and even there the minimum is $1,999 at Monte Carlo P5. This asymmetry is favorable.

What must be true for the current price to be justified? The market is pricing in either (a) permanently lower earnings power from the reinsurance exit, (b) reserving risk in legacy lines that materially impairs book value, or (c) a significant decline in investment returns. The first is temporary -- management expects specialty insurance to more than offset the premium reduction over 2-3 years. The second is a genuine risk but manageable given Markel's historically conservative reserving. The third would require a broad market downturn that is not MKL-specific. On balance, the market appears to be overweighting the transitional earnings dip.

Analyst consensus targets average $2,082-$2,086 with the most recent Truist upgrade to $2,100, implying roughly 10% upside. All five covering analysts rate it Hold, suggesting the Street sees fair value modestly above current price but no urgency. The quant model is materially more optimistic than consensus, which may reflect the DCF's fuller capture of Markel Ventures' value and the equity portfolio's compounding (both often underappreciated in sell-side models that focus on underwriting income).

The 15% five-year CAGR in intrinsic value per share, combined with ongoing buybacks ($430M in 2025, reducing share count from 12.8M to 12.6M), creates a natural value accretion flywheel. At 1.3x book, investors are paying a modest premium for a management team with a demonstrated track record of capital allocation -- buying businesses at reasonable prices, investing in equities, and repurchasing shares below intrinsic value.

## Signal Summary
- **Bull case:** Specialty insurance refocusing delivers sub-93% combined ratios while Ventures and equity portfolio compound at 12-15%, and buybacks accelerate below intrinsic value, driving IV toward $3,500-$4,000.
- **Bear case:** Legacy reserve deterioration, catastrophe losses, and a prolonged equity bear market compress book value growth to low single digits, justifying only 1.0x book (~$1,500).
- **Confidence:** High -- three independent value engines (insurance, Ventures, investments) with visible track record; 97% Monte Carlo probability of undervaluation provides strong quantitative support.

## Red Flags
- Forward EPS of $126 implies meaningful earnings decline in 2026 from reinsurance runoff; market may not re-rate until 2027 earnings visibility improves
- Only 5 analysts cover the stock, all at Hold; limited buy-side catalysts from sell-side coverage
- Bear IV of $1,680 offers only 11% downside cushion, less than ideal for a concentrated position
- Reserve risk in discontinued IP CPI product line, though management expects immaterial losses in 2026
- Stock at 47% of 52-week range suggests momentum is against the position near-term

## Score: 8 / 10
Markel trades at a meaningful discount to base intrinsic value with 97% Monte Carlo probability of undervaluation, modest P/B for its ROE profile, and a conservative balance sheet -- but the transitional earnings dip and lack of near-term catalysts prevent a higher score.
