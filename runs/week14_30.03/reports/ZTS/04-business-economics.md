## Detailed Analysis

**Returns on Capital.** Zoetis delivers elite returns. ROIC climbed from 18.9% in FY2022 to 24.1% in FY2025 — a trajectory that reflects both margin expansion and capital discipline rather than financial engineering. ROE is exceptionally high at 66% in FY2025, though this is amplified by aggressive buybacks reducing equity (stockholders' equity fell from $4.8B to $3.3B in one year). ROA at 17.3% confirms returns are real and not merely a leverage artifact. The trend is clearly upward and stable — no year below 18.9% ROIC in the four-year window. This is a business that compounds intrinsic value at a high rate.

**Margin Structure.** Gross margins of 71.8% are remarkable for a company manufacturing physical products (biologics, pharmaceuticals, diagnostics equipment). The steady four-year expansion from 69.6% signals genuine pricing power — Zoetis operates in a market where veterinarians and pet owners are relatively price-insensitive, and switching costs for established treatments are meaningful. Operating margins at 38.0% (up from 36.2%) reflect disciplined SG&A management alongside modest R&D increases ($698M in FY2025, 7.3% of revenue). Net margins at 28.2% are best-in-class for a healthcare manufacturer. EBITDA margins at 43.0% leave substantial room to absorb cyclical downturns.

**Cash Generation.** FCF of $2.3B in FY2025 on $9.5B revenue is strong, though FCF was flat year-over-year despite earnings growth — likely reflecting working capital timing or elevated capex. Owner earnings (NI + D&A - CapEx) of $2.5B represent a cleaner picture and grew steadily. FCF conversion (FCF/NI) improved materially from 62.7% to 85.4% over four years, indicating the business is becoming more efficient at converting accounting profits to cash. Stock-based compensation at $83M (3% of net income) is modest and does not meaningfully dilute the FCF picture.

**Capital Intensity.** Zoetis is relatively asset-light for a pharmaceutical manufacturer. Maintenance capex (approximated by D&A) runs ~$487M or 5.1% of revenue. Total capex of $621M in FY2025 implies only ~$134M in growth capex. The quant model confirms maintenance capex at 78% of total capex — the business does not require heavy reinvestment to sustain its earnings power. Total assets of $15.5B support $9.5B in revenue and $2.7B in net income, a capital-efficient setup. The $3.2B in buybacks in FY2025 (funded partly by the $1.75B convertible notes) represents management choosing to return capital rather than chase acquisitions, which is consistent with a mature compounder.

**Revenue Quality.** Approximately 65% of revenue comes from companion animal products, which have strong recurring characteristics: parasiticides (Simparica Trio franchise at ~$1.5B, growing 12% operationally), dermatology treatments (~$1.7B, growing 6%), and diagnostics (growing 13% operationally). Pet healthcare spending is highly non-discretionary — once a pet owner starts a treatment protocol, they rarely switch or stop. The livestock segment (~35%) is more cyclical and commodity-exposed but diversified across cattle, swine, poultry, and aquaculture. Revenue growth is a blend of volume (new product uptake, geographic expansion) and price (mid-single-digit annual increases). The diagnostics business adds a razor-and-blade recurring revenue stream. Zoetis guided FY2026 revenue of $9.825-$10.025B, implying 3-5% growth.

**Operating Leverage.** The business exhibits meaningful operating leverage. FY2025 organic operational revenue grew 6%, while organic adjusted net income grew 7%. EPS grew from $5.47 to $6.02 (10%), amplified by the share count reduction from buybacks. Over the four-year window, revenue grew 17% cumulatively ($8.1B to $9.5B) while net income grew 29% ($2.1B to $2.7B) and EPS grew 34% ($4.49 to $6.02). The fixed-cost manufacturing base and high-margin product mix create a natural profit amplifier as revenue scales.

## Signal Summary
- **Bull case:** Elite ROIC expanding above 24%, 72% gross margins with pricing power in a non-discretionary market, and growing recurring revenue from diagnostics and parasiticide franchises create a durable compounding engine.
- **Bear case:** FCF was flat YoY despite earnings growth, FY2026 guided growth of only 3-5% is modest for the valuation, and the livestock segment remains exposed to cyclical swings and currency headwinds.
- **Confidence:** High — four years of consistent financial data, well-understood business model, transparent segment reporting, and a long public track record since the 2013 Pfizer spin-off.

## Red Flags
- FY2025 OCF ($2.9B) was slightly below FY2024 ($3.0B) despite higher net income — worth monitoring for working capital trends
- $3.2B in buybacks in FY2025 exceeded FCF of $2.3B, funded partly by the convertible notes issuance — management is leveraging the balance sheet for returns
- Guided FY2026 growth of 3-5% (reported) is below recent organic growth rates, reflecting FX headwinds

## Score: 9 / 10
Zoetis exhibits elite business economics: consistently expanding ROIC above 20%, best-in-class margins with pricing power, strong and improving FCF conversion, an asset-light model, and meaningful operating leverage. The only deduction is the flat year-over-year FCF and modest near-term growth guidance; otherwise this is a textbook quality compounder.
