## Detailed Analysis

**Owner Earnings and Baseline.** The quant model's adjusted owner earnings of $2.7B are credible. Zoetis generated $2.9B in OCF and $2.3B in FCF for FY2025, with maintenance capex consuming roughly 78% of total capex. The $2.7B figure appropriately adds back growth capex while deducting maintenance spend. With 455M diluted shares, that works out to roughly $5.93 per share in owner earnings, supporting a 20x owner earnings multiple at $118 — reasonable for a mid-single-digit grower but arguably cheap for a business with 72% gross margins and 24% ROIC.

**Scenario Assessment.** The bear case at $87 assumes revenue growth near zero and some margin compression. I largely agree but would note that Zoetis's non-Librela portfolio (parasiticides, vaccines, livestock) grew mid-single digits in 2025 and provides a durable floor. For the bear case to materialize, you would need Librela to effectively fail as a franchise AND core companion animal growth to stall simultaneously. This is possible but unlikely. The base case at $133 assumes 5.7% revenue growth fading to 3% — consistent with management's 2026 guidance range of 3-5% organic growth. The exit multiple of 15x EV/EBITDA is conservative given the median historical multiple of 24x, though it may reflect the "new normal" if Zoetis is re-rated as a mature pharma company rather than a growth compounder. I would nudge the base case slightly higher to $135-140 if the exit multiple were 16-17x. The bull case at $187 assumes 9.7% growth with lower WACC. The upside optionality the model may underweight includes: (a) Librela safety narrative stabilizing and recapturing lost share, (b) CYTOPOINT long-acting approval in late 2026, (c) the 12 potential blockbuster pipeline products TIKR identified, and (d) international expansion in livestock markets. A successful Librela recovery alone could add $500M+ in incremental revenue by 2028, pushing the bull case toward $200.

**Multiples in Context.** The compression is striking. Zoetis currently trades at 15x EV/EBITDA against its 13-year median of 24x and 5-year average of ~30x. IDEXX, the closest quality peer, trades at 35-40x EV/EBITDA. Even Elanco, a lower-quality competitor with weaker margins and higher leverage, trades at 12-15x EV/EBITDA — meaning Zoetis's premium over Elanco has nearly vanished. On forward P/E, Zoetis at ~16x vs IDEXX at ~44x represents a roughly 64% discount, compared to the historical norm of ~30-40% discount. The market is pricing Zoetis as if it were a commodity pharma company rather than the dominant franchise in animal health.

**Reverse-Engineering the Current Price.** Looking at the sensitivity grid, $118 falls in a cluster of cells at higher WACC (9.7-10.7%) with low growth (1.7-3.7%). Specifically, the grid shows IV of $114.52 at 5.7% growth / 10.7% WACC, and $120.07 at 5.7% growth / 9.7% WACC. The market is effectively pricing in either: (a) permanently lower growth of 2-3% with current risk levels, or (b) a materially higher cost of capital (regulatory/safety risk premium). Given 2026 guidance of 3-5% organic growth and a long track record of mid-to-high single digit growth, the implied assumptions look overly pessimistic.

**Monte Carlo Reasonableness.** The 83.5% probability of IV exceeding current price is derived from 10,000 simulations with the mean at $139 and median at $138. With the stock at $118, the P5 scenario ($107) still represents only 9% downside while the P95 ($176) offers 49% upside. The asymmetry is favorable. The probability estimate seems reasonable given the input distributions, though it depends heavily on the assumed growth and margin variance ranges.

## Signal Summary
- **Bull case:** Zoetis at 15x EV/EBITDA with 72% gross margins and 24% ROIC is the cheapest the stock has been relative to its quality since the IPO; normalization to even 20x implies $160+.
- **Bear case:** Librela franchise is permanently impaired, growth slows to low-single digits, and the market correctly re-rates Zoetis as a mature pharma company worth 14-15x EV/EBITDA.
- **Confidence:** Medium-High — the quant model base case and peer context both point to undervaluation, but Librela uncertainty creates a wide range of outcomes.

## Red Flags
- Librela safety concerns are real and ongoing — 32% Q4 revenue decline is material
- Net debt of $6.7B (2.9x net debt/EBITDA) limits financial flexibility during a downturn
- Revenue concentration: top 5 products at 42% of revenue means franchise risk is not trivial
- The stock has been a falling knife — 47% off ATH, 31% off 52-week high — and value traps exist

## Score: 7.5 / 10
Zoetis trades at a meaningful discount to both its historical multiples and intrinsic value estimates. The quant base case of $133 implies 13% upside from $118, with an 83.5% Monte Carlo probability of being undervalued. However, the margin of safety to the bear case is negative (-35.7%), and Librela uncertainty prevents a higher-conviction undervaluation call. This is "modestly undervalued with a plausible path to significantly undervalued" — not a screaming bargain, but attractively priced for patient capital.
