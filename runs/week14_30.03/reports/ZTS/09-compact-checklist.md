# Compact Checklist — ZTS

**Date:** 2026-04-04

1. **Business**: Zoetis sells medicines, vaccines, and diagnostics for companion animals (~65% of $9.5B revenue) and livestock (~35%), with repeat purchases driven by pet populations and herd health rather than discretionary spending.
2. **Moat**: Regulatory barriers requiring 5-10 year species-specific FDA/EMA approvals, deep vet switching costs, and 17% global market share built over 60+ years deliver 72% gross margins — 10+ points above industry average.
3. **Economics**: ROIC expanded from 18.9% to 24.1% over four years, owner earnings reached $2.5B, and revenue grew 17% while EPS grew 34% on an asset-light model requiring only 5% of revenue in maintenance capex.
4. **Management**: Compensation is 92% incentive-based with PSUs that vested at 0% when TSR lagged, M&A has been disciplined bolt-ons, but $3.2B in buybacks at $140-170 funded partly by $1.75B in convertible debt looks expensive with shares now at $118.
5. **Debt risk**: Interest coverage of 16x, Debt/EBITDA of 2.3x, and $2.3B in cash against well-laddered maturities (nothing due in 2026) mean Zoetis survives even a 30% revenue decline — though the 37% one-year debt increase for buybacks deserves monitoring.
6. **Price**: At 15x EV/EBITDA versus a 13-year median of 24x, the market is pricing in permanent growth deceleration to 2-3% and an elevated risk premium from Librela safety concerns — both of which must persist for the current price to be fair.
7. **Margin of safety**: The 72% gross margins, 24% ROIC, and diversified $9B+ non-Librela revenue base protect earnings power, but the stock sits 35.6% above the $87 bear-case IV, so there is business protection but no price cushion against the worst scenario.
8. **Thesis breaker**: Sell if the FDA recalls or materially restricts Librela prescribing, organic revenue growth falls below 2% for two consecutive years, or net debt exceeds 4x EBITDA without a credible deleveraging plan.
