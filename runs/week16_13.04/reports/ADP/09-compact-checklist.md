# Compact Checklist — ADP

**Date:** 2026-04-19

1. **Business**: ADP runs payroll, HR, benefits, tax filing and PEO services for ~1.1 million employers across ~140 countries, earning recurring per-employee fees plus interest on ~$30B+ of client funds held in transit — the payroll backbone for roughly one in six US workers.
2. **Moat**: Switching costs (6–12 month mid-market migrations, 91–92% client revenue retention) plus 75 years of multi-jurisdiction tax-compliance infrastructure and scale in bank/agency plumbing keep enterprise and mid-market clients from defecting — even when Rippling and Gusto chip away at the SMB flank.
3. **Economics**: Operating margin expanded from 23.1% (FY22) to 26.3% (FY25), FY25 FCF was $4.4B at 21.4% margin and 108% conversion, reported ROIC runs 37–54%, and owner earnings of $4.1B essentially equal GAAP net income — the financial fingerprint of a genuine moat compounder.
4. **Management**: CEO Maria Black (26-year insider) runs a company with 51 consecutive years of dividend increases, modest SBC (1.3% of revenue), clean governance, and $1.1–2.0B/yr of buybacks — competent caretaker-quality but not opportunistic (FY25 buybacks were near base IV, not at the current drawdown prices).
5. **Debt risk**: S&P AA-, net debt/EBITDA 0.9x, interest coverage 12.6x, $7.8B cash, and ~99% recurring revenue — even a 30% two-year revenue drop leaves ADP comfortably profitable and dividend-capable, so survival is not in question.
6. **Price**: At $200.47 the market implicitly prices ~3% long-term growth at ~10% cost of equity on peak EPS — which requires either a sustained US employment contraction or that current earnings are cyclically inflated by float income that will fade hard; both premises are at the pessimistic end of reasonable.
7. **Margin of safety**: Price sits 37% ABOVE quant bear IV ($146) so price MOS is strictly negative, but Monte Carlo P(IV > Price) is 86.8%, 19 of 25 sensitivity cells exceed spot, and the real safety is in the business itself (AA- balance sheet, 92% retention, essential service) — not a deep-discount setup, a quality-at-a-fair-price setup.
8. **Thesis breaker**: Annual client revenue retention falling below 88% for two consecutive years, or FY bookings growth sustained below 3%, would confirm that the switching-cost moat is actually eroding and trigger an exit.
