## Detailed Analysis

**Holding period suitability — excellent.** ADP is the archetype of a Buffett-suitable business: boring, essential, high-retention, cash-generative, rational management, fortress balance sheet, 75-year operating history. The thesis is not catalyst-dependent; the thesis is compounding — ~5–7% revenue, ~9–11% EPS via margin expansion + buybacks + dividend, resulting in ~12% annualized total return over a 5–10 year hold if the multiple simply mean-reverts to mid-20s. This is the kind of position you ideally own for 10 years and re-underwrite annually.

**Volatility tolerance — this is the test.** The stock has fallen 38% in 10 months. Beta is 0.86, so this drawdown is NOT driven by general market weakness — it reflects sector/company-specific concerns about the labor cycle. Historical max drawdowns: COVID ~30% (recovered in 12 months), GFC ~40% (recovered in 24 months), current -38% (ongoing, 10 months). Pattern is clear: ADP drawdowns are macro-recession fear, business stays intact, stock recovers when the macro normalizes. The temperament question is NOT "can I hold through a 30% drawdown" — it is "can I ADD at 15–20% below my entry when the narrative says employment is breaking?" That is the harder test. If the answer is no, smaller position; if yes, this is the exact setup to size into.

**Catalysts.** The principal catalysts are (a) stabilization in monthly ADP private payroll prints (Feb 2026 was +22k; a return to +80–100k would shift narrative), (b) Fed pivot and float income re-acceleration, (c) FY27 guidance in August 2026 showing 7%+ revenue growth, (d) successful Lyric HCM enterprise wins. None of these are controlled by ADP management; all are plausible within 12–18 months. No single binary event — the rerating likely happens gradually over 3–5 quarters.

**Position sizing logic.**
- MC P(IV > Price) = 86.8% — supports a meaningful position.
- Bear-to-bull spread is $146 → $322 = 2.2x — wide enough that I would not go full Own-verdict 5% on first entry.
- Stock momentum is negative (down 10 straight months) — waiting for technical stabilization is prudent.
- Dividend 3.4% pays to wait.

**Recommendation:** **3% starter position** with explicit intent to add to 5% on (a) a second quarter of >6% bookings growth, (b) a positive monthly ADP employment print (>75k), or (c) another 10% price drop with thesis intact. Do not front-load a 5% position until the chart stops bleeding; the opportunity cost of waiting 1-2 quarters is small given the 3.4% dividend floor.

**Correlation and portfolio overlap.** Existing queue concentrations appear in semis (NVDA, AMD, ASML), healthcare (UNH, NVS, ZTS), consumer (COST, LULU, RACE, ULTA), financials (V, MA). ADP is in payroll/HR services — closest overlap is V (payment processing) and MA — but ADP's revenue drivers (employment, wage growth, interest on float) are distinct from consumer spending. It adds diversification. Currency: USD-denominated, matches the bulk of the portfolio. Factor exposure: quality (yes), value (at current price, yes), low-volatility (beta 0.86 helps). No hidden factor clustering.

**Process discipline — entry plan.**
- Entry trigger: price below $210 AND report ≤3 months old (both currently met).
- Starter: 3% at current price ($200.47).
- Add rule 1: another 1% at ≤$180 (technical stabilization below 52-wk low irrelevant — price alone).
- Add rule 2: another 1% on confirmed inflection in monthly employment data.
- Full weight cap: 5% — this is an Own-tier quality compounder but not a once-in-a-decade setup.

**Process discipline — sell rules.**
- Thesis break: annual retention falls below 88% for 2 consecutive years → reduce to 50% weight.
- Thesis break: FY bookings growth below 3% sustained → exit.
- Valuation extreme: forward P/E above 28x (approaching 10-yr peak) → trim 30%.
- Better alternative: only if identified high-conviction Own-verdict name with P(IV > Price) > 90% emerges.
- Review cadence: quarterly (after each ADP earnings call) — NOT reactive to price.

**Process discipline — emotional preparation.** Accept that a 3% position starter may go to 2% on paper before it works. Do not interpret further price weakness as thesis weakness unless the bookings growth or retention number changes. The discipline is in ADDING when the investor brain screams to sell.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** Patient hold through macro turbulence delivers 12–15% annualized (price appreciation + dividend + buyback) over a 5-year window; the multiple simply normalizing to 24x on $13 FY28 EPS = $310 + $30 in dividends = ~75% total return.
- **Bear case:** Labor market weakens further, stock drifts to $175, dividend income of 3.4%/yr offsets paper loss; 3-year flat to +5% total return, then the macro normalizes.
- **Confidence:** High — this is exactly the kind of business Buffett-style investing is designed for: durable, dull, essential, currently out of favor for cyclical reasons.

## Red Flags

- Requires patience for an unknown time (probably 3–6 quarters) while macro stabilizes — investors without staying power should not buy.
- Momentum is negative and 10 months of lower lows means the "no falling knife" rule argues for waiting or sizing small.
- Macro-catalyst-dependent — if US enters a prolonged stagflation, the wait could be 3+ years with flat total return.
- Concentrated SMB exposure within the US — a US-specific recession hits harder than a global one.
- Dividend/buyback combo requires cash generation; if OCF slips meaningfully, one could be trimmed (historically unheard of for ADP).

## Score: 8 / 10

Excellent temperament fit: durable cash-generative compounder, beta <1, 3.4% yield to wait, low sector overlap, clear entry/add/sell rules. Starter-size this (3%) rather than full-weight (5%) given the wide bear-to-bull spread and still-falling chart. The main risk is behavioral — the drawdown is deep and the catalyst is macro, so the investor must have genuine patience and the discipline to add, not panic.
