## Detailed Analysis

**The ROIC story is a forced regression, not a broken business.** DSV's trailing numbers look ugly: 7.6% ROIC in FY2025 is below the company's cost of capital for the first time in a decade. But the anatomy matters. The April 2025 Schenker close added roughly DKK 105B of acquisition price, financed by a ~DKK 42B rights issue and ~DKK 60B of new debt. That injects massive goodwill into invested capital (denominator up from DKK 90.8B end-FY2023 to DKK 184.4B end-FY2025 — roughly doubled), while the numerator — NOPAT — is simultaneously depressed by (a) Schenker's pre-deal ~3% EBIT margin dragging blended operating margin, and (b) DKK 6.5B of expected special items for integration costs in 2026, with a similar magnitude already absorbed in 2025. Back the integration charges out of FY2025 operating income and the underlying ROIC is closer to 10–11%. Add 2–3 years of synergy capture (DKK 9B run-rate by 2027) and the math comfortably rebuilds toward mid-teens ROIC. But "comfortably rebuilds" is not the same as "will rebuild" — execution risk is real.

**Margin structure reflects both mix and integration drag.** Gross margin actually expanded from 25.7% (FY2024) to 27.0% (FY2025), suggesting the combined network is pricing in line with historic DSV discipline. The pain is below the gross line: operating margin dropped 170bps year-over-year (9.6% → 7.9%) and EBITDA margin fell 280bps (12.7% → 9.9%). This is consistent with Schenker's cost structure being carried at its legacy — pre-integration — level for most of 2025. Management's guide of DKK 23.0–25.5B EBIT before special items for 2026 (vs DKK 19.6B operating income in FY2025) implies an operating margin rebuild toward 9–10%. The DKK 9B synergy run-rate, once captured, would lift margin by roughly 360bps on FY2025 revenue, putting the combined entity within striking distance of the 11–12% margins legacy DSV delivered before the deal. If management hits that, this scores 8. If they miss by half, this scores 5.

**The cash engine is doing its job.** FY2025 operating cash flow of DKK 21.5B is the second-highest in company history despite the integration costs hitting the P&L. Free cash flow of DKK 18.9B (DSV reports adjusted FCF of DKK 16.3B after Schenker transaction/integration outflows) was strong enough to allow DKK 7B+ of net debt paydown in the year. The FCF conversion figure of 233% in the financials table is misleading — it reflects net income suppressed by integration charges — but the raw OCF number doesn't lie. Cash is being generated. The question is whether it gets absorbed by ongoing integration ("special items" of DKK 6.5B expected for 2026) or whether it flows through to shareholders as the integration closes out by end-2026 per accelerated management timeline.

**Capital intensity is the quiet hero.** Freight forwarding, at its core, is an asset-light brokerage business: you arrange the move, you don't own the truck/ship/plane. DSV's FY2025 capex of DKK 2.6B against DKK 247B revenue is 1.1% — functionally negligible. Maintenance capex is 100% of total capex per the quant owner-earnings breakdown, meaning there is no growth capex line item; growth has historically come from M&A (UTi, Panalpina, Agility, Schenker), not organic network buildout. This is why pre-Schenker ROIC was 20%+: virtually every dollar of operating profit flowed to free cash flow. That structural advantage did not disappear with the Schenker deal — it just got temporarily diluted by a goodwill-heavy acquisition.

**Revenue quality: volume-driven, cyclical, low recurring.** Freight forwarding revenue moves with trade volumes and freight rates. It is not a subscription business. When global volumes soften (2023: revenue fell from DKK 235.7B to DKK 150.8B as pandemic-era freight rates normalized), DSV's revenue falls with it. What DSV does have is operating leverage on the gross profit line — costs flex with volume better than peers because the business is brokerage, not carrier. FY2023 to FY2024 showed this: revenue grew 11% but operating income fell 9% as freight rates compressed. Still, over a full cycle DSV has compounded operating income per share at double digits for 20+ years.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** Synergy capture comes in at or above management's DKK 9B run-rate by 2027; operating margin rebuilds to 11%+; ROIC climbs back above 15% by FY2028 as goodwill is earned against via growing NOPAT; FCF/share compounds at low-teens from here.
- **Bear case:** Integration disruption costs more customers than modelled (Schenker retention is the hidden variable); synergies come in at DKK 5–6B not 9B; operating margin stalls in the 8–9% band; ROIC plateaus in the high single digits and the stock stays rangebound as the market re-rates a lower-quality combined entity.
- **Confidence:** Medium — the historical DSV franchise is clearly elite (20%+ ROIC, <2% capex intensity, serial M&A execution), but FY2025 financials are mid-integration and not representative either of Schenker's drag or of the synergized run-rate. Forward estimates rely on management delivering on an aggressive synergy target.

## Red Flags

- Trailing ROIC of 7.6% is below any reasonable estimate of WACC (~10% per the quant model). Until synergies show up in reported NOPAT, the company is technically destroying value on a trailing basis.
- Operating margin of 7.9% in FY2025 is the lowest DSV has reported in the last decade. The recovery is modelled, not demonstrated.
- Integration costs are large and recurring: DKK 6.5B expected for 2026 "special items" is not trivial against DKK 23–25.5B EBIT guidance — these "one-offs" are a quarter of operating profit.
- Net income halved in FY2025 (DKK 8.1B vs DKK 10.1B FY2024 and DKK 12.3B FY2023) and diluted EPS fell from DKK 47.00 to DKK 34.30 — share count rose 30%+ from the rights issue, compounding the per-share drag.
- Schenker was a structurally lower-quality asset than legacy DSV. Even after perfect synergy execution, the combined company may run at a permanently lower ROIC than the 20%+ standalone business achieved.

## Score: 6 / 10

Legacy DSV was a 9/10 business (elite ROIC, light capital intensity, exceptional capital allocation). FY2025 is transitional: trailing metrics are ugly, the forward path depends on Schenker synergy execution, and the combined entity will almost certainly run at lower through-cycle ROIC than standalone DSV did. A 6 reflects "temporarily dilutive acquisition of a capital-light franchise, with a credible but unproven path back to quality economics" — worth watching closely, not yet worth leaning on the historical numbers.
