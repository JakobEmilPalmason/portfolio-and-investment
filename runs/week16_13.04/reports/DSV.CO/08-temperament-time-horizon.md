## Detailed Analysis

**Holding period suitability — 5–10 year fit.** DSV is a compounder with structural tailwinds: global trade volumes grow ~3%/year over long periods, and DSV's model (asset-light, franchisee-based, cost discipline) lets the franchise take market share every cycle. Over 25 years the company has compounded revenue at ~15% CAGR through M&A plus organic growth, with similar EPS compounding. The Schenker deal just doubled the business. If management executes, DSV becomes the dominant global freight forwarder with a clear path to 10%+ operating margins on DKK 300B+ of revenue — a franchise that should be held, not traded. This is a multi-year investment, not a catalyst trade. The thesis does not depend on macro timing; it depends on integration execution.

**Volatility tolerance — real but manageable.** DSV is not a sleepy stock. Over 5 years the stock has ranged roughly DKK 650 to DKK 2,200. A 30–40% drawdown from current levels is plausible under: (a) a 2022-style macro risk-off with acquisition debt focus, (b) a soft-patch in logistics volumes, or (c) a quarterly integration disappointment. The question is: would I buy more or panic? Given the business quality and integration track record, a 30% drop on cyclical noise would be a strong add opportunity; a drop driven by a real synergy stall (Q4 2026 EBIT well below DKK 5–6B run-rate) would be a sell signal. I need to know which is which — price moves alone are not the signal.

**Catalysts and their calendar.** Three near-term catalysts, all within the business's control:
1. Q2 2026 results (Aug 2026): first clean half with full-year Schenker consolidation — synergy capture metrics will be visible.
2. 2026 full-year results (Feb 2027): integration officially completed end of 2026 per the pulled-forward schedule. This is the "proof point" moment.
3. 2027 guidance (Feb 2027): full DKK 9B synergy run-rate year; market will recalibrate multiples on the post-integration margin profile.
Longer term: a successful Schenker digestion sets up the next transformational M&A (CEVA? Sinotrans? K+N?) — DSV has done one roughly every 5–7 years. That is a 2028+ optionality. Catalysts are within management's control; they are not macro-dependent.

**Position sizing — starter, not core.** Two inputs argue for starter size (2–3% of portfolio) rather than core (5%+):
- My MOS analysis (section 07) showed negative MOS vs bear IV (-18% to -29%) and only +24% MOS vs my adjusted base IV. The price cushion is thin.
- Bear-to-bull spread is wide (~2.2x), meaning outcome dispersion is high. Even with a strong business, you don't commit core capital when the range of plausible 3-year outcomes is DKK 1,450 to DKK 3,200.
The right entry framework: 2–3% starter position now if opening, with a pre-committed plan to add 1–2% on a ≥15% drawdown to DKK 1,450 or below (which would push MOS vs adjusted base above 35%). Cap total position at 5% even on a bull scenario — leverage and integration risk are not zero.

**Correlation and portfolio overlap.** Fit check for a Danish quality-compounder portfolio:
- Already-held Danish names (e.g., Novo Nordisk): low correlation — different sectors, different drivers. DSV adds industrials/logistics exposure that pharma-heavy DK portfolios lack.
- Transportation/logistics: DSV is typically the only forwarder needed — do not pair with K+N, Expeditors, or DHL (same bet, different vehicles).
- Sector exposure: industrials/transport. Correlates with global trade cycle and container rates. A DSV position is effectively a long position on global trade volumes plus the integration story.
- Currency: DKK is effectively EUR-pegged; for a DKK-based ASK investor this is natively local and avoids FX friction.

**Process discipline — entry, exit, review.**

*Entry plan*:
- Buy 2% starter now at ≤DKK 1,720 (quality + integration on track + DKK listing + ASK-friendly).
- Add 1% on any drawdown to DKK 1,550–1,600 (~10% move), provided Q2 2026 integration update confirms synergy pace.
- Second add (+1%) only on drawdown to DKK 1,400–1,450 with thesis intact.
- Never exceed 5% total weight.

*Sell rules* (thesis-break triggers — not price moves):
- Q3 2026 or later EBIT run-rate implies FY2026 EBIT below DKK 22B (would signal synergies falling meaningfully short of guidance).
- Operating margin stuck below 8.5% into 2027 (synergies banked but not flowing to the bottom line).
- Management announces major new M&A before Schenker is fully integrated (capital-allocation discipline break).
- Net Debt/EBITDA rising above 3.0x for reasons other than temporary cyclical EBITDA dip.
- Price reaches DKK 3,000+ (my adjusted base IV upper bound with limited further upside).

*Review cadence*: Read every quarterly report carefully (4x/year). Specifically track: (i) EBIT trajectory vs 2026 guidance, (ii) Road/Air+Sea/Solutions segment margins vs historical DSV norms, (iii) integration milestones (countries migrated, headcount trajectory, IT systems consolidated), (iv) cash conversion and debt paydown rate. Revisit thesis formally after Q2 2026 (first full post-merger quarter with year-over-year comparability).

**Temperament check.** Would I hold this through a 35% drawdown? Yes — if the drawdown is driven by cyclical noise or integration quarterly volatility. No — if it is driven by a genuine synergy stall or a covenant concern. The distinction matters; it's a thesis-driven sell, not a price-driven one. DSV's integration track record (Panalpina hit DSV margins within 12 months; UTi, DFDS, ABX all hit targets) creates a rational base rate to trust: management has earned the benefit of the doubt, but not unlimited patience.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** DSV integration completes ahead of schedule through 2027, margin restored to 10%+, multiple holds 14x+; stock compounds to DKK 2,800–3,000 by 2028 with sturdy FCF generation funding dividends plus opportunistic M&A.
- **Bear case:** Integration stalls or logistics cycle softens, margin stuck at 8.5%; stock drifts to DKK 1,400–1,500 for 2–3 years while debt pays down; eventual recovery to current levels is likely but opportunity cost is real.
- **Confidence:** Medium — strong business quality and management track record support patient ownership, but integration execution is the swing variable and cannot be known in advance. Position sizing must reflect that.

## Red Flags

- Narrative-heavy stock for the next 18 months. Price will overreact to quarterly integration data. Requires a temperament that can ignore swings.
- Leverage is the highest it has been in DSV's history. Not dangerous, but limits the "buy more on weakness" reflex — at some point the balance sheet becomes the reason for the drawdown, not just the cycle.
- No clean exit signal below the bear case scenario — if the stock falls on macro, you need to actively check whether the thesis is broken or just out of favour. Cannot trade this on price moves alone.
- Wide bear-to-bull range argues against sizing up. Do not let a ~25% MOS vs base case convince you to take a 5%+ position; dispersion is not priced into that MOS figure.
- Logistics is cyclical — if a trade-volume shock arrives during the integration window (e.g., renewed US-China tariff escalation beyond Nov 2026), both the cyclical and integration stories drag together.

## Score: 7 / 10

Good fit for a patient 5–10 year holder who can tolerate integration-period volatility. Clear business, clear catalysts, understandable scenario paths, and a manageable size (2–3% starter, 5% cap). The score is held below 8 by thin price MOS and a leveraged balance sheet; held above 6 by business quality, management track record, and defined catalyst calendar. This is a starter position, not a core position, and best bought into weakness or on a confirming Q2 2026 integration update.
