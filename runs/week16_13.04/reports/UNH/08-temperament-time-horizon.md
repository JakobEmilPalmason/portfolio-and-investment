## Detailed Analysis

### Holding period suitability — clear 5-10 year fit

Medicare Advantage is not a trend; it is a demographic fact. Baby boomers aging into Medicare continue through 2029-2030. MA penetration is projected to grow from 51% today to 54%+ by 2030 per CBO. UNH is the #1 player in this market with durable advantages. Optum Health (value-based care delivery) is still in early innings — largest employer of physicians in the US but a tiny fraction of the overall care-delivery market. OptumRx is one of three PBMs controlling ~80% of US prescription volume. None of these theses are 12-month stories. They are decade stories.

This is exactly the kind of business you buy once and hold. Activity is the enemy here — the long-term return comes from margin normalization + revenue growth + buybacks + dividend, compounding at 10-13% annually over a decade. If an investor can't hold through a 30% drawdown without selling, they should not be in this stock today. The maximum drawdown from April 2025 high of $453 to August 2025 low of $230 was -49% — recent, real, and the kind of volatility that forces an owner mindset.

### Volatility tolerance — the business is calm, the stock is not

Financial volatility is low: revenue $325B → $448B over 4 years (steady up), operating cash flow $26-29B in normal years, FCF $20-25B. Beta 0.41 reflects that underlying business stability.

But the stock trades on narrative shifts more than fundamentals. 2024 Change Healthcare cyberattack ($3B hit), December 2024 CEO assassination (-25% in one month), May 2025 CEO transition (-15%), July 2025 DOJ probe disclosure (-20% in three days), January 2026 MCR shock guidance (-18%). These are not routine moves. They are narrative dislocations in what is fundamentally a boring, durable business.

An owner who can separate the business from the headlines will do well. Someone who checks the price daily will have a bad time.

### Position sizing logic

This is where I depart from a typical Own-verdict recommendation. The setup argues for **starter-size position, not full size**:

- Wide IV range ($300 bear to $520 bull) = $220 of uncertainty per share, or ~70% range of central value. Wider than typical.
- Live DOJ criminal probe with expanding scope = real tail risk.
- CEO is 73 and came back unplanned = succession question mark.
- Quant Monte Carlo is over-optimistic (100% P(IV>Price) is not credible) — the risk of being wrong on margin normalization is not well-captured by the model.

**Recommended position size: 2-3% starter, max 4%.** Willing to add at $290 or below (would improve price MOS to 3-5%) or on resolution of DOJ clarity. Full conviction 5% sizing only after: (a) two clean quarters of MCR improvement data, (b) DOJ probe resolved or materially narrowed, (c) stock either cheaper or thesis materially validated.

The quant model's sensitivity range suggests a much wider comfortable position — disregard. The model's optimism on margin is the exact error that should make you smaller, not larger.

### Correlation and portfolio overlap

UNH is a healthcare/managed care exposure with:
- Low correlation with technology, AI infrastructure, semiconductors
- Low correlation with consumer discretionary
- Some correlation with other managed care (Elevance, Humana, Cigna) — don't own multiple insurers
- Some correlation with US political regime risk (MA program is perennial political football)

For a portfolio that skews tech/growth, UNH adds genuine diversification. For a portfolio already heavy in CVS, ELV, HUM, or CI, it's duplication. Beta 0.41 means it dampens overall portfolio volatility on paper — but the idiosyncratic risk episodes can be severe, so don't mistake low beta for low drawdown risk.

### Process discipline — entry, sell, review

**Entry plan:** Starter 2-3% at current price ($325) if other gates pass, or hold the bid and wait for $290-300. Avoid loading until Q2-Q3 2026 gives actual MCR data.

**Sell rules (thesis break = sell, not dips):**
1. MCR guidance stays above 88% for 2027 after a year of repricing effort.
2. DOJ settlement forces material business model change (divestiture of Optum, price caps).
3. Optum Health or Optum Rx revenue growth falls below 5% for two consecutive quarters.
4. Management credibility permanently damaged (third major guidance cut, accounting restatement).
5. Stock trades above my base IV of $380 with no underlying business change — trim.

**Review cadence:** Quarterly, on earnings. Don't react to news cycle headlines unless they materially change the sell-rule trigger set.

**What I would NOT sell on:** Price dropping 20% without a fundamental change. DOJ headline noise without resolution. Short-seller reports rehashing known issues.
