## Detailed Analysis

**Holding period suitability.** As a business, ASML is a textbook patient-owner holding: the node-shrink roadmap extends well past 2030, High-NA is still in early innings, and the company's 2030 target of €44–60B revenue doubles FY2025 sales. If you could buy at $750–900, this would be a 10-year hold without hesitation. The problem is not the business — it's the starting price. At $1,459.80 with Monte Carlo P(IV > Price) = 0%, even a 10-year hold may deliver low-single-digit annualized returns from multiple compression alone, before any cyclical event. Math: if the business earns its 2030 guide mid (€52B revenue, ~22% net margin → ~€11.4B net income or ~$12.5B, ~$32 EPS) and trades at 25x fwd by then, that's a $800 price in 2030 — *below* today's quote. You need the guide to overshoot AND multiple to sustain 30x+ to merely tread water.

**Volatility tolerance — can you hold?** This is the central temperament question. ASML has drawn down 40–50% twice in the last four years (2022 SOX sell-off; Oct 2024–April 2025 trough where it fell from $1,047 to $608). If you buy at $1,460 today and the stock is at $875 in 18 months — a repeat of the 2024–25 move — will you (a) add aggressively, (b) hold through, or (c) panic-sell? If the honest answer is (c), do not buy. If (a) or (b), size to a level where a 50% drawdown reduces portfolio value by no more than you can stomach (e.g., 2% position × 50% = 1% portfolio impact).

**Catalysts — none supportive at current price.** Positive catalysts: 2026 revenue reach toward €40B upper guide, further 2030 confidence boosters, High-NA order momentum. Negative catalysts: MATCH Act passage (likely 12–24 months if it moves), any semi-cycle rollover in 2027–28, rate-driven multiple compression. Most near-term catalysts are priced in (Q1 beat already released, 2026 guidance already raised). The asymmetric catalysts are more bearish than bullish from here.

**Position sizing logic.** Given (a) deeply negative MOS, (b) wide IV bear-to-bull spread of $500–$1,000 (2x range), (c) Monte Carlo P(IV > Price) = 0%, (d) 45–50% historical drawdown precedent, the disciplined answer is: **do not initiate a new position at $1,460**. If the stock were in range at $800–$900, it would deserve a 2–3% starter, possibly scaling to 4–5% over time for a high-conviction compounder. At $1,460, zero position is the correct allocation. For holders with existing cost basis well below current, the decision is different — trim to target if overweight, hold if at size.

**Correlation and portfolio overlap.** ASML is a pure-play semiconductor equipment bet with deep correlation to (a) the SOX index, (b) TSMC / Samsung / Intel / SK Hynix capex cycles, (c) AI infrastructure narrative. Owning ASML alongside any AI-infra names (NVDA, AVGO, TSMC ADRs) creates a single-factor bet. For a Danish ASK-optimized book that already includes tech compounders, ASML's correlation to them is very high — adds concentration, not diversification.

**Process discipline — entry/exit plan:**
- **Entry:** Wait for the stock to approach $850–950 (approximately my base-to-bull IV range). This likely requires a cyclical event or multiple compression. Given historical drawdown frequency, such a window appears roughly every 2–3 years.
- **Sell rules (for current holders):**
  - Thesis break: High-NA ramp fails materially (2+ years of delays), or loss of monopoly status in EUV (unlikely within 10 years).
  - Valuation extreme: already here — if price reaches $1,800+ with no guide raise, trim aggressively.
  - Better alternative: any quality compounder at MOS >20% deserves capital ahead of ASML at current price.
- **Review cadence:** Quarterly earnings + Q1-26 / Q3-26 pivot moments (China updates, High-NA ramp signals); plus any MATCH Act legislative progress triggers.

**Temperament red flag — the FOMO risk.** The hardest part of a long-runway compounder at full valuation is sitting on the sidelines while momentum continues. ASML could grind to $1,700 before rolling over. Discipline means accepting the possibility of further upside-missed without the probability of downside-captured. The 0% Monte Carlo probability is the anchor — it is not a statement that the stock will decline, but that *on a probability-weighted basis*, the expected return from here is unfavorable.
