# Compact Checklist — GOOG

**Date:** 2026-03-11

1. **Business**: Alphabet sells advertiser access to 14 billion daily search queries and YouTube's 2 billion monthly users, while renting AI-powered cloud infrastructure to enterprises — customers keep buying because Google owns the default entry points to the internet through Android (72% of smartphones) and Chrome (65% of desktops).

2. **Moat**: A 25-year data flywheel (more queries → better results → more users → more advertiser data → higher CPCs) combined with $20 billion in annual Apple TAC payments that no competitor can match prevents any challenger from displacing Google's ~90% global search share.

3. **Economics**: FY2025 delivered $73.3 billion in free cash flow on $402.8 billion in revenue at 31.6% operating margins and ~29% ROIC — exceptional by any measure, though FCF conversion has already compressed from ~85% to ~55% of net income as the AI capex cycle accelerates.

4. **Management**: Page and Brin's permanent 51.4% voting control enforces long-term thinking, and the buyback program has reduced share count 2–3% annually; however, the $175–185 billion 2026 capex commitment — nearly double 2025's $91.4 billion — is the largest capital bet in company history and its return on investment remains unproven.

5. **Debt risk**: With $126.8 billion in cash against $46.5 billion in long-term debt (net cash of $80 billion), interest coverage of 218x, and $164.7 billion in annual operating cash flow, Alphabet could not face a solvency crisis under any remotely realistic scenario.

6. **Price**: At $308.45, the market requires Google to simultaneously defend ~90% search share through the AI transition, grow Cloud to a $120–150 billion run rate by 2030, and return operating margins to 32–35% after the capex investment phase — all five conditions must hold, and the base-case DCF already implies ~$258/share, 16% below today's price.

7. **Margin of safety**: The business itself provides structural protection — 90% search market share, $240 billion cloud backlog, $80 billion net cash, and YouTube's 2 billion users — but the price offers no financial margin of safety; the bear-case DCF implies ~$155/share, meaning the stock would need to fall to $195–220 before a Buffett-style discount to intrinsic value would exist.

8. **Thesis breaker**: Sell if Google Search global market share falls below 85% for two consecutive quarters, OR if Google Cloud revenue growth decelerates below 20% for two consecutive quarters while the $175–185 billion capex commitment remains in place — either event would confirm that the AI infrastructure bet is failing while the core advertising franchise is simultaneously eroding.
