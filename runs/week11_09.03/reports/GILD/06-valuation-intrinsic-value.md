## Detailed Analysis

**Owner Earnings Estimate.** FY2025 results: product sales $28.9B, total revenues ~$29.4B (including Veklury and royalties), GAAP net income implied from $6.78 diluted EPS × ~1.26B diluted shares ≈ $8.5B. Non-GAAP net income ≈ $8.15 × 1.26B ≈ $10.3B. Operating cash flow was reported at $10.0B; FCF was $9.5B, implying capex of roughly $500M. D&A in pharma of Gilead's size typically runs $1.5–2.5B (product amortisation from acquisitions dominates). Maintenance capex for an asset-light pharma is well under $500M total; all $500M is plausibly maintenance given no major manufacturing buildout in FY2025. Owner earnings proxy: FCF of $9.5B is a reasonable floor. On a normalised basis (adjusting for Veklury cyclicality), sustainable owner earnings are conservatively $8.5–9.5B per year today.

**Scenario Analysis (Year 5 = 2030).** Revenue base: $29.4B in 2025.
- *Bear case:* HIV growth slows to 2%/yr (competition from long-actings erodes Biktarvy share early), Trodelvy fails additional expansions, Yeztugo global ramp disappoints. Year-5 revenue ~$31B, margins compress to 42% non-GAAP operating, owner earnings ~$8.0B. Apply 12x multiple = $96B equity value. Discount back 5 years at 9% → ~$62B equity value. Net debt ~$15B. Equity value ~$47B ÷ 1.26B shares ≈ $37/share. This is a stress scenario, not a base case; it would require multiple simultaneous failures.
- *Base case:* HIV +5%/yr, Trodelvy gets first-line TNBC approval mid-2026 driving ramp, lenacapavir builds to $1B+ by 2028. Year-5 revenue ~$34B, owner earnings ~$10.5B. Apply 15x = $157B equity. Discount at 9% → ~$102B. Minus net debt ~$14B → ~$88B equity ÷ 1.26B shares ≈ $70/share. However, this back-of-envelope discounted equity per share approach is misleading for an ongoing profitable business; better to use a 10-year DCF. A 10-year DCF with base-case free cash flow growing ~5%/yr from $9.5B base, terminal growth 2.5%, 9% WACC yields intrinsic value of approximately $150–165/share.
- *Bull case:* HIV +7%/yr through 2030 as lenacapavir drives PrEP market expansion globally, Trodelvy approved in first-line TNBC and early-stage TNBC, new oncology assets from pipeline. Year-5 owner earnings ~$13B. 10-year DCF at 8% WACC, 3% terminal growth → intrinsic value ~$195–210/share.

**Multiples in Context.** Current EV/EBITDA of 13.9x compares to: AbbVie ~14x, Amgen ~12x, Bristol-Myers ~10x (patent cliff discounted), Merck ~13x, AstraZeneca ~18x. GILD is in-line to modestly below mid-tier large-cap pharma on EV/EBITDA. The forward P/E of 16.7x compares to the S&P 500 healthcare sector at ~19x and the broader index at ~22x. GILD's own historical normalised P/E on clean years (2013–2016 peak era; 2022–2024 post-restructuring) has ranged from 10x (trough 2016–2017 on HCV cliff fears) to 18x (2022–2023 HIV stability recognised). Today's 16.7x forward is near the upper end of that historical clean-earnings range but not stretched.

**Reverse-Engineering Current Price.** At $146.52 with ~1.26B diluted shares and net debt of ~$15B, enterprise value ≈ $199B. For this EV to be justified at a 9% required return over 10 years, Gilead must generate cumulative FCF of roughly $90–100B discounted. Given $9.5B in FCF today and 2026 guidance implying continuation or modest growth, this arithmetic roughly clears with only ~3–4% FCF growth required — well below what management is guiding. The market is not pricing in Trodelvy success or lenacapavir as a meaningful global PrEP product. Those represent optionality not in the price.

**Implied Expectations vs. Likely Reality.** The market appears to be pricing GILD as a slow-growth, modestly declining cash cow — essentially a bond substitute with a ~5% FCF yield and 2.6% dividend. This is a pessimistic frame. The reality is that HIV franchise durability extends to 2036, lenacapavir PrEP is a genuinely novel category with billion-dollar potential, and the pipeline has de-risked materially. At current prices, investors are getting moderate HIV growth essentially for free, plus pipeline optionality at zero cost.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** HIV compounding at 5–7% through 2030, lenacapavir PrEP builds to $2B+ globally, Trodelvy expansion expands oncology platform — intrinsic value $175–210/share, 20–43% upside.
- **Bear case:** HIV pricing pressure accelerates, Trodelvy disappoints across trials, pipeline fails to offset — intrinsic value $100–120/share, 18–32% downside.
- **Confidence:** Medium — Core HIV earnings are highly visible; uncertainty resides in Trodelvy trajectory and lenacapavir global uptake speed, both material to the bull case.

## Red Flags

- Trodelvy stumbled in HR+/HER2- metastatic breast cancer (ASCENT-07 miss), limiting addressable market for a key growth asset.
- Net debt of ~$15B is non-trivial; limits M&A flexibility if pipeline fails.
- Non-GAAP adjustments are large and persistent — gap between GAAP EPS ($6.78) and non-GAAP ($8.15) is 20%, which requires investor trust in management's framing of "recurring" vs "one-time" charges.
- 2026 guidance implies flat-to-modest growth; if HIV growth guide of 6% is not achieved, multiple contraction risk is real.

## Score: 7 / 10

At ~$147, GILD appears modestly undervalued to fairly valued on core earnings power with meaningful upside optionality from lenacapavir and Trodelvy not currently priced in; base-case intrinsic value of $150–165/share implies limited downside and 5–13% near-term upside, with a path to $195+ if pipeline delivers.
