# Compact Checklist — GILD

**Date:** 2026-03-11

1. **Business**: Gilead sells branded HIV drugs — primarily Biktarvy ($14.3B in FY2025, ~49% of product revenue) — to patients who take them for life, creating de facto recurring revenue with near-zero switching since physicians rarely change a stable regimen without clinical cause.

2. **Moat**: Generic manufacturers are contractually barred from launching Biktarvy equivalents until April 1, 2036, and lenacapavir (Yeztugo) extends that IP runway beyond that — backed by 51% U.S. HIV treatment market share and 86% non-GAAP gross margins that demonstrate real pricing power.

3. **Economics**: The HIV franchise converts ~$29B in revenue to ~$9.5B in free cash flow annually at ~2% capital intensity (capex ~$500M), with gross margins stable at 78–86% — one of the most capital-efficient recurring revenue profiles in large-cap pharma.

4. **Management**: Management has grown the dividend for 10 consecutive years and returned $5.9B to shareholders in FY2025, but paid $21B for Immunomedics in 2020 and wrote down $4.2B of that in 2024, then spent another $7.8B on Arcellx in 2026 — a pattern of disciplined dividends offset by expensive, uneven oncology M&A.

5. **Debt risk**: With $10.6B in cash, ~$14.3B net debt at 1.2x EBITDA, and 9–10x interest coverage from $10B in FY2025 operating income, Gilead can service all debt from HIV cash flows alone — even a 30% revenue decline leaves the business cash-flow positive.

6. **Price**: At $146.52 (16.7x forward earnings, 5.2% FCF yield), the market is pricing Gilead as a slow-growth cash cow with no credit for lenacapavir's $2B+ PrEP potential or Trodelvy's first-line TNBC expansion — which means the price is fair only if you believe the HIV core delivers ~3–4% FCF growth with no pipeline contribution.

7. **Margin of safety**: The margin of safety is in the business, not the price — Biktarvy's contractually locked 10-year exclusivity makes a permanent loss of capital nearly impossible, but at $147 you are paying close to base-case fair value ($150–165) with a bear case that cuts 18–32% to $100–120.

8. **Thesis breaker**: Sell if Biktarvy loses more than 5 percentage points of U.S. market share within 12 months — that is the only signal that would indicate genuine HIV franchise disruption before 2036, which is the single assumption the entire thesis rests on.
