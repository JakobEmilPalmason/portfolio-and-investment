## Detailed Analysis

**Owner Earnings Calculation**

FY2025 net income was $101.8B. D&A was $34.2B. Total CapEx was $64.6B for FY2025, rising to $83.1B on a trailing twelve-month (TTM) basis through December 2025. The critical question is how much of this CapEx is maintenance versus growth. Microsoft's historic maintenance CapEx (pre-AI investment surge) ran approximately $10–15B annually; the remaining $50–70B is expansion into Azure datacenters and AI infrastructure. Using a conservative maintenance CapEx of $15B, owner earnings approximate: $101.8B + $34.2B - $15B = ~$121B. Even using a more cautious $20–25B maintenance CapEx estimate, owner earnings land in the $110–115B range. Reported free cash flow of $71.6B (FY2025) and $66–70B TTM (as reported, net of all CapEx) significantly understates economic owner earnings due to the AI infrastructure build cycle.

**Scenario Analysis**

*Bear Case (Year 5):* Azure growth decelerates to 15–18% annually, AI monetization disappoints, operating margins compress 3–4 percentage points from regulatory pressure and wage inflation. FY2026E revenue of ~$320B grows at 12% for 4 more years to reach ~$500B by FY2031. Operating margins of 40% (vs. current ~47%) yield ~$200B operating income. After 20% tax and D&A/CapEx normalization, owner earnings of ~$80B. Applying a 22x multiple (below historical norm) yields a market cap of ~$1.76T, or ~$237 per share discounted back at 10% over 5 years: approximately $147/share intrinsic value. At $405, this scenario implies roughly 64% downside — but this bear case requires multiple simultaneous failures and is low probability.

*Base Case (Year 5):* Revenue grows at 14–15% annually (consistent with current trajectory, modest deceleration). FY2031 revenue of ~$550–580B. Operating margins hold at 44–46% on AI leverage. Owner earnings of ~$130–140B. Apply a 25x multiple: market cap ~$3.3–3.5T, or $440–470 per share undiscounted, ~$270–290 per share discounted at 10% over 5 years. This suggests the stock is roughly fairly to modestly undervalued today at $405.

*Bull Case (Year 5):* Azure sustains 30%+ growth through FY2028 before gradually decelerating, AI Copilot achieves mass commercial penetration, operating margins expand to 50%+ as AI reduces labor cost. FY2031 owner earnings of $160–180B. At a 28x multiple: market cap ~$4.5–5.0T, or $600–670 per share undiscounted, ~$370–420 per share discounted at 10%. This is above today's price, suggesting meaningful upside in the bull case.

**Multiples in Context**

The current trailing P/E of ~26x is the lowest since 2018–2019 (pre-Azure rerating). The EV/EBITDA of 15–17x is 20% below Microsoft's own 10-year median. P/FCF of approximately 26–30x on reported FCF looks elevated in isolation but normalizes dramatically once the growth CapEx cycle is recognized. Peers: Alphabet trades at ~22x earnings, Apple at ~28x, Amazon at ~30x on EBIT. Microsoft is not cheap by absolute historical standards, but it is notably cheaper relative to its recent trading range and the quality of its franchise.

**Reverse-Engineering Today's Price**

At $405 and ~3.75B shares outstanding, market cap is ~$1.52T. With net cash/short-term investments (net of debt) of approximately $40–50B, enterprise value is ~$1.47–1.48T. For today's price to be justified at a 10% required return, Microsoft needs to generate approximately $150B in owner earnings within 5 years or $200B+ within 7–8 years. Given FY2025 owner earnings (normalized) of ~$110–120B, this requires roughly 8–10% annual owner earnings growth — which is well below the 14–18% revenue growth rate. The market is NOT pricing in perfection; it is pricing in a mild deceleration scenario.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** Azure and AI Copilot drive 14–16% annual owner earnings growth, the stock re-rates toward historical multiples, yielding 70–100% total return over 5 years.
- **Bear case:** AI monetization disappoints, regulatory unbundling hits Office/Teams/Azure bundling moat, margins compress, and the stock remains range-bound with single-digit returns.
- **Confidence:** Medium-High — Current valuation is below historical norms on multiple metrics; normalized owner earnings are strong; the primary uncertainty is whether CapEx-heavy AI investments convert to durable margin expansion.

## Red Flags

- Reported FCF significantly compressed by $83B TTM CapEx; normalization requires judgment about maintenance vs. growth split
- Bear case DCF estimates from third parties range from $220 to $393, showing wide dispersion; aggressive terminal value assumptions drive results dramatically
- EPS in Q1 FY2026 was distorted by $3.1B OpenAI investment loss (–$0.41/share); Q2 was distorted by $7.6B OpenAI gain (+$1.02/share) — GAAP earnings are noisy
- No margin of safety in a scenario where AI capex cycle extends without monetization

## Score: 6 / 10

Microsoft is fairly to modestly undervalued relative to its own history and normalized earnings power, but the compressed current FCF and uncertainty around AI capex payback prevent a higher score; the business deserves a premium multiple and is getting one, just a lower premium than its 2021–2024 peak.
