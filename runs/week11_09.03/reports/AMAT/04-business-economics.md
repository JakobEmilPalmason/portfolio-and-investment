## Detailed Analysis

**Returns on Capital**

Applied Materials has delivered ROIC in the 27–34% range over recent years, with GuruFocus citing ~27.45% as of late 2025 and financecharts.com tracking it higher during peak cycle years. ROE peaked at ~53% in FY2021 and has settled to approximately 35–38% in FY2024–FY2025, still well above any cost-of-capital hurdle. The trend direction over the 2019–2025 period is structurally higher than the 2012–2018 era, reflecting the shift to advanced-node equipment where pricing power is stronger and the competitive moat is more durable. These are genuinely elite returns on capital for an industrial-technology company operating in a cyclical industry.

**Margin Structure**

Gross margins expanded from 44.7% in FY2020 to 48.7% in FY2025 (GAAP), with non-GAAP gross margin reaching 48.8% — a 25-year high. This expansion reflects two reinforcing dynamics: (1) mix shift toward advanced-node systems where ASPs are higher and margin is structurally richer, and (2) increasing AGS subscription revenue which carries higher gross margin than new equipment. Operating margin has ranged from ~25% in FY2020 to a peak near ~30% in FY2022, with the current level around 27–29% on a GAAP basis. The slight moderation from peak reflects investment spending (R&D, Epic Center), not competitive pressure. Net margin in FY2025 was ~24.7%, consistent with a highly profitable industrial-technology franchise.

**Cash Generation**

FY2025 operating cash flow was approximately $8.0B on revenue of $28.4B (~28% OCF margin). Free cash flow was $5.7B after $2.3B in capex, much of which was for the Epic Center campus — an unusual large investment that inflated FY2025 capex meaningfully above normalized levels. In prior years (FY2023–FY2024), FCF was $7.5–7.6B on net income of $6.9–7.2B, implying FCF conversion well above 100% of net income — a sign of clean accruals accounting and strong working capital management. Q1 FY2026 FCF was $1.04B on quarterly revenue of $7.01B, suggesting normalization of FCF conversion as the large capex cycle winds down. Owner earnings (net income + D&A − maintenance capex) are structurally higher than reported FCF in FY2025 due to the non-recurring Epic Center spend.

**Capital Intensity**

Applied Materials manufactures and ships capital equipment, which requires meaningful inventory, service infrastructure, and R&D. However, compared to the chip fabs it supplies, AMAT is far less capital-intensive per dollar of revenue. Maintenance capex is estimated below $700–800M per year; the $2.3B in FY2025 included the Epic Center facility, a large but one-time headquarters/R&D campus investment. The AGS services segment is largely asset-light (spare parts, software, contracts), which improves the blended capital intensity profile. As AGS grows its subscription share, the overall business should trend modestly more asset-light over time.

**Revenue Quality**

Total FY2025 revenue was $28.37B, up ~4% year-over-year. The mix is approximately: Semiconductor Systems (~72%), AGS (~23%), Display and Adjacent (~5%). Within AGS, management disclosed that more than two-thirds of revenues are from subscriptions (service contracts, software licenses) — over $4.3B in recurring, sticky revenue. This is a meaningful quality differentiator relative to peers. The equipment side is inherently cyclical and lumpy, but contract wins on leading-edge node transitions provide multi-year revenue visibility. Starting Q1 FY2026, AMAT moved the 200mm equipment business out of AGS, making AGS a pure recurring subscription business — improving investor visibility.

**Operating Leverage**

As revenue grows from the current base, operating leverage is visible: the step from $17B (FY2020) to $28B (FY2025) in revenue increased operating income from ~$4.3B to ~$8.5B, implying operating income grew roughly twice as fast as revenue. This is classic operating leverage — fixed R&D and SG&A spread over a larger revenue base. With management guiding for 20%+ growth in the semiconductor systems business in calendar 2026, incremental margins on systems revenue are likely to be above the corporate average, driving further margin expansion. The biggest risk to operating leverage is a sharp WFE (wafer fab equipment) spending downturn, which compresses revenue faster than costs.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** Record-high margins, ROIC above 25%, and a growing $6B+ recurring services base position AMAT for strong compounding as AI-driven capex sustains equipment demand through 2027.
- **Bear case:** WFE spending is inherently cyclical, and a downturn in leading-edge investment (e.g., TSMC capacity pause, China restrictions) could cause rapid revenue and FCF compression given the high fixed-cost R&D base.
- **Confidence:** High — AMAT's financials are extensively reported and the margin/ROIC trends are well-documented across multiple independent data sources spanning 5+ years.

## Red Flags

- FY2025 FCF of $5.7B was meaningfully below FY2023–2024 levels (~$7.5B) due to the Epic Center capex; investors should distinguish this one-time capital commitment from a structural deterioration in cash generation.
- Export control settlement ($253M charge in Q1 FY2026) highlights ongoing compliance risk around China-related equipment sales, which represent a non-trivial share of revenue.
- Operating margin has not fully recovered to FY2022 peak levels despite revenue growth, suggesting elevated R&D and opex investment is absorbing some scale benefits.

## Score: 8 / 10

Applied Materials demonstrates sustainably high ROIC (27–34%), best-in-25-year gross margins, strong FCF generation with above-100% conversion in normalized years, and a growing recurring services base — offset only by the inherent cyclicality of WFE spending and a one-time capex surge that temporarily depressed FY2025 FCF.
