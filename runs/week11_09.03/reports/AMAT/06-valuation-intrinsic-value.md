## Detailed Analysis

**Owner Earnings Calculation**

FY2025 reported GAAP net income was approximately $7.5B (implied by $8.66 EPS x ~864M diluted shares). FCF was $5.7B after $2.3B in capex. D&A for AMAT runs approximately $800M-$1.0B annually. Maintenance capex — stripping out the elevated EPIC R&D center buildout and new capacity expansion — is estimated at roughly $600-700M annually, as management has explicitly flagged elevated capex in FY2025-2026 as non-recurring infrastructure investment. Owner earnings therefore: ~$7.5B net income + ~$0.9B D&A - ~$0.65B maintenance capex = approximately **$7.75B** in FY2025. FCF of $5.7B is somewhat understated due to the expansion capex cycle. Normalized owner earnings are closer to $7.5-8.0B.

In 3-5 years (FY2028-2030): with revenue growing toward $37B+ and operating margins held steady or improving (management targets 30%+ operating margins), owner earnings could reach $10-12B if the cycle delivers. With 6-8% share count reduction per year from buybacks, per-share owner earnings grow faster still.

**Scenario Analysis**

*Bear case (Year 5 = FY2031):* Semi equipment cycle turns sharply down; China restriction headwinds persist; AI capex normalizes. Revenue flattens at $30B with margin compression to 26% operating margin. Net income ~$6.5B. Apply 14x multiple (trough cycle) = ~$91B equity value. Discount back at 10% for 5 years: PV ~$56.5B. Shares outstanding ~820M after buybacks: ~$69/share intrinsic value.

*Base case (Year 5 = FY2031):* Revenue grows to $36-37B per analyst consensus, operating margins at 30%, net income ~$9B. Apply 18x multiple (mid-cycle for high-quality compounder) = $162B equity value. Discount at 10%: PV ~$101B. Per share: ~$123/share intrinsic value.

*Bull case (Year 5 = FY2031):* AI infrastructure buildout sustains above-trend equipment spend; Gate-All-Around and backside power rail transitions expand AMAT's SAM; revenue reaches $42B, operating margins expand to 32%, net income ~$11B. Apply 22x = $242B. Discount at 10%: PV ~$150B. Per share: ~$183/share intrinsic value.

Current price is approximately $150 (as of early March 2026 — note: AMAT has pulled back significantly from its mid-2024 peak of ~$255). At current price, base case implies modest upside (+18-20%) and bull case implies substantial upside (+22%). Bear case implies meaningful downside (-54%). The distribution is not particularly asymmetric at current price.

**Multiples in Context**

At ~$150/share with FY2026E EPS consensus near $8.80-9.00 (reflecting the China headwind trough), the forward P/E is approximately 16-17x. EV/EBITDA (EBITDA ~$9.3B for FY2025) at current market cap ~$128B plus net debt ~$1.2B = EV ~$130B, or roughly 14x EBITDA. Historically AMAT has traded between 12-25x forward P/E through cycles, with the median closer to 16-18x during normal periods. The current multiple is at the lower end of normal, suggesting the market has priced in near-term headwinds. P/FCF based on $5.7B FCF and market cap ~$128B = ~22x, higher than P/E due to elevated capex suppressing FCF.

Versus peers: KLAC trades at approximately 22-24x forward P/E and LRCX at 18-20x. AMAT's discount is partly justified by its greater China exposure and the current trough, but is arguably too wide given AMAT's superior product breadth and installed base leadership.

**What Must Be True for Current Price to Be Justified**

At $150/share, the implied assumptions are: (1) EPS grows at roughly 8-10% per year over the next decade; (2) operating margins remain in the 29-31% range; (3) China headwinds are fully absorbed by FY2027 with non-China AI-driven demand picking up the slack; (4) the semi equipment cycle does not enter a prolonged multi-year trough. These are plausible but not certain assumptions. The market is not pricing perfection — it is pricing reasonable recovery — but it is also not pricing disaster. This is a fairly valued to modestly undervalued situation depending on cycle assumptions.

**Pricing Perfection or Disaster?**

Neither. The stock is down ~40% from its 2024 peak, reflecting genuine near-term headwinds (China restrictions, FY2026 guidance trough). At 16-17x forward earnings, the market is pricing moderate recovery but not assuming a return to peak-cycle conditions. The current price embeds reasonable pessimism about FY2026 and appropriate uncertainty about when the next upcycle begins.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** AI-driven leading-edge capex sustains multi-year equipment cycle, China headwind is temporary, and AMAT's GAA/HBM exposure drives above-consensus revenue to $40B+ by FY2028 with margin expansion.
- **Bear case:** Semi equipment cycle turns down, China restrictions expand further, AI capex normalizes earlier than expected, and AMAT trades at trough multiples on compressed earnings.
- **Confidence:** Medium — FY2025 results are known and strong; near-term FY2026 headwind is quantified; but 3-5 year intrinsic value depends heavily on cycle timing and AI capex sustainability, both of which are uncertain.

## Red Flags

- Current EV/EBITDA and P/FCF multiples are not cheap in absolute terms; base case upside is modest (~18-20%), requiring a bull scenario for significant returns
- China revenue decline from 37% to mid-20s% removes a meaningful earnings contributor
- Elevated capex depresses near-term FCF; FY2026 FCF may be below FY2025's $5.7B
- Analyst consensus for FY2026 EPS growth is essentially flat (0-1% YoY), creating limited near-term earnings catalyst

## Score: 6 / 10

AMAT is fairly valued to modestly undervalued at current prices — the stock has de-rated meaningfully from peak, multiples are at the low end of historical norms, and the business quality is high — but the base case upside is only ~18-20% and the bear case carries real 40-50% downside, leaving the risk/reward balanced rather than compelling.
