## Detailed Analysis

**Revenue Scale and Product Concentration**

FY2025 net sales reached DKK 309,064 million, +6% in Danish kroner and +10% at constant exchange rates. Ozempic generated approximately DKK 127,089 million (+6% DKK, +10% CER), representing ~41% of group revenue. Wegovy (full-year run-rate based on Q4 DKK 21.9B quarterly) contributed roughly DKK 75–82 billion. Combined, semaglutide in its two primary marketed forms almost certainly accounts for over 70% of total group revenue. This concentration — once a compounding asset — is now the central economic vulnerability. Revenue quality depends entirely on whether semaglutide retains pricing power and volume share, both of which are deteriorating simultaneously.

The deterioration is product-specific and competitive. In U.S. GLP-1 diabetes, Novo's share fell from ~53% to ~46% over the year. In obesity, share collapsed from ~68% to ~51% in twelve months. The driver is unambiguous: Lilly's tirzepatide (Zepbound/Mounjaro) now holds approximately 60% of the global branded GLP-1 market. In the U.S., 7–8 of every 10 new GLP-1 prescriptions are going to Lilly products. REDEFINE 4, reported February 23, 2026, confirmed what the market had feared: CagriSema — Novo's principal next-generation obesity asset — failed to achieve non-inferiority to tirzepatide 15 mg (23.0% vs. 25.5% weight loss at 84 weeks). The FDA submission for CagriSema was made in December 2025 and a decision is expected late 2026, but the commercial opportunity is now uncertain given the head-to-head result. The REDEFINE 11 trial (higher-dose CagriSema) will not report until H1 2027. There is no near-term pipeline catalyst to reverse competitive share losses.

**Margin Structure: Structural Compression, Not Cyclical**

Gross margin was 81.0% in FY2025, down from 84.7% in FY2024. The prior report (2026-03-05) characterized this as partly one-time (Catalent amortization, restructuring costs). The REFRESH assessment is less charitable: the 70% U.S. price reduction on GLP-1 products announced for 2026 is not a one-time item — it is a permanent repricing of the company's core franchise. Combined with Medicaid coverage changes and Most Favored Nations pricing, the net realized price per unit will be materially and durably lower. Gross margins in 2026 will compress further, potentially into the 75–78% range, as lower U.S. net prices flow through against a fixed-cost manufacturing base that remains at DKK 60B capex scale.

Operating margin was 41.3% in FY2025, down from ~44% in FY2024. Operating profit grew only 6% CER against 10% CER revenue growth, meaning operating leverage has reversed. The 2026 guidance of a 5–13% decline in adjusted sales and operating profit implies operating profit in the range of approximately DKK 112–121 billion. At the low end (−13% CER, plus ~3 ppts currency), operating profit could be DKK 100–108 billion. Net profit margin was 33.0% in FY2025. The 2026 net margin will likely fall into the mid-to-high 20s.

**Free Cash Flow and Capital Intensity**

This is the sharpest deterioration in Novo's economic profile. Capex was guided at ~DKK 60 billion in FY2025, with 2026 guidance of ~DKK 55 billion still elevated. FCF guidance for FY2025 was DKK 20–30 billion. Against net income of DKK 102.4 billion, FCF conversion is approximately 20–29% — extraordinary low for a company historically generating 75%+ FCF conversion. The gap is almost entirely growth capex for the manufacturing build-out (Catalent sites, API expansion, aseptic capacity across North America and Europe).

The economic problem is timing: Novo built a DKK 60B/year capex machine to supply GLP-1s at the previous pricing and market-share regime. Capacity was sized for a world where Novo had 65%+ share and premium U.S. pricing. That world no longer exists. The fixed costs of a manufacturing base this size do not easily compress. Owner earnings (net income + D&A − maintenance capex) are materially higher than reported FCF — probably in the DKK 85–95B range — but even owner earnings face downward pressure as net income falls 5–13% in 2026.

**Return on Capital: Deteriorating from an Elite Base**

ROIC at December 2025 is approximately 21% per GuruFocus methodology (which includes the Catalent-inflated capital base) or 41% per trailing calculations that may not fully reflect the asset build-up. Either way, the direction is unambiguously downward from the 37–41% ROIC range of 2021–2023. ROE is approximately 61%, still high, partially reflecting the debt-funded capital structure post-Catalent. The core problem: invested capital is growing at ~DKK 60B/year while return (EBIT) is about to decline 5–13% in 2026. This combination mathematically drives ROIC lower, likely toward the mid-teens by end of 2026 absent a recovery catalyst.

**The 2026 Inflection — Why This REFRESH Scores Lower**

The prior report (2026-03-05) scored Business Economics at 7/10, treating 2026 headwinds as partly cyclical and noting the pipeline. This REFRESH scores lower for three reasons that have crystallized since that report: (1) CagriSema's REDEFINE 4 failure removes the near-term pipeline solution to competitive erosion; (2) the FDA warning letter (adverse-event reporting failures, issued 2026-03-05) adds regulatory overhang and signals potential supply disruption risk from a second GMP warning letter related to the Bloomington, Indiana facility; and (3) the 70% U.S. price cut is confirmed and permanent — not a negotiating position. The combination of collapsing pricing, shrinking share, failed next-gen pipeline, and a manufacturing base built for a different world is an unusual accumulation of simultaneous headwinds for what was, three years ago, one of the world's best businesses.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** Manufacturing scale-up creates durable long-run cost advantages; oral semaglutide (Wegovy pill) is launching extremely strongly; a higher-dose CagriSema (REDEFINE 11) or other pipeline assets could restore efficacy leadership by 2027–2028; even at guided declines, the business generates ~DKK 100B+ in operating profit and is far from distress.
- **Bear case:** The 70% U.S. price cut is permanent, tirzepatide dominance is structural, CagriSema failed the key head-to-head test, the manufacturing base was built for a different pricing regime, and regulatory headwinds (FDA warning letters on both adverse-event reporting and GMP) add operational risk — the entire earnings and ROIC trend is now pointing down with no near-term catalyst to reverse it.
- **Confidence:** Medium — the financial facts for FY2025 are well-sourced and clear; the uncertainty centers on how deep the 2026–2027 earnings trough will be and whether any pipeline asset (oral semaglutide, higher-dose CagriSema) can meaningfully restore competitive position.

---

## Red Flags

- Gross margin compressed 370 bps in FY2025; further 2026 compression is certain given 70% U.S. price reduction flowing through net revenue
- Operating leverage has reversed: operating profit growing slower than revenue in FY2025, and both declining in FY2026
- FCF conversion at 20–29% of net income — uncharacteristically low; capex-to-revenue at ~19% versus pharma norm of 5–8%
- CagriSema failed non-inferiority to tirzepatide in REDEFINE 4; no approved next-gen obesity asset until at least late 2026 and likely meaningfully later if CagriSema receives pushback at FDA
- Tirzepatide market share gains are accelerating, not plateauing: obesity share went from 68% to 51% in 12 months; trend shows no sign of stabilizing
- ROIC declining from 41% (2021–2023) to ~21% (Dec 2025) as capital base expands into a deteriorating pricing environment
- 2026: first guided revenue decline in modern history, arriving simultaneously with peak debt levels and a management restructuring (new CEO, two senior executives departed with the February earnings release)
- FDA adverse-event reporting warning letter (issued 2026-03-05) plus GMP warning letter for Bloomington, Indiana facility — dual regulatory pressure is unusual and creates potential supply disruption risk

## Score: 5 / 10

The underlying economic engine (81% gross margins, 41% operating margins, >DKK 100B operating cash flow) remains exceptional in absolute terms, but the directional trend across every key metric — ROIC, gross margin, operating margin, FCF conversion, revenue growth, competitive share — is now negative. The combination of permanent pricing resets, confirmed pipeline setback (CagriSema REDEFINE 4), manufacturing cost base built for a different regime, and regulatory headwinds warrants a materially lower score than the prior report's 7/10. This is still a very good business, but it is a good business under genuine and multidimensional stress.
