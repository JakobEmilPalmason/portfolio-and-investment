## Detailed Analysis

**Owner Earnings Today**

Owner earnings are best approximated as: net income + D&A – maintenance capex. SPGI reported GAAP diluted EPS of $14.66 in 2025 and adjusted diluted EPS of $17.83. Free cash flow of ~$5.46B on roughly 306 million diluted shares gives FCF/share of approximately $17.84. After modest maintenance capex adjustments (SPGI's capex is low for a largely IP-based business — roughly $200–300M annually), owner earnings per share are broadly in the $17–19 range. This is the cash the owner actually receives; adjusted EPS is not far off given the business has very limited tangible reinvestment needs.

Looking out 3–5 years, management's own organic revenue guidance of 6–8% for 2026 and the medium-term growth narrative of the Indices (10–12% organic) and Ratings recovery suggest owner earnings in the $22–27/share range by 2029–2030 under a reasonable base case.

**Scenario Analysis (5-Year, Year-5 Owner Earnings)**

Bear case: Debt issuance volumes disappoint due to rising rates or credit market stress (Ratings only grew 4% in the bear scenario). Market Intelligence faces AI-driven seat erosion. Revenue growth averages 4% organically; margins are flat. Year-5 owner earnings: ~$21/share. Apply a 20x multiple (discount for uncertainty): ~$420. Discount at 10% for 5 years: ~$260 present value. At $429, this is below the bear case PV — a genuine risk.

Base case: Organic revenue growth averages 7% (inline with guidance midpoint); Mobility is spun off cleanly; margins expand modestly (50 bps/year). Year-5 adjusted owner earnings: ~$25/share. Apply 25x: ~$625. Discount at 10%: ~$388. Add in dividends ($4+ annually) and buybacks: intrinsic value in the $400–450 range over this horizon, essentially in line with current price.

Bull case: Indices accelerate to 12%+ on passive investing and private markets data; Ratings benefits from refinancing supercycle; Mobility spin-off re-rates remaining business. Year-5 owner earnings: ~$29/share. Apply 28x: ~$812. Discount at 9% (lower rate environment): ~$528. Current price represents a 19% discount to bull case PV.

**Multiples in Context**

At $429, SPGI trades at approximately:
- 22x forward 2026 adjusted EPS (~$19.53 midpoint) — well below the 5-year average of ~30–37x
- ~24x 2025 FCF/share ($17.84)
- EV/EBITDA of approximately 17.5x (reported by FinanceCharts)
- P/B of ~5x

For a business with 95%+ proprietary data revenues, a near-monopoly in bond ratings and financial indices, consistent double-digit EPS growth, 53 consecutive years of dividend increases, and $5B in annual buybacks, the historical multiple range of 28–37x was arguably stretched. But 22x forward earnings for this quality of business is historically inexpensive.

**What Must Be True at $429?**

To justify $429 on a 10-year DCF at a 9% discount rate, the market is implying roughly: 7–8% FCF growth for 5 years, tapering to 3–4% thereafter, with a 20–22x terminal multiple. These are not heroic assumptions — they are moderate and broadly consistent with management's own guidance. The market is NOT pricing in perfection; it is pricing in a slightly-below-historical growth scenario, likely reflecting concern about debt issuance cycles and AI disruption risk to Market Intelligence.

**Implied Expectations vs Likely Reality**

The stock's 26% pullback from its August 2025 high looks like the market discounting several concerns simultaneously: the 2026 guidance miss (management guided lower than Street expectations), the Mobility spin-off uncertainty, and general risk-off in financial services. These are real but not thesis-breaking risks. The base case assumptions embedded in the current price are conservative rather than pessimistic, and the business fundamentals (recurring revenues, dominant market positions, strong FCF) remain intact.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** Mobility spin-off completes cleanly mid-2026, re-rating the remaining pure-play data business to 27–28x while owner earnings grow 8–10% annually, yielding a $580–620 fair value within 18 months.
- **Bear case:** Debt issuance slows materially, credit market stress hits Ratings revenues, and AI disrupts Market Intelligence faster than expected — compressing earnings to the $20/share range and keeping multiples suppressed around 20x (~$400 floor).
- **Confidence:** Medium — the business quality is high and the multiple contraction is real, but the wide range of DCF estimates ($251–$435) reflects genuine disagreement about terminal growth rates and the spin-off impact.

## Red Flags

- Bear-case DCF (GuruFocus projected FCF model) yields ~$251 intrinsic value — far below current price; this model may be too conservative on terminal growth, but it cannot be dismissed.
- 2025 FCF came in modestly below the $6B guidance, suggesting some FCF conversion pressure that warrants monitoring.
- GAAP EPS of $14.66 vs adjusted EPS of $17.83 — a $3.17/share gap driven by amortization of acquired intangibles (IHS Markit). GAAP P/E (~29x) is higher than adjusted P/E (~22x); investors should be aware they are paying for an acquisition that is still running off goodwill.
- The current price is essentially at the midpoint of analyst DCF intrinsic value estimates — there is no clear pricing anomaly.

## Score: 5 / 10

At ~$429 the stock is roughly fairly valued on a base-case analysis — no meaningful margin of safety exists, but the stock is not egregiously overpriced; the compressed multiple vs history is real but the base-case value is essentially in line with the current price.
