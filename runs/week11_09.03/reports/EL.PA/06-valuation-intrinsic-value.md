## Detailed Analysis

### Owner Earnings Estimate

EssilorLuxottica generated $3.8B in free cash flow and $3.9B in owner earnings (net income + D&A - capex) in FY2025. FCF conversion at 163% of net income is exceptionally strong, driven by $3.1B in D&A against only $1.5B in capex — meaning roughly half of depreciation charges do not require reinvestment, a hallmark of asset-light economics within a capital-intensive infrastructure. Adjusting for the approximately $280M in stock-based compensation, adjusted owner earnings sit around $3.6B, or roughly €3.3B at current exchange rates. On 463M shares outstanding, that translates to approximately €7.10 per share in normalized owner earnings. At the current price of €204.70, investors are paying roughly 29x owner earnings — a reasonable but not cheap multiple for a dominant global franchise.

### Scenario Analysis (5-Year DCF-Lite)

The company's 2026 targets (confirmed despite tariffs) call for €27-28B revenue and 19-20% adjusted operating margins. Taking the midpoint (€27.5B revenue, 19.5% adjusted operating margin) yields ~€5.4B adjusted operating profit, or roughly €3.8B in net income assuming a 24% effective tax rate and current interest costs. That equates to ~€8.20/share in normalized EPS — consistent with the forward EPS estimate of $8.65.

**Bear case (Year 5, 2031):** Revenue grows 4% annually (tariff drag, smart glasses competition caps growth), margins settle at 17% adjusted (below target due to competitive investment). Year 5 EPS ~€9.50. At 25x earnings (de-rating toward medtech average): **intrinsic value ~€238/share**. Discounted at 8%: **~€162/share.**

**Base case (Year 5, 2031):** Revenue grows 7% annually (in line with FY2025 constant-currency pace), margins expand to 19-20% as guided. Year 5 EPS ~€13.00. At 30x earnings (modest premium to medtech peers): **intrinsic value ~€390/share**. Discounted at 8%: **~€265/share.**

**Bull case (Year 5, 2031):** Revenue grows 10% annually (AI glasses become a major platform, medtech transformation accelerates), margins reach 21%+. Year 5 EPS ~€17.50. At 35x earnings (premium for platform economics): **intrinsic value ~€613/share**. Discounted at 8%: **~€417/share.**

### Multiples in Context

The trailing P/E of 41.2x looks optically expensive, but this reflects a depressed earnings year. FY2025 reported operating margin was only 11.9% (IFRS) versus the 16.0% adjusted figure, which itself was 70bps below FY2024 at constant currency due to tariff and AI-glasses ramp costs. As margins normalize toward the 19-20% target, EPS should step up materially. The forward P/E of 23.7x — if achievable — would represent the lowest forward multiple in the stock's modern history.

Historically, EssilorLuxottica has traded at a significant premium to the medical equipment sector (sector average ~28x vs. EL's 10-year median of ~40x). This premium is justified by the company's near-monopoly position in prescription lenses, vertically integrated manufacturing-to-retail model, and recurring revenue characteristics (people need new glasses regularly). However, the premium has narrowed sharply: at €204.70, the stock trades at just 23.7x forward earnings, which is actually below the medtech sector average.

### Reverse-Engineering the Current Price

At €204.70 and using forward EPS of ~€8.65, the market is pricing EL.PA at 23.7x forward earnings. If we assume the market typically assigns this business a 30-35x forward multiple, the implied message is either: (a) the market does not believe the €8.65 EPS is achievable, or (b) the market is pricing in a structural de-rating of the franchise. Using a reverse DCF framework at 8% discount rate and a terminal multiple of 30x, the current price implies only ~4-5% annual earnings growth over the next decade — substantially below the company's historical track record of 8-10% and below its own medium-term guidance. This suggests the market is pricing in a meaningful probability of structural deterioration (smart glasses competition, tariff headwinds, margin pressure).

### Implied Expectations vs. Likely Reality

The disconnect is stark. The company just delivered 11.2% constant-currency revenue growth, sold 7 million AI glasses (up from 2 million cumulative in prior years), has production capacity scaling to 10-20 million units, and has a locked-in decade-long partnership with Meta. Meanwhile, the market is pricing in GDP-like growth. The key question is whether Apple or other tech giants entering smart glasses will commoditize EssilorLuxottica's role. If EL retains its position as the manufacturing and brand partner of choice for smart eyewear (likely given its optical expertise and brand portfolio), the current valuation significantly underestimates the growth runway.

## Signal Summary
- **Bull case:** At 23.7x forward earnings for a global monopoly with an emerging AI-glasses platform, this is the cheapest EssilorLuxottica has been in years, with base-case intrinsic value 30-50% above current price.
- **Bear case:** If smart glasses competition structurally disrupts the brand premium and tariffs persistently compress margins, the earnings recovery may disappoint and the stock deserves a lower terminal multiple.
- **Confidence:** Medium — The valuation is unambiguously attractive relative to history, but the magnitude of the recent selloff (-37%) suggests the market sees risks that pure fundamentals may not fully capture; the smart glasses competitive landscape is genuinely uncertain.

## Red Flags
- Trailing P/E of 41x masks the true earnings picture; investors relying on backward-looking multiples will see the stock as expensive
- The gap between IFRS operating margin (11.9%) and adjusted operating margin (16.0%) is significant — 410 basis points of adjustments require scrutiny
- Revenue is reported in EUR but much is earned in USD; currency moves can materially affect reported results
- The 19-20% adjusted margin target for 2026 has not yet been achieved; execution risk remains
