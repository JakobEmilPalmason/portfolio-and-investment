## Detailed Analysis

**Owner Earnings Construction.** FY2025 financials (fiscal year ended January 2026): net income $14.2B, D&A $4.1B (per IR release), total capex $3.7B. Home Depot does not break out maintenance vs. growth capex explicitly; however, given ~2,300 existing stores, a reasonable maintenance capex estimate is 60–70% of total capex, or ~$2.2–$2.6B. Using $2.5B as maintenance capex: owner earnings ≈ $14.2B + $4.1B – $2.5B = **$15.8B**, or approximately **$15.90/share** on ~993M diluted shares. At today's price of $338.93, the stock trades at approximately **21.3x trailing owner earnings** — not expensive on an absolute basis for a business of this quality, but not cheap either.

**Scenario Analysis — Year 5 (FY2030) Intrinsic Value.**

*Bear case:* Housing market remains "frozen" through 2028 with only a gradual thaw. Owner earnings grow at 2% annually (volume offset by macro headwinds). Year 5 owner earnings: ~$17.4B. Apply 17x multiple (stress multiple reflecting no recovery). Equity value ≈ $296B, less ~$47B net debt ≈ $249B equity. Per share: ~$251. Discounted at 9%: **~$163/share**. Current stock is ~108% above this scenario.

*Base case:* Modest housing recovery begins in 2027, with comp sales growth averaging 3% annually through FY2030. Owner earnings grow 5–6% annually (SRS integration lifts pro segment). Year 5 owner earnings: ~$20.2B. Apply 21x multiple (in-line with historical median for a high-quality retailer). Equity value: ~$424B, less net debt $42B (some paydown) ≈ $382B. Per share: ~$385. Discounted at 9%: **~$250/share base**. Alternatively, using a simpler no-discount perpetuity approach with 5% growth at 9% discount: owner earnings yield of 4.7% + 5% growth = ~$330–$360 fair value range. **Base case intrinsic value: $310–$360.**

*Bull case:* Housing market recovery accelerates in 2026–2027, pent-up demand unleashes, SRS fully synergized, Pro segment takes share. Owner earnings grow 8–9% annually. Year 5: ~$23.3B. Apply 24x multiple. Equity value: ~$559B, less net debt $38B ≈ $521B. Per share: ~$525. Discounted at 9%: **~$340–$380** intrinsic; at a 24x terminal + growth, stock could be worth $420–$450 in this outcome. **Bull case intrinsic value: $420–$450.**

**Multiples in Context.** Current metrics: P/E (TTM) ~23.9x, forward P/E ~23–24x on FY2026 guidance, EV/EBITDA ~16.6x, P/FCF (TTM) ~26.8x. Historical context: HD's 10-year P/E range was 14.8x–34x with a median of ~23x. The current P/E is essentially at the 10-year median. EV/EBITDA of 16.6x is elevated relative to the pre-acquisition historical average of ~13–14x, partly reflecting the SRS debt load. Versus peers: Lowe's (LOW) trades at ~18–19x forward earnings — HD commands a premium consistent with its larger scale and Pro business exposure.

**What Must Be True to Justify Today's Price.** At $338.93, the market is pricing in approximately: (1) a genuine, if gradual, housing market recovery beginning in 2026–2027; (2) SRS Integration delivering $500–$750M in incremental EBITDA within 3 years; (3) continued EPS compounding of 5–7% annually through buybacks and modest comp growth; (4) no material deterioration in gross margin from tariffs or mix shift. This is a reasonable but not easy bar to clear — it assumes normalization, not acceleration.

**Market Pricing Assessment.** The market is not pricing perfection. HD is ~20% below its December 2024 all-time high of $421. The stock is also not pricing disaster. It sits near base-case fair value, with upside conditional on housing normalization. The risk/reward is not asymmetrically attractive from a pure valuation standpoint — buyers at current prices are essentially paying fair value for a high-quality franchise and hoping for a re-rating catalyst.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** Housing market recovery in 2027 + SRS synergies re-rates HD to 25–26x earnings, driving shares to $420–$450.
- **Bear case:** Prolonged rate environment keeps housing frozen, EPS stagnates, debt burden weighs on multiple, stock drifts to $230–$270.
- **Confidence:** Medium — the business quality is high and well-documented; the valuation uncertainty is entirely a function of the housing recovery timing, which is unknowable with precision.

---

## Red Flags

- No margin of safety vs. base case intrinsic value at current price (~$339 vs. ~$310–$360 base range)
- Forward P/FCF of ~27x is elevated; FCF declined ~22% YoY in FY2025 due to SRS acquisition integration costs
- $47B+ long-term debt (acquired via SRS) constrains financial flexibility and adds ~$2.3B annual interest drag
- FY2026 guidance explicitly warns growth is contingent on housing market recovery — management itself is flagging macro dependency
- Tariff risk on imported building materials and appliances not yet quantified in guidance

---

## Score: 5 / 10

HD is a high-quality business priced near fair value with no meaningful margin of safety at current levels; the stock is appropriate for patient capital awaiting a pullback to the $270–$300 range, not as a buy at $339.
