## Detailed Analysis

### Owner Earnings Estimate

ASML reported 2025 net income of €9.6 billion, with D&A approximately €0.8–1.0 billion (extrapolated from prior years; 2025 specific figure not confirmed in press release summary). Total 2025 capex was approximately €1.8 billion, with a meaningful portion attributable to facility expansion and High-NA EUV tooling — growth capex rather than pure maintenance. Estimating maintenance capex at roughly €0.9–1.1 billion (close to D&A given asset-light intellectual property profile), owner earnings for 2025 are approximately:

- Net income: €9.6B
- Add D&A: ~€1.0B
- Less maintenance capex: ~€1.0B
- **Owner earnings estimate: ~€9.6B**

Note: Free cash flow reported by ASML for 2025 was ~€11 billion, slightly higher due to working capital changes and other items. Owner earnings on this method land at €9.5–10.5 billion depending on assumptions. The stock at €1,260/share and approximately 396 million diluted shares implies a market cap of ~€499B. Owner earnings yield is therefore approximately **1.9–2.1%** — expensive on an absolute cash-yield basis.

**Bear Case — China revenue collapses, growth stalls**
- Revenue grows to €36B by 2030 (3% CAGR from 2025 base), reflecting DUV ban implementation and no meaningful recovery
- Gross margin stays flat at 52–53% (China mix-shift pressures, High-NA ramp delays)
- Owner earnings reach ~€11B by 2030
- Exit P/E: 28x (de-rating to below-average given growth disappointment)
- Implied market cap 2030: ~€308B
- Discounted back at 10%: ~€191B
- Per share (assuming ~395M shares): ~**€483 / ~$520** — approximately 59% downside from current

**Base Case — Guided trajectory, measured China headwinds**
- Revenue reaches €44–46B by 2030 (7% CAGR), aligned with the low end of ASML's own 2030 scenario
- Gross margin expands to 56% as High-NA EUV mix increases
- Owner earnings reach ~€15–16B by 2030
- Exit P/E: 32x (modest compression from current 40x forward multiple)
- Implied market cap 2030: ~€490B
- Add dividends (~€3.50/share/year x 5 years ≈ €17.5/share)
- Discounted back at 10%: ~€305B + dividends
- Per share: ~**€771 + dividends** — approximately 39% upside before dividends, modest return for the risk

**Bull Case — AI capex supercycle sustains, High-NA ramp accelerates**
- Revenue reaches €55–60B by 2030 (11–13% CAGR), near top of ASML's guidance range
- Gross margin expands to 58–60%
- Owner earnings reach €22–25B by 2030
- Exit P/E: 35–38x (premium tech multiple sustained)
- Implied market cap 2030: ~€800–900B
- Discounted back at 10%: ~€500–560B

The current P/E of 52x trailing is 45% above the 10-year average of 36x. Even the forward multiple of ~40x is above the historical average, meaning the stock is pricing in meaningful execution on ASML's long-term roadmap.

### What Must Be True for the Current Price to Be Justified?

At a ~€499B market cap and a 10% discount rate, ASML must deliver approximately €16–18B in owner earnings by 2030 and sustain a 30–35x exit multiple. This requires:
1. Revenue reaching €44–50B by 2030 (7–9% CAGR)
2. Gross margin expanding to at least 55–56% (from today's 52.8%)
3. No catastrophic export control event eliminating China revenue permanently
4. High-NA EUV ramping on schedule with strong customer adoption

These are achievable but not trivial. The base case barely justifies today's price. The bull case produces modest upside. Only below-base scenarios produce meaningful alpha from the current entry point.

### Implied Expectations vs. Likely Reality

The market is pricing ASML as though it will execute precisely on its 2030 ambitions with no significant setbacks. At a 40x forward P/E, the stock reflects optimism that AI-driven semiconductor capex continues at high rates, High-NA EUV ramps smoothly, and China headwinds are manageable rather than severe. While ASML's competitive moat is extraordinary and these outcomes are plausible, the market is not offering a meaningful discount for the execution risk. This is not a "disaster priced in" situation — it is closer to perfection being priced in.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** If AI semiconductor capex sustains a multi-year supercycle and High-NA EUV adoption accelerates, ASML's owner earnings could reach €22–25B by 2030, potentially justifying the current price with modest upside.
- **Bear case:** A combination of China DUV ban and semiconductor capex moderation could cause a significant re-rating, with intrinsic value closer to €500–600 per share — nearly 60% below current price.
- **Confidence:** Medium — ASML's qualitative moat is extremely well understood and high conviction; the uncertainty is in the price, not the business quality.

## Red Flags

- Current P/E of ~48–52x is 45% above the 10-year historical average, leaving almost no valuation cushion.
- Bear case scenario suggests ~55–60% downside to conservative intrinsic value — unusually wide range.
- Owner earnings yield of ~2% is very low; the investment relies almost entirely on growth to justify the price.
- Analyst consensus price target of ~$1,625 (from ~$1,368) implies only 19% upside — thin for a business with this level of execution risk.
- No scenario in this analysis produces the 30%+ conservative upside that would constitute a clearly undervalued opportunity.
