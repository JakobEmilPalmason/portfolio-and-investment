## Detailed Analysis

**Owner Earnings Today.** Berkshire eschews traditional EPS in favor of "operating earnings" — net income excluding unrealized investment gains/losses. FY2025 operating earnings were $44.49B, down from ~$47.4B in 2024. On a per-BRK-B share basis (~2.16B equivalent shares): approximately $20.60/share. The decline stemmed from: (1) insurance underwriting profit falling 54% to ~$1.56B after catastrophe losses; (2) insurance investment income declining ~25% to ~$3.1B as short-term rate tailwinds faded; and (3) $8.3B in Kraft Heinz and Occidental impairments. The $369B cash pile generates roughly $15-17B annually at current T-bill rates — this income is embedded in operating earnings. Underlying operating business cash generation (FCF) was $25.1B in FY2025, up sharply from $11.6B in 2024 on improved working capital and the OxyChem acquisition contribution.

**Sum-of-Parts Intrinsic Value.** Berkshire's three components:
1. *Cash and T-bills:* $369B → ~$171/BRK-B share at face value.
2. *Equity portfolio:* ~$264B → ~$122/BRK-B share (top holdings: Apple, AmEx, Coca-Cola, Moody's — high quality, long-duration positions).
3. *Operating businesses:* The 60+ wholly-owned subsidiaries (BNSF, GEICO, BHE, Precision Castparts, Pilot, OxyChem, etc.) generate roughly $27-30B in operating earnings ex-investment income. At 13x (conservative): ~$351-390B; at 15x (fair): ~$405-450B. Per BRK-B share: ~$163-208.

Conservative sum: $171 + $122 + $163 = $456/share. Reasonable sum: $171 + $122 + $185 = $478/share. Bullish sum: $171 + $122 + $208 = $501/share. This range of $456-$501 is tight around the current price of $492, confirming "roughly fairly valued" with slight downside skew if operating earnings don't recover.

**Scenario Analysis (5-Year).**

*Bear case:* Insurance underwriting losses persist, BHE faces ongoing regulatory/liability headwinds, T-bill yields fall to 2% as the Fed cuts aggressively, Abel fails to deploy cash productively, equity portfolio flat. Year 5 operating earnings: ~$38B. Apply 14x to operating businesses (ex-investment income) = $378B businesses value; add $300B residual cash/portfolio. Total: ~$678B, or ~$314/BRK-B share. Discount back 5 years at 8%: ~$213/share. This is an extreme scenario — true liquidation, not going-concern. On a going-concern basis at current P/B of 1.0x: ~$340-360/share floor.

*Base case:* Operating earnings recover to ~$50B by Year 5 (4% CAGR off depressed 2025 base), driven by insurance normalization and partial cash deployment. Apply 16x operating earnings: ~$800B business value; add $250B residual cash/portfolio. Total ~$1.05T or ~$486/BRK-B share. Discounted at 8%: ~$331/share. On an undiscounted forward basis (i.e., what it's worth in Year 5 and you sell then): ~$486. This is consistent with roughly fair value today given modest growth.

*Bull case:* Abel deploys $150-200B in acquisitions at 9-11% returns by Year 5, insurance normalizes, operating earnings reach $65B. Apply 18x: ~$1.17T businesses; portfolio appreciates 6%/yr to ~$353B. Total ~$1.52T or ~$704/BRK-B share. 43% upside from today. Discounted at 8%: ~$479. Even the bull case barely clears current price on a DCF basis — cash optionality is the key unpriced asset.

**What the Market Is Pricing In.** At $492 and $1.07T market cap, the market effectively values the operating businesses at ~$437B after marking cash and equities at face ($633B combined). This implies ~14-15x on normalized operating earnings — not stretched, but not cheap. The $369B cash is being valued at par, with no credit for transformative future deployment. This seems conservative — if Abel replicates even a fraction of Buffett's capital allocation track record, there is meaningful embedded upside.

**Multiples in Context.** P/E ~16x vs 10-yr avg 20.5x: 22% discount, but earnings are in a trough. P/B 1.48x vs median 1.41x: essentially at median. EV/EBITDA ~7-11x (sources vary due to complexity): moderate for quality. Vs. S&P 500 at ~21-22x earnings, BRK offers a quality discount, especially on ex-cash metrics. Vs. Morningstar fair value of $479 (3% above), the stock appears modestly overvalued; vs. analyst consensus target of ~$528 (7% upside), modestly undervalued.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** Abel deploys the $369B cash pile at 9%+ returns within 3-5 years, operating earnings re-accelerate to $60-70B, and the succession discount fades — fair value $600-650/share.
- **Bear case:** Cash earns T-bill returns indefinitely, insurance losses persist, equity portfolio marks down 20%, and stock drifts toward $390-420 (the 1.2x book "Buffett repurchase floor" of ~$399).
- **Confidence:** Medium — the three-part sum-of-parts is transparent and supports fair value near current price; the key uncertainty is whether the $369B cash gets deployed productively, which is unknowable today.

## Red Flags

- FY2025 operating earnings declined 6% YoY with Q4 down ~30% — the base trend entering 2026 is weak
- Insurance underwriting fell 54% in 2025; catastrophe exposure and reserve adequacy are ongoing concerns
- $369B in cash and T-bills creates drag vs. higher-return deployment; opportunity cost compounds annually
- Kraft Heinz and OXY impairments ($8.3B) signal some legacy positions are value-eroding
- Buybacks were suspended throughout 2025 (only resuming March 2026), confirming management saw no bargain at prices well below today's level until very recently

## Score: 6 / 10

BRK-B is roughly fairly valued at ~1.48x book and ~24x reported operating EPS, with sum-of-parts analysis supporting a narrow intrinsic value range of $456-$501 per share; the resumption of buybacks near $492 provides a modest upside signal, but there is limited margin of safety at current prices and the near-term earnings trend is negative.
