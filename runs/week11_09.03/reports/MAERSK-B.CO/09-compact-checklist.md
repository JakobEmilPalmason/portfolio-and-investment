# Compact Checklist — MAERSK-B.CO

**Date:** 2026-03-09

1. **Business**: Maersk moves standardized containers for global manufacturers and retailers on 700+ vessels across every major trade lane, and is mid-execution of an ~$8bn M&A campaign to upsell those same customers on warehousing, customs, air freight, and final-mile delivery — but Ocean shipping still drives two-thirds of revenue and nearly all earnings volatility.

2. **Moat**: Maersk's genuine competitive advantages are APM Terminals' 53 ports in 28 countries (a decades-long barrier to replicate) and the Gemini Cooperation with Hapag-Lloyd achieving 91.2% schedule reliability versus an ~56% industry average — but the core Ocean business is a commodity, market share has fallen five consecutive years from 19.4% to ~14.2%, and the logistics moat is a thesis, not yet evidenced by margins.

3. **Economics**: Maersk's own normalized ROIC target is 7.5%, EBITDA collapsed 89% in a single year (2022 to 2023), free cash flow was guided negative for 2025, and Logistics & Services EBIT margin of 4.9% trails logistics-native peers by 300–700 bps — these are the economics of a capital-intensive commodity business, not a compounder.

4. **Management**: The family-controlled Moller structure provides long-term stability, but the board deployed over $8bn on logistics acquisitions at peak-cycle valuations, suspended the $12bn buyback before completion, and after 3+ years of integration, Logistics & Services organic growth is 3.2% against a 10% target and margins remain below the 6% stated floor — the strategic bet is reasonable but the execution record is mixed.

5. **Debt risk**: Maersk held a net cash position of $7.4bn at end-2024 with $29bn of gross liquidity and investment-grade ratings (Baa1/BBB+), and could absorb two to three years of 30%+ revenue decline without raising equity — but this buffer eroded by $4.8bn in nine months and $11.4bn of capital lease liabilities create a fixed-cost floor that cannot quickly shrink in a downturn.

6. **Price**: For the current price of 16,420 DKK (~$36bn market cap) to make sense, 2026's deeply uncertain EBITDA guidance of $4.5–7.0bn must represent the cycle trough, through-cycle EBITDA must recover to $8–10bn by 2028–30, and the logistics transformation must add durable higher-multiple earnings — all three assumptions must be correct simultaneously.

7. **Margin of safety**: There is no margin of safety at 16,420 DKK — the stock is trading 46% above the base-case 5-year present value of ~11,200 DKK, the analyst consensus target of 12,673 DKK implies 23% downside, and the bear-case intrinsic value of 4,000–7,000 DKK implies 57–76% downside if freight rates collapse and the logistics strategy fails.

8. **Thesis breaker**: Sell if Logistics & Services EBIT margin is still below 6% and contributing less than 15% of group EBIT by fiscal year 2028, because that would confirm that $15bn+ of logistics acquisitions failed to build the replacement moat and Maersk is simply a commodity shipping company priced like something more.
