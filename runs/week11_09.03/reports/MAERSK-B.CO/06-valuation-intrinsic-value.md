## Detailed Analysis

**Owner Earnings Estimate**

Owner earnings = Net income + D&A − maintenance capex. For FY2025: net income was $2.9bn, EBITDA was $9.5bn, implying D&A of approximately $6.0bn (EBITDA minus EBIT of $3.5bn = $6.0bn). Total capex in 2025 was approximately $5.2bn (annualizing the $3.9bn through 9 months at a slightly higher Q4 rate consistent with the $10–11bn two-year guidance). Of this, a rough estimate of maintenance capex — that required to keep the existing fleet and infrastructure operational without growth — is perhaps $2.5–3.0bn annually, consistent with a large fleet operator sustaining some 700+ vessels and a global terminal/logistics network. On that basis: owner earnings ≈ $2.9bn + $6.0bn − $2.8bn = approximately $6.1bn in 2025. However, 2025 still benefited from elevated H1 container rates (Red Sea disruption premium). Adjusting downward for 2026 earnings guidance midpoint ($5.75bn EBITDA implies net income perhaps $1.5–2.0bn), through-cycle owner earnings probably land in the $2.5–4.0bn range. This is the number that matters for long-term valuation.

**Scenario Analysis (Year 5 = 2030)**

Bear case: Red Sea normalizes fully, major overcapacity hits (7 million TEU new build on order = ~20% of global fleet), rates collapse to 2016 lows, logistics transformation stalls. 2030 EBITDA = $4.0bn, owner earnings ~$1.5bn. Apply 8x owner earnings (commodity business discount) = ~$12bn enterprise value, minus net debt (~$3bn net, adjusting for lease liabilities) = ~$9bn equity value. At ~15.3m diluted shares, that implies ~$590/share or roughly 4,200 DKK. That is a severe downside from today's 16,420 DKK.

Base case: Moderate rate normalization, logistics segment grows to ~20% of EBIT, Maersk executes on cost reduction, Red Sea reopens partially in 2026–27 but rates stabilize above 2016 lows. 2030 EBITDA = $7.0bn, owner earnings ~$3.0bn. Apply 10x owner earnings (partial logistics premium) = $30bn EV, minus net debt $3bn = ~$27bn equity. That implies roughly 18,000 DKK/share at current share count — modest upside over 4–5 years but not compelling given the earnings uncertainty path to get there.

Bull case: Logistics transformation succeeds, Maersk earns a recurring 15–18% EBITDA margin on $60–65bn revenue, through-cycle owner earnings reach $5bn+. Apply 12x = $60bn EV, minus net debt = $57bn equity, or roughly 37,000 DKK/share. This outcome requires the integrator strategy to work at scale, which has not been demonstrated.

Discounting base case back at 10% over 5 years: present value ~11,200 DKK. The stock at 16,420 DKK implies the market expects something between base and bull, which is an optimistic assumption in a freight down-cycle.

**Multiples in Context**

At 16,420 DKK per share with ~15.3m diluted shares, market cap is approximately $36bn (DKK 250bn). On 2025 earnings: P/E ~12x ($2.9bn net income), EV/EBITDA ~2.8x ($9.5bn EBITDA, EV ~$26bn after adding ~$2bn net debt). P/FCF is ~16x on 2025 FCF of $2.2bn. On 2026 guided midpoint EBITDA of $5.75bn, forward EV/EBITDA is roughly 4.5x — still below the sector median of ~8–10x for logistics, but the container shipping sector has historically traded well below logistics multiples due to its cyclicality. Hapag-Lloyd trades at ~4.9x EV/EBITDA, suggesting Maersk's premium is modest. Historically (2018–2019 through-cycle), Maersk traded at 6–8x EV/EBITDA; today's 4.5x forward (based on depressed 2026 guide) would look cheap by that lens — but only if 2026 truly represents the trough.

**What Must Be True for Today's Price to Be Justified?**

At 16,420 DKK (market cap ~$36bn), the stock requires: (1) that 2026 guidance of $4.5–7.0bn EBITDA represents the trough and that through-cycle EBITDA recovers to $8–10bn by 2028–30; (2) that the logistics transformation succeeds in adding durable, higher-multiple earnings to the base; (3) that Maersk's heavy capex program ($10–11bn in 2024–25) generates adequate returns on capital as new vessel capacity comes online; and (4) that trade volumes grow 2–4% annually as guided without a significant macro shock from tariff escalation or global recession. None of these assumptions is unreasonable, but they are collectively optimistic.

**Market Pricing: Pessimism or Perfection?**

The analyst community has swung sharply negative — 12 of 18 analysts rate the stock a Sell, with an average target of 12,673 DKK. This suggests the sell-side believes the market is still pricing in too much optimism. The key risk is the freight rate cycle: SCFI in February 2026 averaged ~1,284 points, down significantly from the June 2024 peak, and further declines are expected as Red Sea routing normalizes and the large newbuild orderbook is delivered. The market appears to be pricing in neither the worst-case freight collapse nor the logistics transformation upside — it is in the middle, which leaves limited margin of safety in either direction.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** Logistics integration matures by 2028–2030, lifting through-cycle EBITDA margins and warranting a structural re-rating toward 8–10x EV/EBITDA, with intrinsic value of 25,000–37,000 DKK/share.
- **Bear case:** Freight rates collapse to 2016 lows, logistics strategy fails to scale, overcapacity persists through 2028, and normalized owner earnings stay below $2bn, implying intrinsic value of 4,000–7,000 DKK/share.
- **Confidence:** Low — the wide range of outcomes (bear to bull is nearly 10:1) reflects genuine uncertainty; the business is deep into a cyclical trough with a structural transformation underway simultaneously, making intrinsic value estimation unusually imprecise.

---

## Red Flags

- 2026 EBITDA guidance of $4.5–7.0bn represents a 27–53% decline from 2025's $9.5bn — a very wide and deeply uncertain range at this stage of the year.
- Total capex of $10–11bn over two years while owner earnings are falling rapidly; ROCE will deteriorate sharply in 2026–27.
- The 7 million TEU orderbook (20% of global fleet) structurally suppresses freight rates through 2027 at minimum.
- Analyst consensus is a clear Sell with a 23% downside price target from current levels.
- Free cash flow of $2.2bn in 2025 is already materially below net income of $2.9bn; it will likely be negative in 2026 given guidance.

---

## Score: 4 / 10

At current price, Maersk does not offer compelling valuation — the optically low trailing multiples are misleading because earnings are in freefall, and the stock is likely above fair value on normalized through-cycle owner earnings; a 30%+ drawdown to the analyst consensus target is plausible before the investment becomes genuinely attractive.
