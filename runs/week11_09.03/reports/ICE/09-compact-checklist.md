# Compact Checklist — ICE

**Date:** 2026-03-16

1. **Business**: ICE owns the irreplaceable plumbing of global financial markets — Brent crude futures, the NYSE, fixed income data used by every major institution, and the dominant US mortgage origination and servicing software — selling access, benchmarks, and data that customers cannot operationally replace.
2. **Moat**: ICE's benchmarks (Brent, TTF, EU carbon) are embedded in every global commodity contract, its clearinghouses create self-reinforcing liquidity network effects, and its fixed income pricing is mandated by regulation — displacing any of these would require simultaneous coordination by the entire industry.
3. **Economics**: ICE generates $4.1B of owner earnings annually with 53% EBITDA margins, 100%+ FCF conversion, and 56% recurring revenues, demonstrating real pricing power — the 8% ROIC is an accounting artifact from acquisition goodwill, not evidence of poor underlying returns.
4. **Management**: Jeff Sprecher built ICE from $1 in 2000 to a $90B institution through a 25-year string of value-creating acquisitions, and the $1.4B FY2025 buyback was intelligently timed at 18x forward earnings during a trough in mortgage sentiment — though 20 insider sales and zero purchases over five years is a pattern worth watching.
5. **Debt risk**: $18.8B net debt at 3.0x EBITDA is elevated but rapidly declining on $3.9B annual FCF, A-/A3 credit ratings with stable outlooks, no near-term maturity cliff, and a recurring revenue base that provides a stable floor even in a severe market downturn.
6. **Price**: At $159.20 and 28.5x EV/owner earnings, today's price is essentially fair value and requires ~7-9% annual owner earnings growth for a decade — achievable, but not a price that rewards the buyer generously; it demands the base case plays out.
7. **Margin of safety**: The business margin of safety is exceptional — the NYSE and critical exchange infrastructure cannot be replicated — but the price margin of safety is thin, with the stock only ~$40 above a bear case floor of $100-119/share and no meaningful discount to intrinsic value.
8. **Thesis breaker**: Sell if management announces a major new acquisition (>$5B) before net debt falls below $15B, or if Black Knight synergy targets are materially cut, signaling permanent impairment of the strategic rationale for the mortgage platform.
