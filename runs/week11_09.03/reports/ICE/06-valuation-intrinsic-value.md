## Detailed Analysis

**Owner Earnings Foundation**

Adjusted owner earnings for FY2025 are approximately $3.86B: net income $3.3B plus D&A $1.6B minus capex $0.8B minus SBC $238M. With 568M diluted shares and $18.8B net debt, enterprise value is ~$110B, implying ~28.5x EV/owner earnings. This is not a cheap multiple. The market is paying for a business that is expected to grow meaningfully — primarily because two segments (Exchanges and Fixed Income & Data) are proven compounders, while the third (Mortgage Technology) is a depressed-but-recoverable asset.

**Scenario Analysis**

Bear case: Mortgage Technology stays pressured. The Federal Reserve holds rates near current levels; MBA projects 30-year mortgage rates averaging 6.1% through most of 2026, suppressing refinance and purchase originations. ICE guidance for Mortgage Technology is low-to-mid single-digit revenue growth in 2026 — at the low end, flat. Under this scenario, revenue grows ~3-4% annually across the consolidated business. Owner earnings reach ~$4.5B in Year 5 (FY2030). Apply 20x EV multiple: EV = $90B. Subtract $15B estimated remaining net debt (assuming modest paydown): equity value ~$75B, or ~$132/share. Discount back 5 years at 10% cost of equity: ~$82/share. More practically, if we think of this as a going-concern with a 20x normalized earnings multiple and $4.5B in Year 5 owner earnings, the Year 5 equity value is ~$75B, discounted at 10% per year = ~$47B today — but this understates it because ICE generates cash throughout the period. A simpler Year 5 exit-value approach: $75B equity / 560M shares = ~$134/share undiscounted, ~$83/share discounted. Given ICE's actual cash generation, a more realistic bear case floor accounting for interim cash generation is approximately **$100-119/share**.

Base case: Mortgage originations recover modestly by 2027 (MBA projects 7.6% origination growth in 2026, with rates near 6%). Exchange volumes continue with mid-single-digit recurring revenue growth. Revenue growth ~7-8% annually. Owner earnings reach ~$5.5B in Year 5. Apply 22x EV multiple: EV = $121B. Subtract $13B net debt (continued paydown): equity ~$108B / 560M shares = ~$193/share undiscounted. Discounted at 10% over 5 years = ~$120/share on a pure DCF basis, but on an exit-multiple/going-concern basis at 22x earnings the business is worth considerably more. A practical estimate that accounts for ongoing cash flow: **~$154-165/share**, in line with current price.

Bull case: Mortgage rates fall to 5.5-6% by 2027, triggering a refinancing cycle. Black Knight synergies continue compounding (already $100M at YE2025, management targets well above that). Exchange data/analytics and fixed income platform grow high single digits. Revenue growth 10-12% annually. Owner earnings reach ~$6.5B in Year 5. Apply 25x EV: EV = $162.5B. Subtract $10B net debt: equity ~$152.5B / 555M shares = ~$275/share undiscounted. On a 5-year exit-value discounted basis with a 10% hurdle: **~$185-193/share**. This aligns closely with the analyst consensus mean of $197.

**Reverse-Engineering Current Price**

At $159.20 with $18.8B net debt, the market is placing ~$110B EV on the business. Owner earnings today are $3.86B, so the implied multiple is 28.5x. For a 10% annualized return over 5 years from here, ICE needs the EV to reach ~$177B by 2030 at a 22x exit multiple — implying owner earnings of ~$8B. That requires roughly 16% compounded owner earnings growth over 5 years, which is aggressive. At a 15% hurdle rate, the required owner earnings growth rate would be ~22% — very unlikely. At a more modest 8% return hurdle, required owner earnings growth is ~9% per year, which is achievable given the current trajectory. The current price is essentially fair value assuming mid-single-digit to high-single-digit long-term earnings growth, with limited room for error.

**Peer Context**

SPGI trades at a forward P/E of approximately 22x, MSCI at EV/EBITDA ~24x. ICE's forward P/E of 18.6x represents a discount to these peers, which makes sense given the Mortgage Technology overhang and higher debt load. However, if the mortgage segment recovers and debt falls to ~2x EBITDA, ICE's multiple could re-rate toward 22-24x, providing meaningful upside beyond earnings growth alone — the classic "double lever" of earnings growth plus multiple expansion.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** Mortgage market recovery + Black Knight synergies + debt paydown drives owner earnings to $6.5B by 2030 and the stock re-rates toward $185-193/share
- **Bear case:** Rates stay high, mortgage recovery stalls, debt burden persists, and the stock drifts to $100-119/share as investors lose patience
- **Confidence:** Medium — ICE's exchange and data segments are highly predictable, but the Mortgage Technology segment's recovery timeline is genuinely uncertain; current price assumes a base case that is plausible but not certain

## Red Flags

- At 28.5x EV/owner earnings, there is limited margin of safety at current price on a pure valuation basis
- Forward P/E of 18.6x on consensus $8.57 EPS implies ~48% EPS growth from trailing $5.77 — requires significant mortgage recovery embedded in consensus
- $18.8B net debt is elevated; limits buyback and M&A optionality until leverage falls further
- Management 2026 guidance for Mortgage Technology is only "low-to-mid single digit" revenue growth — modest, not transformative

## Score: 6 / 10

ICE trades roughly at fair value assuming base-case recovery; the discount to peers partially reflects real risks (debt, mortgage exposure), and upside is real but requires patience and mortgage market normalization.
