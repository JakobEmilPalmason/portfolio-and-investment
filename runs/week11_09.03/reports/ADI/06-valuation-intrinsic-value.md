## Detailed Analysis

**Owner Earnings Estimate (Today)**

For FY2025 (ended November 1, 2025): Revenue was $11.02 billion; GAAP net income was approximately $2.27 billion (implied by 38.65% YoY earnings growth from prior year); operating cash flow was $4.8 billion; free cash flow was $4.3 billion. CapEx was $0.5 billion. ADI's business model relies heavily on fabless/fab-lite manufacturing, meaning a large share of reported D&A relates to acquired intangible assets (from the $21B Maxim acquisition in 2021) rather than true economic maintenance expenditure. Maintenance capex is likely well below reported total capex; the company's long-term capex model targets 4–6% of revenue, and the $0.5B figure is at the low end of that range. A reasonable owner earnings estimate for FY2025 uses FCF as a close proxy: approximately $4.3 billion, or roughly $8.90 per share on ~483 million diluted shares. TTM through Q1 FY2026, FCF has grown to approximately $4.6 billion.

**Owner Earnings in 3–5 Years**

Given Q2 FY2026 guidance of $3.5B (an annualized run rate approaching $14B revenue), and management's characterization of FY2026 as a potential "banner year," a base-case FY2028 revenue assumption of $13–14 billion seems achievable with continued recovery across industrial and communications end markets plus AI-driven data-center content growth. At a 40% FCF margin (consistent with historical peaks), FY2028 owner earnings could reach $5.2–5.6 billion, or approximately $11–12 per share assuming modest share count reduction.

**Simple Scenario Analysis (Bear / Base / Bull)**

Bear case: Cycle turns sharply in late 2026/early 2027 due to inventory correction or macro slowdown. FY2028 revenue flattens at ~$11 billion, FCF margin compresses to 35%, yielding ~$3.85B FCF. Apply a 25x FCF multiple (appropriate for a quality-but-cyclically-stressed analog chip company): enterprise value ~$96B. Net debt is modest (ADI carries approximately $8–9B in long-term debt from the Maxim deal with significant cash offset); adjusted equity value roughly $88–90B. Intrinsic value per share: ~$185–190. Discounted at 10% over 3 years: approximately $140–145. Significant downside from current price.

Base case: ADI continues recovery; FY2028 revenue of $13B, FCF margin 39%, FCF ~$5.1B. Apply a 30x FCF multiple: enterprise value ~$153B. Equity value after net debt adjustment ~$145B. Per share: ~$300. Discounted at 10% over 3 years: approximately $225. Modestly below today's price.

Bull case: AI data-center, automotive electrification, and industrial automation drive FY2028 revenue to $15B with margin expansion to 42% FCF, yielding FCF of ~$6.3B. Apply 32x FCF multiple: EV ~$202B. Equity value ~$194B. Per share: ~$400. Discounted at 10% over 3 years: approximately $300. Roughly in-line to modestly above today's price.

**Multiples in Context**

At $318.81, ADI trades at: trailing P/E ~58x (GAAP), forward adjusted P/E ~29–32x, EV/EBITDA ~32x, P/FCF ~34x. Texas Instruments, the closest analog peer, trades at a lower P/E and EV/EBITDA (~24x and ~28x respectively) and is not growing as fast. ADI's premium reflects superior margin profile (70% gross margin vs. TXN's 60–65%), faster near-term growth, and greater AI/data-center exposure. However, these multiples are elevated compared to ADI's own five-year average. The stock has surged approximately 61.5% over the past 12 months on cyclical recovery and AI-driven re-rating.

**What Must Be True for Today's Price to Be Justified?**

Reverse-engineering the $318.81 price: at a 10% discount rate with a 25x terminal FCF multiple, the stock requires FY2028 FCF of approximately $6B+ to generate an IRR of 10%. That implies revenue of roughly $14.5–15B with 40%+ FCF margin — the high end of the bull case. This essentially demands that the current cyclical recovery proves durable, that ADI captures meaningful AI/data-center content wins, that no significant inventory correction occurs, and that margins hold at record levels. It also assumes no multiple compression from higher interest rates or sector rotation.

**Implied Expectations vs. Likely Reality**

The market is pricing in an optimistic-to-perfect scenario for ADI. The Q2 FY2026 guidance beat is real and impressive — but much of the good news appears to already be priced in. The 12-month analyst target of $381.70 implies further re-rating rather than valuation normalization. For a long-term investor buying at today's price, the IRR is likely in the 5–8% range in the base case — acceptable but not exciting. The margin of error is thin.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** ADI becomes the dominant analog/mixed-signal supplier to AI data centers and industrial automation systems, sustaining 15%+ revenue growth and 40%+ FCF margins through FY2029, justifying a $400+ share price.
- **Bear case:** A cyclical inventory correction in late 2026 combined with multiple compression from rising rates drives ADI back toward $180–200 — roughly a 40% drawdown from current levels.
- **Confidence:** Medium — FY2026 momentum is real and the business quality is unambiguous, but valuation risk is material at current prices.

## Red Flags

- At ~34x P/FCF and ~32x forward adjusted P/E, ADI is priced for near-perfect execution with minimal margin of safety.
- Trailing GAAP P/E of ~58x reflects ongoing amortization drag from the Maxim acquisition; while this is non-cash, it also highlights that GAAP earnings remain far below adjusted figures.
- The 61.5% 12-month price surge suggests significant momentum-driven buying, which can reverse quickly.
- Revenue growth in FY2025 was partly cyclical recovery from a depressed FY2024 base; sustaining this rate requires secular growth drivers to dominate cyclical ones.
- Net debt from the Maxim acquisition (approximately $8–9B gross, partially offset by cash) adds financial leverage that could constrain flexibility in a downturn.

## Score: 4 / 10

ADI is an exceptional business, but the current price of ~$318 offers limited intrinsic value upside in the base case and meaningful downside in the bear case — fair-to-modestly-overvalued for a long-term buyer at today's price.
