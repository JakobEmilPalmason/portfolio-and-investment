## Detailed Analysis

**Returns on Capital**

TransDigm's ROIC sits at approximately 17–19% on a trailing basis (GuruFocus reports 17.95% TTM as of early 2026, with WACC estimated at ~8%). Because TDG carries a large intangible asset base from acquisitions, ROIC computed on GAAP invested capital is arguably understated; on a cash-based view of "owner capital deployed," returns are higher still. ROE is not a useful metric here given deeply negative book equity (a consequence of decades of leveraged recapitalizations). What matters economically is the spread between ROIC and WACC, which is clearly positive and has been so for at least a decade. The ROIC trend has been upward as organic growth has outpaced cost, and the company's ability to raise prices on proprietary aftermarket parts annually at above-inflation rates is the engine.

**Margin Structure**

Gross margins are not separately reported in the EBITDA As Defined framework TransDigm uses, but EBITDA As Defined margin — which adds back acquisition-related amortization and non-recurring items — reached 53.9% in FY2025, up from 52.6% in FY2024 and 51.6% in FY2023. This is a steady upward trend driven by aftermarket mix shift and operating leverage. GAAP operating margins are lower, in the low-to-mid 20% range, because TransDigm carries heavy amortization of acquired intangibles (~$600-700M annually based on disclosed data). Net profit margin for FY2025 was approximately 21.7% on a GAAP basis. The key takeaway: when you strip out non-cash acquisition accounting, the underlying business earns ~54 cents of EBITDA on every dollar of revenue, which is exceptional for an industrial manufacturer.

**Cash Generation**

FY2025 free cash flow was $1.82B, a slight (~3.4%) decline from FY2024 due to elevated interest expense following the $5B+ special dividend funded by new debt. This was a deliberately engineered step-down, not organic deterioration. Management guided FY2026 FCF at ~$2.4B, implying a ~$580M recovery — consistent with higher revenues, stable margins, and slightly lower debt service growth rate. FCF conversion from GAAP net income ($2.07B FY2025) is approximately 88%, a high figure. Owner earnings (net income + D&A - maintenance capex) are difficult to calculate precisely because TDG does not separately break out maintenance vs. growth capex, but with total capex of only $165M (less than amortization of ~$600-700M), cash generation is vastly in excess of GAAP earnings. For FY2026 Q1 alone, operating cash flow was $830M and FCF ~$900M — on an annualized basis consistent with full-year guidance.

**Capital Intensity**

TransDigm is among the most capital-light industrial businesses in aerospace. Total capex in FY2025 was $165M on $8.83B of revenue — a capex intensity of just 1.9%. The company manufactures engineered components but sources much of the manufacturing labor and overhead to subsidiaries that are operationally lean. Critically, the vast majority of capex is maintenance in nature; there is minimal growth capex requirement because the company grows through pricing and acquisition, not capacity expansion. D&A (which includes significant acquired intangible amortization) runs several hundred million dollars ahead of actual capex, meaning tangible assets are more than self-funding. This capital-light model, combined with high margins, is what enables TDG to generate free cash flows far in excess of GAAP earnings and to recycle capital into acquisitions and special dividends at will.

**Revenue Quality**

Revenue quality is among the highest in the industrial sector. Key attributes: (1) approximately 90% of products are proprietary — no comparable substitute is typically available, meaning customers cannot easily shop price; (2) approximately 55% of revenue comes from aftermarket channels (repairs, replacements, and overhauls on in-service aircraft) which are highly recurring and contractually sticky; (3) the remaining 45% is split between OEM (building into new aircraft) and defense programs, both of which have long product lifecycles and switching costs. The revenue split in FY2025 was roughly 32% commercial aftermarket, 28% commercial OEM, and 40% defense, providing balance. Revenue is primarily price-driven (TDG historically takes 3–5% annual price increases on aftermarket parts), not volume-driven, which makes it inflation-resistant and less susceptible to aerospace production cycles. Customer churn is essentially nil in aftermarket.

**Operating Leverage**

TransDigm exhibits strong operating leverage. From FY2022 to FY2025, revenue grew from $5.43B to $8.83B (+62%), while EBITDA As Defined grew from ~$2.8B to $4.76B (+70%). Margins expanded 2–3 percentage points over that period as fixed overhead was spread over a larger revenue base. This operating leverage is somewhat dampened at the GAAP level by acquisition amortization, which scales with deal size rather than revenue. Interest expense is the main swing factor at the net income level — FY2026 net income guidance midpoint is slightly below FY2025 due to higher interest costs from recently issued debt, but EBITDA is expected to grow 9–10% to ~$5.15B midpoint. The operating leverage story is real and durable; the financial leverage is the variable that introduces noise.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** A near-monopoly aftermarket pricing engine with 54% EBITDA margins, sub-2% capex intensity, and $2.4B+ of annual FCF compounds capital at well above its cost for years to come.
- **Bear case:** FCF is a function of EBITDA minus ~$1.4B in cash interest; any margin compression or revenue shortfall flows directly to a levered bottom line, amplifying downside.
- **Confidence:** High — TransDigm's financial reporting is detailed and consistent; margin and FCF data are well-documented across multiple earnings releases and the 10-K.

## Red Flags

- FY2025 FCF declined slightly despite revenue growing 11% — entirely due to higher interest expense from the $90/share special dividend debt issuance; the underlying cash generation quality is unimpaired but investors must model interest expense carefully going forward
- GAAP net income is significantly below EBITDA As Defined due to non-cash amortization and interest costs — the gap requires careful interpretation; TDG reports both metrics and the reconciliation is clean, but less sophisticated analysis could misread profitability
- D&A excluding interest is not separately reported as maintenance vs. growth capex; the $165M total capex figure likely understates long-run maintenance requirements slightly, though the company has maintained this level for years

## Score: 9 / 10

TransDigm's business economics are exceptional — structural pricing power, near-asset-light model, 54% EBITDA margins, and $2.4B+ of recurring FCF — with the only meaningful complexity being how interest expense layers onto the high-margin EBITDA to produce more volatile net income.
