## Detailed Analysis

**Owner Earnings Today**

TransDigm's FY2025 results: net sales $8.83B, EBITDA as Defined $4.76B (53.9% margin), adjusted net income $2.17B ($37.33/share). Reported net income was lower — approximately $1.2–1.4B after GAAP interest expense, amortization of intangibles, and one-time items. To estimate owner earnings, the relevant starting point is cash net income plus D&A less maintenance capex. TDG's total capex runs roughly $200–250M annually; management characterizes the vast majority as maintenance (the business has low physical capital intensity — its "capex" is largely acquisition-driven). D&A is elevated due to the acquisition model (intangible amortization). A reasonable owner earnings estimate for FY2025: adjusted net income of ~$2.17B minus the interest tax shield distortion plus/minus normalization for one-time charges lands at approximately **$1.7–1.8B** in true owner earnings. The key drag is cash interest: at ~$1.5–1.6B annually on $25B gross debt at blended rates near 6–7%, interest consumes a substantial portion of operating income. At 56.8M diluted shares, owner earnings per share are approximately **$30–32**.

**Scenario Analysis — Year 5 (FY2030)**

*Bear Case:* Aviation cycle weakens materially (commercial aerospace destocking, defense budget pressure), M&A pipeline dries up, leverage constrains capital allocation. Revenue growth slows to 4–5% annually. EBITDA margin holds near 50% (some pricing compression under regulatory scrutiny). By FY2030: revenue ~$10.7B, EBITDA ~$5.35B. After interest, taxes, and minority items, owner earnings ~$2.0B. Apply 18x owner earnings multiple (compressed for slower growth). Equity value: ~$36B. Less net debt (~$20B): equity ~$16B, or ~$280/share. Discount at 10% for 5 years: **bear case intrinsic value ~$700–800/share**, representing ~40–45% downside from today.

*Base Case:* Current trends continue — 8–10% annual revenue growth, EBITDA as Defined margins hold near 52–54%, management executes 1–2 acquisitions annually, leverage deleverages to ~4–5x. By FY2030: revenue ~$13.0B, EBITDA ~$6.8B. Owner earnings ~$2.7–2.9B. Apply 22x multiple. Equity value before debt: ~$60–64B. Net debt declining to ~$17–18B. Equity value: ~$43–46B, or **~$760–820/share**. Discounted at 9%: **base case intrinsic value ~$1,050–1,200/share**.

*Bull Case:* Defense spending accelerates, commercial aviation supercycle extends, TDG completes 2–3 large acquisitions that each achieve aftermarket integration. Revenue compounds at 12–14%, EBITDA margins edge above 55% as pricing power remains intact. By FY2030: revenue ~$15.5B, EBITDA ~$8.5B. Owner earnings ~$3.5B+. Apply 24x multiple on lower risk profile. Equity value: ~$84B. Net debt: ~$15B (paid down via FCF and special dividends). Equity: ~$69B, or **~$1,215/share** pre-discount. Discount at 8%: **bull case intrinsic value ~$1,450–1,600/share**.

**Multiples in Context**

At $1,258/share and ~56.8M diluted shares outstanding, market cap is ~$71B. With net debt of approximately $22.8B (per Q2 FY2025 filing), enterprise value is approximately $94B. Against FY2025 EBITDA as Defined of $4.76B, EV/EBITDA = ~19.7x. Against FY2026 guided EBITDA of $5.21B, forward EV/EBITDA = ~18x. The reported P/E of ~41x overstates the true earnings yield due to GAAP amortization of acquisition intangibles — on an adjusted basis (~$38.38 EPS guided for FY2026), P/E is ~33x. Price-to-FCF using $1.8B owner earnings and ~56.8M shares ($31.70/share) implies P/FCF of ~40x — rich, but TDG has historically commanded 30–45x P/FCF during strong cycle phases. Peers: Heico (HEI) at 18–20x EV/EBITDA and 55–65x P/E is arguably more expensive on earnings but carries less leverage. Curtiss-Wright trades near 15x EV/EBITDA. TDG's premium reflects its uniquely high EBITDA margins and aftermarket model.

**What Must Be True to Justify Today's Price**

At $1,258/share, the market is pricing in sustained compounding. Reverse engineering: if an investor requires a 9% annual return and expects to sell in 10 years at 20x owner earnings, TDG would need to grow owner earnings from ~$1.75B today to ~$4.5B by FY2035 — a 10% CAGR. That requires: (1) revenue growing at 8–10% annually through acquisitions and organic pricing, (2) EBITDA margins sustaining above 50%, (3) gradual deleveraging reducing the interest burden by $400–500M over the decade, and (4) no regulatory constraint on pricing power. All four are plausible but not guaranteed. The market is not pricing in failure; it is pricing in continuation of a proven playbook.

**Implied Expectations vs Likely Reality**

The street consensus price target of $1,565 implies ~25% upside from current levels. The implied expectation gap is relatively narrow — the market broadly agrees TDG is a quality compounder but debates whether 19–22x EV/EBITDA already captures the moat. The risk is not that TDG is a bad business — it almost certainly is not — but that at current prices, the margin of safety is thin. The stock is fairly valued on base case assumptions and requires bull-case execution to deliver satisfactory returns from here. The leveraged balance sheet is both a feature (capital efficiency, return on equity is effectively infinite as book equity is negative) and a risk (refinancing exposure, covenant sensitivity in a deep downturn).

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** If commercial aerospace supercycle extends and TDG continues its acquisition-integration flywheel, the stock can compound at 10–12% annually from current levels through FY2030.
- **Bear case:** A structural aviation downturn combined with regulatory pricing constraints and refinancing headwinds in a higher-rate environment could push the stock down 35–45% before recovering.
- **Confidence:** Medium — TDG's business quality and earnings power are well-understood; the primary uncertainty is whether today's valuation already embeds too much optimism, and whether the debt load creates fragility in a cycle turn.

---

## Red Flags

- Net debt of ~$22.8B (~5x EBITDA) leaves minimal buffer if EBITDA contracts materially; a 20–25% EBITDA decline would push leverage above covenant thresholds
- GAAP reported earnings are substantially depressed by intangible amortization, making standard P/E comparisons misleading and potentially obscuring true capital allocation cost
- EV/EBITDA of ~18–22x (depending on period) is at the high end of TDG's own history; the stock has previously de-rated sharply when growth disappointed
- Owner earnings of ~$1.75B on a $71B market cap implies a ~2.5% owner earnings yield — thin for an investment requiring a growth premium

---

## Score: 5 / 10

TDG is a genuinely exceptional business priced at a fair-to-full valuation; the intrinsic value range suggests the stock is close to fair value on base-case assumptions, offering limited margin of safety without bull-case execution.
