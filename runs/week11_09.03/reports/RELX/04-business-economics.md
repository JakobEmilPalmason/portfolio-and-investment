## Detailed Analysis

**Returns on Capital.** RELX's ROIC has been consistently high over the past decade, averaging close to 20% over a 10-year span and standing at approximately 14.85–15.3% on a reported basis in 2024–2025 (GuruFocus, annualised from TTM data). The slight moderation in reported ROIC versus the historical peak reflects the compounding of goodwill and intangibles from acquisitions; on a tangible capital basis, returns are substantially higher. RELX's own reported post-tax return on average invested capital was 13.6% in 2019 (13.2% in 2018) when calculated under their framework, but management-defined adjusted measures show stronger return profiles. The trend is one of stable-to-improving returns as the business mix shifts further toward high-margin digital analytics and Risk products. ROE is not explicitly disclosed in standard company metrics but is implied to be high given net income of ~£2.3bn against a modest equity base heavily reduced by buybacks.

**Margin Structure.** Gross margins have been broadly stable at approximately 64–66% over the past five years (65.0% in 2024, 64.9% in 2023, 64.4% in 2022), reflecting the data and content cost structure of a largely digital business. Adjusted operating margins have been on a steady upward trajectory: 33.1% (2023) → 33.9% (2024) → 34.8% (2025). Reported EBITDA margins reached 40.1% in 2025, up 0.6 percentage points. Net margin (reported) has been approximately 20–22% in recent years. The steady operating leverage stems from management's explicit strategy of keeping cost growth below revenue growth through process innovation and AI-assisted workflows. With four business segments all expanding operating margins simultaneously, this is a structural rather than cyclical trend.

**Cash Generation.** RELX is a cash generation machine. Free cash flow was £2,313m in 2025, up from approximately £2,200m in 2024 and ~£1,900m in 2023. The conversion rate of adjusted operating profit to adjusted cash flow has been 98–101% across recent years (99% in 2025, 98% in 2023, 101% in 2022). This extremely high conversion reflects the dominance of software/data revenue (low accounts receivable, no inventory, minimal physical fulfilment), subscription-based billing that smooths cash flows, and very low maintenance capex requirements. Owner earnings (net income + D&A - maintenance capex) are difficult to precisely separate since virtually all capex is stated as development cost capitalisation (which is more akin to R&D investment than maintenance). However, the ~99% adjusted cash flow conversion on a £3.3bn adjusted operating profit base implies cash earnings are very close to reported adjusted profits.

**Capital Intensity.** RELX is a genuinely asset-light business. Total capex in 2025 was £525m, of which £504m was capitalised development costs — essentially software and data product development expensed on balance sheet. Physical asset capex (property, equipment) is minimal. Capex as a percentage of revenue is approximately 5.5%, well below the >10% threshold that would signal capital intensity concerns. The business does not require significant tangible asset reinvestment to sustain its competitive position; its moats are embedded in proprietary data assets, workflow software, and customer switching costs — none of which require ongoing heavy capital spending. This is a fundamental characteristic of a high-quality compounder.

**Revenue Quality.** Revenue quality is one of RELX's most compelling attributes. Electronic formats account for 84% of total revenue in 2025 (up from ~70% five years prior), driven by the near-complete digitisation of content delivery across Scientific, Technical & Medical, Legal, and Risk segments. Subscription and transactional-recurring revenue constitutes approximately 54% of total revenue, with transactional analytics (mostly in Risk) adding further quasi-recurring character. Print has fallen to just 4% of revenue — effectively a rounding error. The Exhibitions segment (the only truly cyclical, non-recurring piece) accounts for roughly 8% of group revenue. The four major segments have delivered underlying revenue growth of 5–9% consistently for multiple years, driven by a mix of volume (new customers, new datasets) and price (annual contractual escalators, upselling of AI-enhanced tools). Customer retention is structurally high: researchers, lawyers, and risk analysts are deeply embedded in RELX workflow tools, creating institutional stickiness.

**Operating Leverage.** Operating leverage is clear and consistent. Revenue grew 7% in 2025; adjusted operating profit grew 9%. Revenue grew 8% in 2024; adjusted operating profit grew 9%. Revenue grew 7% in 2023; adjusted operating profit grew approximately 9–10%. This 1–2 percentage point spread between revenue and profit growth has compounded into meaningful margin expansion over five years (from approximately 30% to 34.8% adjusted operating margin). The primary driver is that a large portion of RELX's cost base is fixed or semi-fixed (content creation, technology infrastructure, sales force) and does not scale linearly with revenue. As the business grows, incremental revenue flows through at higher margins — a textbook operating leverage profile.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** RELX is a structurally asset-light, subscription-heavy, high-ROIC compounder with consistent operating leverage, near-100% FCF conversion, and a clear AI-enabled secular growth tailwind across all four segments.
- **Bear case:** Valuation is demanding, ROIC on a reported basis (~15%) is below the headline "near 20% historical" figure once recent goodwill is included, and development capex intensity means owner earnings are somewhat lower than adjusted cash flow implies.
- **Confidence:** High — the financial characteristics of this business are well-documented and consistent across many years; the economics are transparent and repeatable.

## Red Flags

- Capitalised development costs (£504m in 2025) are a significant non-cash charge that inflates near-term reported profits; if product investment cycles lengthen, amortisation drag could suppress reported EPS unexpectedly.
- ROIC on a fully-loaded GAAP basis (including all acquired intangibles and goodwill) is moderately lower (~15%) than the historical near-20% figure sometimes cited; investors should use the right denominator.
- Revenue growth in STM (5% in 2025) trails Legal (9%) and Risk (8%), reflecting ongoing open-access pressure on academic publishing; this is a slow-moving but real structural headwind for that segment.

## Score: 9 / 10

RELX exhibits near-ideal business economics — a highly recurring, asset-light, digitally-delivered subscription model generating ~99% FCF conversion at 34.8% operating margins with a decade of consistent ROIC above 15% and earnings compounding at ~10% per year; the one point deducted reflects the modest ROIC dilution from goodwill accumulation and the fact that STM faces structural secular headwinds that constrain group growth upside.
