## Detailed Analysis

### Owner Earnings Estimate

For FY2025, Intuitive Surgical reported net income of ~$2.86B, depreciation and amortization of ~$1.45B, and total capital expenditure of ~$540M. Estimating maintenance capex at roughly 60-70% of total capex (~$340M, given significant growth capex for da Vinci 5 manufacturing expansion), owner earnings come to approximately $3.8-4.0B. On ~355M diluted shares, that is roughly $10.70-$11.25 per share in owner earnings. At a share price of ~$490, the stock trades at approximately 43-46x owner earnings.

### Scenario Analysis (Year 5 — FY2030)

**Bear Case (15% probability):** Procedure growth slows to 8-10% as Medtronic Hugo gains traction and Chinese competitors erode international share. Revenue CAGR of 10%, operating margin compression to 27% from tariffs and pricing pressure. Year 5 owner earnings: ~$6.5B (~$18.30/share). Apply a 30x multiple (de-rating from premium as growth decelerates). Discounted at 10%: intrinsic value ~$340/share. Downside: ~30%.

**Base Case (60% probability):** Procedure growth of 13-15% through 2027, then moderating to 11-12%. Revenue CAGR of 14%. Operating margins stable at 29-30% as da Vinci 5 consumables mix improves. Year 5 owner earnings: ~$8.5B (~$23.90/share). Apply a 35x multiple (slight premium compression). Discounted at 10%: intrinsic value ~$520/share. Modest upside of ~6%.

**Bull Case (25% probability):** Da Vinci 5 accelerates adoption globally, cardiac indication adds meaningful volume, Ion platform scales. Revenue CAGR of 17%. Operating margin expansion to 32% on scale. Year 5 owner earnings: ~$11B (~$31.00/share). Apply a 38x multiple (sustained premium). Discounted at 10%: intrinsic value ~$730/share. Upside: ~49%.

**Probability-weighted intrinsic value:** ~$530/share (0.15 x $340 + 0.60 x $520 + 0.25 x $730).

### Multiples in Context

ISRG currently trades at a trailing P/E of ~62-67x, EV/EBITDA of ~48x, and P/FCF of ~49x (though note the P/FCF historically averaged 112-165x, and the current lower ratio reflects the near-doubling of FCF in 2025). The medical devices industry median EV/EBITDA is ~16x, meaning ISRG commands a 3x premium to peers. Compared to its own 5-year average P/E of ~55-70x, the current multiple is within historical range but at the upper end in absolute terms.

Forward P/E on consensus 2026 EPS of ~$10.00 is approximately 49x, and on 2027 EPS of ~$11.44 is approximately 43x. These are demanding but not unprecedented for a dominant franchise with 18% procedure growth and a recurring revenue model.

### Reverse-Engineering the Current Price

At ~$490/share and a terminal multiple of 30x owner earnings (reasonable for a maturing premium compounder), the market is implying ~16-18% annual owner earnings growth over 5 years. Given 2025 owner earnings of ~$4.0B growing to ~$8.8B by 2030. This requires sustained mid-teens revenue growth plus modest margin expansion — achievable but demanding. Any stumble in procedure growth, competitive share loss, or margin pressure from tariffs/Hugo competition makes this growth trajectory less likely.

## Signal Summary
- **Bull case:** Da Vinci 5 cycle, cardiac indication expansion, and Ion platform scaling could sustain 17%+ earnings growth, justifying premium multiples on a $10B+ revenue franchise with 80% market share.
- **Bear case:** At 62-67x earnings, the stock prices in near-perfection; competitive entry from Medtronic Hugo, tariff headwinds, and procedure growth deceleration could trigger a meaningful de-rating.
- **Confidence:** Medium — The business quality is exceptional and reasonably predictable, but valuation leaves very little room for disappointment; probability-weighted value suggests the stock is roughly fairly valued to slightly overvalued.

## Red Flags
- Trailing P/E of 62-67x requires sustained high-teens earnings growth for 5+ years to justify
- Probability-weighted intrinsic value (~$530) only ~8% above current price — thin margin
- Even modest deceleration in procedure growth (from 18% to 12%) could compress multiples significantly
- Tariff headwind of ~1.2% on gross margins already guided for 2026
- Forward consensus estimates bake in continued execution; any miss would be punished harshly at this valuation

## Score: 4 / 10
ISRG is a world-class business trading at a price that reflects most of its excellence — the probability-weighted intrinsic value of ~$530 vs. a ~$490 price offers only ~8% upside, meaning the stock is roughly fairly valued with limited margin of safety for a new buyer.
