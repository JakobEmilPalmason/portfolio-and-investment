## Detailed Analysis

**Owner Earnings Calculation (2025 base)**

GAAP net income: $2.90B. Operating cash flow: $4.53B. Capital expenditures: $876M. Free cash flow (reported): $3.66B. D&A is embedded in operating cash flow; management backs out amortization of acquired intangibles (~$1.5B estimated based on the gap between GAAP EPS of $1.94 and adjusted EPS of $3.06) to arrive at adjusted figures. For owner earnings, the appropriate treatment is to add back D&A (~$900M operating + ~$1.5B intangible amortization) and deduct maintenance capex. Maintenance capex is roughly 2–2.5% of revenue (~$400–500M on $20B in sales), with the rest being growth capex. Estimated owner earnings: $2.90B net income + $0.90B depreciation + $1.50B intangible amortization − $0.45B maintenance capex = approximately $4.85B. This aligns closely with the reported FCF of $3.66B when netting out growth capex, validating the math. Owner earnings yield at $104B market cap: approximately 4.7%.

**Scenario Analysis — Year 5 (2030) Owner Earnings and DCF**

*Bear Case:* EP market competition intensifies, WATCHMAN faces reimbursement headwinds, litigation settlement costs drag cash flows. Revenue grows at 7% CAGR ($28B by 2030), margins compress slightly. Owner earnings reach ~$6.0B. Apply 18x multiple = $108B equity value. Discount back at 10% over 5 years = ~$67B. Per share (1.5B shares): ~$45. This implies modest downside from current levels (~$69).

*Base Case:* BSX executes on 10–11% organic growth in 2026 and sustains 8–10% CAGR thereafter. Margins expand 50–100 bps per year as guided. Owner earnings reach ~$7.5B by 2030. Apply 22x multiple = $165B. Discount at 10% = ~$102B. Per share: ~$68. This essentially matches today's price, suggesting current pricing reflects a base case scenario accurately — no margin of safety but also no obvious overvaluation.

*Bull Case:* EP and WATCHMAN retain category leadership, new adjacencies (neuromodulation, urology, imaging) add incremental growth drivers, M&A is accretive. 12–13% CAGR, margin expansion accelerates. Owner earnings reach ~$9B by 2030. Apply 25x multiple = $225B. Discount at 10% = ~$140B. Per share: ~$93. Represents ~35% upside from current price.

**Multiples in Context**

Forward P/E of ~20x (on adjusted EPS of $3.46 midpoint) is at the lower end of BSX's historical range (which peaked above 50x in 2021–2022). The trailing P/E of ~36x (GAAP) overstates the true earnings power given non-cash intangible amortization. EV/EBITDA of ~22x is above the medtech industry median of ~17x but is appropriate for a business growing EBITDA at 15–20% annually. P/FCF of ~28x (on $3.66B 2025 FCF, $104B market cap) is more demanding, but drops to ~25x on the guided $4.2B 2026 FCF. Peers: Medtronic trades at ~13–15x EV/EBITDA (lower growth), Abbott at ~18–20x, Stryker at ~22–24x. BSX's premium to Medtronic is clearly warranted given superior growth rates.

**What Must Be True for Today's Price to Be Justified**

At $69/share (~$104B market cap), the market is essentially pricing in the base case — 10% organic growth in 2026 decelerating to 7–8% by 2028–2030, modest margin expansion, and no catastrophic litigation outcome. The litigation overhang (securities fraud class actions) is likely priced in given the -32% decline from the 52-week high. For the stock to re-rate upward, the market needs evidence that the EP segment growth normalization was already fully anticipated by management (i.e., no further negative surprises) and that the new growth drivers (neurovascular, urology, imaging) are gaining traction. The required assumptions are plausible but not demanding — BSX has consistently beaten its own guidance over the past three years.

**Market Pricing: Perfection or Disaster?**

Neither extreme. The post-earnings -32% decline from the peak has removed most of the perfection premium. The market is now pricing in a credible deceleration scenario without assuming catastrophic competitive collapse. The securities fraud litigation adds a tail-risk discount. At current prices, the market appears to be pricing in "controlled deceleration" — not the disaster scenario that a total loss of EP leadership would represent, and not the bull scenario where growth reaccelerates. This makes the risk/reward asymmetric to the upside if the class action resolves favorably and EP growth stabilizes.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** Post-correction valuation embeds realistic growth assumptions and multiple compression is already severe; litigation resolution + EP stabilization could drive 50%+ re-rating toward analyst consensus targets.
- **Bear case:** The securities fraud class action could force a large settlement, GAAP earnings remain well below adjusted figures limiting true yield, and further EP deceleration could compress multiples further toward 15–16x forward adjusted EPS (~$52/share).
- **Confidence:** Medium — The DCF math is internally consistent and the FCF yield at ~4.7% is reasonable for a high-quality compounder, but the litigation and EP trajectory uncertainty are genuine unknowns that prevent high conviction.

## Red Flags

- GAAP earnings substantially below adjusted figures due to ongoing intangible amortization from acquisitions; adjusted EPS flatters the true cost of acquisition-driven growth
- Securities fraud class action filed March 2026 alleging management concealed EP growth unsustainability — material overhang with uncertain settlement cost
- 2026 FCF guidance of $4.2B implies management expects strong cash conversion, but guidance has historically been conservative; if missed, valuation support weakens
- Market cap of ~$104B implies nearly no margin of safety in the base case — the stock is not cheap, it is reasonably priced assuming execution

## Score: 5 / 10

BSX is fairly valued at current prices in the base case — the post-correction stock price reflects a realistic deceleration scenario without a meaningful discount from intrinsic value, leaving little margin of safety but also limited downside beyond litigation tail risk.
