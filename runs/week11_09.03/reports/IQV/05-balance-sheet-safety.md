## Detailed Analysis

**Debt Levels**

IQVIA carries substantial leverage. As of December 31, 2025, total gross debt was $15,724M against cash of $1,980M, for net debt of $13,744M. At end-2024, net debt was $12,281M. The $1,463M increase in net debt during 2025 reflects acquisition activity and ongoing balance sheet management. The company's own reported Net Leverage Ratio (net debt / trailing twelve-month Adjusted EBITDA) was 3.33x at end-2024 and 3.63x at end-2025 — above the 3x level that would signal manageable leverage for a business of this type.

On the Debt/EBITDA scale, 3.6x falls in the "elevated but not alarming for a stable-cash-flow services business" range, provided revenue and EBITDA remain stable. However, it is meaningfully above the 2x threshold that would represent comfortable leverage for a Buffett-style value investor. IQVIA does not operate at pre-crisis balance sheet conservatism. This leverage profile results directly from a long string of acquisitions, most notably the 2016 Quintiles/IMS Health merger and subsequent bolt-ons.

**Interest Coverage**

Net interest expense in 2024 was approximately $623M (sourced from Yahoo Finance income statement). Against GAAP operating income (EBIT) of $2,269M in 2024, this implies interest coverage of approximately 3.6x — which sits between the 3x warning threshold and the 5x comfort threshold. On an Adjusted EBITDA basis, coverage is stronger: $3,684M EBITDA / $623M interest = 5.9x, which is more reassuring. The divergence arises because GAAP EBIT includes $900M+ in amortization that is non-cash.

Importantly, 2026 guidance calls for ~$80M higher interest expense due to 2025 financing activities and expected 2026 refinancing, meaning interest coverage will compress slightly in 2026 unless EBITDA grows proportionately.

**Liquidity**

Cash on hand was $1,980M at December 31, 2025. In addition to cash, IQVIA maintains revolving credit facilities (terms not fully disclosed in search results, but typical for this credit profile would be $1.5-2B). Annual FCF of approximately $2B provides substantial operating liquidity. The company can self-fund 12+ months of operations and debt service without accessing capital markets under normal operating conditions. Current ratio data is not found in the available search results, but given $2B cash and strong operating cash generation, short-term liquidity is not a concern.

**Refinancing Risk and Debt Maturity Schedule**

IQVIA has been active in addressing refinancing risk. Key known maturities:
- **$1,250M Senior Secured Notes due February 2029** (issued November 2023)
- **~$1,250M Term Loan B due 2031** (new term B issued alongside the 2029 notes)
- **$2,000M Senior Notes due June 2032** (issued 2025 at 6.250%)

This ladder of maturities is well-structured — no single large wall of debt in the near term. The 2029, 2031, and 2032 maturities are 3-6 years out as of the analysis date, providing time for cash flow deleveraging. Refinancing risk is moderate: IQVIA depends on investment-grade or near-investment-grade credit market access, and a significant credit market dislocation could create refinancing pressure. However, the staggered structure limits concentration risk.

**Off-Balance-Sheet Obligations**

IQVIA has operating lease obligations (facilities, equipment) that are material for a business of its scale but not independently disclosed in the press release summaries reviewed. Pension obligations are present but not disclosed as a major balance sheet risk in available sources. The Q3 2024 balance sheet showed short-term debt and capital lease obligations of $1,219M alongside long-term debt of $12,481M — indicating some lease-related obligations are embedded in the debt figure. No material contingent liabilities or off-balance-sheet financing vehicles are flagged in available disclosures.

**Stress Test: 30% Revenue Decline for Two Years**

If IQVIA's revenue fell 30% from $16.3B (2025) to $11.4B, and if variable costs scaled proportionately, Adjusted EBITDA might fall from ~$3.8B toward $2.0-2.4B (assuming 40-50% drop in EBITDA). At $2.0B EBITDA, Net Leverage would rise to approximately 6.9x ($13.7B net debt / $2.0B EBITDA), well above any typical covenant threshold (usually 5-5.5x for leveraged credit agreements). In this scenario, IQVIA would almost certainly need to either draw revolver capacity, sell assets, or issue equity to stabilize. The business would survive — given its long-term contracted R&DS backlog of $31B provides meaningful cash flow floor — but equity holders would face significant dilution risk. This is not a fortress balance sheet.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** Staggered long-dated maturities (2029-2032), strong FCF of ~$2B annually, and a $31B contracted backlog mean the company can service and gradually reduce debt without capital market dependency in normal conditions.
- **Bear case:** Net leverage of 3.6x and interest coverage of only 3.6x EBIT leave limited margin of safety; any sustained revenue contraction or credit market disruption could force dilutive capital actions.
- **Confidence:** Medium-High — Debt quantum, maturity profile, and leverage ratios are directly sourced from company press releases; stress test assumptions involve judgment but are grounded in disclosed figures.

## Red Flags

- Net leverage ratio increased from 3.33x (end-2024) to 3.63x (end-2025) — leverage is moving higher, not deleveraging as expected for a mature business.
- Interest expense will increase further in 2026 (~$80M guided increase), compressing EBIT-based coverage below 3.5x.
- 2025 debt issuance ($2B in senior notes at 6.25%) signals the company is refinancing at higher rates than legacy debt, increasing the ongoing interest burden.
- No disclosed revolving credit facility utilization or headroom — liquidity buffer size beyond cash is not confirmed in available sources.
- A severe CRO industry downturn (extended biotech funding drought) combined with elevated leverage represents a tail risk scenario.

## Score: 5 / 10

Moderate balance sheet — long-dated maturities and strong FCF generation prevent near-term distress, but net leverage of 3.6x and rising interest expense represent a meaningful financial risk that eliminates this from "fortress" territory and requires sustained execution to reduce over time.
