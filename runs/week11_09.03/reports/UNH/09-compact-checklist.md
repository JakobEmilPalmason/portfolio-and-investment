# Compact Checklist — UNH

**Date:** 2026-03-11

1. **Business**: UnitedHealth Group collects insurance premiums from employers, governments, and individuals to cover medical costs, and simultaneously runs Optum — a pharmacy benefit manager, care delivery network, and analytics platform — capturing more of every healthcare dollar through vertical integration at $447.6B in annual revenue.
2. **Moat**: Scale-driven contracting leverage across 50 million members, deep switching costs from Optum's embedded clinical and claims technology, and proprietary utilization data that improves risk pricing create a multi-source moat — but government reimbursement rates set by CMS mean UNH is effectively a price-taker in its fastest-growing segment.
3. **Economics**: Operating cash flow has remained $19–20B annually even through the 2024–2025 crisis, converting at 1.5–1.6x GAAP net income, but ROIC collapsed from ~14.6% in 2023 to ~9.5% in 2024 as the medical loss ratio surged to 88.9%, and a recovery toward historical 13–17% levels is assumed but not yet demonstrated.
4. **Management**: Stephen Hemsley's return as CEO brought a $25M open-market share purchase at crisis prices and the track record of building the Optum platform, but the undisclosed Cloverfields Capital conflict of interest, combined Chairman/CEO role, and a 45% miss to FY2025 adjusted EPS guidance ($16.35 vs. $29.50–$30.00 guided) are material stewardship deficiencies that require years of execution to rebuild trust.
5. **Debt risk**: With $78.4B in total debt, interest coverage at ~4.7x (below the 5x comfort threshold), and debt-to-capital at 44.1% exceeding management's own 40% target, UNH is not in financial distress — the $21B revolving credit facility and $19B+ annual operating cash flow provide sufficient liquidity — but the balance sheet has less cushion than at any point in the past decade.
6. **Price**: At ~$282/share and a forward P/E of ~16x on $17.75 guided 2026 adjusted EPS, the market is pricing in roughly 4–6% perpetual owner earnings growth — well below UNH's 10-year CAGR — but the base case intrinsic value of $230–245 is below the current price, meaning fair value is only achieved in a bull scenario requiring DOJ resolution, MCR normalization, and Optum multiple recovery.
7. **Margin of safety**: There is no meaningful margin of safety at $282: the base case intrinsic value (~$230–245) is 13–18% below current price, the bear case ($110–130) implies 54–61% downside if DOJ penalties are severe and MCR remains structurally elevated, and the realistic downside-to-upside ratio of ~1.1–1.5x does not meet the 2–3x threshold required for a high-conviction entry.
8. **Thesis breaker**: Sell if the DOJ announces criminal charges or a settlement exceeding $15B, or if MCR guidance for 2027 does not show improvement toward 87–88% — either event would confirm the margin pressure is structural and the intrinsic value has permanently re-rated to the $110–150 range.
