## Detailed Analysis

**Owner Earnings Estimate**

UNH reported 2025 operating cash flow of $19.7B on $447.6B revenue. Net income in 2025 was approximately $12.1B (implied from ~$16.35 adjusted EPS x ~940M diluted shares, less adjustments). D&A for a business of this scale runs approximately $3–4B annually. Maintenance capex is modest for a services-heavy business — estimated at $2–3B per year. Owner earnings estimate for 2025: approximately $13–16B, or roughly $14–15B as a central estimate. For 2026, with guided operating cash flow of at least $18B and some margin recovery (adjusted EPS guided at $17.75+), owner earnings likely recover toward $14–16B. In 3–5 years (2029–2031), if margins normalize and membership stabilizes, owner earnings could reach $20–28B depending on the growth path.

**Scenario Analysis — Year 5 (2030)**

Shares outstanding assumed at ~920M (modest buybacks offset by some dilution).

*Bear Case:* DOJ investigation results in a large settlement ($10–20B), Medicare Advantage faces additional rate cuts, medical cost inflation remains elevated at 88–90% MCR structurally. Revenue grows only 3% annually to ~$520B; EBIT margins compress to 4.5%; owner earnings ~$14B. Apply 12x multiple (regulated utility-like) = $168B equity value. Discount at 10% for 5 years ≈ $104B present value, or roughly $113/share. **Bear case intrinsic value: ~$110–130/share.** Current price represents little margin of safety in this scenario.

*Base Case:* DOJ investigation resolved via manageable settlement ($3–6B), MCR stabilizes at ~88% by 2027, membership recovers modestly, Optum continues to compound at high single digits. Revenue grows 6% annually to ~$600B by 2030; EBIT margin recovers to 5.5%; owner earnings ~$20B. Apply 17x multiple = $340B equity value. Discount at 10% ≈ $211B PV, or roughly $230/share. **Base case intrinsic value: ~$220–245/share.**

*Bull Case:* DOJ probe resolved favorably, MCR improves toward 87%, Optum Health gains market share, GLP-1 drug costs partially offset by new plan pricing. Revenue grows 9% annually to ~$690B; EBIT margin recovers to 6.5%; owner earnings ~$28B. Apply 20x multiple = $560B equity value. Discount at 10% ≈ $348B PV, or ~$378/share. **Bull case intrinsic value: ~$360–400/share.**

**Multiples in Context**

At $282/share and ~940M diluted shares, market cap is approximately $265B. With net debt of roughly $30–35B, enterprise value is approximately $295–300B. On 2025 EBITDA (estimated ~$21–22B), EV/EBITDA is ~13.7x — near the 10-year median of ~14x. On 2026 adjusted EPS guidance of $17.75, forward P/E is approximately 15.9x. UNH's 5-year average forward P/E has historically been ~20–22x. Peer comparables (CVS, Humana, Cigna/Elevance) trade at 10–16x forward earnings given the managed care sector's current distress. UNH's multiple implies minimal premium for its Optum integrated model — a clear derate from historical norms.

**Reverse-Engineered Assumptions**

For the current $282 price to be fair value using a 10% discount rate, UNH would need to grow owner earnings at approximately 4–5% perpetually — far below historical performance. Alternatively, at a 12x steady-state multiple and 8% discount rate, the market is pricing in approximately $20B normalized owner earnings in 5 years with no premium for the Optum platform. These are conservative assumptions that suggest the market is pricing in persistent structural damage to the business model, not merely a cyclical trough.

**Perfection vs. Disaster?**

The market is neither pricing in perfection nor outright disaster — it is pricing in prolonged uncertainty. The stock has halved from its peak, implying a sustained loss of the premium multiple UNH earned as a reliable healthcare compounder. The key unknowns — DOJ outcome, Medicare Advantage reimbursement rates, and medical cost normalization — are all unresolved. The current price appears to offer potential upside in the base/bull scenarios but carries genuine binary risk from the DOJ investigation. This is a value trap if the regulatory outcome is punitive, or a generational buying opportunity if the business normalizes.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** If the DOJ probe resolves without existential penalties and medical costs normalize by 2027, UNH is deeply undervalued at $282 versus a base case intrinsic value of ~$230–245 and bull case of $360–400.
- **Bear case:** If regulatory penalties are severe and MCR remains structurally elevated, the bear case intrinsic value of ~$110–130 implies the stock is still materially overvalued.
- **Confidence:** Low — the wide dispersion between bear case (~$110) and bull case (~$400) reflects genuine binary uncertainty from the DOJ investigation and MCR trajectory; this is not a situation where conviction is appropriate without more resolution on the regulatory front.

## Red Flags

- Active DOJ criminal and civil investigation into Medicare Advantage billing fraud — outcome unknown, potential penalties unknown
- 2026 revenue guided to decline — extremely rare for UNH; signals member attrition and pricing discipline not yet restored
- Medical care ratio elevated at 88.9% (2025) versus 85.5% (2024); Q4 2025 MCR was 91.5%, suggesting the quarterly trend has not yet turned
- CEO Andrew Witty resigned in May 2025; leadership instability amplifies execution risk
- 55% drawdown from all-time high; stock now trades at levels not seen since early 2022

## Score: 5 / 10

At $282/share, UNH is priced for substantial uncertainty and may be cheap on normalized earnings, but the wide range of intrinsic value outcomes ($110–$400) driven by unresolved DOJ liability and MCR trajectory makes a confident valuation call impossible — fair value is assigned pending regulatory clarity.
