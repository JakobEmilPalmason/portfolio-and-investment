# Compact Checklist — RELX.L

**Date:** 2026-03-12

1. **Business**: RELX sells subscription-based access to proprietary data platforms — Elsevier journals for scientists, LexisNexis for lawyers, LexisNexis Risk for banks and insurers, and trade show access via RX — generating 84% electronic and 70%+ recurring revenue that customers renew because the platforms are embedded in their professional workflows.

2. **Moat**: The Risk segment's proprietary records database (billions of court, insurance, and identity records assembled over 20+ years through exclusive data-licensing deals) cannot be replicated at any price, while Legal and STM hold customers through deep workflow entrenchment — retraining staff and migrating decades of workflow integrations would cost more in time and reputational risk than any competitor's price discount can offset.

3. **Economics**: RELX converts 99% of its £3.3bn adjusted operating profit to free cash flow (£2,313m in 2025), operates at a 34.8% adjusted operating margin that has expanded every year for a decade, and earns a ROIC consistently above 15% on a fully-loaded basis — the textbook fingerprint of an asset-light compounder.

4. **Management**: CEO Erik Engstrom has compounded adjusted EPS at high single digits every year of his 16-year tenure through disciplined bolt-on M&A (averaging £200–460m per year, exclusively capability-filling acquisitions), progressive dividend growth, and accelerating buybacks (£1bn in 2024, £1.5bn in 2025, £2.25bn planned for 2026) — a rational, consistent capital allocator with no history of empire-building.

5. **Debt risk**: At 2.0x net debt/EBITDA, A- rated (S&P, Fitch) and A3 (Moody's), with 11.8x interest coverage, a fully-funded pension, and a $3.5bn undrawn revolving credit facility maturing in 2030, RELX's balance sheet can absorb any plausible recession without threatening solvency — a 30% revenue drop (implausible given 84% subscription/electronic revenue) would still leave the business cash-flow positive.

6. **Price**: At 2,594p (20x adjusted 2025 EPS of 128.5p and 14x EV/EBITDA, both at decade lows), the market is pricing in approximately 3% perpetual owner earnings growth — a scenario where AI causes sustained stagnation across Legal, STM, and Risk simultaneously — despite 2025 results delivering 7% underlying revenue growth and record adjusted cash flow with no reported customer churn.

7. **Margin of safety**: The business margin of safety is substantial (99% recurring revenue, no customer concentration, 2.0x leverage, proprietary data moats decades in the making), but the price margin of safety is thin — today's 2,594p sits only ~6% above the bear-case intrinsic value of approximately 2,450p, meaning an investor is relying on the AI disruption thesis being wrong rather than buying at a demonstrable discount to conservative value.

8. **Thesis breaker**: Sell if two consecutive half-year earnings reports show underlying revenue growth decelerating below 4% alongside evidence of rising customer churn or declining Lexis+ AI paying user counts — that combination would confirm AI is substituting rather than supplementing RELX's workflow tools, validating the structural disruption scenario that current pricing has so far assumed without reported evidence.
