## Detailed Analysis

**Returns on Capital**

Veeva's ROIC is exceptional and improving. ROIC for TTM periods in FY2025–FY2026 measured between 31% and 37%, well above its WACC of approximately 11%. This gap — the economic spread — is the core measure of whether a business is genuinely creating value, and Veeva's spread is one of the widest in enterprise software. ROE is somewhat lower (trailing twelve months ~13–14% as of late 2025) due to the large and growing cash hoard on the balance sheet ($5B+) diluting the equity base; on an ex-cash basis, underlying returns are materially higher. The directional trend in ROIC is improving as operating leverage compounds — non-GAAP operating income grew 19% in Q4 FY2026 on 16% revenue growth, demonstrating the characteristic pattern of high-ROIC software businesses widening their spread over time.

**Margin Structure**

Gross margins have been structurally stable in the low-to-mid 70s over the past five years, with a notable positive inflection in FY2025 (+4.5 percentage points to 74.5%). This improvement reflects an accelerating mix shift toward higher-margin subscription and data products and away from lower-margin professional services. GAAP operating margins improved from roughly 18% in FY2024 to 25% in FY2025. On a non-GAAP basis — which better reflects cash economics by excluding stock-based compensation and amortization — operating margins reached approximately 42% in FY2025 and ~45% in FY2026. Net margin on a GAAP basis was approximately 26% for FY2025 (net income $714M on $2.75B revenue), up from roughly 19% the prior year. The FY2026 non-GAAP operating margin of ~45% puts Veeva in the elite tier of enterprise SaaS businesses.

**Cash Generation**

Veeva's cash generation is excellent and accelerating. Operating cash flow grew from $780M (FY2023) to $911M (FY2024) to $1,090M (FY2025), a ~40% increase over two years. Free cash flow for FY2025 was $1.07B — implying capex of only ~$20M, an almost negligible capital requirement for a $3B-revenue business. The FCF margin of approximately 39% is outstanding. Owner earnings (FCF as a proxy) are growing faster than reported GAAP earnings, which is the preferred pattern: it means accounting charges are overstating economic costs. The three-year FCF CAGR implied by FY2023→FY2025 data is approximately 17%, roughly in line with revenue growth, indicating no meaningful margin compression in cash terms.

**Capital Intensity**

Veeva is among the most capital-light businesses in enterprise software. Its product is cloud-delivered software and data — it requires no physical infrastructure ownership, no inventory, and no significant tangible asset base. Total assets of roughly $7.8–8.1B are dominated by cash and intangibles, not PP&E. The narrow gap between operating cash flow ($1.09B) and free cash flow ($1.07B) in FY2025 confirms that maintenance capex is negligible (sub-$25M annually). Growth capex is primarily R&D (expensed, not capitalized), which runs at 20–25% of revenue — this is an investment in future product capability rather than physical capital. The asset-light model means incremental revenue produces high marginal returns.

**Revenue Quality**

Revenue quality is very high. Subscription services comprise approximately 83–84% of total revenue and carry high net revenue retention — historically above 120% NRR in earlier years, reflecting land-and-expand dynamics. The most recent specific NRR disclosures cited ~119–121% in FY2022; while the company does not report this metric as prominently today, subscription revenue growing 17–20% YoY in FY2025–FY2026 — faster than total revenue — indicates continuing upsell and expansion within the installed base. Professional services (the remaining ~16% of revenue) are lower-margin but serve as a strategic attachment to the core subscription platform. Revenue is almost entirely volume-driven through customer expansion and new product adoption rather than price increases, which is durable. The life sciences vertical focus means customer relationships are deeply embedded and switching costs are high.

**Operating Leverage**

Operating leverage is strongly visible in the data. From FY2024 to FY2025, revenue grew 16.5% while non-GAAP operating income grew materially faster, driving a ~6 percentage-point improvement in non-GAAP operating margin (35.6% → 42%). In Q4 FY2026, non-GAAP operating income grew 19% against 16% revenue growth. This is exactly the pattern expected from a high-gross-margin software business with a largely fixed cost structure: as subscription revenue scales, incremental revenue drops heavily to the operating line. The company still invests substantially in R&D (20–25% of revenue) and sales, so margins have not yet reached their long-run potential — guidance for FY2027 suggests continued margin expansion as the Vault CRM migration accelerates subscriber density.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** A near-monopoly life sciences cloud platform with 84% recurring revenue, ~45% non-GAAP operating margins, 31%+ ROIC, and a clear decade-long runway to expand Vault CRM into the commercial cloud — operating leverage will drive FCF well above $2B annually within five years.
- **Bear case:** Life sciences is a narrow vertical; customer concentration risk, CRM migration execution risk, and non-GAAP vs. GAAP margin gap (SBC is real dilution) could disappoint if upsell slows or the migration drags.
- **Confidence:** High — FY2026 earnings were just reported (March 4, 2026), key metrics are current and from official sources, and the economic pattern is consistent over multiple years.

## Red Flags

- GAAP vs. non-GAAP margin gap is material: stock-based compensation runs at roughly 15–20% of revenue, making GAAP operating margin (~25%) significantly lower than non-GAAP (~45%) — SBC represents real economic dilution to shareholders.
- Professional services revenue is a lower-margin segment that dilutes consolidated economics; if this segment grows disproportionately during implementation cycles (Vault CRM migration), near-term consolidated margins could modestly compress.
- ROE appears low (13–14% trailing) relative to ROIC because ~$5B in cash earns below-ROIC returns — capital allocation discipline on that cash pile is a watch item.

## Score: 9 / 10

Veeva's business economics are exceptional: high and improving ROIC, 74%+ gross margins, 45% non-GAAP operating margins, asset-light model, $1B+ FCF growing at ~17% annually, and 84% recurring subscription revenue with a dominant market position — docked one point for meaningful SBC dilution and the concentration of the business in a single vertical.
