## Detailed Analysis

**Owner Earnings Estimate**

Veeva's owner earnings are best approximated by taking GAAP net income and adding back non-cash charges while subtracting maintenance capex. For FY2026 (ended January 31, 2026): GAAP net income was $908.9M; D&A is not separately disclosed in search results but non-GAAP adjustments brought non-GAAP net income to $1,352.5M, implying approximately $440M in stock-based compensation and amortization adjustments. Maintenance capex for a software business is minimal — likely under $50M annually. Operating cash flow was reported at $1.42B for FY2026. Using operating cash flow less maintenance capex as a proxy for owner earnings gives a figure in the range of $1.35–1.42B, or approximately $8.00–8.50 per diluted share on ~167M shares. At $190/share, this implies a trailing P/Owner Earnings of roughly 22–24x — a historically low multiple for VEEV. Over a 3–5 year range, assuming 12–18% annual FCF growth (consistent with the analyst consensus trajectory from $1.07B in FY2025 to $2.37B by FY2030 as estimated by MacroTrends), owner earnings per share would reach approximately $12–17 by FY2030.

**Scenario Analysis**

Three scenarios are modeled over 5 years (FY2031, ending January 31, 2031), discounted back at a 10% required return.

*Bear case:* Revenue growth decelerates to 8–10% annually as Salesforce wins 15%+ of the CRM replacement cycle; FCF margins compress to 30% from current ~43%; Year 5 owner earnings ~$1.8B; exit multiple 18x FCF = $32.4B equity value; discounted back at 10% over 5 years = ~$20.1B; per share ~$121. Current price ($190) is a 36% premium to this bear case — there is no margin of safety relative to a stressed scenario.

*Base case:* Revenue grows at 13% annually through FY2031; FCF margin holds at 40–42%; Year 5 owner earnings ~$2.5B; exit multiple 22x FCF = $55B equity value; discounted back = ~$34B; per share ~$204. Current price approximately at fair value under base assumptions.

*Bull case:* Revenue accelerates to 16–18% as Vault CRM migration completes successfully and new Vault products (Quality, Safety, Clinical) penetrate deeper into mid-market; FCF margin expands to 45%; Year 5 owner earnings ~$3.2B; exit multiple 27x FCF = $86B equity value; discounted back = ~$53B; per share ~$320. Upside of ~68% from current price.

**Multiples in Context**

VEEV currently trades at: trailing P/E ~35–36x, forward P/E ~21.9x, EV/EBITDA ~25x, and P/FCF approximately 22–29x (depending on whether FY2025 or FY2026 FCF figures are used, with FY2026 operating cash flow of $1.42B implying a lower multiple). Versus own history, these are at the low end of the 5-year range — in 2021 VEEV traded at 70–100x FCF. Versus peers, Veeva trades at a premium to Salesforce (forward P/E ~26x) and a significant premium to IQVIA (~19x forward P/E), but IQVIA is a services-heavy, leveraged business with lower growth. Versus the S&P 500 (forward P/E ~20–22x), Veeva trades at a modest premium appropriate for a higher-growth, capital-light compounder.

**Reverse Engineering the Implied Assumptions**

At $190/share with ~167M diluted shares, the market cap is approximately $31.7B. Subtracting net cash (Veeva carries substantial cash with minimal debt; enterprise value reported at ~$23.4B), the implied enterprise value is in the low-to-mid $20 billion range. To justify this at a 10% discount rate with an 18x terminal FCF multiple in Year 10, the market appears to be pricing in 10–12% annual FCF growth — roughly half the company's recent growth rate. This is consistent with investors pricing in material risk from the Salesforce competitive threat and a successful but partial CRM migration.

**Market Pricing Perfection or Pessimism?**

The market is pricing moderate pessimism. The stock is down ~39% from its 52-week high of $310.50 and trades well below the analyst consensus of ~$294. The implied growth rate baked into current prices (10–12% FCF CAGR) is meaningfully below Veeva's historical growth rate of 15–20% and below the consensus estimate trajectory. This suggests the market has priced in partial execution failure — not catastrophe, but a meaningful competitive headwind. The stock is not "priced for perfection" — it is priced for a mediocre outcome.

- **Bull case:** Vault CRM migration completes on schedule, Salesforce threat proves manageable, and Veeva expands organically to $6B+ in revenue by FY2030 — stock worth $300–350.
- **Bear case:** Salesforce wins 20%+ of the CRM transition, migration failures erode customer trust, and FCF growth slows to single digits — stock worth $100–130 at conservative multiples.
- **Confidence:** Medium — Veeva's FY2026 results are fresh and high-quality; the main uncertainty is forward competitive dynamics in CRM which are not yet resolved; DCF models cluster in a useful range.

## Red Flags

- FCF multiple compression from 50–60x to 22x reflects a structural re-rating, not just a cyclical dip — the re-rating may not fully reverse even on good news
- Analyst price targets averaging $294 may embed optimistic assumptions about the CRM transition completing without material customer losses
