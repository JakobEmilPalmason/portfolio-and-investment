## Detailed Analysis

**Debt Levels**

Reddit is debt-free. Total debt as of FY2025 year-end is $0, and the debt-to-equity ratio is effectively 0%. This is an exceptional balance sheet characteristic. The company has no funded debt, no term loans, no convertible bonds, and no public debt maturities to worry about. The only debt-adjacent item is an undrawn revolving credit facility, which is a contingent liability rather than a balance sheet obligation. Net debt is deeply negative — the company has a net cash position of approximately $2.5B. For Buffett-style analysis, this is a fortress balance sheet.

**Debt Maturity Schedule**

There is no debt maturity schedule to analyze. Reddit has no outstanding funded debt. The revolving credit facility was amended and extended to July 1, 2030, with commitments reduced to $500M from a prior higher level. This facility is undrawn. If Reddit ever drew on it, the maturity is well-laddered at 2030. There is no near-term refinancing risk of any kind.

**Interest Coverage**

With no debt and no interest expense, interest coverage is not a meaningful metric. EBIT for FY2025 was approximately $443M (revenue of $2.203B at a 20.1% operating margin). Interest income is positive — the company earns a return on its $2.5B cash pile. There is no interest burden to cover. This is the strongest possible outcome on this dimension.

**Liquidity**

Reddit's liquidity position is exceptional on every measure. Cash and cash equivalents at Q4 2025 year-end: approximately $2.5B. Current ratio: approximately 12x. Total current assets are approximately $2.5B against total current liabilities of approximately $210M. Management has stated a policy of maintaining over $1B in cash on the balance sheet, and the current cash position is 2.5x that target. In addition to cash on hand, the company has the undrawn $500M revolving credit facility available through 2030. FCF of $684M in FY2025 means the company organically generates cash at a rate that would rebuild even a fully depleted cash position within four years. Liquidity risk is effectively nil.

**Refinancing Risk**

Reddit faces no refinancing risk. The company generates substantial positive free cash flow ($684M in FY2025), has $2.5B in cash, and has no debt. It does not depend on capital markets being open to fund operations, service debt, or meet near-term maturities. Even in a severe market dislocation, Reddit could operate indefinitely without external financing. The only scenario where Reddit might access capital markets would be for an acquisition or if the buyback program depletes cash materially — neither is a survival risk.

**Off-Balance-Sheet Obligations**

Specific operating lease and pension data from the 2025 10-K footnotes were not accessible in granular detail from public financial data aggregators. However, total liabilities of $245.2M against $2.5B in cash suggests total off-balance-sheet and on-balance-sheet obligations are modest relative to the company's financial resources. Reddit operates a digital platform with minimal physical infrastructure, so operating lease obligations (primarily office space) are expected to be limited in size. No pension obligations have been identified. Contingent liabilities may exist related to ongoing regulatory scrutiny (GDPR, content moderation), but these are qualitative risks, not quantified financial exposures at this time.

**Stress Test: Revenue Down 30% for Two Years**

FY2025 revenue: $2.203B. A 30% decline would bring revenue to approximately $1.54B in Year 1 and approximately $1.08B in Year 2 (a further 30%). Under this scenario:

- At 91% gross margins, gross profit in Year 1 would be approximately $1.40B.
- Operating expenses (R&D + S&M + G&A) in FY2025 totaled approximately $1.56B. If these expenses remain fixed, the company would be operating near break-even in Year 1.
- However, Reddit has demonstrated it can cut costs: R&D declined 16% YoY in FY2025. A meaningful reduction in headcount and SBC could restore profitability even in a downturn scenario.
- Starting cash of $2.5B means Reddit could absorb operating losses of $500M/year for five years without issuing equity.
- The $500M revolving credit facility provides an additional buffer.
- No debt maturities force any action.

Conclusion: Reddit would survive a severe two-year revenue decline of 30% without issuing equity. The combination of $2.5B in cash, zero debt, high gross margins, and a variable cost structure (particularly SBC) provides substantial downside protection. This is a strong result on the stress test.

## Signal Summary
- **Bull case:** A debt-free fortress balance sheet with $2.5B in cash, $684M in annual FCF, zero refinancing risk, and an undrawn $500M credit facility means Reddit can invest aggressively, return capital via buybacks, and weather any recession without existential risk.
- **Bear case:** The $1B buyback program will consume a meaningful portion of the cash cushion, and if advertising revenue declines materially in a recession (historically ad budgets are cut first), FCF could deteriorate faster than expected — though the starting balance sheet position is so strong this is a concern, not a crisis.
- **Confidence:** High — the balance sheet data is straightforward, well-sourced from Q4 2025 earnings and FY2025 10-K filings, and the conclusion (fortress balance sheet) is unambiguous.

## Red Flags
- Regulatory risk (EU GDPR, content moderation litigation) could create contingent liabilities not currently quantified on the balance sheet.
- The $1B share buyback program, while shareholder-friendly, will reduce the cash buffer from $2.5B — management's stated floor of $1B in cash is the binding constraint going forward.
- Operating expenses remain high relative to revenue ($1.56B in FY2025 vs. $2.203B revenue), meaning the business is not yet at a self-sustaining "lean" operating mode — a sharp revenue decline would require rapid cost reduction.

## Score: 9 / 10
Reddit's balance sheet is a genuine fortress — zero debt, $2.5B in cash, a current ratio of 12x, no refinancing risk, $684M in annual FCF, and an undrawn $500M revolver — the only reason this is not a 10 is the lack of a longer profitability track record and the pending $1B buyback that will reduce the cash cushion.
