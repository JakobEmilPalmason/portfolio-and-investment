# Compact Checklist — RDDT

**Date:** 2026-03-11

1. **Business**: Reddit sells advertising space (~94% of $2.2B in 2025 revenue) alongside user-generated community discussions across 100,000+ subreddits, and a secondary AI data licensing stream (~$140M in 2025) to companies like Google and OpenAI who pay for 20 years of authentic human text they cannot replicate.

2. **Moat**: Reddit's moat rests on a 20-year, non-replicable corpus of human-generated text that sits at the center of AI training pipelines, reinforced by community-specific network effects in 100,000+ self-sustaining subreddits where content quality compounds over time — but individual users have zero switching costs and the platform depends on Google's algorithm for new user discovery.

3. **Economics**: In FY2025 Reddit generated $684M in free cash flow on $2.2B revenue with 91.2% gross margins, near-zero capex of $6.7M, and ROIC of ~61% — but GAAP FCF overstates true owner earnings because $387M in stock-based compensation (18% of revenue) is a real cost that reduces SBC-adjusted earnings to roughly $350-400M.

4. **Management**: CEO Huffman holds ~$420M in stock and initiated a $1B buyback in February 2026 — the first real capital allocation signal — but SBC at 18% of revenue, a dual-class voting structure with no time-based sunset, and a capital allocation track record of only one year prevent a strong stewardship verdict.

5. **Debt risk**: Reddit carries zero long-term debt, holds $2.5B in cash, generates $684M in annual FCF, and has an undrawn $500M revolver through 2030 — stress-tested to survive a 30% two-year revenue decline without issuing equity.

6. **Price**: For the current price of ~$135 to make sense, Reddit must sustain 40-50% revenue CAGR through 2028-2029, expand EBITDA margins to 38-42%, and earn a 22-25x terminal multiple on SBC-adjusted owner earnings — achievable assumptions, but not conservative ones, and the bear case DCF of ~$78 is below the current price.

7. **Margin of safety**: There is no price margin of safety at $135 — the bear-case intrinsic value (~$78) is 42% below the current price, the upside-to-downside ratio is only 1.4x ($213 base vs. $78 bear), and the only real protection is the fortress balance sheet ($2.5B cash, zero debt) and a community moat that has survived 20 years.

8. **Thesis breaker**: Sell if two consecutive quarters report revenue growth below 40% with no structural explanation, or if the FTC issues a formal complaint or EU enforcement order targeting Reddit's data licensing practices — either event would impair the core monetization narrative simultaneously across both revenue streams.
