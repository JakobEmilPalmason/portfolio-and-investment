## Detailed Analysis

**Returns on Capital**

Reddit's return profile has undergone a dramatic transformation. As recently as FY2024, ROIC was negative — the company was burning cash and recording net losses of $484M for the year, heavily distorted by $801M in IPO-related stock-based compensation charges. By FY2025, the picture reversed sharply: ROIC (TTM) reached approximately 61%, ROE (annualized, Q4 2025) reached 36.3%, and net income for the full year was $529.7M. The Q4 2025 annualized run-rate for net income was approximately $1B, implying returns on equity are trending even higher as the business scales. The 5-year ROIC history is largely uninformative because the company was pre-profitability until 2025, but the trajectory from deeply negative to 61%+ in a single year is exceptional — if it is durable.

**Margin Structure**

Gross margins are outstanding. Reddit's gross margin of 91.2% reflects the near-zero incremental cost of distributing user-generated content and serving ads on top of an existing platform. This is comparable to the best software and media businesses in the world. The more important story is the operating margin expansion: from -37% in FY2024 to +20.1% in FY2025, achieved on revenue roughly doubling. Research and development expenses declined 16.3% in FY2025 (to ~$783M) — an unusual feature that reflects the unwinding of IPO-related SBC. Operating leverage is clearly present: gross profit grew faster than operating expenses, which grew slower than revenue. Net margin was 24.1% in FY2025, a remarkable swing from deeply negative a year prior. The 2026 adjusted EBITDA margin guide of ~36% at the midpoint signals management expects further margin expansion.

**Cash Generation**

Free cash flow in FY2025 was $684.2M — a 217% YoY increase — on operating cash flow of $690.9M. FCF conversion relative to GAAP net income of $529.7M is approximately 129%, meaning Reddit generates more cash than GAAP earnings, which is typical for asset-light businesses where D&A (non-cash) adds back to cash but capex is minimal. Owner earnings (net income + D&A - maintenance capex) approximate $684M, consistent with reported FCF. The notable caveat is that GAAP net income includes stock-based compensation as an expense ($387M in FY2025), so cash earnings excluding SBC dilution impact are much higher. Investors must decide whether SBC (targeting "high teens" as a % of revenue in 2026) represents true economic cost — Buffett-style analysis says it does.

**Capital Intensity**

Reddit is among the least capital-intensive businesses in public markets. Total capex of $6.7M against $2.203B in revenue implies a capex intensity ratio of 0.3%. The company does not own data centers, physical infrastructure, or manufacturing assets of meaningful size. Revenue is generated from ads served against user-generated content and from API data licensing — neither requires material growth capex. Maintenance capex appears negligible. This asset-light model means nearly all earnings translate to free cash flow with minimal reinvestment drag, which is a structural advantage in a Buffett framework.

**Revenue Quality**

Advertising accounts for approximately 93.9% of revenue — a structural concentration risk. Advertising revenues are cyclically sensitive, correlated with macro GDP and consumer spending, and subject to competition from larger platforms (Meta, Google/YouTube, TikTok). However, several factors partially offset this: (1) Reddit's user base (471M weekly active users, up 24% YoY) is a large and growing addressable market; (2) user intent on Reddit is particularly high for purchase research, making it a valuable advertising medium; (3) data licensing (Google, OpenAI) is a growing and high-margin revenue stream projected to reach $400M annually by 2027; (4) Q1 2026 revenue guidance implies 52-54% YoY growth — deceleration from 69%, but still strong. Revenue is primarily volume-driven (more users, more ad impressions) with emerging price uplift from better targeting. Recurring quality is moderate — advertising is inherently lumpy and non-contracted — but data licensing agreements are multi-year and contracted.

**Operating Leverage**

Operating leverage is clearly present and powerful. From FY2023 to FY2025, revenue grew from $804M to $2.203B (+174%). Over the same period, gross profit grew proportionally, while operating losses converted to $443M in operating income. The R&D expense decline of 16.3% YoY while revenue grew 69% demonstrates the non-linear nature of the cost structure. As Reddit adds users and advertisers, incremental revenue drops to the bottom line at high rates. The 2026 EBITDA margin guide of ~36% vs. ~20% operating margin in 2025 suggests management expects continued operating leverage as revenue grows another 52-54%. This is the hallmark of a scalable digital platform.

## Signal Summary
- **Bull case:** Reddit is an asset-light digital platform at an early point in its monetization curve, with 90%+ gross margins, explosive FCF growth, and a unique data moat that generates contracted AI licensing revenue — as scale increases, operating leverage should drive margins significantly higher.
- **Bear case:** Nearly 94% of revenue is cyclical advertising, the business was only profitable for one year (FY2025), SBC dilution remains substantial at ~18% of revenue, and the 2026 revenue guidance implies meaningful deceleration.
- **Confidence:** Medium — FY2025 financials are real and exceptional, but the track record of profitability is just one year, and the operating history at scale is thin.

## Red Flags
- Advertising revenue concentration (~94%) creates significant cyclicality and platform competition risk.
- FY2024 net income was -$484M; profitability is a single-year phenomenon as of this writing — durability is unproven.
- Stock-based compensation of $387M (18% of revenue) is material economic dilution and inflates reported FCF vs. true owner earnings when adjusted for equity cost.
- 2026 revenue growth is guided at 52-54%, a notable deceleration from 69% — whether the growth runway is as long as bulls expect is uncertain.
- R&D expenses of $783M against $2.203B revenue (36% of revenue) remain very high, suggesting the platform still requires heavy engineering investment.

## Score: 7 / 10
Reddit's business economics in FY2025 are genuinely excellent — 91% gross margins, 20%+ operating margins, $684M FCF, near-zero capex intensity, and rapidly improving ROIC — but the single-year profitability track record, advertising concentration, and heavy SBC prevent a higher score until durability is demonstrated over multiple cycles.
