## Detailed Analysis

### Owner Earnings Today

Owner earnings ≈ GAAP net income + D&A – maintenance capex. For FY2025: GAAP net income $530M; D&A is not separately disclosed in available search results but is embedded in the EBITDA bridge (EBITDA $457.9M vs. net income $530M suggests interest/tax offsets; adjusted EBITDA $845M suggests significant stock-based compensation add-back). Using FCF of $684M as the cleanest proxy for owner earnings (FCF = operating cash flow minus capex of only $7M), Reddit's normalized owner earnings today are approximately **$650-700M**, with the caveat that stock-based compensation (~$300-350M estimated, given the large gap between GAAP net income and adjusted EBITDA) is a real cost not reflected in FCF. Adjusting for SBC, true economic owner earnings are likely closer to **$350-400M** on a fully diluted basis (~200M shares). That implies ~$1.75-2.00 per share in SBC-adjusted owner earnings, putting the SBC-adjusted P/E at approximately 67-77x at current price.

### Scenario Analysis: Owner Earnings in Year 5 (FY2030)

**Assumptions shared across scenarios:**
- Current shares: ~200M diluted (slight dilution from SBC, offset by $1B buyback program)
- Discount rate: 10% (appropriate for a high-growth, higher-risk business)
- Terminal multiple applied on Year 5 owner earnings (SBC-adjusted FCF)

**Bear Case**

- EBITDA margin stagnates at 35% (competition, rising content/infra costs, data licensing headwinds)
- Adjusted EBITDA FY2030: ~$1.75B; subtract SBC (~$500M) and capex (~$30M) → owner earnings ~$1.2B
- Terminal multiple: 20x owner earnings → enterprise value $24B; less net debt (company is net cash ~$950M) → equity value ~$24.95B
- Per share (200M shares): **~$125**
- Discounted back 5 years at 10%: **~$78**

**Base Case**

- EBITDA margin expands to 42% (ad tech leverage, data licensing scale)
- Adjusted EBITDA FY2030: ~$3.44B; subtract SBC (~$700M) and capex (~$50M) → owner earnings ~$2.7B
- Terminal multiple: 25x owner earnings → enterprise value $67.5B; net cash add-back → equity value ~$68.5B
- Per share: **~$342**
- Discounted back 5 years at 10%: **~$213**

**Bull Case**

### Multiples in Context

At ~$135/share and ~$27B market cap ($26B enterprise value, given ~$950M net cash):
- **Trailing GAAP P/E:** ~51x ($135 / $2.62 EPS)
- **Forward P/E (2026 consensus):** ~34x (consensus ~$4.00 EPS implied)
- **EV/EBITDA (trailing adjusted):** ~31x ($26B EV / $845M adj. EBITDA)
- **EV/EBITDA (reported trailing):** ~57x ($26B / $457.9M reported EBITDA)
- **P/FCF (GAAP FCF):** ~39x ($27B / $684M FCF)
- **P/S:** ~12x ($27B / $2.2B revenue)

**Vs. peers:** Meta trades at ~6.9x P/S, ~29x forward P/E; Pinterest at ~3.9x P/S; Snap at ~2.1x P/S. Reddit commands a 3x+ premium on P/S to Meta and an order-of-magnitude premium to Snap and Pinterest. This premium is justified only if Reddit sustains 50%+ revenue growth for multiple years — something neither Meta (at Reddit's age) nor Pinterest achieved.

**Vs. own history:** IPO price was $34 (March 2024); ATH was ~$283 (September 2025); current price is ~$135 — roughly 4x the IPO price. The stock has already been through a severe 52% drawdown from ATH, suggesting the market has begun to reprice the growth premium.

### What Must Be True for $135 to Be Justified?

Reverse-engineering the base case: at $135/share and a 10% discount rate, the market is pricing in approximately 40-50% revenue CAGR over the next 3-4 years, EBITDA margins reaching 38-42%, and a terminal multiple of ~22-25x owner earnings. These are achievable but not conservative assumptions. The bear case — where growth decelerates to 25-35% and margins disappoint — yields intrinsic value below the current price.

### Pricing In Perfection or Disaster?

At $135, the market is not pricing in perfection (that was $283 in September 2025) but it is not pricing in disaster either. The stock is roughly fairly valued under a reasonable base case — perhaps with a slight lean toward overvalued when one adjusts rigorously for SBC. The most dangerous scenario for the long-term investor is not a catastrophic collapse but rather a gradual de-rating as growth decelerates, much as happened to Twitter and Snap after their peak growth phases. However, the significant drawdown from ATH has already removed the most egregious premium.

## Signal Summary
- **Bull case:** 50%+ revenue growth sustains through 2027-2028, data licensing becomes a high-margin third revenue stream, and Reddit earns $200+ per share in intrinsic value at 10% discount rate.
- **Bear case:** Growth decelerates to 25-30% by 2027, data licensing faces regulatory disruption, SBC dilution persists, and true economic value sits below $135.
- **Confidence:** Low-Medium — Reddit achieved genuine profitability in 2025 and the business model is increasingly validated, but the growth trajectory and data licensing durability contain significant uncertainty; the discount from ATH is real but does not guarantee margin of safety.

---

## Red Flags
- SBC is extremely high relative to GAAP earnings, making reported FCF misleading; true economic owner earnings are materially lower
- Trailing EV/EBITDA of 31-57x (depending on EBITDA definition) leaves almost no room for execution errors
- Data licensing (~12% of revenue) faces FTC scrutiny and EU AI Act headwinds; contracts with Google/OpenAI may not renew at current terms
- Revenue growth decelerating from 70% (Q4 2025) to ~53% (Q1 2026 guide) — trajectory matters more than level
- P/S of ~12x is 3x Meta's multiple and 6x Pinterest's; Reddit must dramatically outgrow peers to justify this premium
- Advertising concentration (95% of revenue) makes Reddit highly sensitive to macro ad spending cycles

---

## Score: 4 / 10

At ~$135, Reddit is modestly overvalued when SBC is properly accounted for and growth is risk-adjusted; the base case yields ~$213 intrinsic value but the bear case is below current price, providing insufficient margin of safety for a Buffett-style investor who demands 30%+ discount to conservative estimates.
