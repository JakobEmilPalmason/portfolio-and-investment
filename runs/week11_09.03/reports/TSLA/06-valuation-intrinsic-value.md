## Detailed Analysis

### Owner Earnings Today

Owner earnings = Net income + D&A – maintenance capex. Tesla's 2025 net income was $3.794B. D&A ran approximately $5.7B (TTM per GuruFocus). Total capex was $8.5B; a reasonable split assigns roughly $4–5B to maintenance (sustaining existing factories and fleet) and the remainder to growth (new Gigafactories, Cybercab ramp, Optimus tooling). Using $4.5B maintenance capex: owner earnings ≈ $3.794B + $5.7B – $4.5B = **~$5.0B**. On a $1.47T enterprise, the owner earnings yield is approximately 0.34%, or a 295x multiple on owner earnings. Even using the more generous reported FCF of $6.2B, the FCF yield is 0.42%.

### Scenario Analysis — Intrinsic Value in Year 5 (2030), Discounted at 10%

**Bear Case (probability: 35%)**
Assumptions: Vehicle deliveries stagnate at 1.6–1.8M units; automotive margins compress further to 10–12% as BYD and others undercut on price; FSD/robotaxi generates minimal revenue; Optimus is pre-revenue; energy storage doubles to $25B but at lower margins. Owner earnings in 2030: ~$7–8B. Apply a 25x multiple (still a premium to mature auto, justified by energy and software optionality): enterprise value ~$175–200B. Equity value per share: **~$50–55**. Downside from today's $405: approximately **-86%**.

**Base Case (probability: 45%)**
Assumptions: Deliveries recover to ~2.2M by 2030; automotive margin recovers to 16–17%; FSD subscriptions contribute $3–5B/year in high-margin revenue; robotaxi is operational in limited U.S. cities but not yet transformative; energy storage reaches $22–25B. Owner earnings in 2030: ~$14–18B. Apply a 40x multiple (tech-auto hybrid premium): enterprise value ~$560–720B. Equity value per share: **~$165–210**. Downside from today: approximately **-50 to -59%**.

**Bull Case (probability: 20%)**
Assumptions: Deliveries reach 3M+ by 2030 with Model Q (affordable EV) driving volume; automotive margin expands to 20%+ via software attach rates; robotaxi launches broadly and contributes $10–15B revenue at 60%+ margins; Optimus ships 50,000+ units to external customers; energy storage is $30B+. Owner earnings in 2030: ~$30–40B. Apply a 60x multiple (justified if robotaxi and Optimus are validated): enterprise value ~$1.8–2.4T. Equity value per share: **~$540–710**. Upside from today: approximately **+33–75%**.

### Multiples in Context

At ~$405/share: trailing P/E ~380x, forward P/E ~180x (on $2.25 consensus EPS), Price/FCF ~237x, EV/EBITDA ~128x. Compare: BYD trades at ~14x forward P/E; GM at ~8x; Ford at ~7x; Toyota at ~10x. Even tech peers are far cheaper — Apple at ~33x, Nvidia at ~55x. Tesla's multiple embeds the assumption that it is not an automaker at all but a robotics/AI/autonomy platform. That may ultimately be true. But as of today, roughly 95% of revenue is automotive, and automotive margins are under pressure.

### What Must Be True for Today's Price to Be Justified?

Reverse-engineering $1.47T at a 30x normalized exit multiple (reasonable for a high-growth platform in 2030) requires $49B in owner earnings by 2030 — a ~10x increase in five years from today's ~$5B. Achieving that requires: (a) robotaxi generating at least $15–20B in near-100%-margin net income, (b) Optimus contributing meaningfully, and (c) automotive stabilizing at strong margins. Every one of these must materialize on schedule. None have been proven commercially at scale.

### Market Pricing Perfection or Pessimism?

The market is unambiguously pricing in a version of perfection. The current multiple is defensible only if Elon Musk's most optimistic autonomous/robotics scenarios prove correct within 3–5 years. Historical base rates for technology transitions of this magnitude materializing on schedule are not favorable. The Energy division is a genuine bright spot and arguably underappreciated, but it alone cannot justify anything close to the current valuation.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** FSD/robotaxi achieves broad commercial deployment by 2027–2028, unlocking recurring high-margin revenue that transforms Tesla's earnings trajectory and partially justifies the premium.
- **Bear case:** Robotaxi and Optimus continue to slip timelines, vehicle sales remain under pressure from BYD, and the stock re-rates toward a fair auto/tech hybrid multiple in the $80–150 range.
- **Confidence:** High — that the stock is significantly overvalued on any reasonable DCF basis using current financials; Low — on the precise downside target, as sentiment and Musk-driven momentum can sustain elevated multiples longer than fundamentals suggest they should.

---

## Red Flags

- Trailing P/E of ~380x on a business reporting its first-ever annual revenue decline is extraordinary by any standard.
- Owner earnings yield of ~0.34% offers zero margin of safety; a 10-year Treasury yields more.
- The entire bull case rests on unproven, repeatedly delayed technology (FSD/robotaxi) and speculative future products (Optimus).
- Automotive gross margin (~14%) is well below the 25–30% needed to justify even a moderate tech premium.
- Management credibility on timelines is severely impaired: full autonomy has been "one year away" since 2016.
- BYD now outsells Tesla globally and is rapidly improving software/battery capabilities at lower price points.

---

## Score: 2 / 10

At a trailing P/E of ~380x and a Price/FCF of ~237x on a business reporting declining revenue and compressed margins, Tesla is priced for a future that has not arrived and may not arrive on schedule — conservative intrinsic value estimates range from $50 to $210, implying the stock is trading at 2–8x fair value depending on scenario.
