## Detailed Analysis

**ROIC and Return Metrics**

Aeluma's ROIC stands at approximately -11.7% as of the most recent data, which is expected for an early-stage semiconductor company that has not yet achieved commercial scale. The company went public in 2021 and has only recently crossed $1M in quarterly revenue. There is no meaningful 5–10 year ROIC track record to assess; the company simply has not existed long enough at any scale. ROE is similarly negative given persistent net losses. These metrics are structurally uninformative for now — what matters is trajectory and whether the underlying economics can eventually support high returns. The gross margin signal (discussed below) suggests the unit economics could eventually be attractive, but that is a long-distance projection.

**Margin Structure**

Gross margin is the most encouraging data point in Aeluma's economics. In Q1 FY26 (quarter ended September 30, 2025), gross profit was $0.684M on $1.385M of revenue, yielding a 49.4% gross margin. The trailing twelve-month gross margin is approximately 59.6%. These margins are consistent with a fabless or near-fabless semiconductor model where the company designs products and outsources manufacturing — producing structurally high gross margins at scale. However, operating margin is deeply negative: Q1 FY26 operating expenses of $2.99M against $1.39M revenue produce an operating loss, and the operating margin for the company's history has never been positive. Net margin for FY2025 was approximately -65% (net loss of ~$3.0M on $4.67M revenue). The direction is improving (FY2025 loss was 34% smaller than FY2024) but the gap between gross margin and operating margin is enormous and closing slowly.

**Cash Generation and FCF**

Free cash flow is negative. Operating cash outflow for Q1 FY26 was $0.815M. Capex was modest at $0.21M for the same quarter, so FCF for Q1 FY26 was approximately -$1.0M. The company burns cash primarily through personnel costs (salaries, stock-based compensation, benefits), not capital investment. FCF conversion of net income is not meaningful to compute in a net loss position. The company's cash position ($38.6M at December 31, 2025) is primarily the product of equity raises — specifically a $22.1M public offering in September 2025 — not operating cash generation. At the current burn rate of approximately $1–2M per quarter, the company has roughly 20+ quarters of runway, which is a genuine safety buffer, but the cash is not self-generated.

**Owner Earnings and Capital Intensity**

Owner earnings (net income + D&A - maintenance capex) are negative. D&A is minimal given the asset-light model; maintenance capex appears to be a small fraction of total capex (estimated at $0.1–0.2M/year). The negative owner earnings simply reflect the pre-profitability stage. The capital intensity model is genuinely light: the company does not own fabrication facilities and leverages government-funded R&D contracts to offset development costs. This is a favorable structural choice — if the technology reaches commercial scale, the incremental capital required for revenue growth should be low.

**Revenue Quality**

Revenue quality is the most significant concern in the business economics analysis. The vast majority of Aeluma's revenue comes from government R&D contracts (DARPA, NASA, U.S. Navy, and similar agencies), with a small and currently immaterial commercial sampling component. This is not recurring subscription revenue — it is project-based government work. Contract revenue is inherently lumpy, subject to government budget cycles, and does not create durable customer relationships in the same way that commercial product revenue does. The 408% revenue growth in FY2025 reflects contract wins, not commercial market penetration. Q2 FY26 revenue of $1.3M actually declined year-over-year (Q2 FY25 was $1.6M), illustrating this lumpiness. There is no disclosed customer concentration data, but the reliance on a small number of government contracts implies high concentration risk. Operating leverage has not yet been demonstrated — revenue growth has not yet translated into any reduction in the operating loss ratio.

**Operating Leverage**

Operating leverage is absent at this scale. As revenues doubled and then quadrupled in FY2024–FY2025, operating losses continued because the company simultaneously expanded headcount and R&D investment. Q2 FY26's higher adjusted EBITDA loss versus Q2 FY25 is explained by higher salaries, stock compensation, and benefits from new hires — the company is investing ahead of commercial scale. This is rational for a technology company in early commercialization, but it means operating leverage will only appear if and when commercial revenue inflects materially above the current $4–6M annual run rate.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** Proprietary photonics/compound semiconductor technology with government validation from DARPA/NASA achieves commercial inflection in AI, defense, or consumer electronics, at which point the capital-light model and ~60% gross margins produce excellent economics.
- **Bear case:** Government R&D contracts remain the primary revenue source indefinitely, commercial scale never materializes, the company continues burning cash through equity dilution, and the economics never close.
- **Confidence:** Low — The company has only 2–3 years of meaningful financial history, almost no commercial revenue, and is pre-profitability; current financial metrics are not informative about the ultimate economics of the business.

## Red Flags

- ROIC deeply negative (-11.7%) with no trajectory to positive returns on current revenue base
- Revenue almost entirely from lumpy government R&D contracts, not recurring commercial sales
- Q2 FY26 revenue declined year-over-year — growth is not yet consistent
- Operating expenses ($3M/quarter) are more than double quarterly revenue ($1.3M)
- Company has never been FCF positive; all cash comes from equity issuances
- Shares outstanding increased approximately 50% over the past year from ongoing equity raises
- No demonstrated operating leverage at any point in the company's history

## Score: 2 / 10

Aeluma's business economics are deeply negative across virtually every measurable dimension — negative ROIC, negative FCF, negative operating margin, lumpy non-recurring revenue, and no evidence of operating leverage — reflecting its true status as an early-stage, pre-commercialization technology company rather than an investable business with established economics.
