## Detailed Analysis

**Owner Earnings Today.** Broadcom reported GAAP net income of $23.1B and free cash flow of $26.9B in FY2025 (ended November 2025), on revenue of $63.9B. Capital expenditures were modest at $623M -- remarkably low for a company this size, which reflects the asset-light nature of Broadcom's fabless semiconductor and software model. Depreciation and amortization can be estimated at roughly $11B (the gap between EBITDA of $34.1B and operating income, adjusted for interest and taxes), though a large portion of this is amortization of acquisition-related intangibles from the VMware deal rather than true economic depreciation. Using a conservative approach: GAAP net income of $23.1B + estimated D&A of $11B - total capex of $623M = roughly $33.5B in a gross "owner earnings" sense, but this overstates reality because the intangible amortization does reflect a real (if non-cash) cost of the VMware acquisition. A more honest owner earnings figure uses free cash flow as the proxy: approximately $27B for FY2025. On a trailing 12-month basis through Q1 FY2026, this is likely approaching $29B given the Q1 operating cash flow strength. At $1.57T market cap, that is roughly 54-58x trailing owner earnings.

**Scenario Analysis -- What Could Broadcom Be Worth in FY2030?** Broadcom's current revenue run-rate is approximately $77-80B annualized (based on Q1 FY2026 revenue of $19.3B and Q2 guidance of $22B). The company has two engines: (1) AI semiconductors growing >100% YoY and (2) infrastructure software (VMware) growing low-double-digits with 78%+ operating margins.

*Bear Case:* AI infrastructure spending normalizes after a 2-3 year buildout frenzy. Broadcom's AI revenue growth decelerates sharply to 10-15% by FY2028-2030 as hyperscalers bring more design in-house or shift to alternative architectures. Software grows at 8%. FY2030 revenue: ~$120B. Adjusted EBITDA margin: 60% (compression from competitive pressure). EBITDA: $72B. Owner earnings (FCF): ~$55B. At a 20x FCF multiple (reflecting slower growth): $1.1T enterprise value. Less $45B net debt (assuming continued paydown): ~$1.05T equity value, or roughly $220/share. That is about 33% downside from today.

*Base Case:* AI spending remains robust but growth normalizes to 25-30% CAGR through FY2030. VMware delivers on its $30B+ software revenue target and grows at 10-12%. Total FY2030 revenue: ~$155B. EBITDA margin holds at 65%: EBITDA of ~$100B. Owner earnings (FCF): ~$80B. At 25x FCF (premium for quality and growth): $2.0T enterprise value. Less $35B net debt: ~$1.97T equity value, or roughly $415/share. That is about 25% upside from today.

*Bull Case:* AI infrastructure spending accelerates further. Broadcom captures dominant share of custom AI accelerator market (currently 4 major hyperscaler customers, could add more). AI chip revenue reaches or exceeds $100B by FY2027 as management claims, and continues growing. Software revenue exceeds $40B. FY2030 revenue: ~$200B. EBITDA margin: 68% (scale benefits). EBITDA: $136B. Owner earnings (FCF): ~$110B. At 28x FCF: $3.1T enterprise value. Less $25B net debt: ~$3.07T equity value, or roughly $650/share. That is roughly 95% upside.

**Multiples in Context.** Broadcom's trailing P/E of ~58x and EV/EBITDA of ~37-39x are elevated relative to both its own history (10-year average P/E of ~52x, though this includes years before the AI boom) and its semiconductor peers. QCOM trades at 28x trailing P/E and 11x EV/EBITDA; TXN at 35x P/E and 23x EV/EBITDA; AMD at 76x P/E and 48x EV/EBITDA; MRVL at 28x P/E and 30x EV/EBITDA. Among this group, only AMD trades at a comparable or higher premium, reflecting similar AI growth expectations. The forward P/E of ~25x is more reasonable if you believe in the consensus earnings trajectory ($9.39 non-GAAP EPS for FY2026, $12.72 for FY2027), but those estimates require AI revenue to more than double again -- a heroic but not impossible assumption given Q1 results.

**Reverse-Engineering the Current Price.** At $331/share and $1.57T market cap, the market is implying that Broadcom will roughly triple its free cash flow over the next 4-5 years (from ~$27B to ~$80B+) while maintaining or expanding margins. This requires revenue to grow from $64B to $150B+ -- a ~19% CAGR. That is aggressive for most companies, but Broadcom is currently growing revenue at 29% YoY (Q1 FY2026) and guiding to 47% YoY growth in Q2. The implied growth rate is actually below the current trajectory, which suggests the market is already discounting some deceleration. The question is whether deceleration will be gradual (price is fair) or sudden (price is too high). The $100B AI chip revenue target for FY2027 from CEO Hock Tan -- if achieved -- would by itself justify much of the current valuation. But that target requires an extraordinary ramp.

**What Must Be True.** For today's price to deliver a reasonable return (say 10% annualized over 5 years), Broadcom needs to be worth roughly $2.5T by 2031. That implies FCF of ~$90-100B at a 25x multiple. Revenue would need to reach $160-180B with 55-60% FCF margins. This is achievable in the base-to-bull case but leaves little room for error. The market is not pricing in perfection, but it is pricing in a very good outcome. A miss on AI growth -- even to "merely" strong levels -- could result in significant multiple compression.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** AI infrastructure spending proves to be a multi-decade buildout (not a cycle), Broadcom's custom silicon and networking position gives it a durable moat, and FY2030 FCF reaches $100B+, making today's price a bargain.
- **Bear case:** Hyperscaler customers bring AI chip design in-house, AI capex spending normalizes sharply by 2028, and Broadcom's growth rate converges with traditional semiconductor peers, triggering a painful multiple compression to 20-25x earnings.
- **Confidence:** Medium -- The near-term numbers are exceptional and well-documented, but the 5-year outlook depends heavily on whether AI infrastructure spending is a structural shift or a cyclical boom, which is genuinely uncertain.

## Red Flags

- At 37-39x EV/EBITDA, the stock prices in near-flawless execution on AI growth for multiple years; any stumble will be punished severely
- Customer concentration risk: Broadcom's custom AI accelerator business depends on a small number of hyperscaler customers (reportedly 4 major ones); losing even one would be material
- $57B in net debt, while being paid down aggressively, limits financial flexibility and increases downside risk in a slowdown
- CEO Hock Tan's $100B AI chip revenue target for FY2027 is a bold claim that, if missed, could damage credibility and the stock
- The GAAP P/E of ~58x already embeds significant acquisition-related amortization distortion, making true earnings power harder to assess -- investors must trust non-GAAP adjustments
- VMware customer dissatisfaction (widely reported) could create a competitive opening for alternatives, pressuring the software segment's growth and margins over time

## Score: 5 / 10

Broadcom is a phenomenal business trading at a price that reflects that quality -- the current valuation roughly matches intrinsic value under base-case assumptions, offering neither a clear margin of safety nor obvious overvaluation, which makes it a "fairly valued" hold rather than a compelling buy at today's price.
