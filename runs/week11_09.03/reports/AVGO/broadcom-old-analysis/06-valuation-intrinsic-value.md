## Detailed Analysis

**Owner Earnings Estimate.** Broadcom's owner earnings are unusually clean to calculate because the business requires very little capital expenditure to maintain. In FY2025, GAAP net income was $23.1B, free cash flow was $26.9B, and total capex was only $623M. The difference between net income and FCF is largely depreciation and amortization (much of it from acquired intangibles related to VMware), which runs well above actual maintenance spending. A conservative estimate of true maintenance capex is around $500-700M -- the company is an asset-light design house, not a fab operator. So owner earnings for FY2025 were approximately $23.1B (net income) + roughly $4-5B in excess D&A over maintenance capex, yielding something in the range of $25-27B. In Q1 FY2026, GAAP net income was $7.35B on $19.3B revenue, with FCF of $8.0B. Annualizing Q1 alone would suggest ~$30B in owner earnings, but with revenue accelerating (Q2 guided at $22B, implying a full-year trajectory of $85-90B vs FY2025's $64B), owner earnings for FY2026 could reasonably reach $35-40B. The adjusted EPS consensus of $10.29 for FY2026 on 4.74B shares implies ~$49B in adjusted net income, though this is a non-GAAP figure that excludes stock-based compensation and amortization of acquired intangibles. The truth for an owner lies somewhere between GAAP and adjusted -- call it $35-40B in real economic earnings power for FY2026.

**Bear / Base / Bull Scenario Analysis.** I will estimate Year 5 (FY2030) owner earnings under three scenarios, apply a reasonable terminal multiple, and discount back at 10% (roughly the opportunity cost of capital).

*Bear Case -- AI Spending Slows, Competition Intensifies.* Assume AI semiconductor growth decelerates to 10-15% annually after FY2027 as hyperscaler spending normalizes and custom chip alternatives emerge. Software segment grows at 5% annually. Total revenue reaches ~$120B by FY2030. Net margins compress modestly to 30% (from ~36% today) as competition pressures pricing. Owner earnings: ~$36B. Apply a 20x multiple (mature tech company). Enterprise value: $720B. Subtract $40B net debt (after paydown). Equity value: $680B. Per share: ~$143. Discounted back 4.5 years at 10%: ~$93/share. This represents roughly 72% downside from today's price.

*Base Case -- Strong but Decelerating Growth.* AI revenue grows 50%+ in FY2026-2027, then 20-25% annually through FY2030. Software grows 8-10% annually. Total revenue reaches ~$150B by FY2030. Net margins hold at 33-35%. Owner earnings: ~$50B. Apply a 25x multiple (premium tech/infrastructure business). Enterprise value: $1.25T. Subtract $25B net debt. Equity value: $1.225T. Per share: ~$258. Discounted back 4.5 years at 10%: ~$168/share. This implies roughly 49% downside from today's price.

*Bull Case -- AI Dominance, Software Compounds.* AI semiconductor revenue exceeds $100B by FY2027 (per management's stated goal) and continues growing 25-30% annually. Broadcom becomes the dominant custom silicon partner for 5+ hyperscalers. Software segment grows 12-15% as VMware integration unlocks pricing power. Total revenue reaches $190B+ by FY2030. Net margins expand to 37-38% as software mix increases. Owner earnings: ~$70B. Apply a 30x multiple (dominant franchise). Enterprise value: $2.1T. Subtract $15B net debt. Equity value: $2.085T. Per share: ~$440. Discounted back 4.5 years at 10%: ~$287/share. This implies roughly 13% downside from today's price -- meaning even the bull case barely justifies the current stock price.

**Multiples in Context.** Broadcom's trailing P/E of ~68x is well above its 5-year average of ~51-60x, and significantly above the semiconductor industry median of ~44x. However, the forward P/E of ~25x on FY2026 adjusted earnings looks more reasonable -- roughly in line with NVIDIA (22x forward), and well below Marvell Technology (~25x forward but with much smaller revenue base). Qualcomm trades at only 12x forward, but Qualcomm has lower growth and is more cyclically exposed to smartphones. Texas Instruments trades at 30x forward with much slower growth. The EV/EBITDA of 37-45x (depending on the calculation) is elevated but partly reflects the massive amortization from the VMware acquisition that suppresses GAAP earnings relative to cash flow. On an EV/FCF basis, at $1.76T EV and ~$27B trailing FCF (FY2025), Broadcom trades at roughly 65x trailing free cash flow. Even projecting FY2026 FCF of $40-43B (per Seeking Alpha's estimate of 60% FCF growth), that is still ~41-44x forward FCF. These are premium multiples that require sustained high growth to justify.

**What Must Be True for Today's Price to Be Justified.** At $330/share and ~$1.56T market cap ($1.76T EV), an investor buying today needs to believe: (a) owner earnings will grow from ~$27B (FY2025) to roughly $55-60B within 4-5 years, representing ~15-17% compound annual growth in real economic earnings; (b) the terminal multiple stays at 25-30x owner earnings, which requires the business to still be growing double digits in FY2030; and (c) the AI spending cycle is durable, not a one-time build-out. Working backwards: if you demand a 10% annual return over 5 years, you need the stock to be worth ~$530/share in FY2030. At 4.74B shares, that is ~$2.5T market cap. At a 25x owner earnings multiple, you need ~$100B in owner earnings by FY2030. That would require roughly 30% annual growth in owner earnings for 5 straight years. This is achievable only under the most optimistic scenarios. At a 30x multiple, you need ~$83B -- still requiring ~25% annual growth. The market is pricing in near-perfection.

**Implied Expectations vs Likely Reality.** The current price embeds aggressive but not completely unreasonable assumptions. Broadcom's AI revenue doubled year-over-year in Q1 FY2026, and management is guiding for AI semiconductor revenue of $10.7B in Q2 alone (140% YoY growth). The $100B AI revenue target for FY2027 is ambitious but Broadcom has credibility -- they have three confirmed hyperscaler custom chip partnerships (widely believed to be Google, Meta, and Apple), and they are the leading supplier of AI networking solutions. The VMware software business provides a stable recurring revenue base. The risk is not that the business is bad -- it is clearly excellent. The risk is that the stock price already reflects most of this excellence. With 48 out of 50 analysts rating it a Buy and a median price target of $458, the consensus is overwhelmingly bullish. When everyone agrees, the upside is usually priced in. The margin of safety at today's price is thin. Even in my base case, the stock appears to be trading above intrinsic value. The bull case roughly matches the current price, which means you need everything to go right just to earn a market-rate return.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** If AI infrastructure spending sustains 25%+ growth through 2030 and Broadcom captures dominant share in custom silicon plus networking, the stock could reach $400-450 in 3-5 years, justifying today's price with modest upside.
- **Bear case:** If AI spending normalizes sooner than expected or competition from in-house chips and alternative suppliers intensifies, the stock could re-rate to $150-200, representing 40-55% downside.
- **Confidence:** Medium -- The business quality is high-confidence (clearly excellent), but the valuation assessment depends heavily on how long hyper-growth in AI revenue persists, which is inherently uncertain at this stage of the cycle.

## Red Flags

- Current price requires near-perfection: even the base case scenario suggests the stock is overvalued by ~50% on a discounted basis
- Trailing P/E of 68x is 30% above the 10-year historical average of ~52x
- $65B in debt from VMware acquisition, though being paid down rapidly, creates risk if cash flows disappoint
- 48/50 analyst Buy ratings signals extreme consensus bullishness -- historically a contrarian warning sign
- AI semiconductor revenue ($8.4B in Q1) is concentrated among 3 hyperscaler customers, creating customer concentration risk
- The gap between GAAP earnings ($1.50 EPS) and adjusted earnings ($2.05 EPS) is large, driven by stock-based compensation and acquired intangible amortization -- investors should be cautious about which "earnings" they are paying for

## Score: 4 / 10

Broadcom is an outstanding business trading at a price that requires optimistic assumptions to justify. My base case suggests ~$168/share fair value (discounted), and even the bull case (~$287) falls short of the current ~$330 price. The market appears to be pricing in sustained AI hypergrowth and margin expansion for years to come. While this outcome is possible, it leaves very little margin of safety. For a Buffett-style investor, paying a full price for even an excellent business is a recipe for mediocre returns. The right move may be to admire the business and wait for a more attractive entry point.
