# Compact Checklist — AVGO

**Date:** 2026-03-09

1. **Business**: Broadcom sells custom AI accelerator chips to hyperscalers (44% of $19.3B quarterly revenue, +106% YoY) and enterprise infrastructure software (VMware), where 93% gross margins and 150-1,500% price increases prove customers have no practical alternative.
2. **Moat**: ~90% share in cloud switching ASICs, multi-year co-design lock-in with 5 hyperscaler XPU customers, and VMware's deeply embedded virtualization stack create switching costs so high that even 800-1,500% price hikes haven't yet caused mass defection.
3. **Economics**: 48% average FCF margins on just ~1% capex-to-revenue make this one of the most capital-efficient semiconductor businesses in history, generating $26.9B in free cash flow on $63.9B FY2025 revenue.
4. **Management**: Hock Tan is a ruthless cost optimizer who turned VMware from 15% to 77% operating margins and returned $10.9B to shareholders in Q1 FY2026 alone, but he has sold every one of his 19 stock transactions over 5 years and authorized $5.7B in stock-based compensation (11% of revenue).
5. **Debt risk**: With debt/EBITDA at 1.5x, $27B annual FCF, only $0.5B in maturities due this year, a $7.5B undrawn revolver, and stress-test survivability through a 30% revenue decline for two years, the balance sheet is not a concern.
6. **Price**: At $331/share (~25x forward non-GAAP, 58x trailing GAAP), the market requires Broadcom to sustain ~19% revenue CAGR for five years — achievable under base case but leaving no room for AI capex deceleration or VMware churn acceleration.
7. **Margin of safety**: There is almost none — downside/upside asymmetry is roughly even (+25% bull vs -33% bear to FY2030), and with Google's TPU reportedly ~78% of ASIC revenue, a single customer decision could vaporize the growth thesis.
8. **Thesis breaker**: Sell if hyperscaler AI capex declines for two consecutive quarters, if Google announces in-sourcing its TPU silicon design, or if VMware's market share drops below 50% as Nutanix and Proxmox accelerate their displacement campaigns.
