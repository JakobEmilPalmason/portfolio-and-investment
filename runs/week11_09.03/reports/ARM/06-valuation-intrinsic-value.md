## Detailed Analysis

### Owner Earnings Estimate

ARM's FY2025 GAAP net income was approximately $792M on revenues of $4.0B. Depreciation and amortization add back roughly $200–250M (estimated from EBITDA of ~$1.0B less operating income). However, FY2025 free cash flow was only $178M — a dramatic drop — because of elevated cash operating expenses, large SoftBank licensing payments counted as working capital, and high stock-based compensation (~$700M+ annually on a non-GAAP basis). True "maintenance capex" for a royalty/IP business is minimal (perhaps $30–50M annually). A rough owner-earnings estimate for FY2025 is therefore: $178M reported FCF is distorted; adjusting the $792M net income for non-cash SBC (~$700M addback on a cash basis is complex — SBC is a real cost) suggests normalized owner earnings are somewhere between $300M and $600M in FY2025. For FY2026, if the business approaches $1.8–2.2B in non-GAAP operating income annualized, and GAAP FCF recovers toward analyst forecasts of ~$2.2B, owner earnings could realistically reach $1.0–1.5B (GAAP-adjusted, excluding SBC) within the next 12–18 months.

### Scenario Analysis: Owner Earnings in Year 5 (FY2031)

**Bear Case:** Revenue growth decelerates to 12% CAGR (China headwinds, RISC-V erosion, macro slowdown). Operating margins compress to 30% non-GAAP from 41%. FY2031 revenue ~$7.0B, non-GAAP operating income ~$2.1B, GAAP FCF ~$1.4B. Apply a 30x FCF multiple (justified for an IP toll-road with modest growth), discounted 5 years at 10%: implied value ~$26B equity → ~$25/share. Downside case: **~$25–40/share**.

**Base Case:** Revenue grows at 22–25% CAGR. FY2031 revenue ~$10–11B, non-GAAP operating income ~$4.5B, GAAP FCF ~$3.0B. Apply 40x FCF multiple, discounted 5 years at 10%: implied equity ~$74B → ~$71/share. Base case: **~$65–85/share**.

**Bull Case:** AI royalty wave accelerates (v9 adoption, CSS uptake, data center doubles again). Revenue reaches $13–15B by FY2031, FCF ~$5–6B, 50x FCF multiple. Discounted at 10%: implied equity ~$155–190B → ~$150–180/share. Bull case: **~$150–180/share** — roughly in line with or above current price.

### Multiples in Context

ARM's trailing P/E of ~133–169x is extraordinary. Its forward P/E on FY2026 non-GAAP EPS guidance of $1.56–$1.64 at $124/share is approximately 75–80x. On FY2027 estimates (extrapolating ~25% EPS growth), forward P/E falls to ~60x. For comparison: NVIDIA trades at ~32x forward earnings; Qualcomm at ~13x; the PHLX Semiconductor Index at ~33x. ARM's EV/EBITDA is estimated at ~58–108x depending on the EBITDA measure used (GAAP vs. non-GAAP). The only remotely comparable valuation precedents are hyper-growth SaaS companies at peak cycle multiples. ARM is not SaaS — it has royalty cyclicality tied to device shipment volumes.

### Reverse-Engineering Implied Market Assumptions

At ~$130B market cap and $124/share, the market is implying:
- Revenue CAGR of ~25–28% for 5+ years (from ~$4.8B estimated FY2026 to ~$12–15B by FY2031).
- Free cash flow margins expanding from the current ~5% (GAAP) to ~35–40%.
- No meaningful erosion from RISC-V, no China revenue loss, no licensing model disruption.
- A terminal growth rate of 4–5% and a discount rate barely above 8%.

Every one of these assumptions must be correct simultaneously. There is no margin for error in the current price. The market is pricing ARM as if the AI royalty expansion is certain, large, and imminent — leaving no room for delay, competition, or macro disruption.

### Is the Market Pricing in Perfection or Disaster?

The current price of ~$124 — down ~33% from the July 2024 all-time high of ~$186 — reflects some revaluation, but it has not corrected to anything resembling a fundamental anchor. The market is pricing in close to perfection: the bull case scenario must materialize for the stock to simply hold its current value. Independent DCF models cluster around $33–$70 as fair value. The consensus analyst target of $155 is a growth target, not a value target, and implies 25% further upside on already-elevated assumptions. There is no scenario in which this stock is cheap.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** ARM is the irreplaceable IP layer for AI-era silicon; if v9 adoption and data-center royalties continue at current trajectory, FY2031 bull-case value approaches or exceeds the current price.
- **Bear case:** Current price embeds 25–28% CAGR for five years; any deceleration in growth, RISC-V erosion in China, or margin disappointment implies 40–60% downside from here.
- **Confidence:** Medium — the business quality is genuinely high and improving, but the valuation math is unambiguous: at $124 the stock is priced for perfection, not for a margin of safety.

---

## Red Flags

- GAAP FCF collapsed to $178M in FY2025 (82% decline), making cash earnings deeply unreliable for valuation anchoring.
- SBC is ~$700M+ per year, a real economic cost that non-GAAP metrics systematically exclude; true owner earnings are materially lower than non-GAAP figures suggest.
- All major DCF models ($22–$70 range) indicate 40–82% overvaluation; achieving fair value requires extraordinary, multi-year growth execution with no setbacks.
- China (~19% of FY2025 revenue) showing slowing growth (7.5% YoY), with RISC-V accelerating as a structural substitute.
- Reverse-engineered implied assumptions (28% CAGR, 35%+ FCF margins) have no historical precedent for a royalty-based semiconductor IP licensor.
