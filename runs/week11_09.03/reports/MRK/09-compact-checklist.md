# Compact Checklist — MRK

**Date:** 2026-03-15

1. **Business**: Merck sells patent-protected branded drugs — primarily KEYTRUDA, the world's top-selling cancer drug at $31.7B in 2025 revenue (~49% of total) — to hospitals, oncology clinics, and payers who keep buying because the clinical evidence in 40+ approved indications makes switching mid-treatment irrational.

2. **Moat**: A stack of regulatory patents (100+ on KEYTRUDA), years of clinical trial data across 30+ tumor types, and the manufacturing complexity of large-molecule biologics create a multi-year, multi-billion-dollar barrier that biosimilar entrants must overcome before displacing a drug oncologists trust.

3. **Economics**: ROIC of 21–26% far exceeds the ~6.5% cost of capital, gross margins expanded to ~77.7%, and FCF reached $18.1B in 2024 — the business generates real cash at high rates with capital expenditures running at only ~3.4% of revenue.

4. **Management**: CEO Robert Davis runs a credible bolt-on M&A playbook (Acceleron's $11.8B deal delivered WINREVAIR at $1.4B in year-one sales), maintains 16 consecutive years of dividend increases, and has launched a $3B cost-savings initiative — though buyback execution has been modest relative to $18B+ annual FCF and two of three recent acquisitions remain unproven.

5. **Debt risk**: With interest coverage of ~29x, debt/EBITDA of ~1.3–1.5x, and $13–18B in annual FCF, Merck survives a 30% revenue decline without equity issuance — even the December 2025 addition of ~$8B in new debt for the Cidara acquisition leaves the balance sheet comfortably investment-grade.

6. **Price**: At ~$119, the market is pricing a successful but imperfect post-KEYTRUDA transition — requiring roughly $20B in sustainable owner earnings at a 9% discount rate and 3% terminal growth, which is achievable in the bull case by 2030–2032 but demands meaningful pipeline execution from 20+ candidates that have not yet been de-risked.

7. **Margin of safety**: The ~3.1% dividend provides a return floor, the $3B cost-reduction program supports earnings even if revenue declines, and the pipeline carries real optionality — but the price margin of safety is thin at $119 (near the top of the $105–120 base-case DCF range), meaning this is a fair-value entry, not a discounted one.

8. **Thesis breaker**: Sell if any three of the following occur: KEYTRUDA QLEX patient conversion falls below 15% by end of 2027, the IRA-negotiated KEYTRUDA price exceeds a 60% Medicare reduction, three or more major Phase 3 trials fail in 2026–2027, or net debt/EBITDA rises above 3.0x from acquisition activity.
