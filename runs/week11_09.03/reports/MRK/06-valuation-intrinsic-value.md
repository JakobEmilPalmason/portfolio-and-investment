## Detailed Analysis

**Owner Earnings Calculation (2025)**

Based on available data: full-year 2025 net income was approximately $18.25B (reported). D&A was approximately $4.5B (per GuruFocus/Macrotrends data). Maintenance capex is estimated at roughly $3.5–4.0B (approximately 5–6% of revenue, typical for a large pharma with manufacturing and R&D infrastructure). This yields owner earnings of approximately:

- Net income: ~$18.3B
- Plus D&A: ~$4.5B
- Less maintenance capex: ~$(3.5–4.0)B
- **Owner earnings estimate: ~$18.8–19.3B (gross); however, the 2025 net income figure is elevated by prior-year comparisons and likely includes non-recurring items. Adjusted operating/recurring owner earnings are estimated at $14–16B, consistent with reported free cash flow of ~$10.5B for Q4 2025 alone and $18B+ for full-year 2024.**

Note: The market cap is approximately $285–$300B at a price of ~$119–$120. At $16B in recurring owner earnings, the earnings yield is approximately 5.5–6%, which is reasonable for a quality pharma but not deep value given the cliff ahead.

*Bull Case (20% probability):* Pipeline fires on multiple cylinders — 20 potential blockbuster launches materialize, Keytruda QLEX captures 35–40% of patients ahead of biosimilar entry, IRA litigation provides partial relief, international expansion accelerates. **Multiples in Context**

At ~$119, MRK trades at:
- Trailing P/E: ~15.9x (using ~$7.50 reported EPS for 2025, which includes some non-cash charges)
- Forward P/E: ~22–23x on 2026 guided adjusted EPS of $5.00–$5.15 — elevated due to IRA headwinds and transition costs
- EV/EBITDA: ~11x — near a decade low; peers (LLY, ABBV, BMY) trade at 12–18x
- P/FCF: ~17–19x using normalized FCF — reasonable but not cheap
- Dividend yield: ~3.1–3.3% at current prices

The forward P/E of 22x appears high, but adjusted EPS is being compressed by IRA-driven Januvia price cuts (79% Medicare reduction in 2026), Gardasil China headwinds, and elevated R&D spending. Adjusting for these transitory effects, underlying earnings power is closer to $6.50–$7.00/share, implying a more modest 17–18x forward multiple.

**Reverse Engineering the Current Price**

At $119/share and 2.53B diluted shares, market cap is ~$301B. Adding ~$37B in net debt/liabilities gives enterprise value of ~$338B. To justify this at a 9% discount rate with zero terminal growth, you need ~$30B in annual earnings — far above today's levels. At 3% terminal growth and 9% discount, you need approximately $20B in sustainable owner earnings — achievable in a bull scenario by 2030–2032, but requiring meaningful pipeline execution. The market is pricing in a successful (if not perfect) post-Keytruda transition. It is not pricing in disaster, but it is also not pricing in perfection (2021 highs were ~$90 forward P/E on pandemic-era growth stocks).

**Is the Market Pricing Perfection or Pessimism?**

MRK's stock fell from ~$130+ in early 2024 to a 52-week low of ~$73 in May 2025 (a ~44% drawdown) before recovering to ~$120. At the current price, the market is pricing modest success — not a best-case scenario. The 15–16x trailing P/E is below MRK's 5-year historical average of ~18–20x, suggesting valuation is at the lower end of its historical range. This is consistent with elevated uncertainty, not a screaming bargain.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** Pipeline diversification with 20 potential launches, a $70B pipeline opportunity by mid-2030s, and Keytruda QLEX transition enable revenue stabilization after 2028, with the stock re-rating to 17–18x earnings toward $135–155.
- **Bear case:** Keytruda cliff is steeper and faster than expected, pipeline fails to deliver at scale, IRA impacts compound, and the stock revisits $75–85 as earnings decline materially through 2030.
- **Confidence:** Medium — The base-case intrinsic value (~$105–120) is roughly in line with the current price, suggesting fair value rather than significant mispricing in either direction. Uncertainty is high due to Keytruda dependency.

## Red Flags

- 2026 guidance ($5.00–$5.15 adjusted EPS) was below analyst consensus — a sign of management conservatism or a genuine step-down in earnings power
- Keytruda will begin IRA price negotiation in 2026 for pricing effective 2028, adding another layer of revenue pressure on top of patent expiry
- Forward P/E of 22x on depressed guidance implies the stock is not cheap on near-term earnings
- Bear-case DCF ($75–85) shows meaningful downside if pipeline execution disappoints — approximately 35–40% from current levels
- $37B+ in net liabilities limits financial flexibility for large-scale M&A if pipeline gaps emerge

## Score: 5 / 10

MRK appears fairly valued to modestly undervalued versus intrinsic value: the base-case DCF (~$105–120) roughly matches the current price, the EV/EBITDA is near decade lows, and the 3%+ dividend provides a floor; however, the Keytruda cliff creates genuine uncertainty about 5-year earnings power, limiting confidence in any single intrinsic value estimate.
