## Detailed Analysis

### Owner Earnings — Current and Projected

Roper's FY2025 owner earnings stand at approximately $2.3B (FCF of $2.4B less ~$166M in stock-based compensation, plus minor adjustments). This represents a 31% FCF margin on $7.9B revenue — an outstanding cash conversion profile driven by the asset-light vertical software model (CapEx is only $105M, or 1.3% of revenue). Looking ahead 3-5 years, if Roper can grow revenue at 8-10% annually (management guides 8% for FY2026, with 5-6% organic plus acquisitions) and maintain or slightly expand FCF margins, owner earnings should reach $3.0-$3.5B by FY2028 and $3.5-$4.2B by FY2030. The key variable is whether Deltek and Neptune headwinds prove temporary (management's view) or structural.

### Scenario Analysis

**Bear Case** — Organic growth stalls at 3-4%, Deltek remains pressured by government spending cuts, Neptune faces continued tariff/demand headwinds, and acquisition returns decline. Year 5 owner earnings: ~$2.8B. Apply a 17x multiple (below historical trough) = $47.6B enterprise value. Less $9B net debt = $38.6B equity / 107.5M shares = ~$359/share. Discount back at 10% = ~$223. Downside: ~36%.

**Base Case** — Organic growth normalizes to 5-6%, acquisitions contribute 2-3% incremental growth, CentralReach/Subsplash ramp as expected, FCF margins hold at 30-31%. Year 5 owner earnings: ~$3.5B. Apply a 22x multiple (in line with historical median) = $77.0B EV. Less $9B net debt (assuming some deleveraging) = ~$68B equity / 107.5M shares = ~$633/share. Discount back at 10% = ~$393. Upside: ~12%.

**Bull Case** — Organic growth accelerates to 7-8% as AI-driven product enhancements increase net revenue retention, large M&A deal adds a high-growth vertical, government spending stabilizes. Year 5 owner earnings: ~$4.5B. Apply a 25x multiple = $112.5B EV. Less $7B net debt = ~$105.5B equity / 107.5M shares = ~$981/share. Discount back at 10% = ~$609. Upside: ~73%.

Probability-weighted (25/50/25): ~$405, suggesting approximately 15% upside from today's $351.

### Multiples in Context

Roper's current multiples represent a substantial de-rating from peak valuations:
- **P/E:** 24.7x trailing (vs. 5-year average ~32x, vs. peer average ~44.5x for quality software compounders)
- **Forward P/E:** 15.0x (cheapest in over a decade; 5-year forward average was ~26x)
- **EV/EBITDA:** 14.5x (vs. 13-year median of 21.8x; previous trough was ~13.0x during COVID)
- **P/FCF:** 17.8x (vs. 5-year average ~28x)
- **P/S:** 4.8x (vs. 5-year average ~8x)

The stock is statistically cheap relative to its own history on every metric. Compared to peers like Verisk (VRSK, ~28x forward P/E), Danaher (DHR, ~25x), and IDXX (~40x), ROP is meaningfully cheaper despite similar or better FCF profiles.

### Reverse-Engineering the Implied Assumptions

At $351/share (~$37.8B market cap, ~$46.5B EV including $9B net debt), and applying a 10% discount rate, the market is implying roughly:
- FCF grows at ~4-5% annually for the next 5 years (below management's 8% revenue growth guide)
- Terminal multiple of ~15-17x FCF (well below the historical median)
- Essentially zero value creation from the $5B+ M&A deployment capacity

Put differently, the market is pricing in a scenario where Roper's organic growth engine stalls permanently and acquisition returns deteriorate — a structurally impaired compounder thesis.

### Pessimism vs. Perfection

The market is clearly pricing in significant pessimism. The stock sits at just 13% of its 52-week range ($313-$594), down 40% from highs. Multiple analyst downgrades in January 2026 (Stifel, Oppenheimer, Argus all downgraded) have amplified selling pressure. The guidance miss — EPS of $21.30-$21.55 vs. consensus ~$23 — was a genuine disappointment. But the magnitude of the de-rating (from ~30x forward earnings to 15x) appears to overshoot the fundamental deterioration (4% organic growth vs. 5.5% expected for Q4 2025, plus a modest guidance miss). The question is whether Q4 weakness is cyclical (government shutdown, copper tariffs) or signals a permanent step-down in Roper's growth algorithm.

## Signal Summary
- **Bull case:** ROP is a proven serial compounder trading at trough multiples due to transient headwinds (government shutdown, tariffs). If organic growth normalizes to 5-6% and M&A resumes at historical returns, the stock could re-rate significantly toward $500-$600 over 2-3 years.
- **Bear case:** Organic growth has structurally decelerated, Deltek's government exposure is a long-term drag, $9.3B debt limits financial flexibility, and AI disruption threatens the vertical software moat. The stock may be a value trap.
- **Confidence:** Medium — The valuation is genuinely attractive on a historical and peer-relative basis, but the guidance miss introduces real uncertainty about the near-term growth trajectory. Need to see FY2026 execution before high confidence.

## Red Flags
- FY2026 EPS guidance of $21.30-$21.55 is 7-8% below prior consensus, suggesting management sees real near-term headwinds
- Organic growth decelerated to 4% in Q4 2025, well below the 5.5% expected
- Debt/EBITDA at 3.0x is elevated for a software compounder; limits M&A flexibility at the worst time
- Multiple analyst downgrades in January 2026 signal institutional sentiment shift
- ROIC of 6.4% is below cost of capital when goodwill is included, raising questions about long-term acquisition value creation

## Score: 7 / 10
Roper trades at historically cheap multiples with a probability-weighted upside of ~15%, and the market appears to price in overly pessimistic assumptions, but the guidance miss and organic growth deceleration are real concerns that prevent a higher score.
