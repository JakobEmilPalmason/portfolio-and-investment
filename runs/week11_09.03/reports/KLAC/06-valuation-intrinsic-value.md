## Detailed Analysis

**Owner Earnings Estimate**

KLA's fiscal year 2025 (ended June 30, 2025) reported GAAP net income of $4.06 billion on revenue of $12.16 billion. Free cash flow was $3.75 billion for FY2025, growing 23% year-over-year. Capex for FY2025 was approximately $340 million. D&A is not separately disclosed in available press releases but is estimated at ~$450–500 million annually based on asset base and acquisition amortization. Maintenance capex is estimated at ~60–70% of total capex, or roughly $200–240 million, as KLA is relatively asset-light for a semiconductor equipment company.

Owner earnings approximation (FY2025): Net income $4.06B + D&A ~$475M − maintenance capex ~$220M ≈ **$4.31 billion**, or roughly **$32/share** on ~135 million diluted shares outstanding. On a trailing twelve month basis through Q2 FY2026 (ended December 2025), owner earnings run-rate is estimated at ~$4.4–4.6 billion, or ~$33–35/share, reflecting the record Q2 FY2026 revenue quarter and continued margin expansion.

**Scenario Analysis**

*Bear Case (Year 5 = FY2031):*
Assumptions: WFE spending plateaus by FY2027 after AI investment cycle peaks; Chinese export controls permanently remove $400–500M of annual revenue; gross margins compress from ~62% to ~58% due to mix shift and competition; owner earnings grow only 3% per year from current $4.4B base, reaching ~$5.1B in Year 5 (~$40/share on ~128M shares). Apply a 20x owner earnings multiple (below recent norms but above long-run average) = intrinsic value of ~$800/share. Discount back 5 years at 10% = ~$500/share. **Bear case: ~$500**, implying ~65% downside from $1,465.

*Base Case (Year 5 = FY2031):*
Assumptions: WFE grows at mid-single digits annually post-cycle; KLA maintains process control leadership; China exposure stabilizes at ~25–28% of revenue; owner earnings grow ~8–9% per year from $4.4B, reaching ~$6.5B in Year 5 (~$52/share on ~125M shares after continued buybacks). Apply a 25x multiple = ~$1,300/share intrinsic value in Year 5. Discount back 5 years at 10% = ~$807/share. Add ~$30/share in cumulative dividends (PV) = **base case: ~$835–850**, implying ~42% downside from current price.

*Bull Case (Year 5 = FY2031):*
Assumptions: AI semiconductor capex supercycle extends; KLA gains share in advanced packaging inspection; memory recovery adds incremental growth; owner earnings grow 14–15% per year from $4.4B, reaching ~$8.5B in Year 5 (~$70/share). Apply a 28x multiple = ~$1,960/share. Discount back 5 years at 10% = ~$1,217/share. Add PV of dividends = **bull case: ~$1,250–1,280**, implying ~15% downside from $1,465.

**Multiples in Context**

The current trailing P/E of ~43x is 93% above the 10-year historical average of ~22x. The forward P/E of ~34x (based on consensus ~$43/share non-GAAP EPS for FY2026) is still elevated. EV/EBITDA of ~34x compares to the semiconductor equipment industry median of ~23x. ASML, which has a stronger monopoly position in EUV lithography, trades at ~40–47x trailing P/E. Applied Materials (AMAT) trades at ~26x forward P/E. Lam Research (LRCX) trades at a comparable forward multiple to KLA. KLA commands a structural premium due to its near-monopoly in process control (>50% share) and its more stable, non-discretionary nature relative to deposition/etch peers, but the current multiple appears to price in significant future growth.

**What Must Be True for the Current Price to Be Justified**

At $1,465/share with ~$32–35/share in owner earnings, the market is pricing KLAC at ~42–46x owner earnings. For this to generate a 10% annualized return over 10 years, KLAC would need to compound owner earnings at ~12–13% per year and maintain a ~25x exit multiple. That requires WFE to grow at or above consensus for the next decade, KLA to maintain or expand market share, margins to hold near current 60%+ gross margin levels, and no major disruption from Chinese domestic competitors or geopolitical sanctions. This is plausible but not conservative.

**Market Pricing Assessment**

The market is not pricing in perfection, but it is pricing in a sustained growth trajectory that leaves little room for error. The 52-week range of $551–$1,693 reflects the violent rerating that occurred as AI semiconductor capex accelerated. Current price of $1,465 sits ~13% below the 52-week high, suggesting some compression from peak enthusiasm. Analyst consensus price target of ~$1,670 implies modest upside of ~14%. The valuation is pricing in continued execution, not disaster, but also not a bargain.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** KLA's process control near-monopoly, structural WFE share growth from advanced packaging/AI, and aggressive buyback program justify a premium that compounds into solid returns if the AI capex cycle persists.
- **Bear case:** At 43x trailing earnings and 2x historical average multiples, any growth disappointment, WFE cycle downturn, or margin compression could trigger a severe 40–60% re-rating.
- **Confidence:** Medium — The business quality is high and well-understood; the primary uncertainty is whether the current multiple is sustainable given cyclical industry dynamics and mean-reversion risk.

## Red Flags

- Current P/E is 93% above 10-year historical average of ~22x — significant mean-reversion risk
- All three scenario cases (bear, base, bull) imply downside from current price on a 5-year DCF basis, suggesting the stock is pricing in more than reasonable expectations
- EV/EBITDA premium of ~46% to industry median with limited justification beyond quality premium
- 360% stock appreciation from end-2022 to early 2026 driven partly by P/E multiple expansion (~231%), not just earnings growth — this expansion may be fragile

## Score: 4 / 10

KLAC is an exceptional business but the current valuation at ~43x trailing P/E and ~34x forward P/E, representing a near-doubling of its historical average multiple, leaves no margin for error and implies downside even in the base case on a 5-year DCF, warranting a below-average valuation score despite outstanding business fundamentals.
