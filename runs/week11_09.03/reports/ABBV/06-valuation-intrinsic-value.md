## Detailed Analysis

### Owner Earnings Estimate

Owner earnings = Net income + D&A – maintenance capex. AbbVie's GAAP net income is severely distorted by non-cash amortization of intangible assets acquired in the Allergan deal (~$7–8B/year), making reported net income a poor proxy for economic earnings. The better measure is adjusted net income (which strips non-cash intangible amortization and acquired IPR&D charges).

**2025 actuals:**
- Revenue: $61.16B
- Adjusted EPS: $10.00 (implying adjusted net income of approximately $17.7B on ~1.77B diluted shares)
- Free cash flow (management-reported): approximately $17.8–18.0B
- Depreciation (property/equipment only): approximately $740–760M annualized based on H1 2025 data
- Amortization of intangibles: approximately $7.6–7.8B annualized (non-cash; largely Allergan-related)
- Maintenance capex: estimated at $700–900M (pharma is capital-light in manufacturing; R&D is expensed)

**Owner earnings (2025, approximate):**
- Adjusted net income: ~$17.7B
- Add back D&A (property only, already excluded from adj. earnings): minimal adjustment
- Less estimated maintenance capex: ~($800M)
- **Owner earnings 2025: approximately $16.9–17.5B**

Note: Management's FCF guidance of $18.5B for 2026 includes $3.5B Skyrizi royalty inflows (one-time in nature as royalty payment structure). Normalized 2026 owner earnings ex-royalty windfall: approximately $15–15.5B, growing toward $17–19B by 2028–2029 as Skyrizi/Rinvoq scale.

**3–5 year owner earnings trajectory:**
- 2026E: ~$18.5B (including royalties) / ~$15.5B normalized
- 2027E: ~$17–18B (Skyrizi/Rinvoq >$31B combined; neuroscience scaling)
- 2028–2029E: ~$19–22B as immunology matures and neuroscience/oncology contribute

The GAAP P/E of ~95x is almost entirely an artifact of non-cash intangible amortization from the $63B Allergan acquisition (2020). This is a known accounting distortion and should not be used for valuation. The forward adjusted P/E of ~16x and adj. FCF yield of ~4.5% are the meaningful metrics.

On a forward adjusted basis, ABBV trades roughly in line with large-cap pharma peers. It is not obviously cheap on current-year metrics, but the growth profile (Skyrizi/Rinvoq still in high-growth phase) and the royalty windfall in 2026 provide near-term upside to owner earnings.

### Reverse-Engineering Implied Assumptions

At $234/share (~$407B market cap, ~$469B EV), what must be true?

1. **Owner earnings must reach ~$25–27B by 2030** (implying ~7–9% annual growth from 2025 base) — achievable but requires solid pipeline execution beyond Skyrizi/Rinvoq
2. **Terminal multiple of 15–16x** — reasonable for a quality pharma with diversified franchises
3. **No catastrophic patent cliff event** before 2030 — Skyrizi and Rinvoq patents extend into 2030s; manageable near-term
4. **Debt paydown continues** — $18.5B FCF vs. ~$62B net debt; 3.5x leverage paydown over 5 years is realistic
5. **IRA drug pricing rules do not materially clip Skyrizi/Rinvoq** — some risk but not likely at current scale before 2028

The market is not pricing in perfection. It appears to be pricing in a reasonable growth continuation scenario, with the forward multiple (~16x adjusted) reflecting confidence in the immunology franchise but not euphoric multiple expansion.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** Skyrizi/Rinvoq exceed $35B by 2030 while neuroscience pipeline delivers multiple new drug approvals, pushing owner earnings above $25B and supporting a $175–200 intrinsic value.
- **Bear case:** Competitive erosion in immunology plus pipeline failures compress owner earnings to $14B by 2030, implying the stock is significantly overvalued at current prices.
- **Confidence:** Medium — The forward P/E and FCF yield are reasonable, but the scenario analysis reveals that the current price of ~$234 is above all three of my intrinsic value estimates (bear: ~$90, base: ~$119, bull: ~$177); the stock appears modestly to materially overvalued relative to a conservative DCF framework, though adjusted earnings momentum is clearly positive.

---

## Red Flags

- GAAP P/E of ~95x creates surface appearance of extreme overvaluation that could deter value investors who do not strip out intangible amortization
- All three DCF scenarios (including bull) produce intrinsic value estimates below the current market price of ~$234, suggesting the market is paying for growth beyond what my conservative assumptions support
- $62B net debt is a meaningful leverage overhang; FCF is being directed to debt service in parallel with dividend payments, limiting buyback capacity
- 2026 FCF of $18.5B includes $3.5B non-recurring Skyrizi royalty payments — normalized FCF is lower, making the FCF yield appear more attractive than it is on a run-rate basis
- Heavy reliance on two drugs (Skyrizi + Rinvoq = ~42% of 2025 revenue and growing) represents concentration risk in the revenue base

---

## Score: 4 / 10

The current price of ~$234 appears modestly to materially above conservative intrinsic value estimates; the forward adjusted earnings multiple is reasonable but the DCF analysis does not support a meaningful margin of safety — the market is paying a full price for a high-quality business, leaving limited upside and non-trivial downside in adverse scenarios.
