## Detailed Analysis

**1. Owner Earnings Estimate**

For full-year 2025, IDEXX reported: net income ~$1,059M (implied from FCF = ~100% of net income, FCF = $1,057M per the $1.1B headline); D&A estimated at ~$130–140M (capex guidance for 2025 was $160M, consistent with prior years); maintenance capex is roughly $80–90M (total capex $160M, of which approximately half is growth-oriented given instrument placements and new product manufacturing). Owner earnings estimate: $1,059M + $135M D&A − $85M maintenance capex ≈ **$1,109M** in 2025.

In 3–5 years (by 2029–2030), using management's own guided 10–14% EPS growth near-term and the analyst consensus of ~11–12% EPS CAGR through 2030 (EPS reaching ~$21–$24 by 2030 on ~79–80M diluted shares), owner earnings could reach **$1,550M–$1,850M**. This range reflects 7–10% revenue growth with modest margin expansion (operating margin currently ~29–31%, potentially reaching 33–36% on scale).

**2. Scenario Analysis**

*Bear case — growth decelerates, margins compress:*
Assume revenue growth slows to 5–6% CAGR (mature North American market, limited international gains), operating margins hold flat at 29%, and FCF conversion stays at 95%. By Year 5 (2030), owner earnings ≈ $1,350M. Apply a multiple of 28x FCF (de-rated from current premium toward a steady-state compounder). Terminal value = $37.8B enterprise value. Discount at 9% WACC over 5 years: PV ≈ $24.6B equity value, or roughly **$310–$320/share** after netting ~$800M net debt. This scenario prices the stock at less than half today's level — but it requires a meaningful de-rating combined with a material growth slowdown, not the base outlook.

*Base case — reasonable continuation:*
Revenue grows 8% CAGR, margins expand gradually to 32%, FCF conversion 97%. Year 5 owner earnings ≈ $1,700M. Apply 32x multiple (below 5-yr historical avg P/FCF). Terminal EV ≈ $54.4B, discounted at 8%: PV equity ≈ $37B, or roughly **$460–$480/share** on a strict DCF basis. However, if the market continues to apply a 40–45x forward P/E at exit (as it has historically for high-quality compounders growing 10%+), the exit equity value jumps to ~$53B, PV equity ~$36B net — or **$650–$680/share**. This "market-implied multiple held" base case aligns with today's price.

*Bull case — sustained excellence:*
Revenue grows 10–11% CAGR, margins expand to 35%, FCF conversion improves further. Year 5 owner earnings ≈ $1,950M. Apply 38x multiple (below historical peaks but premium to market). Terminal EV ≈ $74B; PV equity ≈ $50B, roughly **$900–$950/share**. This case requires IDEXX to continue taking market share internationally while expanding diagnostics frequency in existing accounts — plausible given the razor-and-blade model's momentum.

**3. Multiples in Context**

At ~$662, IDXX trades at:
- Trailing P/E: ~49x (TTM EPS $13.08 → P/E 50.6x; $662 / $13.08 ≈ 50.6x)
- Forward P/E: ~44x (2026E EPS midpoint $14.55)
- EV/EBITDA: ~34x (market provides 34.22x)
- EV/FCF: ~48x (market provides 48.72x; consistent with ~2.2% FCF yield)

Historical context: IDXX has averaged ~55–57x P/E over the past 5 years (peaked ~75x in late 2021, troughed near 35x in 2015–2016). The current ~44–50x forward multiple sits near the *lower* end of its historical premium band — not cheap in absolute terms, but relatively modest versus its own history. Peers in veterinary/life science tools (Zoetis trades at ~32x forward, Danaher ~30x) are cheaper, but lack IDEXX's >60% North American market share in in-clinic diagnostics and its network-effect-driven switching costs.

**4. What Must Be True to Justify $662**

Reverse-engineering the current price requires: (a) 10–12% EPS CAGR over the next 7–10 years — consistent with company guidance and TIKR consensus of ~11.7% EPS CAGR to 2030; (b) terminal P/E of 28–32x in Year 10 (a reasonable steady-state premium for a high-ROIC compounder); (c) no structural threats to the veterinary diagnostics ecosystem — no disruptive competitor, no adverse regulation, no sustained pet ownership decline. All three assumptions are individually credible; the question is whether paying today's multiple is safe if *any one* breaks.

**5. Implied Expectations vs. Likely Reality**

The market is NOT pricing in perfection (that would require 2021's ~75x P/E). At ~44x forward, the market is pricing in a high-quality compounder with durable 10%+ earnings growth — essentially the base case. There is no "disaster" scenario priced in; IDXX is not in distress. The implied expectation is "steady excellence with moderate growth acceleration," which is IDEXX's historical mode. The risk is not overpaying for a bad business — it is paying a full price for a good business where valuation compression alone could cost 1–3 years of returns even if the business performs as expected.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** A multi-year compounding machine priced near historical-low multiples that could deliver 35–45% upside if the current growth trajectory sustains and multiples hold.
- **Bear case:** With near-zero FCF yield (2.2%) and a base-case DCF barely supporting today's price on a strict discount basis, any growth deceleration or multiple compression leads to flat-to-negative returns for years.
- **Confidence:** Medium — current financial data is solid (2025 actuals confirmed); uncertainty lies in the terminal multiple and growth durability beyond 3 years.

## Red Flags

- FCF yield of ~2.2% provides extremely thin safety margin — virtually all upside depends on continued earnings growth.
- Bear-case DCF intrinsic value (~$310–$320/share) implies >50% downside if growth and multiple both disappoint simultaneously.
- 2026 guidance of 7–9% organic growth represents a deceleration from 2025's 10%+ performance — the market may not fully price this slowdown risk.

## Score: 4 / 10

IDXX is a world-class business trading at a full price: the base-case DCF supports roughly today's price only if the market continues to apply a ~40–45x forward multiple at exit, and strict intrinsic-value math suggests limited upside; a valuation score of 4 reflects modest overvaluation relative to conservative estimates, with meaningful downside if the premium compresses.
