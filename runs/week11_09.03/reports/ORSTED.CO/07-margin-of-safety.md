## Detailed Analysis

**Price Margin of Safety**

The classic Buffett margin of safety question: how much cheaper is the stock than a conservative, bear-case valuation? Using the bear-case intrinsic value estimated in Umbrella 6 (DKK 62-91 per share, discounted), Orsted at DKK 139 actually trades above the bear-case intrinsic value. This is a critical finding: there is negative margin of safety in the most pessimistic but plausible scenario. The base-case intrinsic value (DKK 124 per share, discounted) is also below the current price, meaning the stock is only attractive if you believe the bull case has significant probability. Morningstar's current fair value of DKK 160 does imply a modest discount to intrinsic value, but 15% is far below Buffett's preferred 30-50% margin of safety for a business with this level of uncertainty. If you are wrong on EBITDA growth by 20% (say DKK 22-23 billion in Year 5 instead of DKK 28 billion), the base-case intrinsic value falls to around DKK 100 per share — a 28% loss from today's price. You do not make money by being modestly wrong.

**Business Margin of Safety**

This is where Orsted scores better. The core European offshore wind business — serving the UK, Germany, Denmark, Netherlands, and Taiwan — has long-term government contracts (Contracts for Difference) that provide revenue certainty for 15-20 years on operating assets. These CfDs are real margin-of-safety assets: they cannot be easily cancelled, they reprice above market rates if power prices fall, and they sit with creditworthy counterparties (European governments). The existing ~10 GW operational fleet generates reliable, low-volatility EBITDA. The business safety is strong for what is already built and contracted. The danger is in what is being built: 8.1 GW under construction represents enormous execution risk, and construction-phase assets have no CfD protection against cost overruns, supply chain failures, or delays.

**Downside vs Upside Asymmetry**

Realistic downside: If the bear case materializes (US remains hostile, 1-2 major construction projects run significantly over budget, EBITDA misses guidance), the stock could fall to DKK 80-100, representing a 28-42% loss from DKK 139. This is a real, plausible scenario given the track record of impairments since 2023.

Realistic upside: If the bull case materializes (policy normalizes, construction completes on schedule, rates decline), the stock could reach DKK 200-250, representing 44-80% upside over 3-5 years.

The ratio of upside to downside is roughly 1.5-2x — not the 3x minimum Buffett looks for. This is a speculative special-situation thesis, not a high-conviction margin-of-safety investment. The asymmetry is insufficient for a large position.

**What Could Go to Zero (or Near-Zero)?**

A full loss is extremely unlikely given Danish state ownership and the strategic importance of Orsted's European offshore wind infrastructure. However, scenarios for 70-80%+ additional loss exist: (1) The 8.1 GW construction portfolio experiences systemic failures — multiple projects running 30-50% over budget simultaneously, requiring another massive rights issue at an even more dilutive price; (2) Interest rates spike back to 5-6%, making the economics of new offshore wind projects unworkable across Europe, triggering further impairments and a collapse in EBITDA guidance; (3) A major policy reversal in the UK (Orsted's largest market by EBITDA) strips contract protections or fails to award new capacity. None of these are base-case scenarios, but each has happened (partially or analogously) in the last three years. The stock fell 88% from peak — the near-zero scenario is not purely theoretical.

**Key Ways You Could Be Wrong**

1. Construction execution: Orsted's 8.1 GW under construction is the company's future. Any repeat of the Ocean Wind / Revolution Wind debacles would destroy equity value. Probability: Medium. Severity: Extreme. Early warning: quarterly construction update delays, cost guidance revisions, supply chain announcements from turbine suppliers (Siemens Gamesa, Vestas).

2. US political risk: The Trump administration has been actively hostile to offshore wind. If US hostility extends to European markets through trade policy or if key contracts (Sunrise Wind, which Orsted retained fully after the rights issue) are cancelled or penalized, the equity story collapses. Probability: Medium. Severity: High. Early warning: executive orders, federal permitting holds, IRA credit phase-out announcements.

3. European auction failure: Orsted was shut out of the UK's Allocation Round 7, while RWE won nearly 6.9 GW. If Orsted's pipeline of new projects shrinks because it cannot win auctions at acceptable returns, the long-term EBITDA trajectory disappoints. Probability: Medium. Severity: High. Early warning: auction results in Denmark, Germany, Netherlands, and UK over the next 12 months.

4. Interest rate persistence: Orsted's capital-intensive business model depends on project financing rates. If 10-year rates remain above 4-4.5% in Europe, new project economics deteriorate, reducing returns on new investment and depressing the multiple the market will pay. Probability: Medium. Severity: Medium. Early warning: ECB and Bank of England rate decisions.

5. Dilution surprise: The rights issue at DKK 66.60 was a shock. If EBITDA misses 2026 guidance, a further equity raise — even more dilutive given the current DKK 139 price — could crater per-share value again. Probability: Low-Medium. Severity: High. Early warning: any revision downward to 2026 EBITDA guidance or increase in gross investment guidance.

**Concentration Risks**

Orsted is heavily concentrated in: (1) offshore wind as essentially its only product (it is exiting onshore wind and bioenergy to focus the portfolio); (2) European governments as its counterparties for 15-20 year revenue contracts — sovereign risk is low but real; (3) a single technology supply chain (Siemens Gamesa turbines dominate its fleet); (4) political goodwill — the entire business model depends on governments continuing to want offshore wind and willing to price it at levels that generate adequate returns. Any policy reversal in even one or two key markets is highly damaging.

**Tail Risks**

- Accounting: The pattern of impairments (DKK 50B+ over three years) raises questions about optimism bias in project valuations at inception. Orsted's track record of booking projects at high development-stage values and then writing them down when reality hits is a structural red flag.
- Litigation: US project cancellations generated DKK 8-11 billion in estimated contract cancellation liabilities. The resolution of these claims (with offtake partners, turbine suppliers, port authorities) is ongoing and uncertain.
- Geopolitical: Orsted operates in Taiwan (where it has significant offshore capacity). Any deterioration in cross-strait relations could impair assets that cannot be easily sold or relocated.
- Fraud/governance: No evidence of fraud, but the serial impairments suggest project underwriting discipline needs improvement. Management changes at CEO level in 2023-2024 reflect this.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** A recovering policy environment, on-schedule construction delivery, and falling rates compress risk premium, pushing the stock to DKK 200-250 and rewarding patient holders with 45-80% gains.
- **Bear case:** Another round of construction failures or a sustained US policy freeze triggers a third major impairment cycle and potentially a further dilutive capital raise, sending the stock back toward DKK 80-100.
- **Confidence:** Low — the margin of safety is thin at DKK 139, the risk-reward asymmetry is insufficient for a high-conviction position, and the range of outcomes is exceptionally wide.

## Red Flags

- Stock trades above the discounted bear-case intrinsic value — no cushion if the pessimistic scenario unfolds.
- Over DKK 50 billion in impairments in three years reveals a pattern of systematically optimistic project underwriting.
- Rights issue at a 67% discount to market price (October 2025) was a distress signal; the company needed DKK 60 billion in emergency capital.
- Orsted was shut out of the UK's latest offshore wind auction (AR7) — a worrying sign for the pipeline in its most important single market.
- Construction execution: 8.1 GW under construction with a company that has demonstrated it can badly mismanage US projects; European construction is generally better tracked but not immune.
- Debt-to-equity ratio has risen from ~44% to 86% over the past five years, even after the rights issue recapitalization.
- Tail risk from unresolved US cancellation fee litigation (DKK 8-11B estimated exposure).

## Score: 4 / 10

At DKK 139, Orsted offers insufficient margin of safety — the price sits above the bear-case intrinsic value, the risk-reward asymmetry is below 2:1, and a business with a recent track record of DKK 50B+ in impairments demands a deeper discount than the market is currently offering.
