## Detailed Analysis

### Step 1: Owner Earnings Today

Owner earnings = Net income + D&A – maintenance capex. DSV's 2025 reported figures:

- **EBIT before special items:** DKK 19,611m
- **Estimated tax (effective ~23%):** ~DKK 4,500m → NOPAT ~DKK 15,100m
- **D&A (ex-IFRS 16 lease depreciation):** Management guides capex at ~1.5% of revenue (~DKK 3,700m on DKK 247bn revenue). Reported amortisation/depreciation (excl. leases) estimated at ~DKK 2,000–2,500m for FY2025 based on partial-year Q3 data of DKK 1,129m for 9 months.
- **Maintenance capex:** For an asset-light freight forwarder, maintenance capex is low — management guides total capex at ~DKK 5bn/year. Assuming 60% is maintenance (~DKK 3,000m), growth capex ~DKK 2,000m.
- **Owner earnings estimate (2025):** ~DKK 15,100m (NOPAT) + DKK 2,500m (D&A) – DKK 3,000m (maintenance capex) ≈ **DKK 14,600m**
- Cross-check: Adjusted FCF of DKK 16,335m is close (FCF includes working capital benefits); owner earnings of ~DKK 14,500–15,000m is a reasonable anchor.
- **Per share (235m shares):** ~DKK 62 owner earnings/share

### Step 4: Reverse Engineering — What Must Be True for Today's Price?

At DKK 1,669/share × 235m shares = market cap DKK 392bn. EV ~DKK 480bn (adding net debt of ~DKK 88bn).

For today's price to be justified at a 10% required return over 5 years, the equity value must compound to ~DKK 2,700 by 2030. That requires:
- EBIT of ~DKK 35–38bn by 2030 (roughly double 2025's DKK 19.6bn), AND
- A sustained premium multiple of 18–20x EV/EBIT

This is achievable — DSV's management has guided DKK 9bn synergies, freight market is growing, and DSV has a proven acquisition integration track record. However, it requires executing flawlessly on Schenker. The market is pricing in successful integration but not "perfection" — there is modest upside if execution exceeds expectations.

## Signal Summary
- **Bull case:** Full Schenker synergy realisation plus organic growth drives 2030 EBIT to ~DKK 35–40bn, making today's price a clear bargain with 60–80% upside.
- **Bear case:** Freight market normalization, disappointing synergies, or a macro recession compress EBIT, making the stock modestly overvalued (-15 to -25%) at current price.
- **Confidence:** Medium — DSV's business quality and track record are high, but current valuation is not obviously cheap; base case offers reasonable but not exceptional upside (~25–30%) with patience.

---

## Red Flags
- EPS has declined consecutively for four years (DKK 78.8 → 50.9) due to freight rate normalization and dilutive acquisition; organic improvement not yet visible in per-share metrics
- Net debt/EBITDA of ~3x post-Schenker is elevated; deleveraging will consume FCF through 2027, limiting buybacks
- DKK 11bn total integration cost burden creates ongoing earnings drag through 2026
- P/E of ~33x on depressed earnings looks expensive to casual screeners; risk of multiple compression if synergies delayed

---

## Score: 6 / 10
DSV is a high-quality compounder trading near fair value with a path to meaningful upside if Schenker integration delivers as guided — not a screaming buy, but not overpriced; base case offers ~25% upside over 3–5 years.
