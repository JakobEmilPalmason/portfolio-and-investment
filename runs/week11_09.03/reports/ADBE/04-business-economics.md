## Detailed Analysis

**Returns on Capital**

Adobe's ROIC trajectory over the past five years reflects a business compounding at exceptional rates. ROIC was approximately 21.7% in FY2020, expanded to 29.4% in FY2021 (peak Creative Cloud leverage), dipped to 26.9% in FY2022 and 21.7% in FY2023 as the aborted Figma acquisition created uncertainty and the company incurred transaction-related costs, then recovered to 21.2% in FY2024 before surging to approximately 33% in FY2025. The FY2025 expansion reflects both higher net income ($7.13B GAAP, up 28%) and disciplined control of invested capital. Return on equity is even higher given moderate leverage on the balance sheet. By any standard, ROIC consistently above 20% — and now approaching 35% on some measures — indicates a business that earns far more than its cost of capital.

**Margin Structure**

Gross margins have held in the 87–89% band for at least five consecutive years: 88.2% (FY2021), 87.7% (FY2022), 87.9% (FY2023), 89.0% (FY2024), 89.3% (FY2025). This stability reflects Adobe's software-only delivery model where the marginal cost of serving an additional subscriber is negligible. GAAP operating margins have also expanded: from roughly 34% in FY2020 to 36.6% in FY2025, and 37.8% in Q1 FY2026. Non-GAAP operating margin reached ~46% in FY2025 and 47.4% in Q1 FY2026. The spread between GAAP and non-GAAP (~10 percentage points) is primarily stock-based compensation, which is a real cost but reflects the labor market for engineers rather than structural inefficiency. Net margin was approximately 30% GAAP in FY2025 ($7.13B net income / $23.77B revenue).

**Cash Generation**

Free cash flow of $9.85B in FY2025 — up 25% year-over-year — represents a FCF conversion rate well above 100% relative to GAAP net income ($7.13B), driven by meaningful D&A add-back and modest working capital requirements. Operating cash flow was $10.03B. Owner earnings (net income + D&A − maintenance capex) are close to operating cash flow given minimal physical asset base. Capex of $179M was almost entirely growth/infrastructure (data centers, facilities) with a very small maintenance component. This cash generation profile places Adobe in the top tier of all public companies globally.

**Capital Intensity**

Adobe is one of the most asset-light businesses in the S&P 500. At $179M capex on $23.77B revenue (0.75% of revenue), the business requires almost no reinvestment of capital to sustain or grow. The bulk of investment is in human capital (R&D and sales), which flows through the income statement. R&D spending runs at approximately 18–19% of revenue, which is recurring but discretionary in terms of pace. This asset-light model means that free cash flow tracks closely to profit and that returns on capital will remain high unless Adobe dramatically overpays for acquisitions.

**Revenue Quality**

Adobe's revenue quality is among the highest in enterprise software. Total ARR exiting FY2025 was $25.20B (up 11.5% YoY), meaning the entire business is effectively on subscription. Digital Media ARR (Creative Cloud + Document Cloud) reached $19.2B; Digital Experience (Experience Cloud) provides the remainder. Over 75% of net new ARR in Digital Media came from cross-sell, upsell, and subscription activity rather than one-time pricing actions. Revenue visibility is extremely high: the company entered FY2026 with $25.2B in ARR underpinning guidance of $25.9–$26.1B for the full year. Churn is low structurally — Creative Cloud is embedded in professional workflows worldwide, and switching costs are high.

**Operating Leverage**

Adobe's operating leverage is clearly present. In FY2025, revenue grew 11% while GAAP net income grew 28% and free cash flow grew 25%. Q1 FY2026 continues the pattern: revenue +12%, non-GAAP EPS +19%. The company's cost base — primarily people — does not scale proportionally with revenue. This is the hallmark of a mature, high-margin software business with pricing power: incremental revenue falls disproportionately to the bottom line.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** Exceptionally asset-light compounder with 87%+ gross margins, ~41% FCF margin, 33%+ ROIC, and 100% recurring subscription revenue — a near-perfect business economics profile.
- **Bear case:** Revenue growth is slowing toward the low-to-mid teens, and AI-native competitors (e.g., image generation tools) could commoditize some Creative Cloud use cases, pressuring both pricing and net new ARR growth over a 3–5 year horizon.
- **Confidence:** High — Adobe's financials are well-documented, consistent across multiple data sources, and the most recent quarter (Q1 FY2026, reported March 12, 2026) reaffirms the trend.

## Red Flags

- Stock-based compensation is substantial (~$1.8B annually estimated, approximately 8% of revenue), which suppresses GAAP margins and dilutes shareholders at a non-trivial rate.
- Revenue growth rate has decelerated from the ~25% CAGR of 2018–2022 toward 10–12%, reflecting market maturation in Creative Cloud.
- AI disruption risk to core creative tools is real, though Adobe is actively embedding generative AI (Firefly) into its products to retain relevance.

## Score: 9 / 10

Adobe's business economics are near-exceptional: among the highest FCF margins in enterprise software, consistently high and rising ROIC, 87–89% gross margins, near-zero capex intensity, 100% recurring subscription revenue, and demonstrated operating leverage — the only reason this is not a 10 is the modestly decelerating top-line growth and SBC dilution.
