## Detailed Analysis

**Owner Earnings Estimate.** Adobe's FY2025 results give us a clean base: revenue $23.77B, operating cash flow $10.0B, free cash flow $9.85B. FCF already approximates owner earnings well because Adobe is a pure software business with minimal maintenance capex — most capex is growth-oriented (data center, AI infrastructure). Normalizing: net income ~$5.6B (GAAP) + D&A ~$1.0B - maintenance capex ~$0.3B = owner earnings approximately $6.3B. However, GAAP net income understates economic earnings significantly due to stock-based compensation expense (~$2.5B annually), which reduces GAAP income but represents a real but non-cash cost. A cleaner proxy is non-GAAP EPS × diluted shares: $20.94 × ~440M shares ≈ $9.2B. Splitting the difference, owner earnings fall in the $6.5–$9.0B range depending on how aggressively one adjusts for SBC. In Year 3–5, at 8–12% growth, this reaches $8–13B.

**Bear Case (Year 5).** Assumptions: AI disruption materially slows Creative Cloud seat growth; pricing power erodes 5–8%; revenue growth decelerates to 4–6% annually; FCF margins compress from 41% to 30–32% as AI compute costs escalate. Year 5 owner earnings (conservative): ~$7.5B. Apply a distressed-software multiple of 15x (current market is already there). Terminal value: $112B. Discount at 10% for 5 years: ~$70B equity value. Current market cap at $249: ~$109B (assuming ~440M diluted shares). Bear case intrinsic value: approximately $160–$180/share. Downside: 28–36% from current levels.

**Base Case (Year 5).** Assumptions: Adobe successfully integrates Firefly AI, maintains pricing power, grows revenue at 9–11% annually; FCF margins hold at 38–41%. Year 5 owner earnings: ~$12.0B. Apply a fair SaaS multiple of 22x (below 5-year median of 33x, acknowledging structurally lower growth). Terminal value: $264B. Discount at 9%: ~$171B equity value. Intrinsic value per share: approximately $390. Upside: ~57% from $249.

**Bull Case (Year 5).** Assumptions: Firefly accelerates new revenue streams in AI-generated content licensing; Experience Cloud gains enterprise share; new markets (video, 3D) contribute meaningfully; revenue grows 13–15% annually; FCF margins improve to 43–45% as SBC normalizes. Year 5 owner earnings: ~$16B. Apply 28x multiple (slightly below historical median). Terminal value: $448B. Discount at 9%: ~$291B equity value. Intrinsic value per share: ~$662. Upside: ~166%.

**Multiples in Context.** At $249, ADBE trades at: forward P/E ~15x (5-year median ~33x, peers average ~49x); EV/EBITDA ~10.9x (10-year median ~33x, minimum ever ~14x); P/FCF approximately 11x on FY2025 FCF of ~$9.85B ($109B market cap). These are extraordinary discounts for a business generating 41% FCF margins with 90%+ gross margins, $26B ARR, and minimal customer churn. The market is pricing Adobe as a commodity software business in secular decline — that is an extreme assumption that must be explicitly held to justify today's price.

**What Must Be True for $249 to Be Fair Value?** Reverse-engineering: At $249 (~$109B market cap), a 10% discount rate, and a 5-year terminal multiple of 15x, the market implies owner earnings in Year 5 of roughly $6.0–$6.5B — flat to marginally declining from today's $6.3B (GAAP-based). This requires: essentially zero revenue growth or margin compression that fully offsets any top-line progress, permanent loss of AI pricing power, and continued multiple compression below already-historic-low levels. This is an extremely pessimistic set of conditions. It requires Adobe to not merely slow down but to stagnate — despite a business that just grew ARR 10.9% YoY and guided for another year of double-digit revenue growth.

**Is the Market Pricing In Perfection or Pessimism?** Unambiguously pessimism. The current valuation embeds a near-worst-case scenario for AI disruption with no credit for $26B+ ARR, 41% FCF margins, market leadership in professional creative tools, or the early AI monetization showing (AI-first ARR tripled YoY in Q1 FY2026). This is classic fear-driven repricing: strong near-term fundamentals, severe multiple compression driven by narrative uncertainty.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** AI integration accelerates rather than disrupts, Adobe reaches $390–$660/share in 3–5 years representing 57–165% upside.
- **Bear case:** AI disruption erodes pricing power and growth stalls; intrinsic value falls toward $160–$180, implying 28–36% downside.
- **Confidence:** Medium — financials are clear and strong; the uncertainty is entirely about the AI disruption narrative, CEO transition impact, and whether 9–11% growth continues. The multiple compression is so extreme that even a modestly negative outcome may already be priced in.

## Red Flags

- Market consensus has re-rated the stock to trough multiples — the question is whether these are permanent rather than cyclical
- CEO transition with no named successor introduces a 6–18 month execution risk at a pivotal AI strategy moment
- Goldman Sachs Sell rating at $290 implies some sophisticated institutional money sees further downside even from here
- AI-first ARR "tripling" sounds impressive but off a small base; absolute dollar contribution to total ARR not yet disclosed
- Full-year FY2026 guidance ($25.9–$26.1B) slightly disappointed some estimates — growth deceleration narrative reinforced

## Score: 7 / 10

Adobe is trading at less than half its historical valuation median with a strong FCF-generative business still growing double-digits; the bear case is largely priced in, and base/bull scenarios offer meaningful upside — but a genuine bull call requires confidence that AI disruption remains manageable, which is not yet proven.
