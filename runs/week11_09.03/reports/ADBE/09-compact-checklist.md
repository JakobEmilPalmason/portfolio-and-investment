# Compact Checklist — ADBE

**Date:** 2026-03-15

1. **Business**: Adobe sells software subscriptions — primarily Creative Cloud (Photoshop, Premiere, Illustrator) and Experience Cloud — to creative professionals and enterprises who embed these tools so deeply into daily workflows that recurring revenue of $25.2B ARR and 96%+ subscription mix make the cash flow machine nearly automatic.

2. **Moat**: Switching costs are structural and multi-layered: professionals spend years building muscle memory, proprietary file formats (.psd, .ai, .pdf, .indd) create hard dependency on legacy work, and enterprise workflows are rebuilt around Adobe specifications — evidenced by 89% gross margins held stable for over five consecutive years and price increases in 2023 that produced no material subscriber exodus.

3. **Economics**: Adobe generated $9.85B in free cash flow on $23.77B in FY2025 revenue (41% FCF margin), achieved ROIC of ~33%, maintained 87–89% gross margins for five consecutive years, and requires only $179M in annual capex — placing it in the top tier of capital efficiency among all S&P 500 companies.

4. **Management**: Narayen's 18-year capital allocation track record is strong — a $25B buyback executed at well-below-peak valuations reduced share count ~7% in four years — but the abrupt CEO departure announced simultaneously with Q1 FY2026 earnings, with no successor named, is a material stewardship uncertainty at a critical AI strategy pivot.

5. **Debt risk**: Adobe carries net debt of only ~$0.8B against $10B+ in annual operating cash flow, maintains 33x interest coverage, holds $5.4B in cash with no drawn credit lines, and could retire all outstanding debt from approximately seven weeks of cash generation — the balance sheet is effectively impregnable in any plausible recession or industry downturn.

6. **Price**: At ~$249 (~15x forward earnings, 11x P/FCF on $9.85B FY2025 FCF), the market is pricing Adobe as a business with flat-to-declining owner earnings, implying zero benefit from 10.9% ARR growth, AI-first ARR tripling YoY, or any multiple normalization — conditions that require sustained fundamental stagnation to be fair value.

7. **Margin of safety**: The business itself provides the primary cushion: $26B in recurring ARR with high switching costs, 90%+ gross margins, and $10B annual FCF mean competitive erosion would take years to impair cash flows materially, but the price margin of safety is thin with a bear-case DCF of ~$160–$180 only 28–36% below the current ~$249 — this is not a Graham deep-value entry.

8. **Thesis breaker**: Sell if Creative Cloud ARR growth falls below 5% for two consecutive quarters alongside rising disclosed churn, as that combination would confirm that AI-native alternatives are structurally eroding the subscriber base rather than merely creating narrative uncertainty.
