# Compact Checklist — ADBE

**Date:** 2026-03-13

1. **Business**: Adobe sells subscription software (Creative Cloud, Document Cloud, Experience Cloud) to tens of millions of creative professionals and enterprises who keep buying because their files, workflows, and muscle memory are locked into Adobe's proprietary formats — and switching means months of retraining and productivity loss.

2. **Moat**: 98% of Fortune 500 companies standardize on Adobe's creative suite, proprietary file formats (PSD, AI, INDD, PDF) are the common language of creative production, and raising prices 10–18% in 2023–2024 produced no material churn — these are the fingerprints of a structural switching-cost moat, not a product-quality moat.

3. **Economics**: Adobe generates $9.85B in free cash flow on $23.77B in revenue (42% FCF margin), earns 40%+ ROIC, spends less than 1% of revenue on capex, and converts every dollar of incremental ARR at near-100% gross margins — this is one of the highest-quality economic engines in large-cap technology.

4. **Management**: Narayen's team executed the subscription transition flawlessly and returned $11.28B to shareholders in FY2025 alone, but SBC runs at 8.2% of revenue ($1.94B), the Figma attempt would have paid 50x ARR for a competitor, and Narayen announced his departure on March 12, 2026 with no named successor — capital allocation has been directionally right but not owner-quality.

5. **Debt risk**: Net debt is $0.8B against $9.85B in annual free cash flow (net debt/EBITDA ~0.1x), all debt is fixed-rate with no maturities before 2027, interest coverage is 33x, and the company could survive a 30% revenue decline for two consecutive years without issuing equity.

6. **Price**: At ~$247/share (~11.8x trailing FCF), the market is pricing near-zero owner earnings growth for five years in a business with $26.06B in ARR growing at 10%+ — the only way this math works is if AI disruption simultaneously collapses revenue and margins, which is not what Q1 FY2026 results show.

7. **Margin of safety**: The business margin of safety is strong ($10B+ FCF, 96% recurring revenue, minimal debt, deep professional workflow lock-in), but the price margin of safety is thin — the bear-case intrinsic value of ~$226 is only 9% below the current price, so this is a thesis-dependent investment, not a "safe regardless" one.

8. **Thesis breaker**: Sell if Creative Cloud ARR growth falls below 5% for two consecutive quarters (signaling AI disruption has reached the professional tier, not just the prosumer/stock image segment) or if the incoming CEO is an external hire with no enterprise software or AI background who explicitly de-prioritizes Firefly integration.
