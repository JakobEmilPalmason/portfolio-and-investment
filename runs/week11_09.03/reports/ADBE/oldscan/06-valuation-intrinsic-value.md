## Detailed Analysis

**Owner Earnings Estimate**

Adobe's FY2025 financials give a clean baseline. Net income: $7.13B. Depreciation and amortization: approximately $550M. Maintenance capex: approximately $130–150M (total capex was $172M; the remainder is growth-oriented data center and facility spending). Raw owner earnings: approximately $7.53–7.55B. Stock-based compensation of approximately $2.0B annually (estimated at ~8–9% of revenue) is a real economic cost that inflates FCF relative to true owner earnings. Using a 50% SBC haircut (acknowledging that half offsets option dilution through buybacks and half is a net cost), owner earnings adjust to approximately $7.5B conservative / $9.0B generous. For valuation purposes I use $7.5B (conservative) and $8.5B (base case).

Q1 FY2026 results (reported March 12, 2026) confirm the trajectory: operating cash flow was $2.96B for a single quarter, annualizing to ~$11.8B. Owner earnings are growing. Full-year FY2026 guidance implies continued mid-teens cash flow growth.

**Scenario Analysis**

Key assumptions shared across all scenarios:
- Discount rate: 9% (high-quality, low-leverage, subscription software with minimal cyclicality)
- Terminal multiple applied to Year 5 owner earnings: 18x bear / 22x base / 28x bull (all meaningfully below Adobe's 2019–2021 average of 35–50x)
- Shares outstanding: ~447M (flat to slightly declining from buybacks)
- Starting owner earnings: $7.5B bear / $8.5B base / $9.0B bull

---

**Bear Case: Growth Stalls, Margins Compress**

*Assumptions:* Revenue growth decelerates to 5% per year as AI disruption from Figma/Canva/open-source models erodes Creative Cloud ARPU and seat counts in the prosumer segment. Non-GAAP operating margin falls from 45% toward 40% by Year 5 as Adobe is forced to discount or reinvest aggressively to defend share. Owner earnings grow approximately 3% annually (margin headwind partially offsets modest revenue growth).

- Year 5 owner earnings: $7.5B × (1.03)^5 ≈ $8.7B
- Terminal value at 18x: $156.6B
- Discounted to present at 9%: $156.6B / (1.09)^5 ≈ $101.8B EV
- Minus net debt ($0.8B net): ~$101B equity value
- Per share (447M shares): **~$226/share**

**Bear case intrinsic value: $225–230/share.**

---

**Base Case: Reasonable Continuation**

*Assumptions:* Revenue grows 10% per year for three years then 8% in years 4–5, consistent with FY2026 guidance ($25.9–26.1B) and modest deceleration thereafter. Non-GAAP margins stable at ~45%; GAAP owner earnings grow ~11% annually driven by operating leverage and share count reduction from buybacks.

- Year 5 owner earnings: $8.5B × (1.11)^5 ≈ $14.3B
- Terminal value at 22x: $314.6B
- Discounted to present at 9%: $314.6B / (1.09)^5 ≈ $204.4B EV
- Minus net debt ($0.8B net): ~$203.6B equity value
- Per share (447M shares): **~$455/share**

**Base case intrinsic value: $440–460/share.** Approximately 78–86% upside from $247.

---

**Bull Case: AI Monetization Accelerates**

*Assumptions:* Firefly credit monetization and enterprise AI platform sales drive revenue reacceleration to 13–15% in years 2–3. Operating margins expand toward 48% by Year 5 as incremental AI revenue drops at near-100% gross margins. Owner earnings grow ~15% annually.

- Year 5 owner earnings: $9.0B × (1.15)^5 ≈ $18.1B
- Terminal value at 28x: $506.8B
- Discounted to present at 9%: $506.8B / (1.09)^5 ≈ $329.3B EV
- Minus net debt ($0.8B): ~$328.5B equity value
- Per share (447M shares): **~$735/share**

**Bull case intrinsic value: $720–740/share.** Approximately 190–200% upside from $247.

Adobe is trading at roughly one-third of its 5-year average EV/EBITDA. An EV/EBITDA of ~10.9x is below the S&P 500 aggregate — deeply unusual for a business with 40%+ ROIC, 96% recurring revenue, and double-digit growth.

**Reverse Engineering the Current Price**

At $247/share (~$111B market cap, ~$116B EV): to justify a 9% discount rate and terminal multiple of 20x, owner earnings must remain approximately flat at $7.5B for 5 years (0% CAGR). That implies: no benefit from operating leverage, no benefit from $12B+ annual buybacks reducing the share count, no growth in ARR (which is already booked at $26.1B and compounding at ~10%), and a significant margin contraction from current levels. The only way this math works is if AI disruption hits revenue and margins simultaneously and severely — a real risk, but not a base-case outcome given Q1 FY2026 results just posted.

**Is the Market Pricing in Perfection or Disaster?**

The market is pricing near-disaster. Adobe has declined ~64% from its November 2021 all-time high ($699) and is near 5-year lows. The most recent earnings beat expectations — Q1 FY2026 revenue $6.40B (+12% YoY), record operating cash flow of $2.96B, AI-first ARR tripling YoY, guidance reaffirmed — yet the stock fell ~7% after hours on the CEO transition announcement. The market is applying a sentiment discount on top of an already-pessimistic valuation. This is the opposite of pricing in perfection.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** A world-class economic engine (40%+ ROIC, $10B+ FCF, 96% recurring revenue) is trading at utility-like multiples while growing at 12%; a normalization of the multiple alone from 11x to 20x FCF would produce 80%+ returns even with no earnings growth.
- **Bear case:** AI commoditization of creative tools is a genuine threat to ARPU; if Creative Cloud degrades to low-single-digit growth and margins compress, the bear case of ~$226 is close to current prices and the thesis is broken.
- **Confidence:** Medium-High — Q1 FY2026 earnings just confirmed the business is healthy; valuation math is mechanically clear; the uncertainty is the magnitude of AI disruption over a 3–5 year horizon.

## Red Flags

- Bear case intrinsic value (~$226) is only 9% below the current price — there is minimal cushion if growth disappoints materially, so this is not a "safe regardless of the outcome" situation.
- SBC of ~$2B annually reduces true owner earnings meaningfully; the EV/FCF of 11.8x is somewhat flattered by SBC pass-through.
- CEO succession is an unknown variable — the new CEO's strategic direction, risk appetite, and capital allocation philosophy could materially alter the investment thesis.
- ARR growth deceleration (mid-teens → ~11%) is a persistent multi-year trend; if this reaches single digits, multiple compression will likely follow even if absolute earnings grow.
- Semrush acquisition ($1.9B, expected H1 2026) introduces execution risk and represents a strategic pivot toward digital marketing not historically in Adobe's core competence.

## Score: 8 / 10

Adobe is materially undervalued on a base-case DCF (intrinsic value ~$450 vs. price of ~$247), trading at a third of historical average multiples despite record cash generation and reaffirmed guidance — the market is pricing near-zero growth into a business growing at 12%, making this one of the more asymmetric setups in large-cap software today.
