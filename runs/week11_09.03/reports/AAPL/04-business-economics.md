## Detailed Analysis

**Returns on Capital**

Apple's ROIC is consistently and dramatically above average. GuruFocus reports ROIC at 37.14% as of September 2025, with the trailing annualized figure through December 2025 at 56.14%. Over the past three years, ROIC has ranged from approximately 39.8% to 49.6% depending on the measurement period and methodology. The extreme variation in some third-party sources (one citing 217% in 2022) reflects differences in how "invested capital" is calculated when a company has negative book equity due to aggressive share buybacks — a mechanical distortion rather than a real economic change. On any reasonable methodology, Apple's ROIC has been consistently above 30% for the past five years, well above the threshold for exceptional capital allocation (20%+). Return on Equity was 151.9% in FY2025, also skewed by the buyback-shrunken equity base but directionally consistent with an extraordinarily profitable business. No negative trend is visible in the underlying return quality.

**Margin Structure**

Total revenue for FY2025 was $416.2B (+6.4% year-over-year). Gross margin reached a record 46.9%, up from the low-to-mid 40s range of three to four years ago. The key driver is Services: at 75% gross margin versus 39.3% for the Products segment, each incremental dollar of Services revenue is far more valuable than hardware. iPhone remains the dominant revenue segment at roughly 50% of total revenue, but Services crossed $100B annually in FY2025 and now accounts for 26% of revenue — double its 2017 share. Operating income was $133.1B, implying an operating margin of ~32%, exceptional for a company of this scale. Net margin was approximately 26.9% ($112B net income / $416B revenue). Net income grew 19.5% year-over-year, well ahead of revenue growth of 6.4%, demonstrating clear operating leverage.

**Cash Generation**

FY2025 free cash flow was $98.8B on an annual basis (fiscal year definition), down 9.2% from FY2024's $108.8B. However, the trailing twelve-month figure through December 2025 was $123.3B, up 25.5%, suggesting the FY2025 dip was driven by working capital timing or a large one-time payment rather than deteriorating fundamentals. EBITDA for FY2025 was $144.4B. FCF conversion (FCF / Net Income) on the annual basis works out to approximately 88% ($98.8B / $112B), which is above the 80% healthy threshold and in line with Apple's historical pattern of converting nearly all net income to cash. Owner earnings (a Buffett construct: net income + D&A - maintenance capex) can be estimated as $112B + $11.7B D&A - ~$6B estimated maintenance capex = approximately $117–118B, indicating the reported earnings are very close to true economic earnings.

**Capital Intensity**

Total capex was approximately $12.1B in FY2025, essentially flat with FY2024's $12.7B. Against $112B in net income, this is a capex intensity of roughly 11% — extremely low. Apple does not need heavy physical plant or manufacturing assets to run its business; it outsources hardware manufacturing primarily to Foxconn and other contract manufacturers, and its software and services business is inherently asset-light. Depreciation and amortization were $11.7B — almost exactly matching total capex — which implies that growth capex is minimal and the company is essentially maintaining its existing asset base while growing profits through mix shift and pricing power. This is the hallmark of an asset-light, high-return business.

**Revenue Quality**

Revenue quality is excellent and improving. Apple's installed base of active devices exceeded 2.35 billion as of early 2025, providing the platform for Services monetization. Over 1 billion paid subscriptions exist across the ecosystem. Services revenue grew 15% year-over-year in FY2025 to surpass $100B — a business that five years ago was roughly $50B. Hardware revenue (iPhone, Mac, iPad, Wearables) tends to be lumpy and cyclical, tied to upgrade cycles and new model introductions, but is supported by a loyal customer base with high switching costs. The Services revenue is almost entirely recurring — App Store commissions, iCloud subscriptions, Apple Music, Apple TV+, Apple Arcade, Apple Pay fees, and licensing revenue (including a large Google search deal). This structural shift toward recurring, high-margin revenue meaningfully improves overall revenue quality.

**Operating Leverage**

Apple has demonstrated textbook positive operating leverage in FY2025: revenue grew 6.4% while operating income grew meaningfully faster and net income grew 19.5%. As Services scales — a business with near-zero marginal cost per additional subscriber — a growing share of revenue drops through at 75% gross margin rather than 39%. This dynamic will continue as long as the installed base grows and monetization rates increase. The company's fixed cost base (R&D of ~$31B, SG&A of ~$26B) is large in absolute terms but increasingly small relative to revenue, giving Apple room to expand margins further as the Services mix increases.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** Services revenue continues compounding at 12–15% annually, lifting overall margins toward 50%+ gross margin and pushing FCF well above $120B per year within two to three years.
- **Bear case:** A regulatory ruling (EU Digital Markets Act, DOJ antitrust) forces structural changes to the App Store or the Google search deal, sharply cutting Services revenue and exposing the business to hardware cyclicality.
- **Confidence:** High — Apple's financial statements are among the most transparent and well-documented in the world; the numbers cited are from official SEC filings and widely cross-verified sources.

## Red Flags

- The FY2025 free cash flow dipped 9.2% year-over-year (FY basis); while the TTM figure recovered strongly, the cause of the decline (likely tax payments or working capital) should be confirmed in the 10-K cash flow statement.
- App Store antitrust risk is real and ongoing: any forced reduction in the 30% commission rate or mandatory third-party payment processing would compress Services margins. EU proceedings are active.
- iPhone revenue growth was only modest in FY2025; if the Apple Intelligence / AI features do not drive a meaningful upgrade super-cycle, hardware stagnation could limit total revenue growth even as Services expands.
- Revenue concentration: ~50% of revenue is iPhone. A supply chain disruption (Taiwan/China risk) or major product miss has outsized impact.

## Score: 9 / 10

Apple earns a 9 because it has consistently exceptional ROIC (37%+), record gross margins (46.9%) with a structural mix shift improving quality, strong FCF ($99–123B depending on measurement period), nearly zero capital intensity, and a growing recurring-revenue Services business with 75% gross margins — the one point withheld reflects regulatory risk to the App Store economics and the single-product revenue concentration in iPhone.
