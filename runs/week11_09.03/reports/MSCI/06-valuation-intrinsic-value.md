## Detailed Analysis

**Owner Earnings and What They're Worth**

MSCI's owner earnings (net income + D&A - maintenance capex) came in at approximately $1.3B for FY2025. This figure is clean: capex is genuinely low ($130M against $218M D&A), FCF conversion consistently exceeds 100%, and the business generates cash reliably. At the current market cap of $41.2B, you're paying roughly 31.7x owner earnings — or an owner earnings yield of about 3.2%. That's not cheap in isolation, but it must be evaluated against the quality and growth characteristics of the earnings stream.

**Scenario Analysis: Bear / Base / Bull**

*Bear Case (Year 5)*
Assumptions: Global equity markets undergo a sustained correction (20-25% AUM-weighted decline), cutting asset-based fee revenue materially. Revenue growth slows to 4-5% annually. Operating margins compress slightly to 50% due to rising compensation and data costs. Owner earnings in Year 5 = approximately $1.5B. Apply a 20x multiple (appropriate for a slower-growth, still-quality subscription business). Intrinsic value = $30B. Enterprise value minus net debt of $5.7B = equity value ~$24B, or ~$327 per share. That implies ~40% downside from current price.

*Base Case (Year 5)*
Assumptions: Revenue grows at 9% annually (below the 10-11% FY2022-FY2025 trend, reflecting modest slowdown in ETF AUM growth). Operating margins stable at 54-55%. Owner earnings compound at ~10% to approximately $2.1B by Year 5. Apply a 25x multiple (consistent with a high-quality, recurring-revenue financial data business with durable moat). Intrinsic value = $52.5B EV, minus $5.7B net debt = ~$46.8B equity, or ~$637 per share. That implies ~16% upside from current $547.90 — modest but not compelling.

*Bull Case (Year 5)*
Assumptions: AI-enhanced products accelerate subscription growth to 12-13% annually. ESG/climate analytics become mandatory compliance tools across institutional investors. Equity markets appreciate 8% annually, boosting AUM-linked fees. Owner earnings reach approximately $2.6B. Apply a 28x multiple. Intrinsic value = $72.8B EV, minus net debt = ~$67B equity, or ~$912 per share. That implies ~66% upside.

**Multiples in Context**

MSCI currently trades at 35x trailing P/E, 24.9x forward P/E, and 25.3x EV/EBITDA. The trailing P/E of 35x is well below the 2020-2021 peak of 65-73x. However, those peak valuations reflected a near-zero rate environment and speculative enthusiasm for "quality growth" that has since normalized. The more instructive comparison is against MSCI's 2022-2024 range of 39-47x trailing P/E — on that basis, today's ~35x represents the low end of the post-ZIRP range. Peer comparisons are instructive: S&P Global (SPGI) trades at approximately 28-32x forward earnings, FactSet at roughly 26-28x. MSCI commands a premium, which is partly justified by its higher margins (~55% operating) and faster recurring subscription growth, but also reflects elevated expectations.

**What Must Be True for Today's Price to Be Justified?**

At $547.90 per share (market cap $41.2B), the market is implying that MSCI can compound owner earnings at approximately 10-11% for the next decade and deserves a 22-25x exit multiple at that point, discounted back at 9-10%. That set of assumptions is reasonable — but it is not conservative. It requires the subscription business to continue taking share in fixed income, ESG, and Analytics; it requires equity markets to be broadly constructive for AUM-linked fees; and it requires no material multiple compression as rates, competition, or regulatory forces evolve. At current prices, the investor is getting a fair price for a great business — not a great price for a great business.

**Implied Expectations vs. Likely Reality**

The market is not pricing in perfection — it priced in much more optimistic assumptions in 2021. But it is pricing in continuity: continued mid-to-high single digit subscription growth, stable margins, and continued shareholder-friendly capital allocation. The risk is on the downside if AUM markets correct sharply, if the ESG backlash materially reduces that revenue segment, or if AI fundamentally disrupts the proprietary data moat that underpins MSCI's pricing power. All three of these scenarios are plausible but not base case.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** AI-enhanced products and mandatory ESG compliance drive 12%+ revenue CAGR, pushing owner earnings to $2.5B+ by 2030 and justifying $850-900/share.
- **Bear case:** An equity market selloff reduces AUM-linked fees while ESG political backlash stalls that segment, compressing owner earnings growth to 4-5% and warranting $300-330/share.
- **Confidence:** Medium — the business quality is clearly high and well-understood, but the margin of safety at current prices is thin, making the valuation call dependent on growth assumptions that are reasonable but not conservative.

## Red Flags

- Asset-based fees (ABF) represent meaningful revenue exposure to equity market levels — a 25% market decline directly impairs top-line revenue, something a pure subscription business would not face.
- Current P/E of ~35x offers limited protection if EPS growth disappoints even modestly; a miss to 8% growth instead of 12% could reprice the stock to $430-450.
- Analyst consensus is uniformly bullish (16 analysts, no sell ratings) — crowded consensus views can lead to sharp downside when expectations are not met.
- Net debt of $5.7B (3.3x EBITDA) is elevated and limits balance sheet flexibility if operating conditions deteriorate.

## Score: 5 / 10

MSCI is a genuinely exceptional business priced fairly: the current $548 price approximately matches a reasonable base-case intrinsic value, leaving little cushion and requiring continued execution for the investment to generate acceptable returns.
