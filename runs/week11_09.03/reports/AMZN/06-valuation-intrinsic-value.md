## Detailed Analysis

**Owner Earnings Methodology**

Traditional FCF for Amazon is deeply misleading because of the scale of growth capex. Amazon does not disclose a maintenance/growth capex split, so it requires estimation. D&A for FY2025 was approximately $65.8B (cash-flow basis). A reasonable proxy for maintenance capex is the depreciation charge on existing infrastructure — roughly $55–60B (stripping out amortization of acquired intangibles and right-of-use assets). On this basis, owner earnings approximate: Net income $77.7B + D&A $65.8B - maintenance capex ~$50B = **~$93B**, or roughly $8.60 per diluted share. At $210, that is a ~24x owner earnings multiple. For a business growing owner earnings 20–25% annually with Amazon's compounding characteristics, this is fair-to-slightly-rich, not obviously expensive.

**Bear / Base / Bull DCF Scenarios**

All scenarios use a two-stage DCF over 10 years plus terminal value. Discount rate: 9% (reflects Amazon's scale and moat; beta 1.42 but Buffett-style WACC anchors to opportunity cost). Terminal growth: 3%.

*Bear case* — AWS growth slows to 12% by year 5 (Google/Microsoft take meaningful share, AI tailwinds underwhelm); e-commerce margins compress due to antitrust consent decree; $200B capex generates subpar returns; net income CAGR of 10% through 2030. Owner earnings reach ~$140B by 2030. Terminal multiple 18x. DCF fair value: **~$130–145/share**. Margin of safety at today's price: negative (stock ~45% above bear case).

*Base case* — AWS sustains ~18% growth through 2028, moderating to 12% thereafter; e-commerce maintains current margins; AI infrastructure spend generates reasonable returns on deployed capital after 2027; net income CAGR of ~20% through 2030; owner earnings reach ~$175B by 2030. Terminal multiple 20x. DCF fair value: **~$210–230/share**. Stock is roughly fairly valued.

*Bull case* — AWS accelerates to 25%+ growth through 2027 (Bedrock/AI services take enterprise share from Azure/GCP); Kuiper and Alexa monetization add incremental streams; advertising doubles; net income CAGR 28% through 2030; owner earnings reach $240B by 2030. Terminal multiple 22x. DCF fair value: **~$310–360/share**. 50–70% upside from here.

On virtually every metric, AMZN is at or near multi-year lows in absolute terms and relative to peers, reflecting both earnings growth and a multiple de-rating since 2021. This is structurally favorable for patient investors.

**What Must Be True to Justify Today's Price?**

At $210, the market requires: (1) AWS sustains 18%+ revenue growth through at least 2028; (2) the $200B 2026 capex spend generates returns on invested capital above 15% within 5 years; (3) e-commerce operating margins hold at 5–7%; (4) no material antitrust structural remedy before 2030; (5) net income compounds at 18–22% annually for 5+ years. None of these is implausible; collectively they represent a demanding but achievable baseline.

**Perfection vs. Disaster Priced In?**

Current pricing is closer to "competent execution" than perfection. The 2021–2022 era (80–100x EV/EBITDA) priced in perfection. Today's ~16x EV/EBITDA prices in steady compounding without heroics. A true disaster scenario (antitrust breakup + AWS slowdown simultaneously) is not priced in — that scenario yields $80–100/share. But that scenario requires multiple simultaneous failures that are each individually low-probability.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** AWS + AI infrastructure returns exceed expectations, triggering re-rating to 22–25x EV/EBITDA and $310–360 fair value by 2028.
- **Bear case:** Antitrust consent decree plus $200B capex generating below-cost-of-capital returns compresses margins and multiples to $130–145.
- **Confidence:** Medium — The base-case math is sound but depends heavily on unverifiable assumptions about AI capex returns that will not become clear for 2–3 years.

---

## Red Flags

- P/FCF of 293x is technically accurate but reflects a deliberate capex supercycle; if returns on this capex disappoint, it is a serious value trap signal
- $200B 2026 capex implies Amazon will again generate near-zero or negative FCF; negative FCF for 2+ years strains any yield-based valuation
- No maintenance/growth capex disclosure — owner earnings estimates carry ±$20B uncertainty
- EV/EBITDA near lows could reflect genuine value, or it could reflect the market's doubt that current EBITDA margins are sustainable post-capex normalization
- AWS faces meaningful competition from Azure and Google Cloud, particularly for AI-native workloads; any share-loss acceleration would compress growth and multiples simultaneously

---

## Score: 6 / 10

Amazon is fairly valued at current prices in the base case, with meaningful upside in the bull case and significant downside if the AI capex cycle disappoints — the asymmetry is roughly balanced, earning a neutral-to-slight-positive score that would improve materially on a 15–20% price decline.
