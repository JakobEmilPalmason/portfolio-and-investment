## Detailed Analysis

**Owner Earnings Calculation**

For TTM through September 2025: reported net income was approximately $18.4B. Depreciation and amortization was approximately $4–4.5B (based on $3.9B for the twelve months ending June 2024, rising with acquisitions and capacity build). Maintenance capex is harder to isolate — Lilly guided total capex of approximately $10–12B for 2025, but the vast majority is growth/manufacturing expansion capex to serve the GLP-1 ramp, not maintenance. A reasonable estimate of maintenance capex is $2–3B (approximately the 2022–2023 capex run rate before the GLP-1 surge). Owner earnings estimate: $18.4B + $4.2B D&A − $2.5B maintenance capex ≈ **$20B TTM owner earnings**. Per diluted share (approximately 900M shares): roughly **$22/share** in owner earnings, closely approximating the reported adjusted EPS of ~$24.21 for full 2025.

At today's price of ~$1,001, the trailing owner earnings yield is approximately **2.2%**, which is very low. However, given the 40%+ growth trajectory, the forward owner earnings picture changes rapidly. At $34 midpoint 2026 guidance EPS with similar D&A and maintenance capex dynamics, 2026 owner earnings per share are likely in the $35–38 range, giving a **2026 owner earnings yield of ~3.5%** — still modest but improving quickly.

**Scenario Analysis — Five-Year Intrinsic Value**

For all scenarios, I use a 10% discount rate and apply a terminal P/E multiple appropriate to the business's maturity at Year 5 (2031).

*Bear Case:* GLP-1 pricing pressure accelerates (IRA, Trump Rx policy), competition from Novo Nordisk orals/Rybelsus, and a pipeline disappointment (orforglipron obesity indication rejected or delayed). Revenue growth decelerates to +10% in 2026, then +8%, +6%, +5%, +4% through 2030. Operating margins compress from ~40% to ~32% by 2031 as pricing headwinds bite and R&D spending rises. 2030 adjusted EPS: approximately $40. Terminal P/E at 18x (mature large pharma) = **$720 intrinsic value**. Discounted back at 10% for 5 years: approximately **$447/share**. This implies ~55% downside from current price.

*Base Case:* Revenue follows company guidance of $81.5B in 2026 (+25%), then growth moderates to +18%, +15%, +12%, +10% through 2030. Margins stable at ~40–42%. 2030 adjusted EPS: approximately $70–75. Terminal P/E of 22x (premium pharma with strong pipeline) = **$1,540–1,650**. Discounted at 10% for 5 years: approximately **$960–1,025/share**. This suggests the stock is roughly **fairly valued** in the base case today.

*Bull Case:* Orforglipron obesity approved mid-2026, becomes blockbuster adding $10B+ annually by 2029. Retatrutide advances to approval. Medicare Part D obesity coverage expansion boosts volumes. Revenue grows +30% in 2026, +25%, +20%, +18%, +15% through 2030. Margins expand to 44–46% as mix improves. 2030 adjusted EPS: approximately $110–120. Terminal P/E of 25x (dominant franchise, long runway) = **$2,750–3,000**. Discounted at 10% for 5 years: approximately **$1,710–1,865/share**. This represents **70–85% upside** from current price.

**Multiples in Context**

The trailing P/E of approximately 43x (reported) and forward P/E of ~29–31x (on 2026 guidance EPS) represent a substantial premium to large-cap pharma peers. Johnson & Johnson trades at ~16x forward, Abbvie at ~14x, Pfizer at ~12x, Merck at ~13x. Novo Nordisk trades at ~22x forward as a nearer-term comp. LLY's premium is partially justified by superior growth — but it implies the market is pricing in continued execution on an exceptional trajectory. The P/FCF ratio based on reported FCF (~$6.4B TTM) is extraordinarily high at ~150x reported FCF; however, this is distorted by the capex investment cycle. On a normalized FCF basis (removing growth capex), the ratio is more like 45–55x — still rich but more defensible given the growth rate.

What does the multiple imply about growth? At 29x forward earnings, using a Gordon Growth / PEG framework: a 29x P/E requires roughly 25–30% long-term EPS growth to be reasonable by standard PEG analysis (PEG = 1.0). Lilly is guiding to ~40% EPS growth in 2026 alone, so near-term the PEG is actually below 1.0 — appearing cheap by that measure. The question is what happens beyond 2026. If growth decelerates to 10–15% by 2028–2029 (plausible as the GLP-1 market matures), the multiple looks more stretched in retrospect.

**Reverse Engineering: What Must Be True?**

At $1,001/share and ~900M diluted shares, the market cap is approximately $900B. At a 10% required return, the market is pricing in net present value of all future owner earnings equaling $900B. Working backwards: if the terminal value (discounted) represents 70% of that ($630B), then year 5 owner earnings at a 20x multiple must be ~$31.5B — implying ~$35+ per share in owner earnings by 2030. That is approximately 2–2.5x today's owner earnings base of ~$20B, requiring roughly 15% annual owner earnings compounding for 5 years. Given the guidance trajectory, this seems *achievable in the base/bull case* — but the margin for error is thin. The bear case scenario fails this test significantly.

**Pricing Perfection vs. Disaster**

The market is not pricing in perfection — it is pricing in *continued strong execution* on an exceptional growth trajectory. The stock has already corrected roughly 11% from its November 2025 peak of ~$1,108, suggesting some multiple compression has occurred as investors assess GLP-1 pricing risks. Analyst consensus of ~$1,166–1,255 suggests modest 17–25% upside is priced in, consistent with a base-case scenario but not a bull case. The stock is not at a "disaster" pricing either — the implied bear case downside (~55%) is substantial, indicating meaningful risk if the GLP-1 thesis breaks.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** Orforglipron obesity approval, Medicare coverage expansion, and retatrutide pipeline progress extend Lilly's GLP-1 dominance, driving $100+ EPS by 2030 and stock price of $1,700+.
- **Bear case:** IRA pricing pressure, faster-than-expected competition from oral GLP-1s, and a pipeline setback drive margin compression and multiple contraction to $450–700.
- **Confidence:** Medium — current financial data is well-supported by Q4 2025 earnings; scenario ranges are wide because the intrinsic value is highly sensitive to GLP-1 trajectory assumptions that are genuinely uncertain.

## Red Flags

- Trailing P/FCF of ~150x (reported) reflects massive capex cycle; normalized FCF must be trusted to be much higher, but this requires execution on manufacturing ramp.
- EV/EBITDA of ~35x is 2–3x the large-cap pharma peer average, leaving no room for thesis deviation.
- The bear case intrinsic value (~$447) implies ~55% downside, which is uncommon for a company of this quality but real given concentration risk.
- LLY's own history shows the stock traded at $600–700 as recently as early 2024 — a reminder of how quickly premium multiples can deflate.

## Score: 4 / 10

At ~$1,001, LLY is modestly to materially overvalued in the bear case and only fairly valued in the base case; the bull case offers meaningful upside but requires near-perfect GLP-1 execution, yielding a score that reflects limited margin of safety and a fair-to-rich entry point.
