# Compact Checklist — CSU.TO

**Date:** 2026-03-11

1. **Business**: Constellation Software sells mission-critical niche vertical market software (utility billing, school management, golf tee-times, and 100+ other niches) to ~100,000 end customers who almost never switch, generating 75% recurring revenue and $1.68B in free cash flow in FY2025 — then redeploys that cash to buy more of the same businesses, indefinitely.
2. **Moat**: Customers are structurally locked in by decades of embedded workflows, proprietary data, staff training, and regulatory context — not contracts — making the cost of switching higher than the cost of staying, and no competitor can replicate 1,000+ independent niche software businesses simultaneously.
3. **Economics**: FCFA2S has compounded at roughly 16–18% annually for a decade, reaching $1.68B on $11.6B revenue in FY2025 with capex under 0.2% of revenue, confirming a genuinely asset-light, high-incremental-return model where capital goes in via acquisitions and comes out as cash.
4. **Management**: Executives are required to buy CSU shares with 75% of their cash bonuses (directors 100%), founder Mark Leonard held ~$430M in common shares personally, and IRR hurdle rates of 20–30% were held through the zero-rate era — though Mark Leonard's September 2025 health resignation puts the capital allocation culture under its first real succession test with Mark Miller.
5. **Debt risk**: Net debt of ~$2.2B against annual FCF of $1.68B (1.3x net leverage) with 8.4x interest coverage, $2.8B cash, and a $1.1B fully undrawn credit line means the company would survive a severe revenue downturn on the strength of its 75% recurring revenue base alone, with the only near-term flag being the revolving credit facility due for renewal in November 2026.
6. **Price**: At CAD 2,939 (forward P/FCFA2S of ~26x), the current price requires only ~10–11% annual FCFA2S growth for 10 years — well below the 16–18% historical rate — meaning the market is already pricing in meaningful deceleration, not continuation.
7. **Margin of safety**: The ~23–35% discount to base-case fair value (Alpha Spread: CAD 3,616; analyst consensus: CAD 4,200+) is real but not extreme, and the deeper structural protection comes from the business itself — 75% recurring revenue from thousands of mission-critical VMS customers with near-zero historical churn means permanent capital impairment requires AI to simultaneously destroy most of the VMS market and new leadership to misallocate capital, a compound probability that is very low.
8. **Thesis breaker**: Sell if organic maintenance and recurring revenue turns negative on a constant-currency basis for two consecutive quarters — that would be direct evidence that AI disruption is structural and the switching-cost moat is genuinely eroding, not just feared.
