## Detailed Analysis

**Returns on Capital.** Traditional ROIC calculations for Constellation Software produce figures in the 11–12% range (GuruFocus: 11.31%, AlphaSpread: ~12.1% TTM). These figures are suppressed by the large goodwill balance on the balance sheet, which accumulates with every acquisition. The more meaningful metric is CSI's own "free cash flow available to shareholders per share" (FCFA2S/share) compounded over time, which has grown at a ~25% CAGR over the past decade. The business earns very high incremental returns on each new acquisition (management targets 15%+ hurdle rates), even though accounting ROIC appears modest. ROE has been volatile: high historically (mean ~38% over 10 years, peak ~20.6% in Dec 2018), dipping negative briefly in early 2021 due to mark-to-market losses on investments, and recovering since. The underlying economic engine — buying vertical market software businesses at reasonable multiples and compounding their cash flows — consistently generates well above its cost of capital.

**Margin Structure.** Gross margins are in the high-30s to low-40s percent range (approximately 37–42% depending on the period and methodology). Operating margins have trended modestly downward from the ~17% range in 2020 to ~14–16% more recently. This compression is deliberate: CSI has been acquiring larger and more complex businesses (including Topicus and Lumine Group spinoffs), some of which carry lower initial margins but offer long-term improvement potential. Net profit margin was approximately 7.3% in FY2024 (up from 6.7% in FY2023) and declined to approximately 4.4% in FY2025 ($512M / $11,623M) due to higher acquisition-related costs and non-cash charges in a heavy deal year. Margin compression at the net income level should be viewed with caution given the company's non-IFRS cash-flow-focused reporting philosophy.

**Cash Generation.** Free cash flow is the primary financial metric CSI uses and communicates. FCFA2S grew from $1,160M (FY2023) to $1,472M (FY2024, +27%) to $1,683M (FY2025, +14%). Operating cash flow for FY2024 was $2,196M on $10,066M revenue, yielding an operating cash flow margin of approximately 21.8%. FCF margin on revenue was approximately 21% in FY2024 and 14–15% in FY2025 (FCFA2S basis). The distinction matters: FCFA2S deducts acquisition spending for tuck-in deals, which CSI treats as quasi-maintenance. Owner earnings (net income + D&A – maintenance capex) are considerably higher than reported GAAP net income given large D&A from acquired intangibles and minimal true maintenance capex. FCF conversion from operating cash flow is strong and consistent.

**Capital Intensity.** This is one of CSI's greatest strengths. Quarterly capex of approximately $16M against quarterly revenue of ~$2.9B in FY2024 represents a capex intensity of under 0.2% of revenue. The business requires almost no physical capital — it buys software businesses, integrates them, and runs them with minimal reinvestment in fixed assets. The real capital deployment is M&A: $1.58B in total acquisition consideration in FY2025. This is productive deployment of capital rather than maintenance spending.

**Revenue Quality and Operating Leverage.** Revenue quality is exceptionally high. Approximately 73–74% is maintenance and other recurring fees — software support and subscription revenue from highly sticky vertical market software customers. These are mission-critical niche applications (e.g., transit management, utility billing, healthcare administration) with very high switching costs. Churn is structurally low. Professional services (~25%) are less recurring but tied to the installed base. Organic growth is modest (2–4% FX-adjusted), reflecting mature niches; this is a known and accepted feature, not a concern. Operating leverage is modest given the acquisitive growth model — acquired businesses come with their own cost structures — but the existing portfolio shows consistent margin maintenance over time.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** A compounding machine with unmatched acquisition volume and discipline, growing FCFA2S at double-digit rates for two decades, with a near-irreplaceable pool of niche vertical software businesses and an asset-light model that converts >20% of revenue to operating cash.
- **Bear case:** Organic growth is structurally weak (2–4%), requiring continuous M&A to sustain growth; as deal sizes increase, it becomes harder to maintain high hurdle rates, and FY2025's net income decline (−30%) from heavy deal activity illustrates how GAAP earnings can be misleading or volatile.
- **Confidence:** High — CSI's business model, revenue composition, and FCF trajectory are well-documented across multiple sources including company filings and third-party aggregators.

## Red Flags

- FY2025 net income fell 30% YoY to $512M despite revenue growth of 15%, reflecting elevated acquisition charges and non-cash items. This warrants monitoring but is not unusual in heavy deal years.
- Traditional ROIC of 11–12% is below the 15%+ threshold often cited as a quality benchmark — though this reflects accounting methodology, not economic reality.
- Organic growth of 2–4% is structurally low; if M&A deal flow dries up or capital markets tighten, growth could stall meaningfully.

## Score: 8 / 10

Constellation Software operates one of the most capital-efficient, recurring-revenue software compounding models in public markets, with proven FCF growth and minimal capex; the score is held back slightly by accounting ROIC that appears modest, declining net margins in FY2025, and near-complete dependence on M&A for growth.
