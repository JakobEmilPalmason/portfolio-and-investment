## Detailed Analysis

**Owner Earnings and Normalization.** Moody's reported 2025 adjusted EPS of $14.94 (up 20% YoY) and FCF of approximately $2.3–2.5B on revenue of $7.72B. However, 2025 benefited from a constructive debt issuance environment. MIS revenue (the ratings segment) grew 9% in 2025 and carries ~60% operating margins at peak. A normalized mid-cycle MIS revenue assumption — roughly 10–15% below the 2025 run rate — would reduce group adjusted EPS to approximately $12.50–13.50, implying the stock trades at ~35–37x normalized earnings at $466. That range sits near the 10-year median P/E of ~33x but modestly above it when cycle-adjusted.

**DCF Scenarios.** The three scenarios below use a 10% discount rate, 3% terminal growth, and normalized (not peak) FCF:

- **Bear case** ($310–340): Revenue growth of 4–5% over 5 years, MIS margin compression to 55%, MA margins flat at 30%. Terminal FCF ~$2.0B. Implied IV ~$310–340.
- **Base case** ($450–490): Revenue growth of 7–8% (consistent with management 2026 guidance), MIS margins ~60%, MA margins expanding to 33%. Terminal FCF ~$3.0B. Implied IV ~$450–490.
- **Bull case** ($570–620): Revenue growth of 10%+, sustained MIS margins ~62%, MA reaching 35%+ margins, driven by AI-powered analytics growth. Terminal FCF ~$3.6B. Implied IV ~$570–620.

At ~$466, the stock sits squarely in the base-case range. There is meaningful upside to the bull case but meaningful downside to the bear case.

**What Must Be True for Today's Price.** At $466 and a ~28x forward P/E on $16.70 guidance, the market is pricing in: (1) high-single-digit revenue growth persisting for 5+ years; (2) adjusted operating margins holding at 49–51%; (3) no structural degradation of the NRSRO franchise; (4) continued FCF conversion of ~30–32% of revenue. None of these assumptions is extreme, but none provides a margin of safety either. This is a "fair price for a great business" setup, not a "great price for a great business."

**Multiples vs History and Peers.** MCO's current trailing P/E of ~31–33x is below its 5-year average of 37x and its 3-year average of 42x, suggesting some de-rating has occurred from the 2021–22 peak. SPGI trades at ~29x trailing — a narrower-than-usual discount to MCO — reflecting SPGI's more diversified revenue (Market Intelligence, Indices) versus MCO's heavier MIS weighting. EV/EBITDA of ~24x and EV/FCF of ~35x are consistent with a high-quality compounder at a moderate premium to the market.

**Perfection or Disaster?** The current valuation does not price in perfection, but it does price in continued execution. A return to 2022-era issuance drought or a regulatory shock to the NRSRO regime would likely compress the multiple toward 22–25x, implying 25–35% downside. Conversely, a re-rating back toward the 5-year average multiple plus earnings growth could support 30–40% upside over 3–4 years.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** Re-rating toward historical 37x P/E plus 10%+ EPS growth drives $600+ price within 3 years.
- **Bear case:** Issuance cycle turns down + regulatory pressure compresses multiple to 22–25x, implying $300–370 trough.
- **Confidence:** Medium — base-case valuation is reasonably well-supported by data, but normalized earnings estimates carry meaningful uncertainty given MIS cyclicality.

## Red Flags

- Current FCF yield of ~2.8–3.0% is thin; investors are paying heavily for future growth
- Normalized earnings (cycle-adjusted) put the multiple closer to 35–37x — near the upper bound of historical norms
- 2026 guidance of $16.70 adjusted EPS midpoint already assumes a constructive issuance environment; a credit downturn would miss this materially
- EV/FCF of ~35x leaves very limited downside protection

## Score: 5 / 10

MCO is a high-quality business trading at a fair but not discounted price — near base-case intrinsic value with limited margin of safety at current levels.
