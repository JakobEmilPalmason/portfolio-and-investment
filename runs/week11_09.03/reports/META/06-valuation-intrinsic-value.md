## Detailed Analysis

### 1. Owner Earnings Today

Owner earnings = Net income + D&A – maintenance capex. For FY2025:
- Net income: $60.5B
- D&A: approximately $15B (estimated from infrastructure asset base; growing rapidly with capex surge)
- Total capex: $69.7B — but approximately $30–35B is growth/AI infrastructure investment, not maintenance. Maintenance capex is harder to isolate but can reasonably be estimated at $25–30B for the core Family of Apps business to sustain its current earnings power.
- Conservative maintenance capex estimate: $28B
- Owner earnings (FY2025): $60.5B + $15B – $28B ≈ **$47.5B**

Owner earnings per diluted share (approximately 2.57B diluted shares): ~$18.50/share. At the current price of $647, the owner earnings yield is roughly 2.9%, which is unimpressive in isolation but must be assessed against growth.

Owner earnings in 3–5 years: If META's AI infrastructure investment generates returns and the business sustains 15–20% annual Family of Apps growth while capex intensity normalizes, owner earnings could reach $70–90B by 2028–2029. This is the central question — the capex surge is either a return-generating asset-build or value destruction.

### 2. Scenario Analysis (Year 5 = FY2030)

**Key shared assumptions:**
- Shares outstanding: ~2.55B (modest buyback offsetting dilution)
- Discount rate: 10% (WACC approximation for a high-quality large-cap)
- Terminal multiple applied to Year 5 owner earnings, then PV discounted at 10% for 5 years (factor: 0.621)

- Revenue growth: 8% CAGR → FY2030 revenue ~$295B

- FY2030 net income: ~$55B

- This is catastrophic — implies ~75% downside. Only materializes if capex fails to generate returns AND growth stalls simultaneously.

**Base Case — Capex Generates Reasonable Returns, Steady Growth:**

- Note: This implies current price is modestly *above* base-case intrinsic value at today's price of $647.

### 4. What Must Be True at $647?

Reverse-engineering the current price:
- Sustained 15–18% revenue CAGR through 2029
- Operating margins holding at 38–42% despite rising depreciation
- AI infrastructure investments generating measurable revenue contribution by 2027–2028
- Reality Labs losses plateau or shrink (currently $19B/year and rising)
- No material regulatory break-up or revenue constraint from EU DMA, FTC appeal, or US legislation
- Capex normalizes from $115–135B in 2026 back toward $60–70B by 2028
- Share count stays flat or declines modestly through buybacks

These are achievable but not guaranteed. The current price prices in a successful execution of one of the most ambitious technology infrastructure programs in corporate history.

### 5. Perfection or Pessimism?

The market is pricing in a **competent execution case** — not outright perfection, but not much room for error. The stock is down ~18% from its August 2025 all-time high of $788.82, reflecting investor discomfort with the capex ramp. At $647, the market is giving META some benefit of the doubt on AI ROI materializing but is demanding it happens on schedule. The forward P/E of ~21x for a business with 15–22% expected EPS growth is not extreme, but the compressed FCF yield means investors are financing this capital program with patience rather than cash returns.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** AI infrastructure pays off at scale, advertising revenues surpass $300B by 2028, and owner earnings reach $100B+ as capex normalizes — implying 25–35% upside from current price.
- **Bear case:** $115–135B in capex generates poor returns, Reality Labs remains a $20B/year cash drain, regulators impose structural remedies, and compressed FCF persists — implying 30–50% downside.
- **Confidence:** Medium — The business quality and earnings power are clear, but the capex cycle introduces genuine uncertainty about whether capital allocation is value-creating or destroying. Conviction on earnings trajectory is moderate; conviction on FCF trajectory is low for the next 12–24 months.

## Red Flags

- 2026 capex guidance of $115–135B is staggering — if ROI does not materialize by 2027-2028, value destruction is material and irreversible
- Reality Labs has lost $73B cumulatively with no path to profitability disclosed; $19.2B loss in 2025 alone
- FCF declined 14.7% in FY2025 despite 22% revenue growth — a warning sign that capital intensity is outpacing earnings growth
- FY2025 EPS ($23.49) was actually 1.6% lower than FY2024, reflecting the capex compression effect
- The P/FCF ratio of ~36x (trailing) leaves little room for capex disappointment

## Score: 5 / 10

META is not clearly undervalued at $647 — base-case DCF suggests intrinsic value in the $430–560 range today, with the bull case required to justify the current price; the stock is priced for a successful AI capex cycle, making this fairly valued to modestly overvalued absent a strong conviction call on that outcome.
