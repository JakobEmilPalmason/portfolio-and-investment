## Detailed Analysis

**Owner Earnings Today**

Owner earnings — the real cash a business produces for its owners — are best approximated as: GAAP net income + depreciation/amortization − maintenance capex. ServiceNow reported FY2025 GAAP net income of $1.748 billion. D&A was approximately $564 million as of late 2024 (the 2025 full-year figure is not yet confirmed in available sources, but growth in the asset base suggests it may be modestly higher — we will use $600 million as a conservative estimate). ServiceNow defines its own free cash flow as operating cash flow minus purchases of property and equipment; that came to $4.576 billion in FY2025, which implicitly captures the maintenance vs. growth capex split in a different way. The gap between GAAP net income ($1.75B) and FCF ($4.58B) is largely explained by $2.4 billion in stock-based compensation (a real cost to shareholders even if non-cash) and working-through of deferred revenue. If we treat the $4.576 billion FCF as the cleanest measure of owner earnings — which is reasonable for a subscription software business with low physical capital needs — then the business is already generating substantial real cash. A reasonable Year 5 (FY2030) owner earnings range, based on guidance trajectory, is $7–$12 billion.

**Scenario Analysis — Year 5 (FY2030)**

For each scenario we estimate FY2030 FCF, apply an exit FCF multiple, and discount back at 10% annually (roughly a hurdle rate for a high-quality growth business).

*Bear Case:* Revenue growth decelerates to 10% per year (AI disruption erodes seat-based licensing, Microsoft competitive pressure intensifies, macro softens). Revenue reaches ~$21 billion by FY2030. FCF margin compresses to 28% as AI infrastructure costs rise. FCF = ~$5.9 billion. At a 25x FCF exit multiple (peer-level), equity value = ~$147 billion. Discounted back 5 years at 10% = ~$91 billion. At approximately 204 million shares outstanding (post-split), intrinsic value per share ≈ $446. Wait — this implies even the bear case is above current price. That is not a typo; it reflects how severe the market's current repricing has been. However, note this bear case still assumes real growth; a true disaster scenario (revenue stagnation or decline) could be much worse.

*Base Case:* Revenue grows at ~18% through FY2028, slowing to 15% thereafter. Revenue reaches ~$26 billion by FY2030. FCF margin stable at 35–36%. FCF = ~$9.1 billion. Exit multiple of 30x FCF (reflecting maturation). Equity value = ~$273 billion. Discounted at 10% for 5 years = ~$170 billion. Per share ≈ $833.

*Bull Case:* AI monetization accelerates, Moveworks integration drives new growth vectors, margin expansion to 40% FCF. Revenue reaches ~$30 billion. FCF = ~$12 billion. Exit multiple of 35x. Equity = ~$420 billion. Discounted = ~$261 billion. Per share ≈ $1,280.

Note on share count: ServiceNow completed a 5-for-1 stock split in December 2025. The current approximate diluted share count post-split is roughly 204–206 million shares. All per-share figures above use ~204 million.

**Multiples in Context**

At ~$121/share and ~$120B market cap, the stock trades at roughly 26x trailing FCF ($4.58B), about 9x EV/Revenue (trailing), and has a GAAP P/E of approximately 69x (market cap / $1.748B net income). These are not cheap multiples in absolute terms. However, context matters: NOW's own historical range has been 60–120x earnings and 50–80x FCF. At 26x FCF, the stock is at its lowest FCF multiple since the company was much smaller. Peers Salesforce and Workday trade at 11–13x EV/EBITDA and 13–16x FCF, reflecting slower growth (Salesforce ~9%, Workday ~14% revenue growth). ServiceNow is growing at 20%+ with better margins and a stronger competitive position — some premium is justified, but how much is the core question.

**What Must Be True for Today's Price to Be Justified**

At $121 and a $120B market cap, you are paying roughly 26x today's FCF. For that to be a fair price and not an overpayment, you need to believe: (1) FCF grows at least 10–12% annually for 5–7 years, AND (2) the exit multiple doesn't compress below 20x. Both assumptions are individually easy to believe; together, they simply require the business to be roughly what it already is today. The bull case requires no heroic assumptions — just continuation of current trends. This is a significant shift from 18 months ago when valuation required everything to go right. The market has handed buyers a significant margin of safety by pricing in maximum fear.

**Implied Expectations vs. Likely Reality**

The market, by pricing NOW at $121, is implying one of: (a) revenue growth decelerates sharply to single digits within 2–3 years; (b) AI genuinely disrupts the workflow automation category and NOW loses share; or (c) margins collapse due to heavy AI infrastructure spending. Meanwhile, the actual business in FY2025 grew revenue 21%, expanded FCF margins to 35%, and guided FY2026 at 20% growth with further margin expansion. The gap between what the market is implying and what the business is doing is unusually wide for a company of this quality.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** ServiceNow compounds FCF at 20%+ for three more years before settling into a 15% growth pattern, and the stock re-rates toward 35x FCF as AI disruption fears dissipate, yielding 5-year total returns of 5x+.
- **Bear case:** AI disruption proves real, Microsoft Agent 365 and open-source alternatives erode ServiceNow's workflow moat, growth falls to ~8%, and the stock drifts to $80–$90 range as FCF multiples compress to peer levels.
- **Confidence:** Medium — The business fundamentals are clearly strong and the valuation is genuinely more attractive than it has been in years, but the AI disruption narrative is real and not fully dismissible, which introduces meaningful uncertainty about the terminal multiple.

## Red Flags

- The stock fell ~50% from its all-time high to current levels despite consistently beating earnings — this kind of multiple compression typically reflects a structural re-rating, not just sentiment, and warrants respect.
- Stock-based compensation remains very high (~$2.4B annually against $1.75B GAAP net income), meaning GAAP earnings dramatically understate real shareholder dilution; the FCF picture is much better but SBC dilutes it.
- Microsoft's AI agent strategy (Agent 365) directly targets enterprise workflow automation — if Microsoft bundles AI workflow tools into its existing enterprise agreements, ServiceNow's pricing power could erode faster than expected.
- GAAP P/E of ~69x means any slowdown in non-GAAP earnings or FCF growth could trigger further multiple compression even if the business remains healthy.
- FY2026 guidance includes ~100bps contribution from the Moveworks acquisition — ex-acquisition, organic growth is slightly slower, and integration risk is real.

## Score: 5 / 10

ServiceNow is a high-quality business at a meaningfully compressed valuation versus its own history, but it still requires above-average growth and multiple assumptions to go right — and the AI disruption risk is non-trivial — so fair value rather than "cheap" is the most defensible characterization at current prices.
