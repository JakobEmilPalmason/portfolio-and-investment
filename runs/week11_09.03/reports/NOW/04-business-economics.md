## Detailed Analysis

**Returns on Capital**

ServiceNow's GAAP-based ROIC, as reported by GuruFocus for the period ending September 2025, was approximately 7-8%, which sits below the company's estimated WACC of ~10-11%. This figure is misleading for Buffett-style analysis for two reasons. First, the goodwill and intangible asset base has expanded rapidly through acquisitions (including the December 2025 Moveworks acquisition), inflating the denominator. Second, the company expenses billions in SBC annually that represents genuine economic dilution but is not a cash outflow in the traditional sense; the non-GAAP operating margin of 31% versus the GAAP operating margin of ~16% illustrates the magnitude. A more relevant proxy for underlying business return quality is the non-GAAP free cash flow return: $4.576B FCF on a total revenue base of $13.28B is a 34% FCF margin on a business that requires minimal fixed capital to grow. For reference, one data source (StockAnalysis statistics page) cited ROIC at 17.8% on a trailing basis using a methodology that adjusts for operating leases — this is directionally more consistent with the business quality observed through cash flow metrics. Trend direction on economic returns is improving.

**Margin Structure**

The margin profile is exceptional by software industry standards. Subscription gross margin held at approximately 82.7% in 2025 (contracted ~160 bps YoY due to elevated AI infrastructure and compute costs). Professional services gross margin ran at a deliberate slight loss (approximately -5% in 2025, vs. -2% in 2024) as ServiceNow intentionally uses professional services to accelerate customer onboarding and ecosystem partner investment rather than profit-optimize this line. GAAP total gross margin was approximately 77-79% on a blended basis. GAAP operating margin was approximately 15-16% in 2025, with the gap to the 31% non-GAAP figure accounted for almost entirely by SBC. The direction of margin expansion is clear: non-GAAP operating margin improved from approximately 27-28% in 2022-2023 toward 31% in 2025, demonstrating operating leverage as revenue scales. Guidance for 2026 calls for a further 100 bps improvement to 32% non-GAAP operating margin.

**Cash Generation**

Free cash flow is the most impressive metric in ServiceNow's P&L. The FCF trajectory over the past four years is:
- FY2022: ~$2.17B FCF (estimated; MacroTrends reports ~$2.70B for this period — the data varies slightly by source)
- FY2023: ~$2.70B FCF
- FY2024: ~$3.42B FCF
- FY2025: $4.576B FCF (+34% YoY)

The FY2025 FCF margin of approximately 34-35% is outstanding for an enterprise software company growing at 20%+. Operating cash flow was $5.444B in 2025 vs. $4.267B in 2024 — a 28% increase. Capex was approximately $868M in 2025 (implied: operating cash flow minus FCF = $5.444B - $4.576B = $868M), representing roughly 6.5% of revenue. This is predominantly data center and infrastructure investment to support the platform; maintenance capex is considerably lower. FCF conversion from GAAP net income is high because depreciation and amortization provide meaningful add-backs; SBC also adds back as a non-cash charge in the cash flow statement.

**Capital Intensity**

ServiceNow is a predominantly asset-light, software-subscription business. The platform runs on cloud infrastructure (including owned data centers and hyperscaler contracts); the $868M capex figure in 2025 covers both maintenance of existing capacity and growth investment for new AI workloads. The company has made non-cancellable cloud/infrastructure purchase commitments totaling $7.9B as of December 31, 2025, primarily with hyperscalers — this is an operating expense commitment rather than capitalized capex, but it is worth noting as a form of quasi-fixed cost as AI demand scales. Despite this, the incremental dollar of revenue requires very little incremental fixed capital, confirming the asset-light characteristics that Buffett prizes.

**Revenue Quality**

Revenue quality is among the best in enterprise software. Subscription revenue was $12.88B in FY2025, comprising ~97% of total revenue ($13.28B total). Subscription renewal rate has been 98-99% consistently. NRR of ~125% means that even if ServiceNow never signed a new customer, existing customers would grow the base by 25% per year through expansions and upsells. The remaining performance obligations (RPO) stood at $28.2B at end of Q4 2025, with current RPO (next 12 months) at $12.85B — approximately 97% of annualized subscription revenue is already contracted and visible. Now Assist (AI product) net new ACV more than doubled YoY in Q4 2025, indicating the AI upsell layer is gaining meaningful traction as a new revenue driver on top of the existing base.

**Operating Leverage**

The multi-year trend is unambiguous: non-GAAP operating margin expanded from roughly 26-27% in FY2022-2023 to 31% in FY2025 while the company grew revenue from approximately $7.2B to $13.3B. That is approximately 400 basis points of non-GAAP operating margin expansion over three years on $6B of incremental revenue. FCF margin expanded even faster — from approximately 30% to 35% over the same period. R&D is still running at approximately 14-15% of revenue (non-GAAP), reflecting continued heavy investment; as that moderates, further margin expansion is likely. The 2026 guidance of 32% non-GAAP operating margin and 36% FCF margin signals continued operating leverage.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** The platform flywheel — high NRR, locked-in renewal rates, AI upsell layers — compounds FCF at 25-30%+ annually for five or more years as AI workloads require additional ServiceNow modules.
- **Bear case:** AI infrastructure costs (compute, hyperscaler contracts) permanently compress subscription gross margins from 83%+ toward 79-80%, and customer budget pressure in an enterprise recession creates unexpected renewal weakness.
- **Confidence:** High — The core business economics (FCF margin, renewal rate, NRR, revenue visibility via RPO) are reported by the company and confirmed by multiple independent sources. ROIC figures are noisy due to SBC and acquisition goodwill, but the cash return quality of the underlying business is well-documented.

## Red Flags

- SBC at approximately 16% of revenue ($2.1B+ annualized) represents real dilution to owners. Owner earnings are meaningfully lower than FCF once SBC is treated as a real cost.
- The Moveworks acquisition (closed December 2025) and the $7.9B in non-cancellable cloud commitments introduce cost rigidity that could pressure FCF margins if revenue growth decelerates.
- GAAP ROIC below WACC per third-party calculations suggests that on a strict accounting basis, the company has not yet demonstrated capital efficiency sufficient to satisfy a strict Buffett-style return hurdle — though this is primarily an artifact of acquisition accounting and SBC treatment rather than operational weakness.
- Subscription gross margin contracted 160 bps in 2025; if AI infrastructure costs continue to rise faster than pricing power, further compression is possible.

## Score: 8 / 10

ServiceNow operates an exceptional subscription business with best-in-class FCF generation (35% margin), near-perfect renewal rates, 125% NRR, and clear operating leverage; the primary deductions are the SBC dilution burden and modest GAAP ROIC relative to a strict economic hurdle.
