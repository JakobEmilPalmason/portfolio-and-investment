## Detailed Analysis

**Owner Earnings Estimate.** Cadence reported FY2025 GAAP net income of approximately $1.11B ($4.06 per diluted share on ~273M shares). Operating cash flow was $1.73B. Capital expenditures were roughly $140M, almost entirely maintenance and infrastructure for a capital-light software business. This gives owner earnings of approximately $1.59B, or roughly $5.83 per share. This is the cash the owner actually gets to keep, and it is substantially higher than GAAP net income because of non-cash charges like stock-based compensation amortization and acquisition-related intangibles. Using GAAP EPS as the earnings anchor is more conservative and accounts for the real dilution from SBC.

**Scenario Analysis (Year 5, FY2030).** For each scenario, I estimate owner earnings per share in Year 5, apply a terminal multiple, and discount back at 10%.

*Bear case:* Revenue growth decelerates to 8% CAGR (from ~14% in FY2025) due to China export restrictions tightening, slower AI spending, or competitive pressure. Non-GAAP operating margins stay flat at 45%. Owner earnings per share reach ~$9.50 by FY2030. At 30x owner earnings (compression from current 50x given slower growth), that is ~$285. Discounted back 5 years at 10%: ~$177. This implies roughly 40% downside from today's $293.

*Base case:* Revenue grows at 12% CAGR (in line with industry growth and management guidance trajectory). Margins expand modestly to 47%. Owner earnings per share reach ~$11.50 by FY2030. At 35x owner earnings (still a premium multiple for a high-quality compounder): ~$403. Discounted back at 10%: ~$250. This implies roughly 15% downside from today's price, meaning the stock is modestly overvalued even under reasonable assumptions.

*Bull case:* Revenue grows at 16% CAGR as AI design complexity drives outsized EDA spending, Cadence gains share, and system design/analysis becomes a larger revenue stream. Margins expand to 49%. Owner earnings per share reach ~$14.00 by FY2030. At 40x owner earnings: ~$560. Discounted back at 10%: ~$348. This implies roughly 19% upside, but requires optimistic assumptions on both growth and multiple.

**Multiples in Context.** CDNS currently trades at a trailing P/E of ~73x GAAP, ~41x non-GAAP, and an EV/EBITDA of ~42x. Its 5-year average P/E has ranged from roughly 45-75x, so while the current multiple is within historical range, it sits in the upper half. Compared to Synopsys (the closest peer), Cadence typically trades at a slight premium due to faster organic growth and higher cash conversion (ROIC of 28.6% vs Synopsys 19.3%). Relative to the broader software industry average P/E of ~32x, CDNS commands a significant premium.

**What Must Be True for Today's Price to Work.** At $293 and ~$5.83 in current owner earnings, the stock trades at 50x. For a buyer today to earn a 10% annual return over 5 years, the stock needs to reach ~$472 by 2031. If the terminal multiple compresses to 35x (reasonable for a mature grower), that requires owner earnings of ~$13.50/share, implying an 18% CAGR in owner earnings from here. That is achievable under the bull case but is well above the base case. In short, the market is pricing in near-perfection: sustained mid-teens revenue growth, continued margin expansion, and a premium multiple that persists. There is very little room for disappointment.

**Implied Expectations vs Reality.** The market is pricing CDNS as a durable compounder that will grow earnings at 15%+ for the next five years while maintaining or expanding its premium multiple. This is not impossible given the EDA duopoly dynamics and AI tailwinds, but it leaves no margin of safety. If growth merely matches the industry average (~12%) and multiples contract even modestly, a buyer at today's price earns a below-market return. The stock is priced for a very good outcome, not a merely adequate one.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** AI-driven semiconductor complexity creates a decade-long tailwind for EDA spending, and Cadence's expanding system design platform drives 15%+ earnings growth, justifying the premium multiple.
- **Bear case:** Growth decelerates to high-single-digits due to China restrictions, cyclical slowdowns, or AI commoditization of some EDA functions, and the multiple compresses from 50x to 30x owner earnings, resulting in significant capital loss.
- **Confidence:** Medium — The business quality is high and predictable, but the valuation leaves little room for error, making the outcome highly dependent on growth rate assumptions.

## Red Flags

- Current valuation (~50x owner earnings, ~73x GAAP P/E) requires sustained near-perfection in execution
- Even the base case scenario suggests modest overvaluation at today's price
- Stock-based compensation is significant ($600M+ annually estimated), diluting true owner economics beyond what GAAP captures
- The $140M BIS penalty for China export violations signals compliance risk that could resurface

## Score: 4 / 10

Cadence is a wonderful business priced at a premium that demands optimistic assumptions to justify — under conservative to base-case estimates, the stock appears modestly to meaningfully overvalued, offering no clear margin of safety for a value-oriented buyer.
