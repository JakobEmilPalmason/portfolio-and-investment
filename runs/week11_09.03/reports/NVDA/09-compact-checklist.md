# Compact Checklist — NVDA

**Date:** 2026-03-06

1. **Business**: NVIDIA designs AI accelerator chips (Blackwell/Rubin GPUs) sold to a handful of hyperscalers who account for 61% of its $216B in annual revenue, with ~90% concentrated in the Data Center segment and no recurring contractual revenue floor.

2. **Moat**: CUDA's 20-year, 4M+ developer ecosystem creates months-long, six-figure switching costs that lock in customers, but over 70% of data center revenue comes from inference workloads where custom ASICs (growing at 44.6% vs. 16.1% for GPUs) are a direct and accelerating substitute.

3. **Economics**: NVIDIA earned $120B net income on $216B revenue (56% net margin) with 145% ROIC, $97B in free cash flow, and just $6B in capex — software-tier economics on a fabless hardware model that has never been seen at this scale.

4. **Management**: Jensen Huang holds $150B+ in NVIDIA stock (3.6% of shares) and made one of the decade's best acquisitions (Mellanox at $6.9B, now generating $10B+/quarter in networking revenue), but spent $40.6B on buybacks at 30-50x trailing P/E with no disclosed succession plan.

5. **Debt risk**: With $54B net cash, $8.5B total debt, 503x interest coverage, and $97B annual free cash flow, NVIDIA could lose money for years and never face a balance sheet crisis — this is a fortress.

6. **Price**: At $183/share ($4.45T market cap, ~37x trailing earnings), the stock trades above most base-case DCF estimates ($124-$165) and requires ~20% annual owner earnings growth for five years just to deliver a 10% return, meaning you need the AI capex supercycle to continue without a meaningful pause.

7. **Margin of safety**: The business itself provides enormous cushion (75% gross margins, $54B net cash, dominant market share), but the stock price offers none — base-case DCFs suggest 10-32% overvaluation, and the downside ($68 bear case) is larger than the upside ($260 bull case) relative to today's price.

8. **Thesis breaker**: Sell if two or more hyperscalers materially cut AI capex guidance, or if custom ASIC market share gains cause NVIDIA's Data Center revenue growth to stall for two consecutive quarters, because that would confirm the 61%-customer-concentration risk and the erosion of pricing power that the current valuation cannot absorb.
