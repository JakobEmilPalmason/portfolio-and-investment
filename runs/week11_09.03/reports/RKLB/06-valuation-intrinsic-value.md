## Detailed Analysis

**Owner Earnings Today: Effectively Zero or Negative.** Rocket Lab generated $602M in revenue in FY2025 with a GAAP gross margin around 37% (Q3 2025 record), implying gross profit of roughly $220M. However, the company carried a net loss of $198M after R&D, G&A, and Neutron development costs. Depreciation and amortization run substantial from prior acquisitions (SolarFlux, Advanced Solutions Inc., Planetary Systems Corporation), but maintenance capex — particularly for the Electron production line, launch pads, and space systems manufacturing — is also high. Total Neutron development spend to end-2025 was approximately $360M with ongoing quarterly workforce costs of ~$15M. Owner earnings, defined as net income + D&A minus maintenance capex, are currently negative. The company generates no surplus cash for owners. Any intrinsic value exercise must be entirely forward-looking, based on a scenario where the business eventually reaches profitability.

**Simple Scenario Analysis (Year 5 = 2030).** All scenarios assume ~529M shares outstanding (with modest ongoing dilution of ~3–5% annually, reaching ~580–600M by 2030).

*Bear Case:* Neutron faces further delays or technical failures and never achieves meaningful commercial cadence before 2030. Electron remains the revenue base, growing at ~15% annually through 2030. Space Systems continues growing but Neutron-related capex drains cash. 2030 revenue: ~$1.1B. Net margin: still negative or breakeven (~0–2%). Owner earnings: ~$0–22M. Apply a 30x revenue multiple for a pre-profitability space company (generous): implied equity value ~$33B. With ~600M shares, that implies ~$55/share. From today's $69.70, that is a loss of ~21%. Note: this bear case assumes the company survives and Electron/Space Systems continues to grow.

*Base Case:* Neutron launches successfully in late 2026 and achieves 5–7 flights by 2030, capturing some medium-lift market. Space Systems grows strongly off the SDA Tranche 3 $816M contract. 2030 revenue: ~$1.6–1.8B. Net margin: 5–8% (early-stage profitability). Owner earnings: ~$80–140M. Apply 35x earnings (high growth premium): implied equity value ~$2.8–4.9B in earnings terms, but the market will likely still value on revenue: 20–25x 2030 revenue = ~$32–45B. Intrinsic value per share: ~$55–75. At the high end of the base case, today's price is roughly fair. At the midpoint (~$60–65), today's price is modestly overvalued.

*Bull Case:* Neutron becomes a commercial and national security launch workhorse. Space Systems scales with Tranche 3 and follow-on defense contracts. 2030 revenue: ~$2.5–3B. Net margin: 10–15%. Owner earnings: ~$250–450M. Apply 40x earnings (SpaceX premium for dominant-player outcome): implied equity value ~$10–18B in earnings terms, but the more relevant metric is revenue: 15–20x 2030 revenue = ~$37–60B. Bull case intrinsic value per share: ~$65–100. Discounting back from 2030 at 10%: ~$55–85 in today's dollars. At the very top of the bull case ($85+), there is some valuation support for the current price.

**Multiples in Context.** RKLB currently trades at approximately 66x trailing revenue (EV ~$40B / TTM revenue ~$602M) and approximately 45x forward 2026 consensus revenue (~$885M). EV/EBITDA is not meaningful given deeply negative EBITDA. For comparison: SpaceX (private) was valued at ~$350B in late 2025, implying roughly 25–30x revenue — and SpaceX is already massively profitable with Starship operational. Rocket Lab thus trades at a premium to SpaceX on a revenue multiple basis despite being 15–20 years behind in scale and profitability. Peers like Aerojet Rocketdyne trade at 2–3x revenue with positive earnings. The premium RKLB commands is purely speculative and narrative-driven — it is the market's way of pricing in the possibility that RKLB becomes the "next SpaceX."

**What Must Be True for Today's Price to Be Justified.** Reverse-engineering the current ~$40B market cap: if we apply a 25x P/E at maturity, the company needs ~$1.6B in net income. At a 15% net margin (very high for aerospace), that requires ~$10.7B in revenue. At 20% CAGR from 2026, reaching $10.7B takes approximately 15 years (to ~2041). Discounting that back at a 10% discount rate over 15 years gives a present value factor of 0.24, implying the terminal equity value needed is over $160B. This is an extraordinarily aggressive scenario that requires: (1) Neutron succeeding and achieving massive launch cadence, (2) Space Systems maintaining 30–40% growth for a decade-plus, (3) no competitive disruption from SpaceX or new entrants, (4) margin expansion to software-like levels in a capital-intensive industry, and (5) the company surviving without major dilutive capital raises. Each assumption is plausible in isolation, but the conjunction of all five is a very low-probability outcome.

**Implied Expectations vs. Likely Reality.** The market is not pricing in perfection — it is pricing in a specific narrative: Rocket Lab becomes the world's second-largest launch company and a dominant defense space systems integrator, with Neutron as a transformative asset. This narrative has merit. The company has executed well on Electron (21 missions in 2025, 100% success rate), built a genuinely differentiated Space Systems business, and secured a landmark $816M government contract. The backlog of $1.85B is real and growing. But the gap between the execution required over the next decade and the certainty the market seems to be pricing in is wide. By Buffett's framework — paying a price that gives you a margin of safety even if you're wrong about the future — RKLB fails the test. You are paying for a nearly perfect execution of an optimistic scenario over a decade-plus time horizon.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** Neutron executes on schedule and builds launch cadence by 2028–2029, Space Systems doubles again off the SDA contract, and RKLB reaches $2.5B+ revenue by 2030, justifying a $70–100 price range.
- **Bear case:** Neutron faces another major delay or a mission failure, competition from SpaceX drives pricing pressure on medium-lift, and the stock re-rates down 50–70% to $20–35 as growth-at-any-price sentiment fades.
- **Confidence:** Low — The absence of earnings, the extreme reliance on speculative future scenarios, and the sentiment-driven nature of the stock make any point estimate of intrinsic value unreliable. The range of plausible outcomes is extremely wide.

## Red Flags

- EV/revenue multiple (~66x trailing, ~45x forward) is in the top 3–4% most expensive in aerospace and defense globally.
- No path to positive owner earnings until at least 2027–2028, and that requires Neutron to succeed.
- Reverse DCF analysis implies the market needs 15+ years of exceptional execution to justify current price at a reasonable discount rate.
- Ongoing share dilution (~3–6% annually) is an additional headwind to per-share intrinsic value growth.
- FY2025 adjusted EBITDA loss of $101M means the company is burning cash for operating operations, not just capital investment.
- Neutron development costs were ~$360M through 2025 with no near-term revenue contribution; further spending will be needed post-first flight for commercial ramp.
- All analyst "buy" ratings and $82–120 price targets are built on speculative 5–10 year revenue models, not earnings multiples — the market consensus is itself speculative, not value-based.

## Score: 2 / 10

By Buffett standards, RKLB is significantly overvalued: the company has no positive owner earnings today, requires a decade-plus of nearly flawless execution to justify current prices, and trades at one of the highest EV/revenue multiples in its industry — leaving no margin of safety and enormous downside if the narrative falters.
