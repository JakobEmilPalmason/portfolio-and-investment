## Detailed Analysis

### Owner Earnings Estimate

Synopsys reported FY2025 GAAP net income of $1.336B and non-GAAP net income of $2.138B on $7.054B revenue. The large gap reflects $800M+ of amortization from the Ansys acquisition goodwill and intangibles. Free cash flow in FY2025 was approximately $1.35B, depressed by acquisition-related costs. For FY2026, the company guides $2.2B cash from operations and $1.9B free cash flow after ~$300M capex. Adjusting for the non-cash amortization burden, owner earnings (net income + D&A - maintenance capex) approximate $2.0-2.2B on a normalized non-GAAP basis. On ~191M diluted shares, that equates to roughly $10.50-11.50 per share in owner earnings, or about 2.5% owner earnings yield at the current $433 price. The guided $14.32-14.40 non-GAAP EPS includes stock-based compensation add-backs that inflate the figure relative to true owner economics.

### Scenario Analysis (5-Year Forward)

**Bear Case:** Revenue grows at 8% CAGR (organic EDA + sluggish Ansys integration), margins compress slightly due to debt service costs and competitive pressure. Year 5 owner earnings: ~$2.8B. Apply 25x multiple = $70B enterprise value. Subtract ~$8B net debt remaining = ~$62B equity value / 185M shares = ~$335/share. Discounted at 10% = ~$208. Implies ~52% downside.

**Base Case:** Revenue grows at 12% CAGR (healthy EDA + Ansys cross-sell synergies realized), margins expand as acquisition amortization runs down and debt is repaid. Year 5 owner earnings: ~$3.8B. Apply 30x multiple = $114B EV. Subtract ~$4B net debt = $110B equity / 185M shares = ~$595/share. Discounted at 10% = ~$369. Implies ~15% downside.

**Bull Case:** Revenue grows at 16% CAGR (AI-driven chip design boom, Ansys simulation becomes mission-critical for multi-physics). Year 5 owner earnings: ~$5.0B. Apply 35x multiple = $175B EV. Subtract ~$2B net debt = $173B equity / 185M shares = ~$935/share. Discounted at 10% = ~$580. Implies ~34% upside.

### Multiples in Context

SNPS trades at a trailing P/E of ~68x (GAAP, heavily distorted by Ansys amortization) and ~30x forward non-GAAP P/E. The 5-year average forward P/E is ~37x, meaning the stock is 19% below its historical average multiple. EV/EBITDA is approximately 32-50x depending on the measurement (GAAP vs adjusted). Against peer Cadence at ~93x trailing earnings and ~40x forward, SNPS appears meaningfully cheaper. Against the broader software sector (median ~25-30x forward P/E), SNPS trades in line but carries superior growth visibility given its EDA duopoly position. The P/FCF ratio on guided FY2026 numbers is approximately 44x ($83B market cap / $1.9B FCF), which is elevated but needs context: FY2026 FCF is suppressed by $300M in integration capex and $1.75B+ in debt repayment from operating cash.

### Reverse-Engineering the Current Price

At $433/share and ~191M shares, the market cap is ~$83B. Adding ~$11B net debt gives an EV of ~$94B. For this to be fair value at a terminal 25x owner earnings multiple (reasonable for a high-quality software compounder), the market is implying Year 5 owner earnings of $94B / 25 * (1.10)^5 discount back = ~$3.76B undiscounted, or about $2.34B discounted. This requires approximately 13-14% CAGR in owner earnings from the current ~$2.0B base. Given SNPS has compounded revenue at 15%+ historically and has the Ansys revenue tailwind, this is achievable but leaves limited margin for error. The market is essentially pricing in successful Ansys integration, continued AI/semiconductor design TAM expansion, and meaningful deleveraging -- but not paying for upside optionality.

### Implied Expectations vs Likely Reality

The stock's current valuation implies roughly 13-15% earnings growth for the next 5 years. Synopsys has delivered ~15-20% non-GAAP EPS growth historically (pre-Ansys), and consensus estimates peg growth at ~15% going forward. The key question is whether the Ansys acquisition creates or destroys value net of the $13.5B debt burden. The $2.9B in expected Ansys revenue for FY2026 at software-like margins should be accretive, but GAAP earnings will remain depressed for years due to ~$1B+ annual purchase price amortization. The implied expectations appear roughly in line with likely reality -- the stock is neither deeply cheap nor obviously overpriced. The margin of safety depends entirely on the Ansys integration thesis.

## Signal Summary
- **Bull case:** AI chip complexity drives sustained 15%+ EDA/simulation growth; Ansys synergies unlock cross-sell and pricing power, justifying $550+ within 18 months.
- **Bear case:** Ansys integration stumbles, China export controls resurface, and $13.5B debt constrains shareholder returns; stock revisits $350 or below.
- **Confidence:** Medium — The business quality is high but the post-acquisition balance sheet introduces uncertainty that makes precise valuation challenging.

## Red Flags
- GAAP net income collapsed to $65M in Q1 FY2026 (from $296M YoY) due to massive Ansys amortization; non-GAAP adjustments are the only path to "earnings growth"
- $13.5B in debt represents a dramatic shift from historically net-cash balance sheet; net debt/EBITDA is likely ~3x+
- Forward P/E of ~30x assumes flawless execution on a transformative acquisition
- China revenue (~12-16% of sales) remains subject to whiplash from export controls; full suspension happened in May 2025 before being reversed in July 2025
- Stock-based compensation is significant and dilutes true owner economics vs non-GAAP EPS presentation
- Wide DCF valuation range ($296-$461) reflects genuine uncertainty about correct assumptions

## Score: 5 / 10
At ~$433, Synopsys trades near the midpoint of a wide fair value range; the valuation is neither compelling nor obviously stretched, but the $13.5B debt overhang and reliance on non-GAAP adjustments to show earnings growth leave insufficient margin of safety for a Buffett-style buyer.
