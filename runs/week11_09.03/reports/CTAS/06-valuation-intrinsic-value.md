## Detailed Analysis

**Owner Earnings Estimate**

Current owner earnings: Net income $1.8B + D&A $494M - CapEx $409M = $1.885B. Because roughly half of CapEx is growth-oriented (new laundry facilities, route expansion), maintenance CapEx is more conservatively estimated at $200–$250M, which would imply normalized owner earnings closer to $2.05–$2.10B. At $78.1B market cap, the owner earnings yield is approximately 2.4–2.7%. A 3–5 year look-ahead at 10% annual growth produces owner earnings of $2.4–$3.0B by Year 3 and $3.0–$3.4B by Year 5, before any UniFirst contribution.

**Bear / Base / Bull Scenario — Year 5 Owner Earnings and Value**

Bear case: Revenue growth slows to 5% annually (macro softness, labor market normalization reduces uniform density; antitrust forces significant UniFirst divestitures). Owner earnings grow from $1.9B to ~$2.4B by Year 5. Apply a multiple of 22x (de-rating toward long-run average as growth decelerates). Implied market cap: ~$53B. Discount back at 9%: present value ~$34B per the enterprise, or roughly $85/share. This is a ~56% downside scenario.

Base case: Revenue grows at ~8% annually (in line with recent organic trajectory plus modest UniFirst contribution post-integration). Owner earnings reach ~$2.8B by Year 5. Apply a 25x multiple (modest premium to market reflecting high quality, recurring revenue). Implied market cap: $70B. Discounted at 9%: present value ~$45B, or ~$113/share. Still ~42% below today's price.

Bull case: Revenue grows at 10–11% annually (UniFirst synergies fully realized, no material divestitures required, margins expand further). Owner earnings reach ~$3.3B by Year 5. Apply a 30x multiple (sustained premium for demonstrated quality compounder). Implied market cap: ~$99B. Discounted at 9%: present value ~$64B, or ~$160/share. This is ~18% below current price, and requires execution of the largest acquisition in the company's history without meaningful antitrust disruption.

**Multiples in Context**

The 10-year average trailing P/E is ~33x; the 10-year median EV/EBITDA is ~18x; the 10-year max EV/EBITDA was ~27.7x. Today CTAS trades at 42x trailing P/E and 28x EV/EBITDA — above the 10-year peak on both metrics by some measures. Peers: Vestis (formerly Aramark Uniform Services) trades at far lower multiples (single-digit EV/EBITDA) but is lower quality; there is no directly comparable pure-play compounder. Versus the S&P 500 at roughly 22x forward earnings, CTAS commands a 60%+ premium, which is historically large even for a quality compounder.

**Reverse-Engineering the Price**

To justify $194/share at a 9% discount rate: the business must grow owner earnings at approximately 11–12% per year for 10 years and exit at 30x. This requires $5.2B+ in owner earnings by Year 10 (2.7x today), sustained high ROIC (27%+), successful UniFirst integration, and no meaningful antitrust setbacks. That is a narrow path. Any deceleration to 7–8% growth or multiple contraction to 20–22x produces very poor forward returns.

**Implied Expectations vs Likely Reality**

The market is pricing the continuation of peak margins, peak growth rates, and a premium multiple simultaneously. Revenue growth decelerated slightly in Q2 FY2026 (organic 8.6% vs prior 9%+ run rates). EPS guidance for full FY2026 is $4.81–$4.88, implying roughly 10% year-over-year growth. Consensus EPS for FY2028 is ~$6.02. At $194 and $6.02 FY2028 EPS, the stock is trading at 32x two-years-out earnings — still at the high end of historical range. The UniFirst acquisition introduces both upside optionality (synergies, route density) and material new risk (leverage, integration drag, divestitures). The market is not pricing any of the downside from that risk, only the upside synergies.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** If UniFirst integrates smoothly with full synergies and no forced divestitures, CTAS could compound earnings 11–12% for several years and justify a price near $160–$180 — still short of current levels.
- **Bear case:** Antitrust forces large-scale divestitures, integration absorbs management attention, growth decelerates to 5–6%, and the multiple contracts to 22–25x, producing a stock in the $85–$110 range.
- **Confidence:** High — the inputs (owner earnings, growth rates, multiples) are well-supported by financial data and history; the conclusion that the stock is materially overvalued relative to intrinsic value is robust across scenarios.

## Red Flags

- Current valuation (42x trailing P/E, 28x EV/EBITDA) is near or above the 10-year historical maximum — the starting multiple alone is a significant headwind to returns
- All three DCF scenarios (bear, base, bull) produce intrinsic values below the current share price; only a hyper-optimistic scenario with sustained near-perfection execution reaches today's price
- The UniFirst acquisition introduces $5.5B of new enterprise value risk, antitrust uncertainty, and integration distraction that is not reflected in the current multiple
- Owner earnings yield at current price is ~2.4% — thinner than most high-quality bonds, with materially more execution risk
- Analyst consensus growth rates (~9–10%) are already well-publicized and priced in; there is little margin for upside surprise

## Score: 3 / 10

CTAS is a world-class business trading at or above the top of its historical valuation range, with all reasonable DCF scenarios producing intrinsic values 15–55% below the current price, and the only path to justifying $194 requiring near-perfect multi-year execution on a large, antitrust-risky acquisition.
