# Compact Checklist — CTAS

**Date:** 2026-03-16

1. **Business**: Cintas rents, launders, and delivers uniforms and workplace supplies (floor mats, first aid kits, fire protection) to over one million businesses on weekly routes under multi-year recurring contracts, generating $10.3B in FY2025 revenue with ~77% from its core Uniform Rental segment.

2. **Moat**: Route density creates a self-reinforcing cost advantage — 12,000+ delivery routes amortize fixed logistics costs over more stops than any competitor can match — while embedded product programs at customer sites (fitted uniforms, installed cabinets) make switching operationally disruptive for marginal savings, evidenced by ROIC expanding from 21.4% to 27.2% over four consecutive years.

3. **Economics**: The business compounds reliably: 50% gross margins, 22.8% operating margins, ~100% average FCF conversion over four years, and EPS growing at a 15.6% CAGR on only 9.3% revenue growth — structural operating leverage is real and accelerating.

4. **Management**: The Farmer family retains ~21% insider ownership (Scott Farmer's stake alone is worth over $13B), CEO Todd Schneider has 35+ years at the company, and capital has been returned consistently via $935M in buybacks and consecutive annual dividend increases in FY2025 — the $5.5B UniFirst acquisition is the largest test of their capital allocation judgment.

5. **Debt risk**: Pre-acquisition net leverage is 0.8x Debt/EBITDA with 23.4x interest coverage and investment-grade ratings from both S&P (A-) and Moody's (A3); even with UniFirst raising leverage to ~1.5x at close, a 30% revenue stress would still leave EBITDA covering debt service comfortably, and $1.75B in annual FCF deleverages rapidly.

6. **Price**: At $194.28 and 42x trailing P/E, the stock requires owner earnings to grow at 11–12% annually for 10 years and exit at a 30x multiple — near-perfect multi-year execution on the largest acquisition in company history, with no antitrust setbacks and no multiple contraction from current decade-peak levels.

7. **Margin of safety**: There is none at $194 — the base-case intrinsic value is approximately $113/share and the bear case is $85/share, meaning 40–56% downside risk dwarfs the 0–10% realistic upside, and even a 30% drawdown only returns the stock near fair value rather than into deep value territory.

8. **Thesis breaker**: If the FTC blocks or forces major divestitures on the UniFirst acquisition (eliminating the $375M synergy basis), or if operating margins compress below 20% for two consecutive years signaling structural route-density deterioration, sell immediately.
