## Detailed Analysis

**Owner Earnings and the Quant Anchor.** The quant model uses FY2025 owner earnings of $8.1B (net income plus D&A minus maintenance capex of $2.4B, treating the remaining $3.1B of capex as growth investment). This is a reasonable starting point. Costco's owner earnings have compounded at roughly 12% annually over the last four years, driven by same-store sales growth, membership fee income increases, and modest margin expansion (operating margin from 3.4% to 3.8%). The base-case DCF assumes 7.9% revenue growth fading to 3% by year five, a 15x exit EV/EBITDA multiple, and a 9.8% WACC. These are not pessimistic assumptions — the base case is generous for a mature retailer. Yet it produces only $456 per share.

**Scenario Stress-Testing.** The quant bear case ($417) uses conservative but not draconian inputs: 4.9% Y1 revenue growth, 10.8% WACC, and a 12x exit multiple. Even the bull case ($834) — which assumes nearly 11% Y1 growth, a low 9.3% WACC, and an 18x exit multiple — still falls $150 short of the current price. The sensitivity grid reinforces this: across 25 combinations of growth (3.9%-11.9%) and WACC (7.8%-11.8%), the highest IV produced is $621, which requires the most optimistic growth AND lowest WACC simultaneously. To reach $984 in a DCF framework, you would need either a dramatically higher exit multiple (25x+ EV/EBITDA) or sustained double-digit growth well beyond the 5-year projection window, or both. This is not impossible for Costco, but it means the stock is priced for near-perfection.

**Multiples in Context.** Costco's current trailing P/E of 51.3x, forward P/E of 43.8x, and EV/EBITDA of 31.7x all represent meaningful premiums to both its own history and peers. Walmart trades at a forward P/E of roughly 41-42x (already elevated), while BJ's Wholesale Club trades at roughly 20-22x forward earnings. Costco has historically commanded a premium — its 10-year average P/E is about 45x — but even by its own generous standards, the stock is at the upper end. The P/FCF ratio of 65.3x is particularly stretched; Costco generates $7.8B of free cash flow on a $437B market cap. The membership fee income ($5.4B+ annually, growing double digits) provides an earnings quality premium that competitors lack, which partially justifies the gap, but the current multiple leaves no room for disappointment.

**Reverse-Engineering the Implied Assumptions.** For $984 to represent fair value, working backward from the current market cap of $437B, you need Costco to generate approximately $18-20B in owner earnings within 5 years (more than doubling from today's $8.1B), assuming the market then assigns a 20-22x multiple. That implies ~17-19% annualized earnings growth over five years. Alternatively, if earnings grow at a more realistic 10-12%, the market needs to sustain a 40-50x exit multiple indefinitely — essentially betting that Costco will permanently trade like a high-growth tech company. Neither scenario is impossible given Costco's exceptional track record, but both require everything to go right: continued membership growth, international expansion success, no margin compression from tariffs or competition, and a market willing to pay a perpetual premium.

**Is the Market Pricing in Perfection or Disaster?** The market is clearly pricing in perfection. Costco is one of the best-run retailers in the world — 24% ROIC, 0.6x debt/EBITDA, 90%+ membership renewal rates, a culture that treats employees well and passes savings to customers. The business quality is not in question. The question is whether a phenomenal business at 51x earnings can deliver acceptable returns to a buyer today. The Q2 FY2026 results (EPS $4.58, up 13.9% YoY; membership income up 13.6%) show the machine is still humming. But at this price, investors are paying a decade of excellent execution upfront and hoping the market never re-rates the multiple downward.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** Costco's membership flywheel, international expansion (30+ new warehouses/year), and e-commerce acceleration sustain 12%+ earnings growth for years, and the market continues to pay a premium multiple for this rare quality compounder.
- **Bear case:** Earnings growth normalizes to high single digits, tariffs compress margins, and multiple compression from 51x to the historical average of ~37x would mean a stock price around $710 — a 28% drawdown with no fundamental deterioration.
- **Confidence:** High — The quant model, Monte Carlo, sensitivity analysis, and multiple comparison all converge on the same conclusion: this is a wonderful business trading at a very expensive price.

## Red Flags

- P/E of 51.3x is at the top decile of Costco's own historical range and 37% above the 10-year median.
- Only 3% probability in 10,000 Monte Carlo simulations that IV exceeds the current price.
- Zero cells in the 25-cell sensitivity grid produce an IV above $621, let alone $984.
- P/FCF of 65.3x means you are paying 65 years of current free cash flow — extreme for any retailer.
- Analyst consensus target of ~$1,067 offers only 8.5% upside, suggesting even bulls see limited near-term appreciation.
- Tariff-related cost pressures and potential lawsuits over tariff charge pass-throughs could pressure near-term margins.

## Score: 2 / 10

Costco is a best-in-class business, but at $984 the stock is significantly overvalued by every quantitative measure — the DCF, the Monte Carlo, the sensitivity grid, and peer multiples all indicate the market is pricing in a decade of flawless execution with zero margin of safety.
