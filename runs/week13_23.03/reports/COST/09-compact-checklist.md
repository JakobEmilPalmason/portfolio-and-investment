# Compact Checklist — COST

**Date:** 2026-03-29

1. **Business**: Costco charges 147 million cardholders an annual fee ($65/$130) to shop in warehouse stores selling ~3,800 curated SKUs at near cost, with membership fees (92% renewal) generating the vast majority of operating profit.
2. **Moat**: A 40-year flywheel of shared scale economics — massive buying power passed to members as the lowest prices in retail — reinforced by the $90B Kirkland Signature private label and 55% U.S. warehouse club market share that no competitor has been able to erode.
3. **Economics**: ROIC has been consistently 20-24% with 97% FCF conversion and $8.1B in adjusted owner earnings, driven by a membership revenue stream that grows at 10%+ annually at near-100% margins.
4. **Management**: Leadership has been exclusively promoted from within for four decades (current CEO started as a forklift driver), with 87% of CEO pay in long-term equity, though net buybacks barely offset $860M in annual stock-based compensation.
5. **Debt risk**: Net cash of $7.1B, AA/Aa3 credit ratings, 71x interest coverage, and $15.3B in cash mean Costco could survive a multi-year depression without accessing capital markets.
6. **Price**: At 51x trailing earnings ($984/share), the market must believe Costco will sustain 12-13% annual earnings growth for a decade with no multiple compression — the quant DCF produces a base IV of $456 and even the bull case ($834) falls 15% short of today's price.
7. **Margin of safety**: The business margin of safety is world-class (fortress balance sheet, essential products, 90%+ recurring membership revenue), but the price margin of safety is deeply negative at -136% versus the bear IV, with only 3% of Monte Carlo simulations showing undervaluation.
8. **Thesis breaker**: A sustained decline in U.S./Canada membership renewal rates below 88%, or any quarter showing negative comparable sales growth, would signal the flywheel is weakening and the premium multiple is unjustified.
