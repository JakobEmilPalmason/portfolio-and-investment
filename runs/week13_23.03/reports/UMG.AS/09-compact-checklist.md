# Compact Checklist — UMG.AS

**Date:** 2026-03-25

1. **Business**: UMG owns the world's largest music catalog and licenses it to streaming platforms, radio, film, and advertisers, generating EUR 12.5B in revenue that is ~78% subscription-driven and recurring.
2. **Moat**: UMG's 36% global recorded music share in a stable three-player oligopoly means no streaming platform can operate without its catalog, giving it structural pricing power demonstrated by the 2024 TikTok standoff and 2025 Streaming 2.0 deals.
3. **Economics**: ROIC of 22-31% over four years on genuinely low maintenance capex (3.6% of revenue), though gross margins have compressed 190bps and FCF dropped from EUR 1.6B to EUR 1.2B as artist advance costs doubled to EUR 402M.
4. **Management**: Lucian Grainge is an operationally excellent CEO whose capital allocation (rising dividends, EUR 1.5B buyback, selective catalog M&A) is disciplined, but his EUR 138M compensation package and the Bollore family's 40% voting control on 18.5% economic ownership are real governance blemishes.
5. **Debt risk**: Net debt/EBITDA of 0.9x with BBB+/Baa1 ratings and 10x interest coverage means UMG survives any plausible downturn, though the EUR 114M cash balance against EUR 7.6B in current liabilities leaves zero buffer for a sudden cash flow interruption.
6. **Price**: At EUR 15.66 (13.3x forward P/E vs. its own 3-year average of ~25x), the market is pricing in streaming growth stalling, no margin expansion, and permanent AI impairment — all of which must go wrong simultaneously to justify the current valuation.
7. **Margin of safety**: A 99.5% Monte Carlo probability of undervaluation, all 25 sensitivity grid cells above the current price, a ~EUR 3B off-DCF Spotify stake, and a 4:1 base-case reward/risk ratio protect you, though the bear IV of EUR 13.91 sits only 11% below the current price.
8. **Thesis breaker**: Sell if AI-generated music captures more than 10% of organic streaming consumption or if streaming revenue growth falls below 5% for two consecutive quarters with no pricing offset.
