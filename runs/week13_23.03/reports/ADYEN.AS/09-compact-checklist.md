# Compact Checklist — ADYEN.AS

**Date:** 2026-03-29

1. **Business**: Adyen charges merchants ~17 basis points on every payment transaction it processes across EUR 1.4 trillion in annual volume — a toll road on global digital commerce with 80% of growth from existing customers expanding usage.
2. **Moat**: Deep enterprise switching costs (sub-1% volume churn) and a single proprietary tech stack built from scratch with direct acquiring in 40+ countries, though Stripe holds 25% market share versus Adyen's 9% and is closing the gap.
3. **Economics**: 41% operating margins expanding, 22% ROE, EUR 1.1B in owner earnings growing 25% annually, and maintenance capex at just 5% of revenue confirm this is a genuinely asset-light, high-return compounder.
4. **Management**: Founder-led for 20 years with zero acquisitions, 1.5% SBC-to-revenue, and modest CEO pay of EUR 807K, but the refusal to return any of the EUR 3-4B in excess cash via buybacks at depressed prices is a real capital allocation flaw.
5. **Debt risk**: Effectively debt-free with EUR 252M in leases against EUR 10.5B in cash, and the business would remain profitable and self-funding even through a 30% revenue decline sustained for two years.
6. **Price**: At EUR 855 the market requires 8-9% sustained revenue growth with flawless execution — the quant DCF base case is EUR 763 and Monte Carlo gives only 10.7% probability the stock is undervalued.
7. **Margin of safety**: The business itself is a fortress (high margins, net cash, sticky customers), but there is zero price margin of safety — bear IV of EUR 632 implies 26% downside versus only 7% upside to bull IV.
8. **Thesis breaker**: Net revenue growth falling below 15% for two consecutive halves, or take rate compressing below 15 basis points, would signal the competitive moat is eroding and the growth premium is unjustified.
