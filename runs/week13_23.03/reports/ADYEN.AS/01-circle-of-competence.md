## Detailed Analysis

Adyen is a payment technology company headquartered in Amsterdam. It provides a single technology platform that lets businesses accept payments online, in-store, and on mobile across 200+ countries and 250+ payment methods. The core product is payment processing: when a consumer pays a merchant, Adyen sits in the middle, routing, authorizing, and settling the transaction. For this, Adyen charges the merchant a small per-transaction fee. That is the entire business in one sentence.

The revenue model is transaction-based and scales with volume. Adyen uses an "interchange++" pricing model -- transparent, per-transaction fees rather than bundled or opaque pricing. In FY2025 the company processed EUR 1.39 trillion in payment volume and earned EUR 2.36 billion in net revenue, implying a take rate of roughly 17 basis points. This take rate has actually been trending upward (from ~15 bps in H1 2024 to ~17 bps in H1 2025), driven by a mix shift toward higher-margin mid-market merchants and value-added services. Separately, Adyen earns net interest income on merchant funds sitting in transit -- with EUR 1.4 trillion in annual flow, even a short float generates meaningful income.

The key business drivers are straightforward. First, processed volume growth: as e-commerce and digital payments expand globally and Adyen wins new merchants, volume grows. In FY2025, volume grew 8% reported but 21% excluding one large low-margin customer, suggesting healthy underlying momentum. Second, take rate: Adyen can grow revenue faster than volume by adding services (fraud tools, data analytics, financial products for platforms) that carry higher margins. Third, geographic and segment expansion: the Platforms business (embedded payments for software companies) grew volumes 51% YoY in 2024 and is opening up the mid-market. North America is the key geographic growth lever.

Revenue predictability is high for a payments company. There are no long-term take-or-pay contracts, but the business is deeply sticky: 80% of growth comes from existing customers expanding usage, and volume churn is below 1%. Once a large enterprise integrates Adyen into its checkout flow across channels and geographies, ripping it out is painful and expensive. The revenue is essentially recurring transaction-based income tied to the secular growth of digital commerce. Seasonality exists (Q4 holiday spending is strongest), but the underlying trajectory is steady.

Customer concentration is low and improving. The top 10 merchants accounted for just 12% of net revenue in FY2024. The customer base spans e-commerce, retail, travel, hospitality, and platforms. Key names include Nike, Uber, eBay, Microsoft, Spotify, H&M, and Booking.com. The Platforms segment diversifies the base further by adding thousands of smaller sub-merchants under platform umbrellas.

Can you explain this business in two minutes? Absolutely. Adyen processes payments for businesses. Every time someone buys something from one of Adyen's merchant customers -- whether online, in a store, or on an app -- Adyen handles the transaction and takes a tiny cut. The more transactions flow through its pipes, the more money it makes. It is a toll-road business on global commerce.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** Adyen's single-platform architecture and expanding take rate mean it can compound net revenue at 20%+ for years while margins expand, turning it into a durable toll collector on global digital commerce.
- **Bear case:** Processed volume growth decelerates as e-commerce penetration matures, and intense competition from Stripe and others compresses take rates, turning payments into a margin-squeezed commodity.
- **Confidence:** High -- this is a simple, well-documented business model with transparent financials and clear drivers. The payments industry is well understood.

## Red Flags

- FY2025 reported processed volume growth of only 8% (distorted by one large customer) could signal slower organic momentum than headline net revenue growth suggests.
- The gap between P/FCF (50.5x) and P/E (25.4x) is unusually wide, suggesting operating cash flow in FY2025 was depressed relative to earnings -- likely due to working capital timing on merchant funds. This merits scrutiny in the financial analysis.

## Score: 9 / 10

Adyen is one of the most understandable businesses in European tech: a transaction-based toll road on digital commerce with clear drivers, low customer concentration, and a revenue model anyone can grasp in under two minutes. Minor complexity around merchant funds flow and the Platforms segment prevents a perfect 10.
