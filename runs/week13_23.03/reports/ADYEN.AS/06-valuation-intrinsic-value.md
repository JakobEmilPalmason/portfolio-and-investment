## Detailed Analysis

**Quant DCF as anchor.** The deterministic DCF model produces a base-case per-share IV of EUR 763 using 14.9% WACC, 6.4% year-one revenue growth, and a 15x exit multiple. This is conservative on growth (actual H2 2025 revenue growth was 17-21% depending on currency) but reasonable on discount rate given Adyen's 1.9 beta. The bear case at EUR 632 assumes 3.4% growth and 12x exit; the bull case at EUR 915 assumes 9.4% growth and 18x exit. Even in the bull scenario, the stock is only fairly valued at today's EUR 855 price, leaving no margin of safety.

**What the price implies.** At EUR 855, the market is embedding expectations somewhere between the quant model's bull case and beyond. Reverse-engineering the DCF, the current price requires roughly 8-9% sustained revenue growth with a 16-17x exit multiple at the base WACC — essentially the optimistic end of management's own guidance range, executed flawlessly for multiple years. This is plausible for Adyen but leaves no room for disappointment, which is exactly what happened in February when slightly-below-consensus guidance triggered an 18% single-day drop.

**Multiples context.** Adyen's forward P/E of ~18x and EV/EBITDA of 13.7x look optically cheap versus payment network peers (Visa at 23x fwd, Mastercard at 30x). However, the comparison is imperfect: Visa and Mastercard are asset-light network toll collectors with 50%+ margins and near-monopoly positions. Adyen is an acquirer/processor with more operational complexity, greater competitive exposure (Stripe, Checkout.com, Braintree), and heavier geographic concentration risk. A more apt comparison is the broader payment processing/fintech cohort, where 15-20x forward earnings is market-rate for high-quality growers. On a P/FCF basis, the 50x multiple is stretched relative to the earnings multiple, reflecting timing differences in capex and working capital.

**Owner earnings trajectory.** The growth from EUR 564M (FY2022) to EUR 1.1B (FY2025) in owner earnings is impressive and demonstrates Adyen's operating leverage — revenue roughly doubled while OE nearly doubled, confirming that incremental margins are strong. If OE can compound at 15-20% going forward (a slower trajectory than the recent past), a 20x OE multiple would produce a value of roughly EUR 700-750 per share in two years, discounted back to today at ~EUR 600-650. This corroborates the quant model's range.

**Scenario stress-testing.** The sensitivity grid shows that even at the most favorable combination tested (10.4% revenue growth, 12.9% WACC), IV reaches only EUR 887 — barely above today's price. The grid's center of mass sits in the EUR 740-810 range. For the stock to be genuinely cheap, you would need to believe either (a) growth will sustainably exceed 10% with a sub-13% appropriate discount rate, or (b) the exit multiple should be well above 18x. Both are aggressive assumptions for a company whose own guidance just disappointed.

**Net cash cushion.** One important offset: Adyen holds EUR 10.5B in cash against EUR 252M in debt. However, most of this cash consists of merchant funds held in trust, not freely deployable capital. The true excess cash position is smaller, though the balance sheet is unquestionably fortress-quality with essentially zero financial leverage.

## Signal Summary
- **Bull case:** Adyen is a structural winner in the EUR 150T+ global payments market, trading at the cheapest valuation in its public history. If growth re-accelerates above 22% and the company recaptures its premium multiple (30-40x earnings historically), the stock could double from here. The net cash position provides downside protection.
- **Bear case:** Growth guidance is decelerating, competitive intensity from Stripe and Checkout.com is intensifying in enterprise payments, and the quant model says the stock is 10-35% overvalued depending on scenario. A 10.7% Monte Carlo probability of being undervalued is a poor setup for a value investor.
- **Confidence:** Medium — The quant model and multiples analysis are reasonably consistent in suggesting modest overvaluation. However, Adyen's business quality is high enough that small changes in growth assumptions create large swings in IV. The wide sensitivity range (EUR 692-887) reflects genuine uncertainty.

## Red Flags
- Monte Carlo P(IV > Price) of only 10.7% — statistically, the stock is more likely overvalued than undervalued
- 2026 guidance (20-22% growth) below prior ambitions and consensus — first clear deceleration signal
- P/FCF of 50x is expensive for a payment processor even with strong growth
- 52-week high of EUR 1,750 means many holders are sitting on large losses, creating potential overhead supply
- Beta of 1.9 means this stock will amplify any broad market downturn

## Score: 4 / 10
Adyen is a high-quality business trading above its DCF-derived intrinsic value across all but the most optimistic scenarios, with a Monte Carlo probability distribution heavily skewed toward overvaluation.
