## Detailed Analysis

**Returns on Capital.** Adyen's ROE has remained firmly in the 22-25% range over FY2022-FY2025, a strong result for a business that does not use financial leverage. ROIC of 17.6% in FY2025 is good but not spectacular at first glance; however, the metric is distorted by Adyen's balance sheet structure. Adyen holds roughly €10.5B in cash and short-term investments, a large portion of which represents merchant settlement funds in transit — money that belongs to merchants but sits on Adyen's balance sheet for 1-3 days during settlement. This inflates invested capital and suppresses computed ROIC. If we strip out merchant float and look at the capital actually deployed in running the business (equity minus excess cash above operating needs), returns on true operating capital are substantially higher — likely north of 40%. The trajectory of ROE (stable at ~23-25%) and the steady growth in net income (+85% over three years) tell the real story: this is a high-return-on-capital compounder.

**Margin Structure.** Adyen's margin profile is among the best in payments. Gross margins above 88% reflect the fact that Adyen reports net revenue (after interchange and scheme fees are passed through). The more telling figure is the operating margin, which expanded from 36.6% (FY2023) to 39.2% (FY2024) to 41.4% (FY2025). EBITDA margin reached 53% in FY2025, up from 50% in FY2024. This expansion is structural: Adyen built its single-platform stack in-house with no acquisitions, meaning incremental revenue falls through to profit at an improving rate. The company guided for EBITDA margin "in-line with 2025" for FY2026 and above 55% by 2028, suggesting continued but gradual leverage. D&A remains modest at €136M (5.2% of revenue), confirming the asset-light nature of the business.

**Cash Generation.** Free cash flow was €905M in FY2025 with an FCF conversion ratio of 85% (FCF/net income). This is a healthy number, though down from the extraordinary 170-340% conversion rates seen in FY2023-FY2024. Those earlier ratios were inflated by working capital movements (growing merchant balances flowing through operating cash flow). Owner earnings — net income plus D&A minus capex — hit €1.07B, growing at a compounded 27% annually since FY2022. Stock-based compensation is modest at €41M (3.7% of net income), so cash earnings closely track reported earnings. Capex is minimal at €126M, or 4.8% of revenue, and is essentially all maintenance (servers, office buildout). There is no heavy growth capex cycle ahead — Adyen's platform is built and scales with transactions.

**Capital Intensity and Revenue Quality.** Adyen is textbook asset-light. The business requires servers, office space, and engineering talent — but no factories, no inventory, no heavy infrastructure. Revenue is purely transaction-based: merchants pay a per-transaction fee (take rate of ~17 bps on processed volume). This is as close to recurring as you can get without a subscription model — merchants process payments daily, and switching away from Adyen requires a full re-integration, which takes months. The three revenue pillars (Digital at €335M, Unified Commerce/POS at €195M, Platforms at €69M) are all growing, with POS volumes up 34% and Platforms up 50% in FY2025. This diversification reduces dependence on any single channel.

**Operating Leverage.** Adyen's cost structure is largely fixed (engineering staff, platform infrastructure), meaning revenue growth drops through to EBITDA at an accelerating rate. In FY2025, revenue grew 18% while EBITDA grew 26% — a clear demonstration of operating leverage. The company has historically managed headcount growth well below revenue growth after a brief hiring spree in 2022-2023 that spooked the market. With ~4,200 employees and revenue per employee above €560K, Adyen runs lean. The path to 55%+ EBITDA margin by 2028 is credible given this cost structure.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** Adyen's single-platform architecture and deepening enterprise relationships drive continued 20%+ revenue growth with expanding margins toward 55-60% EBITDA, producing owner earnings growth of 25%+ annually.
- **Bear case:** Take rate compression from competitive pressure (Stripe, Checkout.com) and macro-driven volume slowdowns bring revenue growth to low-teens, stalling margin expansion and compressing the earnings multiple.
- **Confidence:** Medium-High — The financial data is robust and the trends are clear, but the FY2022 revenue reporting inconsistency limits the lookback window to three clean years. The operating leverage thesis is well-supported by the data.

## Red Flags

- ROIC has gently declined for four consecutive years (21.6% to 17.6%), though this is largely a balance sheet composition artifact rather than deteriorating unit economics.
- FCF was volatile (€905M in FY2025 vs €1.6B in FY2024), driven by working capital swings from merchant settlement balances — this makes headline FCF a poor year-to-year comparison metric.
- 2026 revenue growth guidance (20-22% CC) was below market expectations and prior company ambitions, suggesting possible deceleration in digital volume growth.
- Concentration risk: a single large-volume customer (widely understood to be eBay) has been reducing volumes through Adyen, distorting headline processed volume growth.

## Score: 8 / 10

Adyen is an asset-light, high-margin, cash-generative compounder with genuine operating leverage and sticky, recurring transaction revenue — a near-ideal business engine that falls just short of a 9 due to the emerging signs of digital volume growth deceleration and single-customer concentration effects.
