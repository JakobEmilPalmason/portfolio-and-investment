# Compact Checklist — NVS

**Date:** 2026-03-26

1. **Business**: Novartis sells patented drugs across oncology, immunology, neuroscience, and cardiology to hospitals and health systems worldwide, generating 75.8% gross margins because patients need their medications regardless of economic conditions.
2. **Moat**: Patent protection on 8 blockbuster brands, physician switching costs once clinical superiority is established (Kisqali holds 63% US new-to-brand share in early breast cancer), and $11.2B annual R&D scale create a self-renewing but inherently depreciating moat that must be rebuilt every product cycle.
3. **Economics**: ROIC improved from 7.8% to 19.7% over three years, FCF of $15.2B converts at 109% of net income, and operating margins expanded from 18.3% to 31.1% post-Sandoz spinoff — a genuine compounder when patent cliffs are not actively eroding revenue.
4. **Management**: CEO Narasimhan executed the Sandoz spinoff, expanded margins to 40% core operating, and returned $37B in buybacks over four years that genuinely reduced the share count, though the $12B Avidity acquisition broke his own stated "sub-$2B" bolt-on discipline.
5. **Debt risk**: Net debt/EBITDA of 1.0x, interest coverage of 15.3x, and AA- credit ratings mean Novartis survives any plausible recession — even a 30% revenue decline still covers debt service 8-10x — though net debt rose 36% in 2025 as management levered up for buybacks during the patent cliff.
6. **Price**: At $151 and 15.1x forward earnings, the market must believe the Entresto patent cliff ($7.8B peak revenue collapsing) is permanent rather than temporary and that four drugs growing 40-87% annually will fail to reach their $3-10B peak sales targets.
7. **Margin of safety**: Business quality (75.8% gross margins, diversified therapeutic areas, $15B+ FCF) and the extreme valuation discount (price below every cell in the sensitivity grid, 100% Monte Carlo probability of undervaluation) protect against assumption errors, though the bear-case IV of $155 provides only 2.6% price cushion.
8. **Thesis breaker**: Sell if Kisqali annual revenue stalls below $6B (proving the $10B+ peak target was aspirational), if multiple pivotal pipeline readouts fail in the same year, or if management abandons the focused innovative medicines model through a transformational merger.
