# Compact Checklist — AMD

**Date:** 2026-03-25

1. **Business**: AMD designs CPUs and GPUs sold to cloud hyperscalers, PC OEMs, and console makers, with customers locked in by x86 software compatibility and multi-year server design wins.
2. **Moat**: The x86 duopoly and AMD's role as the only credible second-source to NVIDIA in AI GPUs provide a real but execution-dependent advantage that resets every product generation.
3. **Economics**: Tangible ROIC is 25-30% (GAAP's 4.7% is distorted by $24B Xilinx goodwill), FCF conversion hits 155%, and margins are expanding as data center grows toward 60% of revenue.
4. **Management**: Lisa Su is a generational CEO who took AMD from $2/share near-bankruptcy to a $360B company, with 96% variable pay and buybacks fully offsetting SBC dilution.
5. **Debt risk**: AMD carries $10.6B cash against $3.8B debt with 32.6x interest coverage and a fabless model that lets it slash costs in a downturn — survival is not in question.
6. **Price**: At $220, you must believe AMD captures 10-15% AI GPU share against NVIDIA's CUDA moat, sustains 30%+ revenue growth for three years, and expands non-GAAP operating margins to 33%+ simultaneously.
7. **Margin of safety**: None — the quant model shows 0% probability of undervaluation at $220, the adjusted base-case IV is $185, and even the bear IV of $110 offers no cushion at today's price.
8. **Thesis breaker**: Sell if AMD's AI GPU market share stalls below 5% by end of 2027, confirming that NVIDIA's CUDA ecosystem is an insurmountable moat and the $200B+ AI growth premium is unjustified.
