## Detailed Analysis

**Lisa Su's Track Record — Exceptional Operator:** Lisa Su became CEO in October 2014 when AMD was trading near $2/share with a real risk of bankruptcy. Over the following decade, she executed one of the most impressive turnarounds in semiconductor history. The strategic roadmap was clear: bet on the Zen CPU architecture to challenge Intel's monopoly, invest in GPU compute to compete with NVIDIA, and acquire Xilinx to diversify into embedded/adaptive computing. Each of these bets has paid off. Revenue grew from $5.5B in 2014 to $34.6B in 2025. Server CPU market share went from <5% to ~36-39%. AMD became the only credible alternative to NVIDIA in AI accelerators. Su also serves as Board Chair (since 2022), consolidating leadership authority. The risk of key-person dependency is real — AMD's strategic vision and execution are deeply tied to Su personally.

**Capital Allocation — Mixed but Improving:** The Xilinx acquisition was AMD's defining capital allocation decision. At ~$50B (all-stock), it was strategically defensible: Xilinx brought FPGAs, adaptive computing, high-margin embedded revenue ($3.5B in FY2025), and diversification away from cyclical PC/GPU markets. The integration has been smooth operationally. However, the deal created ~$24B in goodwill and ~$21B in acquisition-related intangibles on a $63B equity base, which is why ROIC (4.7%) and ROE (7.2%) look anemic. These returns on capital are genuinely poor for a company trading at 85x trailing earnings — the goodwill-laden balance sheet means AMD needs years of earnings growth to justify the capital deployed. Aside from Xilinx, AMD has made smaller tuck-in acquisitions (Pensando for DPUs, Silo AI for European AI software) that are strategically coherent and financially immaterial.

**Share Repurchases and Dilution Management:** AMD announced a $6B buyback authorization in May 2025, bringing total authorization to ~$10B. Actual buyback execution in the first half of 2025 was ~$1.2B ($749M in Q1, $478M in Q2). Diluted shares outstanding have been essentially flat: 1.625B (2023) → 1.637B (2024) → 1.636B (2025). This is notable and positive — AMD has successfully offset SBC dilution through buybacks, keeping the share count stable despite $1.6B/year in stock-based compensation. The $10B authorization represents ~2.8% of market cap, which is meaningful but not aggressive.

**Stock-Based Compensation:** SBC of $1.6B is 4.6% of FY2025 revenue. This is elevated relative to mature tech (Microsoft ~3%, Apple <2%) but comparable to growth-stage semis and has been declining as a percentage as revenue scales rapidly (it was ~5.4% of 2024 revenue). SBC is a real cost to shareholders — it represents ~37% of reported net income ($4.3B) and ~24% of FCF ($6.7B). The fact that buybacks roughly offset the dilution mitigates but does not eliminate the economic cost, since those buyback dollars could otherwise accrue to shareholders.

**Compensation Design and Governance:** Executive compensation is well-structured. ~96% of CEO pay is variable/performance-linked. Long-term incentives emphasize multi-year relative TSR with EPS growth modifiers, and the latest PRSU grant (362,906 target units) has a 5-year performance period with 0-200% payout range. Governance provisions are strong: mandatory clawback, no hedging/pledging permitted, minimum 1-year equity vesting, double-trigger change-of-control. Lisa Su's total 2023 compensation was $30.3M (238x median employee) — high in absolute terms but defensible given the value created.

## Signal Summary
- **Bull case:** Lisa Su is a generational CEO who has executed one of tech's best turnarounds, with a well-aligned compensation structure and a capital allocation track record that, despite the Xilinx goodwill burden, has been strategically sound.
- **Bear case:** ROIC of 4.7% and ROE of 7.2% are genuinely poor returns on capital that reflect a goodwill-heavy balance sheet; SBC at 37% of net income is a meaningful drag; and the company's execution is heavily dependent on one individual.
- **Confidence:** High — Su's track record is well-documented, compensation design is transparent, and capital allocation decisions are analyzable.

## Red Flags
- ROIC (4.7%) and ROE (7.2%) are far too low for a company commanding an 85x P/E — reflects the Xilinx goodwill overhang on the capital base
- SBC of $1.6B represents ~37% of net income — real economic cost even though buybacks offset share count dilution
- Key-person risk: AMD's strategy and market credibility are heavily tied to Lisa Su; no obvious internal successor has been publicly groomed
- Lisa Su routinely sells shares under 10b5-1 plans (85K shares sold March 2026) — standard practice but net selling rather than buying
- Board Chair and CEO roles are combined, reducing independent oversight (common in tech but worth noting)

## Score: 7 / 10
Lisa Su is an exceptional operator whose strategic vision transformed AMD from near-bankruptcy to a $360B semiconductor leader, with well-aligned compensation and disciplined buybacks offsetting SBC dilution, but the Xilinx acquisition's goodwill burden depresses capital returns (ROIC 4.7%) and key-person dependency is a real governance risk.
