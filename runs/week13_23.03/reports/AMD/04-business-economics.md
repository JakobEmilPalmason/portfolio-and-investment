## Detailed Analysis

**Returns on Capital — The Goodwill Distortion.** AMD's reported ROIC of 4.7% looks mediocre in isolation, but this figure is almost entirely an artifact of the $49B in goodwill and intangible assets booked from the 2022 Xilinx acquisition. Total equity stands at $63B, of which roughly $24B is goodwill and another $18-19B is amortizing intangibles. Strip those out and AMD's tangible invested capital is far smaller, producing a tangible ROIC in the 25-30% range — consistent with a capital-light fabless semiconductor business earning strong economic returns. The goodwill overhang will persist for years, but it does not reflect ongoing capital requirements. What matters is the marginal return on incremental capital deployed, which is excellent: every dollar of incremental R&D and capex is generating substantial revenue and profit growth. ROIC has improved from 0.5% (FY2023) to 2.9% (FY2024) to 4.7% (FY2025) as earnings grow into the acquisition's cost basis.

**Margin Structure and Trajectory.** AMD operates a multi-segment business with meaningfully different margin profiles. Data Center is the crown jewel, delivering 33% segment operating margins in Q4 2025 and improving. Client/Gaming margins are solid but lower, and Embedded (the Xilinx legacy FPGA business) is cycling through a post-COVID inventory digestion that depressed FY2025 revenue to $3.5B (down 3% YoY). Consolidated GAAP gross margin of 50% and non-GAAP of 52% are respectable but trail NVIDIA by 15-20 percentage points. This gap reflects AMD's challenger economics: competitive GPU pricing, ROCm software investment to break CUDA lock-in, and a less dominant market position. As Data Center grows from 48% to potentially 55-60% of revenue, blended margins should expand toward 55% non-GAAP gross and mid-20s operating margin. The GAAP-to-non-GAAP gap ($3.7B vs $7.8B operating income) is driven primarily by $2.1B in Xilinx/Pensando intangible amortization — a real but non-cash drag that will diminish over the next 5-8 years.

**Cash Generation Quality.** This is AMD's standout metric. FCF of $6.7B on $4.3B net income yields 155% FCF conversion — meaning cash earnings substantially exceed reported profits. Operating cash flow of $7.7B against just $974M in capex reflects the fabless model's capital efficiency: TSMC bears the manufacturing capex burden, AMD invests in design. Owner earnings of $6.4B (after maintenance capex) confirm the business is generating real economic value well in excess of GAAP earnings. The $1.9B in buybacks represents a 28% payout of FCF, leaving ample room for continued investment and shareholder returns.

**Revenue Quality and Operating Leverage.** AMD's revenue is diversified across four segments but increasingly tilted toward recurring/mission-critical data center compute. EPYC server CPUs and Instinct AI accelerators are specification-sold into hyperscalers and enterprises — high switching costs once designed in. Client PC revenue ($10.6B) is more cyclical but benefits from the Ryzen AI PC upgrade cycle. Gaming ($3.9B) is the most volatile segment. The revenue trajectory — from $22.7B (FY2023) to $25.8B (FY2024) to $34.6B (FY2025) — demonstrates strong operating leverage: revenue grew 34% while non-GAAP operating income grew even faster to $7.8B. AMD guided Q1 2026 revenue of ~$9.8B, implying continued momentum. The company targets $20B+ in data center GPU revenue within 2 years, which would represent a further substantial mix shift toward higher-margin compute.

## Signal Summary
- **Bull case:** Tangible ROIC is 25-30%, FCF conversion is exceptional at 155%, and margin expansion is structural as Data Center grows toward 60% of the mix — the business economics are far better than headline ROIC suggests.
- **Bear case:** GAAP ROIC remains sub-5%, the Xilinx goodwill overhang masks true capital efficiency for years, and gross margins structurally trail NVIDIA's, limiting pricing power in the AI accelerator market.
- **Confidence:** Medium-High — The financial data is clean and verifiable; the key uncertainty is whether Data Center margin expansion continues or gets competed away.

## Red Flags
- GAAP ROIC of 4.7% means the Xilinx acquisition has not yet earned its cost of capital on a book-value basis, even three years post-close
- $2.1B annual intangible amortization creates a persistent GAAP vs. non-GAAP divergence that requires careful earnings quality assessment
- R&D at 23% of revenue is necessary but creates high operating leverage in both directions — a revenue slowdown would compress margins quickly
- Embedded segment ($3.5B, -3% YoY) has not recovered to pre-correction levels, raising questions about the Xilinx acquisition's growth thesis
- Gross margin gap vs. NVIDIA (~18 points) reflects a structural competitive disadvantage in AI GPU pricing power

## Score: 7 / 10
AMD demonstrates strong underlying business economics — excellent FCF conversion, improving tangible returns, and structural margin expansion from Data Center mix shift — but the Xilinx goodwill overhang on GAAP ROIC, the persistent gross margin gap vs. NVIDIA, and the still-recovering Embedded segment prevent a higher score.
