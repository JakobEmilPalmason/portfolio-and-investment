# Compact Checklist — MTD

**Date:** 2026-03-29

1. **Business**: Mettler-Toledo sells precision weighing, analytical, and inspection instruments to pharma labs and food factories, then locks in a $1B+ recurring service annuity on the installed base.
2. **Moat**: FDA/GMP validation requirements make switching instruments so costly that pharma customers stay with MTD's 50%+ share in lab balances rather than re-validate a competitor's equipment.
3. **Economics**: 46% ROIC, 59% gross margins, and $849M FCF at near-100% conversion on only 2.7% capex intensity confirm elite capital efficiency with real cash behind the earnings.
4. **Management**: Capital allocation is mechanically excellent — $800M+ annual buybacks with only $22M SBC genuinely retire shares — but insider ownership of 0.27% means management has minimal personal capital at risk.
5. **Debt risk**: At 1.7x net debt/EBITDA and 16x interest coverage, MTD could pause buybacks and retire its entire $2.2B debt stack in under three years from FCF alone, surviving a 30% revenue decline without covenant breach.
6. **Price**: At $1,232, the stock exceeds all 25 cells in the DCF sensitivity grid (max $1,010) and requires sustained 8-9% revenue growth plus a 20x+ terminal multiple — roughly double management's 4% 2026 growth guide.
7. **Margin of safety**: The business quality is genuine (dominant share, regulatory lock-in, recurring revenue), but at 66% above base IV with a 3.3% owner-earnings yield below the 4.5% risk-free rate, there is zero price-based protection.
8. **Thesis breaker**: Sell if ROIC falls below 30% for two consecutive years, if China revenue (16% of sales but 29% of segment profit) drops 50%+ without offset, or if management suspends buybacks to fund a large dilutive acquisition.
