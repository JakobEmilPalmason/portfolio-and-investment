# Compact Checklist — NOVO-B.CO

**Date:** 2026-03-24

1. **Business**: Novo Nordisk sells GLP-1 injectable and oral drugs (Ozempic, Wegovy) for diabetes and obesity — chronic conditions requiring lifelong treatment that drive 90%+ of revenue from a single molecule family (semaglutide).
2. **Moat**: A century of diabetes expertise, 81% gross margins, and massive biologic manufacturing scale (DKK 90B capex in FY2025 across 14 fill-finish sites) create barriers competitors need 3-5 years to replicate, though Eli Lilly has taken the U.S. prescription lead at 58% share with clinically superior tirzepatide.
3. **Economics**: ROIC of 36% and owner earnings of DKK 102B confirm elite economics even in a heavy investment year, but FCF conversion collapsed to 28% due to a capex supercycle and 2026 guidance projects the first revenue decline (-5% to -13%) in over a decade.
4. **Management**: The Novo Nordisk Foundation forced out the CEO in August 2025 after a 55% stock decline, the board chairman also departed, insider ownership is zero, and no executive bought shares during the drawdown — this is a leadership team in transition without skin in the game.
5. **Debt risk**: Debt quintupled to DKK 131B but sits at just 0.8x EBITDA with 32x interest coverage, and even a 30% revenue decline for two years would leave DKK 50-60B in operating cash flow — survival is not in question.
6. **Price**: At DKK 239 (10.4x trailing earnings), the market must believe revenue will decline permanently and margins will compress below 30% — a scenario inconsistent with an 81% gross margin business in a $100B+ market growing to $150B+ by 2030.
7. **Margin of safety**: The stress-tested bear IV of DKK 350 provides 31.7% price MOS, the downside/upside asymmetry is roughly 1:4 (27% downside to DKK 175 vs 109% upside to DKK 500 base case), and no Monte Carlo simulation out of 10,000 produced an IV below the current price.
8. **Thesis breaker**: Sell if Novo's GLP-1 market share drops below 25% (currently 39%), signaling permanent competitive defeat to Lilly, or if an FDA black box warning emerges on the GLP-1 drug class.
