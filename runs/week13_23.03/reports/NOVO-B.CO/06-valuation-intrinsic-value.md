## Detailed Analysis

### Owner Earnings Assessment

The quant model identifies adjusted owner earnings of DKK 102.4B for FY2025, separating DKK 22B in maintenance capex from DKK 68B in growth capex (primarily manufacturing capacity buildout for GLP-1 production). This is the critical distinction: reported FCF of DKK 29B dramatically understates true earnings power because Novo is investing aggressively in future capacity. On a per-share basis, owner earnings are approximately DKK 30.4, meaning the stock trades at roughly 7.9x owner earnings — a level typically reserved for businesses in secular decline. The question is whether Novo's earnings power is truly declining or whether the market is conflating a transient competitive setback with permanent impairment. Revenue grew 6.4% in FY2025 (to DKK 309B) and net income was essentially flat at DKK 102.4B. These are not the numbers of a dying franchise. Gross margins remain at 81%, operating margins at 41.3%. The business is still enormously profitable.

### Scenario Analysis (Stress-Testing the Quant Model)

**Bear Case — Quant: DKK 539, My Adjusted: DKK 350.** The quant bear case assumes 4.4% Y1 growth fading to 3%, a 41.3% operating margin held flat, and a 12x exit EV/EBITDA at a 6.6% WACC. I believe this is not pessimistic enough. The real bear scenario involves: (1) revenue declining 5-10% in FY2026 (consistent with company guidance of -5% to -13%), (2) operating margins compressing to 35% as pricing pressure intensifies and R&D spend stays elevated, (3) semaglutide generics eroding 20-30% of emerging market revenue by 2028, (4) CagriSema failing to close the efficacy gap with tirzepatide, and (5) a higher WACC of 8.5% reflecting increased business risk. Under these assumptions with a 10x exit multiple, I arrive at approximately DKK 350 per share. Even this harsh scenario is 47% above the current price.

**Base Case — Quant: DKK 800, My Adjusted: DKK 500.** The quant base case is too optimistic. It assumes 7.4% Y1 growth, but the company itself guided for a 5-13% revenue decline in 2026. I model a more realistic path: (1) revenue declining 8% in 2026, recovering to flat in 2027, then growing 3-5% as oral semaglutide and potentially CagriSema gain traction, (2) operating margins settling at 37-39% (below recent 41% due to pricing pressure but stabilizing as the manufacturing buildout completes), (3) a 12x exit EV/EBITDA (reasonable for a mature pharma compounder, below the quant model's 15x), and (4) a WACC of 7.5%. This yields approximately DKK 500 per share — a 109% upside from today.

**Bull Case — Quant: DKK 1,099, My Adjusted: DKK 750.** The bull case requires: (1) the GLP-1 market expanding to $150B+ by 2030, with Novo maintaining 35-40% share, (2) CagriSema higher-dose trials (planned H2 2026) delivering competitive weight loss above 25%, (3) oral semaglutide becoming a major growth driver, (4) successful defense of US patent protection through 2031+, and (5) margins stabilizing near 40%. At a 14x exit EV/EBITDA and 6.5% WACC, this yields approximately DKK 750. The quant model's DKK 1,099 is achievable only if Novo regains meaningful market share and the GLP-1 market exceeds current projections.

### Multiples in Context

Novo Nordisk trades at 10.4x trailing earnings and 11.2x forward earnings. For context: Eli Lilly trades at roughly 35-40x forward earnings; the healthcare sector average is ~17.6x. Novo's historical 5-year average P/E was in the 30-40x range. The current multiple implies the market expects either (a) earnings to fall 60-70% and stay there, or (b) the business to become a low-growth commodity pharma player. At 7.5x EV/EBITDA, Novo trades cheaper than generic drug makers and below large-cap pharma peers like Merck (~12x) and Pfizer (~10x). The P/FCF of 2,816x is a red herring driven entirely by the massive DKK 90B capex cycle — the business is funding construction of manufacturing capacity that will serve demand for a decade.

### What Must Be True for Today's Price

Reverse-engineering: at DKK 238.90 with DKK 23 in EPS, the market is applying roughly a 10.4x multiple. For this to be a fair terminal multiple, the market must believe either (a) earnings are at a cyclical peak and will decline 40-50%, or (b) Novo deserves the same multiple as a no-growth utility. Running the DCF backwards, the current price implies roughly 0% revenue growth forever with 10-15% margin compression — essentially a flat-to-declining business. The sensitivity grid confirms this: even at the most pessimistic combination (3.4% growth, 7.6% WACC), the model produces DKK 642. To get to DKK 239, you need to assume either (a) negative revenue growth for multiple years followed by zero growth, (b) permanent margin compression below 30%, or (c) a structural breakup of the GLP-1 franchise. While 2026 will be a tough year, the obesity/diabetes market is projected to exceed $150B by 2030, and Novo remains one of only two scaled players.

### Implied Expectations vs Reality

The market is pricing in disaster. Monte Carlo P(IV > Price) = 100% from the quant model, meaning no simulation out of 10,000 produced an IV below the current price. Even adjusting my inputs downward significantly, the probability of permanent value destruction to DKK 239 levels requires assumptions that are extreme by any reasonable standard. The stock is down 55% from its 52-week high, driven by legitimate concerns (CagriSema non-inferiority failure, 2026 revenue guidance, Lilly's competitive gains, emerging market patent expirations). But the sell-off has overshot. Novo still generates DKK 100B+ in net income, has DKK 81% gross margins, holds a dominant position in the DKK 200B+ GLP-1 market, and is investing aggressively for the future. The market is treating a cyclical trough as a structural endpoint.

## Signal Summary
- **Bull case:** At DKK 239, you are buying a DKK 100B earnings business at 8x owner earnings with a 6.7% dividend yield during a temporary competitive reset — the GLP-1 market is still early innings and Novo is one of two scaled players.
- **Bear case:** Lilly's sustained efficacy advantage permanently relegates Novo to a declining #2 position, margin compression from pricing/generics shrinks earnings by 40%+, and the massive capex cycle produces below-cost-of-capital returns.
- **Confidence:** Medium-High — The valuation discount is real and large, but the competitive dynamics are genuinely shifting; the key uncertainty is whether Novo can stabilize market share with its pipeline and oral formulations.

## Red Flags
- Company itself guided for 5-13% revenue decline in 2026 — unusual for a growth pharma
- CagriSema failed non-inferiority vs tirzepatide (23.0% vs 25.5% weight loss), the pipeline's most critical readout
- Semaglutide patent expiry in Canada (Jan 2026), China, Brazil, and India by 2027 — major emerging markets open to generics
- Eli Lilly now holds ~60% GLP-1 market share vs Novo's ~39%, a reversal from 2023
- Total debt surged from DKK 27B (2023) to DKK 131B (2025) to fund capacity buildout — leverage risk is higher than historical norms
- ROIC halved from 70.5% (2023) to 36% (2025) — still excellent but trajectory is concerning

## Score: 8 / 10
Even after making aggressive downward adjustments to the quant model — cutting the base IV from DKK 800 to DKK 500 and the bear IV from DKK 539 to DKK 350 — the stock at DKK 239 offers substantial upside with a meaningful margin of safety; the market is pricing in a worst-case structural decline that is inconsistent with the business's actual earnings power, competitive position, and the sheer size of the addressable obesity/diabetes market.
