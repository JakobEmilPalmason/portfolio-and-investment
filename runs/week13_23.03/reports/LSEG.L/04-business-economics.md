## Detailed Analysis

**Returns on capital: the goodwill distortion.** LSEG's reported ROIC (5.3%) and ROE (5.8%) look mediocre at first glance but are almost entirely a function of the massive Refinitiv acquisition in January 2021, which loaded approximately £27B of goodwill and intangible assets onto the balance sheet. Invested capital stands at £30.9B, of which the vast majority is acquisition-related. On a tangible capital basis, LSEG's returns would be dramatically higher -- the business generates £3.5B+ of adjusted operating profit on relatively modest tangible fixed assets. The correct lens here is cash returns on the operating business, not headline ROIC. The improving trend (ROIC from 3.1% in FY2024 to 5.3% in FY2025) reflects both earnings recovery and buyback-driven equity reduction. Over time, as goodwill amortisation and buybacks shrink the capital base while earnings grow, reported returns should steadily improve.

**Margin structure and operating leverage.** LSEG's margin profile is outstanding for a financial infrastructure business. Gross margins run at 88%, reflecting the low marginal cost of serving additional users on existing platforms. Adjusted EBITDA margins hit 50.3% in FY2025, expanding 150bps on a reported basis and 210bps in constant currency. Adjusted operating margins reached approximately 39% (£3,506M adjusted operating profit on £8,986M total income). The gap between reported operating margin (23.2%) and adjusted operating margin (~39%) reflects amortisation of acquired intangibles and integration costs -- real cash costs that are declining each year. Management guides for a further 150bps of EBITDA margin expansion per year through 2029, which is credible given platform consolidation, AI-driven productivity gains, and declining integration spend. The SwapClear revenue share renegotiation added approximately 100bps of structural margin improvement.

**Cash generation and owner earnings.** Equity free cash flow of £2.4B represents strong conversion from £4.5B of adjusted EBITDA (approximately 53% conversion after capex, interest, and tax). Capital expenditure of £919M (10.2% of revenue) is declining in intensity and management targets 8% by 2029. The distinction between maintenance and growth capex is blurred at LSEG -- much of the spend is on platform migration (moving Refinitiv's legacy infrastructure to Microsoft Azure cloud), which is both necessary to maintain competitiveness and growth-enabling. Reported owner earnings (net income + D&A - capex) of £1.6B in FY2025 understate true cash generation because net income includes large non-cash amortisation charges on acquired intangibles. A better proxy is adjusted operating cash flow minus capex, which yields approximately £2.4B. Buybacks totalled £2.2B in FY2025, indicating management confidence in cash generation sustainability and a commitment to returning excess capital.

**Revenue quality and durability.** This is one of LSEG's strongest attributes. Approximately 60% of total revenue is subscription-based, with the Data & Analytics division (nearly half of group revenue) carrying 90%+ IP-protected data and feeds revenue. FTSE Russell's index licensing fees are largely recurring (linked to AUM in index-tracking products). Post-Trade clearing fees are transaction-linked but benefit from structural growth in cleared derivatives volumes. The subscription base provides a pricing yield of approximately 3.5% annually, essentially built-in organic growth before any volume or new product uplift. Annual Subscription Value (ASV) growth of 5.6% provides good forward visibility. Customer retention rates are high because switching costs for embedded data feeds and analytics terminals are substantial. The combination of subscription dominance, high switching costs, and pricing power creates a revenue base that is unusually predictable and defensible for a business of this scale.

**Capital intensity trajectory.** LSEG is past the peak of its Refinitiv integration capex cycle. Total capex fell from 11.3% of revenue in FY2024 to 10.2% in FY2025, with management guiding toward 8% by 2029. This declining capex intensity, combined with growing revenue, means free cash flow should expand at a rate meaningfully faster than revenue growth. The cloud migration to Azure, while costly upfront, should reduce long-term infrastructure maintenance costs and improve scalability. This is a classic case of a business transitioning from a heavy investment phase to a harvest phase, with significant FCF margin expansion ahead.

**Operating leverage.** The 2025 results demonstrated textbook operating leverage: 7.1% organic revenue growth converted to 11.8% adjusted EBITDA growth and 15.7% adjusted EPS growth. This 2x+ earnings multiplier on revenue growth is structural, not a one-off, because the incremental cost of serving additional users on data platforms and processing additional clearing volume is minimal. Management expects this dynamic to persist through at least 2029 as platform consolidation continues and AI tools reduce operational costs.

## Signal Summary
- **Bull case:** Subscription-heavy revenue mix with 50%+ EBITDA margins, declining capex intensity, and structural operating leverage should drive mid-teens EPS growth and accelerating FCF for years.
- **Bear case:** Reported returns on capital remain optically low due to Refinitiv goodwill, and any slowdown in financial markets activity or data spend could compress the transactional revenue that drives margin expansion.
- **Confidence:** High -- FY2025 reported results, segment disclosures, and management guidance are all publicly available and internally consistent; margin and cash flow trends are well-documented across multiple reporting periods.

## Red Flags
- Reported ROIC/ROE remain below cost of capital on a GAAP basis due to goodwill; if the Refinitiv acquisition ultimately proves overpriced, these returns may never normalise fully
- Large gap between reported and adjusted operating profit (£2.2B vs £3.5B) means significant ongoing non-cash charges; investors must trust that adjustments are legitimate
- FY2025 capex spiked to £1.9B on Yahoo Finance data (versus £919M in LSEG's own reporting) -- this discrepancy likely reflects different treatment of capitalised costs vs CCP-related flows; clarity on true capex matters
- Stock-based compensation (£176M) is modest but growing and should be treated as a real cost

## Score: 8 / 10
LSEG's business economics are strong: 50%+ EBITDA margins, 60% subscription revenue, declining capex intensity, and demonstrated 2x operating leverage on revenue growth. The only reason this is not a 9 is the optical drag from Refinitiv goodwill on reported capital returns and the capex data discrepancy that warrants further investigation.
