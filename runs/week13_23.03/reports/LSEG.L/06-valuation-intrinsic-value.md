## Detailed Analysis

### Quant Model Disclaimer

The automated quant DCF produced per-share intrinsic values of 134-289 GBp, which is clearly nonsensical against a share price of 8,246 GBp. This results from a currency/unit mismatch: the yfinance financial statements report in USD while the stock price is denominated in GBp (pence). The quant model output is disregarded entirely. The valuation below is built from scratch using GBP-denominated company-reported figures.

### Owner Earnings Estimate

LSEG reports in GBP. For FY2025, the key cash generation metrics are:

- Adjusted EBITDA: GBP 4,523M (50.3% margin)
- Operating cash flow: ~GBP 3.6B (converted from yfinance USD data at ~0.79 GBP/USD)
- Capital expenditure: ~GBP 1.5B (elevated due to technology transformation investment)
- Equity free cash flow (company-reported): GBP 2.4B
- Owner earnings (NI + D&A - CapEx): ~GBP 1.6B (depressed by peak capex cycle)

The company has guided that capital intensity is declining, which should lift owner earnings toward GBP 2.0-2.5B over the next 2-3 years as the Refinitiv integration technology spend rolls off.

The forward P/E gap (15.5x vs peer average of ~28x) is partially explained by differences in accounting (LSEG carries heavy Refinitiv acquisition amortization that depresses reported earnings relative to cash earnings), but even on EV/EBITDA, LSEG screens as the cheapest name in this peer group.

### What Must Be True for Today's Price

At 8,246 GBp (market cap ~GBP 41B, EV ~GBP 47.6B), the market is implying:

- EV/EBITDA of ~10.5x on FY2025 adjusted EBITDA of GBP 4.5B -- well below peers
- Organic growth of only ~4% going forward (vs 7.1% delivered in FY2025)
- No further margin expansion (vs management's track record of 150-200bp annual improvement)
- Limited value creation from AI/data monetization initiatives

The market appears to be pricing in either sustained deceleration (perhaps driven by the Q4 FY2026 revenue warning) or a structural de-rating of the franchise. This level of pessimism seems excessive for a business with 88% gross margins, ~70% recurring revenue, and a dominant position in financial data infrastructure.

### Implied Expectations vs Likely Reality

The disconnect is stark. LSEG just delivered 7.1% organic growth, 50.3% EBITDA margins (up 150bp YoY), GBP 2.4B equity FCF, and announced GBP 3B in buybacks. The stock trades at a 30% discount to its 52-week high. The Q4 revenue warning creates near-term noise, but the structural earnings power of a data/analytics franchise with high switching costs and growing index business (FTSE Russell) is not impaired. The base case suggests 19% upside from current levels, with the bear case offering only 13% downside -- an asymmetric setup.

## Signal Summary
- **Bull case:** LSEG re-rates to peer-average multiples as margin expansion continues and capex normalizes, delivering 50%+ upside over 2-3 years.
- **Bear case:** Revenue growth stalls below 5%, the Q4 warning signals deeper structural issues in capital markets, and the stock drifts to 7,200 GBp.
- **Confidence:** Medium -- company-reported GBP financials are solid, but the Q4 revenue warning and currency complexity in available data introduce uncertainty.

## Red Flags
- Q4 FY2026 (ending March 2026) revenue warning issued just 5 days ago -- magnitude unknown
- Heavy acquisition-related amortization obscures true earnings power (trailing P/E of 34.8x vs forward 15.5x gap is unusually wide)
- Rising net debt (GBP 6.6B) while simultaneously running GBP 3B buyback program
- Capital expenditure remains elevated at ~20% of revenue
- GBP/USD translation effects complicate financial analysis for international investors
- P/B of 210x reflects massive goodwill from Refinitiv acquisition -- tangible book value is negligible

## Score: 7 / 10
LSEG appears meaningfully undervalued relative to peers and its own fundamental trajectory, with base-case upside of ~19% and asymmetric risk/reward, though the recent Q4 revenue warning and data complexity warrant caution.
