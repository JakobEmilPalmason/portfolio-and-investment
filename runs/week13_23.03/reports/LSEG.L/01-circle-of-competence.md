## Detailed Analysis

LSEG is a global financial markets infrastructure and data company. Following the transformative 2021 acquisition of Refinitiv (from Thomson Reuters/Blackstone), LSEG shifted from being primarily a stock exchange and clearing house operator to a business where the majority of revenue comes from selling financial data, analytics, indices, and risk intelligence to banks, asset managers, hedge funds, corporates, and other financial institutions. The business is best understood as four interlocking divisions.

**Data & Analytics** (~45% of revenue, ~GBP 4.0bn in FY2025) is the largest division and the former Refinitiv core. It sells real-time and reference data feeds, Workspace terminals (competing with Bloomberg Terminal), and analytics APIs. Growth was 5.0% organically in FY2025, with analytics accelerating to high single digits driven by API sales. Revenue here is overwhelmingly subscription-based with multi-year contracts and high retention rates. The key revenue drivers are terminal seats, data feed licenses, and API consumption.

**Markets** (~35% of revenue) encompasses capital markets (equities, fixed income trading venues), FX (FXall, Matching), OTC derivatives, and the Tradeweb stake. This division grew 8.9% in FY2025 with double-digit growth in FX, Tradeweb, and OTC derivatives. Revenue is a mix of transaction fees (volume-dependent) and connectivity/access fees (recurring). The LCH clearing house, the world's largest interest rate swap clearer, sits here and provides critical infrastructure with regulatory-mandated clearing requirements supporting volumes.

**FTSE Russell** (~10% of revenue, ~GBP 960m) is the index business. Revenue comes from asset-based fees tied to AUM tracking FTSE indices, plus licensing and subscription analytics. It grew 7.3% organically in FY2025. This is a high-margin, capital-light business that benefits from rising global AUM.

**Risk Intelligence** (~6% of revenue, ~GBP 570m) provides KYC, compliance screening, and financial crime data. It was the fastest-growing division at 11.7% organic growth in FY2025, benefiting from increasing regulatory compliance requirements globally.

The business model can be explained simply: LSEG collects, curates, and distributes financial data and operates the plumbing (exchanges, clearing houses, trading venues) through which financial markets function. Customers pay recurring subscriptions for data access and transaction fees for using the infrastructure. The combination means that roughly 70% of revenue is recurring and contractual, with the remainder driven by market activity levels (trading volumes, capital markets issuance).

Key revenue drivers are: (1) net new data subscriptions and ASV growth, (2) trading volumes across FX, derivatives, and equities, (3) global AUM levels (for FTSE Russell), and (4) regulatory tightening (for Risk Intelligence). Revenue predictability is high for the subscription base and moderate for the transaction-driven Markets segment. Customer concentration is low -- LSEG serves thousands of financial institutions globally, with the largest customers being major global banks that use multiple LSEG products simultaneously.

## Signal Summary
- **Bull case:** LSEG is a subscription-heavy data utility for global finance with 70% recurring revenue, strong retention, and multiple secular growth tailwinds (AI data demand, regulatory compliance, index investing).
- **Bear case:** The Data & Analytics division (the largest) grows at only mid-single digits, and AI could commoditize some data distribution if LLMs reduce terminal demand.
- **Confidence:** High — the business model is well-documented, the revenue mix is transparent, and the competitive dynamics are observable.

## Red Flags
- The Markets division (~35% of revenue) is partly transaction-dependent and can decline in low-volatility or risk-off periods
- Data & Analytics organic growth (5.0%) lags the other three divisions, raising questions about whether the core Refinitiv business is ex-growth
- The Workspace terminal product still trails Bloomberg Terminal in mindshare and feature depth among front-office users
- Currency complexity: reporting in GBP but significant USD and EUR revenue creates FX translation noise

## Score: 8 / 10
LSEG is a highly understandable financial infrastructure and data business with transparent revenue streams, predictable subscription economics, and clear competitive dynamics -- well within the circle of competence for anyone who follows financial services.
