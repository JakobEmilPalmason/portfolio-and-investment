Market data was unavailable for all 42 candidates (Saxo API returned HTTP 400 on every price request), so construction relies entirely on fundamental analysis from the analyzed-universe snapshot. The macro backdrop — active US tariff escalation, AI capex uncertainty, and healthcare regulatory pressure — creates divergent outcomes: exceptional businesses are being punished alongside genuinely weak ones, creating exploitable mispricings on both sides of the book.

The long book is concentrated in quality businesses trading at material discounts to their own historical valuations driven by identifiable and potentially temporary fears. Adobe (13%) is the most compelling entry point in the universe: 96% recurring ARR, $9.85B free cash flow on $172M capex, and format/workflow switching costs embedded in 98% of Fortune 500 companies — yet priced at 12x forward earnings, 60% below the 5-year average, as if AI disruption is already complete. Constellation Software (11%) trades at a 23% discount to base-case DCF as the market prices in a single quarter of FCFA2S decline and Mark Leonard's succession — a temporary dislocation in a business with 75% recurring revenue across 1,000+ niche VMS products. ServiceNow (8%) has fallen 50% from its January 2025 high despite a 98% renewal rate and $12.85B cRPO still growing 25% YoY; the multiple compression itself is the margin of safety.

Gilead (10%) and Novo Nordisk (7%) provide healthcare balance. Gilead offers Biktarvy's contractual earnings floor through 2036 at a 5.2% FCF yield with lenacapavir PrEP optionality — the market prices it as a zero-growth cash cow at 16.7x forward P/E, discounting none of the next HIV prevention cycle. Novo Nordisk trades at 11x trough earnings following a 60%+ stock decline, with the market assigning near-zero credit to the world's largest obesity franchise beyond the semaglutide patent cliff. The 2:1 upside/downside ratio is thin by Buffett standards but meaningfully positive at current levels. Copart (9%) and Visa (6%) provide physical and network moat exposure respectively, and MSFT (5%) serves as a quality anchor.

The short book targets businesses where the current price exceeds all DCF scenarios including the bull case. AbbVie at $234 sits above bear ($90), base ($119), and bull ($177) — the market requires above-bull execution with GAAP interest coverage at only 2.3x and $67B in total debt. Caterpillar at ~$705 is the most acute mispricing: all three scenarios (bear $151, base $272, bull $377) are materially below current price, and management has already guided a $2.6B tariff headwind against an $11B operating profit base. Astera Labs and ARM Holdings complete the AI-narrative short basket — both priced for 25%+ revenue CAGRs over five years while fundamental deterioration is already visible (ALAB decelerating, ARM FCF collapsed to $178M). Maersk rounds out the shorts with EBITDA guided 27-53% below 2025 levels and the stock still trading 46% above base-case present value.

The 5% cash reserve reflects the absence of live market data, creating uncertainty about whether recent price moves have already closed some of the gaps identified in the analysis. It provides optionality for allocation once prices can be confirmed.
