## Detailed Analysis

**Owner Earnings Construction**

FY2025 reported net income was $4.1B (diluted EPS $11.16). Operating cash flow was $4.3B; total capex was $83.5M. Because the business is asset-light and capex is already minimal, maintenance capex is estimated at roughly 60-70% of total capex ($50–60M). D&A is embedded in EBITDA: EBITDA of $4.3B vs. operating income of $4.2B implies D&A of roughly $100–120M. Owner earnings = net income ($4.1B) + D&A (~$115M) – maintenance capex (~$55M) ≈ **$4.16B**, or approximately **$11.35/share** on ~367M diluted shares. The five-year range of owner earnings (2021–2025) spans roughly $3.1B to $4.2B, growing at a ~7% CAGR. Current price of $314 implies a P/owner-earnings of approximately **27.7x**.

**Bear Case (Year 5 Intrinsic Value)**

Assumptions: Interest-rate volatility cycle normalizes; Fed holds rates steady for extended period; ADV growth stalls at 0-1%; operating leverage fades. Owner earnings grow at 2% annually for 5 years → Year 5 owner earnings ~$4.59B ($12.50/share). Apply a 20x terminal multiple (below current). Intrinsic value at Year 5: ~$250/share. Discount back at 10% cost of equity for 5 years → PV ≈ **$155/share**. At $314, the bear case implies roughly 51% downside. This scenario is plausible but not probable — it requires a sustained low-volatility macro regime and competitive disruption simultaneously.

**Base Case (Year 5 Intrinsic Value)**

Assumptions: 5% annual owner-earnings growth (in line with recent trend minus some mean reversion), consistent market share, modest crypto derivatives contribution, stable competitive position. Year 5 owner earnings ~$5.31B ($14.45/share). Terminal multiple: 22x. Intrinsic value at Year 5: ~$317/share. Discount back at 9%: PV ≈ **$206/share**. At $314, the base case suggests the stock is approximately 35-40% overvalued relative to a DCF fair value. However, this analysis is sensitive to the discount rate — at a 7% required return, the base PV rises to ~$227/share. Using a simpler perpetuity model: $12/share owner earnings / (9% – 5% growth) = **$300/share** — roughly in line with current price. The stock is approximately fairly valued at base case assumptions.

**Bull Case (Year 5 Intrinsic Value)**

Assumptions: Fed rate cycle remains active; crypto derivatives become material contributor (10%+ of revenue); international expansion gains; ADV growth 8-10%/year. Owner earnings grow at 9% annually → Year 5 ~$6.40B ($17.40/share). Terminal multiple: 24x. Intrinsic value at Year 5: ~$418/share. Discount back at 9%: PV ≈ **$272/share** in DCF terms, but Year-5 market price could reach $420+ for a holder. Upside from current: ~33%.

**Multiples in Context**

Current trailing P/E of ~28x compares to: CME's own 10-year average P/E of 26.1x (modestly above); ICE at ~28x (in line); US Capital Markets sector average of ~23x (premium justified by moat). The 10-year EV/EBITDA range is 13.2x–26.4x with a median of 17.9x; current 19.5x is modestly above median. CME is not in the top quartile of historical valuation, but it is not cheap. The premium over sector peers is historically consistent and justified by CME's higher operating margins (~64% EBITDA margin vs. ICE's ~39%).

**What Must Be True for $314 to Be Justified**

The reverse-engineered scenario: At $314 and a 9% required return, owner earnings must grow at approximately 5.6% annually in perpetuity (Gordon Growth: $11.35 / (9% – g) = $314 → g = 5.4%). For a 10-year horizon with a 20x terminal multiple, the required growth is approximately 6% annually compounded. This is historically achievable for CME — it has grown owner earnings at ~7% CAGR over the past decade. The market is pricing moderate execution, not perfection. But there is minimal margin of safety: if growth comes in at 3-4%, the stock is 20-30% overvalued.

**Market Pricing: Perfection or Pessimism?**

The current price reflects moderate-to-fair optimism. The stock is not priced for perfection — the 10-year high EV/EBITDA was 26.4x vs. current 19.5x — but it is priced for continued steady growth. Wall Street consensus is cautious: average price target of $260–$286 is 9-17% below current price. That said, analyst price targets on quality compounders routinely lag. The stock has returned 14.7% annually over 10 years vs. 10.9% for S&P 500; the premium has persisted because the business keeps delivering.

- **Confidence:** Medium — the business quality and earnings trajectory are high-confidence, but the current price leaves limited margin of safety; intrinsic value under base assumptions is roughly $270–$310, suggesting the stock is trading at or modestly above fair value.
