# Compact Checklist — AMAT

**Date:** 2026-03-12

1. **Business**: Applied Materials sells the deposition, etch, and process control equipment that chipmakers (TSMC, Samsung, SK Hynix, Intel) install inside their fabs to physically manufacture semiconductors, and then earns a growing $6.4B+ annual recurring stream from service contracts, spare parts, and software on its installed base.

2. **Moat**: Once an Applied Materials tool is qualified inside a billion-dollar fab process — a months-long, multi-million-dollar certification — chipmakers have no financial incentive to swap it out, and Applied's unique breadth across all three major tool categories (deposition, etch, process control) means customers can consolidate more spend with a single vendor; Morningstar rates this a wide moat lasting 20+ years.

3. **Economics**: AMAT delivered non-GAAP gross margins of 48.8% in FY2025 (a 25-year high), ROIC of 27–34%, and normalized owner earnings of approximately $7.5–8.0B annually — with FCF conversion above 100% of net income in years not distorted by the one-time Epic Center buildout.

4. **Management**: Over the past decade, management returned nearly 90% of free cash flow to shareholders through buybacks and dividends, reduced diluted shares from ~877M to ~802M over three years (net of SBC), and explicitly disclosed a $600M–$710M China headwind rather than obscuring it — while committing $5B to the EPIC R&D center for the next technology node transition.

5. **Debt risk**: AMAT holds $8.5B in cash against $6.6B in total debt (net cash of $1.9B), carries a 32x interest coverage ratio, and can sustain a 30% revenue decline for two years while still covering all debt service, dividends, and maintenance capex from operating cash flow alone.

6. **Price**: At ~$150/share (forward P/E ~16–17x on FY2026E EPS of ~$8.80), the current price requires EPS growth of roughly 8–10% annually for a decade, operating margins holding at 29–31%, and China headwinds absorbed by FY2027 — the market is pricing moderate recovery, not peak-cycle conditions, and the base case intrinsic value is approximately $123–130/share, meaning the stock sits above fair value.

7. **Margin of safety**: The business margin of safety is strong — dominant market position, $6B+ recurring AGS revenue, low net leverage, and near-impossible permanent impairment — but the price margin of safety is thin, with a bear case DCF of ~$69/share implying 54% downside and an upside/downside ratio of roughly 0.4:1 at current price; a meaningful safety buffer requires the price to decline to $110–120.

8. **Thesis breaker**: Sell if US export restrictions expand to cover all Chinese customers (removing the possibility of any China revenue recovery, which would cut EPS by 30%+), if AMAT loses meaningful market share in gate-all-around deposition to a competitor during the node transition, or if operating margins compress sustainably below 24% — indicating that pricing power has genuinely eroded.
