## Detailed Analysis

**ROIC and Return Structure.** FICO's reported ROIC for FY2025 is approximately 48–54% depending on the calculation methodology, versus a WACC of approximately 12%. The gap of ~35–40 percentage points is extraordinary and indicative of a business with genuine pricing power and structural switching costs. The high ROIC is not merely an artifact of negative book equity from buybacks — it reflects the underlying economics of the Scores franchise: a near-zero marginal cost per incremental score query layered on top of fixed infrastructure. ROIC has improved materially from approximately 35% three years ago to 48% today, meaning the moat is widening, not narrowing. ROE is technically undefined (or infinitely negative) due to negative shareholder equity, but this is a mechanical artifact of the buyback program and conveys no information about the underlying business economics.

**Margin Structure.** Gross margin was 82.2% in FY2025, up from 74.8% in FY2021. The primary driver is revenue mix shift: the Scores segment, which requires almost no incremental delivery cost once infrastructure is in place, grew 27% to $1.169B in FY2025 and now represents ~59% of total revenue. Cost of revenue grew from $332M to $354M over four years — a 6.5% cumulative increase — while revenue grew 51%, demonstrating the near-fixed nature of the delivery cost base. Operating margin is 46.5% in FY2025, among the highest in enterprise software. R&D ($188M, 9.5% of revenue) and SG&A ($513M, 25.8% of revenue) are the dominant operating cost lines; both have grown in absolute terms but declined as a percentage of revenue.

**Free Cash Flow and Capital Intensity.** Capital expenditures were just $8.9M in both FY2024 and FY2025 — approximately 0.4% of revenue. This is a software and data business: there are no factories, no warehouses, no heavy equipment. Capitalized internal-use software (not separately broken out but referenced in filings as ~$22M for nine months of FY2025) is the primary tangible investment. Operating cash flow of $778.8M in FY2025 exceeded net income of $652M by $127M, reflecting strong non-cash charges (primarily stock-based compensation and amortization) and favorable working capital. FCF of $769.9M represents a 22% year-over-year increase and an FCF conversion rate of 118% on net income — confirming that reported earnings are conservative relative to cash reality. Five-year cumulative FCF (FY2021–FY2025) is approximately $2.77B.

**Revenue Quality — Scores vs. Software.** The Scores segment is inherently transactional: FICO charges lenders a per-inquiry fee each time a FICO Score is pulled. This means revenue is directly correlated with US credit origination volumes — mortgage, auto, credit card, and personal loan activity. In high-rate environments (as in FY2023–FY2024), mortgage originations are suppressed, but FICO partially offset this through per-score price increases, which have been substantial (B2B scores pricing has risen significantly over 2020–2025). The business-to-consumer (myFICO) score pull is smaller but growing. Software revenues (~$822M in FY2025) are predominantly subscription/SaaS in nature, which is higher quality than transactional revenue but the segment is in active transition from legacy platform-based delivery to the FICO Platform. Software ARR dollar-based net retention rate was 102% as of FY2025, modestly above breakeven — adequate but not a hypergrowth SaaS profile.

**Operating Leverage.** The mechanics are compelling: from FY2021 to FY2025, revenue grew $674M (+51%) while total operating expenses grew from $812M to $1,055M (+$243M, or +30%). The result is that each incremental dollar of Scores revenue flows to operating income at a very high rate. Management has demonstrated pricing discipline — raising B2B score prices repeatedly — which drops almost entirely to the bottom line given the fixed cost structure. If Scores revenue grows at even 10% per year, operating income should grow at 15–20%+ annually, all else equal.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** FICO is a structurally protected toll road on US consumer credit; pricing power in Scores is essentially unchallenged, operating leverage is extreme, and FCF compounds rapidly with minimal reinvestment required.
- **Bear case:** Scores revenue is volume-dependent on credit originations; a sustained housing/credit contraction or a regulatory mandate forcing FICO Score replacement (e.g., forced adoption of VantageScore) would materially impair the franchise.
- **Confidence:** High — the income statement, cash flow, and ROIC data are consistent, well-sourced, and paint a coherent picture of an exceptional business economics profile.

## Red Flags

- Software segment transition is slow: FICO Platform ARR of $263.6M is only 32% of total software ARR as of September 2025; legacy drag continues
- Scores revenue concentration creates cyclical exposure — mortgage volumes can halve in rising-rate cycles
- B2B score price increases have been aggressive; regulatory or CFPB scrutiny of pricing practices is a non-trivial tail risk
- SG&A of $513M (25.8% of revenue) is high relative to peers and warrants ongoing monitoring

## Score: 9 / 10

Exceptional asset-light economics with ROIC ~48%, gross margins >82%, FCF conversion >100%, and meaningful operating leverage; the one point deduction reflects the cyclical revenue sensitivity of the Scores segment and the incomplete Software platform transition.
