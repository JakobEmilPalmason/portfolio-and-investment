## Detailed Analysis

**Owner Earnings Calculation.** FICO is capital-light by design. FY2025 revenue was $1.991B; operating income ran at ~46.5% margin, implying ~$925M operating income. After cash taxes (effective rate ~28%) and minimal maintenance capex, owner earnings approximate reported FCF of ~$740–770M. Per share on ~24.3M diluted shares, that is ~$31–32. The business requires virtually no incremental capital to grow the Scores segment — royalty-like economics make FCF conversion above 100% of reported net income in many years. Capex intensity is low enough that reported FCF is a reliable proxy for owner earnings.

**Bear / Base / Bull DCF Scenarios.** All scenarios use a 9% discount rate (reflecting the company's competitive moat and predictable cash flows), $770M FY2025 FCF as base, and a 25x terminal exit multiple on year-10 FCF.

- *Bear case* — VantageScore captures 20–25% of mortgage origination volume by 2028 and FICO is forced to roll back pricing 30–40% in the Scores segment. Scores revenue contracts from $1.17B to $850M; Software stays flat at $820M. Total FCF declines to ~$480M by year 3 before stabilizing, growing 6% thereafter. Intrinsic value: approximately **$650–750/share**. This implies ~45–50% downside from today.

- *Base case* — VantageScore adoption is gradual; FICO retains 75%+ of mortgage volume and continues modest annual price increases (5–8%), offset by unit volume softness. Scores segment grows 8% p.a.; Software grows 6% p.a. (aided by platform ARR). Total FCF compounds at ~11% p.a. Intrinsic value: approximately **$1,350–1,500/share**. This is ~5–17% upside from today, near the UBS target.

- *Bull case* — Regulatory concerns prove overblown, FICO 10T is adopted alongside VantageScore (additive not substitutive), international expansion accelerates, and Software ARR re-accelerates to 12–15% growth. FCF compounds at ~17% p.a. Intrinsic value: approximately **$2,200–2,500/share**. This aligns with the broader analyst consensus target and implies ~70–95% upside.

**Multiples vs. History and Peers.** At 43x trailing P/E, FICO sits below its own 10-year average of ~48x and massively below the 2024 peak of 99x. Comparable royalty-like software and financial data businesses (Moody's ~24x, S&P Global ~28x, Verisk ~28x, MSCI ~34x) all trade at meaningful discounts to FICO. The premium reflects FICO's higher growth rate, but the gap is larger than at any historical point except the 2024 bubble. The EV/EBITDA of ~47x compares to ~20–25x for the peer group, implying a premium of roughly 2x that must be justified by pricing power durability.

**What Must Be True for Today's Price?** At $1,285/share with ~24.3M shares, the market cap is ~$31.2B. Assuming $2.7B net debt, enterprise value is ~$33.9B. For this to be fair value at a 9% discount rate and 25x terminal multiple, FICO must compound FCF at approximately 13–14% per year for 10 years. Given 2025 FCF of ~$770M, this requires FCF to reach ~$2.7–2.9B by FY2035. That implies the current pricing power model survives intact, regulatory/competitive challenges remain manageable, and no meaningful pricing concessions are made. It is not an impossible scenario, but it is a scenario with very little room for disappointment.

**Perfection or Disaster?** The valuation is not priced for perfection in the sense of the 2024 peak (99x P/E). After the sharp pullback from $1,943 (Sep 2024) to $1,285, FICO is now priced for a "base+" scenario — continued dominance with modest competitive headwinds. What has changed materially is the competitive landscape: VantageScore at $1/score vs. FICO's multi-dollar fees is a legitimate threat in a rate-sensitive mortgage market. The stock is no longer priced for disaster (bear case implies $650–750), but it is also not cheap by any objective standard. It is priced for execution with very limited tolerance for further pricing deterioration.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** FICO 10T and international expansion absorb VantageScore share loss, FCF compounds at 17%+ justifying $2,200+ intrinsic value.
- **Bear case:** Coordinated bureau/VantageScore pricing war forces FICO to cut Scores fees 30–40%, collapsing FCF and intrinsic value to $650–750.
- **Confidence:** Medium — the business economics are well-understood, but the competitive inflection triggered March 12, 2026 introduces binary risk to the pricing model that is genuinely hard to handicap.

## Red Flags

- P/FCF of ~41x leaves no margin of safety against pricing deterioration; even a 20% FCF decline resets intrinsic value below current price
- VantageScore at $1/score directly attacks FICO's most valuable revenue stream — bear case is now a legitimate probability, not a tail
- UBS cut to $1,350 on March 12, 2026 based on competitive pricing war concerns; further analyst downgrades likely if VantageScore adoption data improves
- Negative shareholder equity (-$1.7B) and $2.84B gross debt mean the company cannot absorb a multi-year revenue shock without financial stress
- Reverse DCF at $1,285 still requires 13–14% FCF CAGR for 10 years — above median but not heroic; problem is the competitive risk premium is now non-trivial

## Score: 5 / 10

FICO's intrinsic value is likely $1,350–1,500 in the base case, making the current price roughly fair but with an uncomfortably narrow margin of safety given the sudden and credible competitive challenge to its Scores pricing model.
