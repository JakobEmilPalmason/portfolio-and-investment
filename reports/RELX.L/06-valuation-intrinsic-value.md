## Detailed Analysis

**Owner Earnings Estimate**

RELX's "owner earnings" — the cash the business actually generates for owners — differ meaningfully from reported net income because the company carries large acquired intangible amortisation charges. For 2025: reported PBT was £2,750m; adjusted operating profit was £3,342m; D&A within adjusted profit was £504m; and total capex was £525m (dominated by £504m of capitalised development costs, which is genuine maintenance/investment spend rather than pure maintenance capex). The company's own measure, "adjusted cash flow," was £3,301m at a 99% conversion rate from adjusted operating profit. True maintenance capex is hard to isolate, but treating all capitalised development as maintenance (conservative), owner earnings approximate £3,342m + £504m D&A - £525m capex = ~£3,321m, very close to adjusted cash flow. This is the figure to anchor intrinsic value work. Shares outstanding are approximately 1.8 billion after 2025 buybacks, giving owner earnings per share of roughly 184p. In three to five years, at 7–8% annual growth, this reaches 230–270p per share.

**Scenario Analysis (Year 5 owner earnings, multiply, discount back at 8%)**

Bear case: AI disruption accelerates meaningfully, growth slows to 3% per year, margins compress 200bps. Year 5 owner earnings ~200p/share. Apply 18x multiple (still a quality recurring-revenue business). Terminal value ~3,600p. Discount at 8% for five years (~0.68 factor) = ~2,450p intrinsic value. At today's 2,594p, the stock is trading near or slightly above the bear case valuation.

Base case: Business continues roughly as is — 6–7% revenue growth, modest margin expansion, buybacks shrink share count 2–3% per year. Year 5 owner earnings ~255p/share. Apply 22x multiple (consistent with historical average for a high-quality compounder). Terminal value ~5,610p. Discounted at 8% = ~3,820p intrinsic value. This is close to the analyst consensus target.

Bull case: AI proves additive, Lexis+ AI accelerates legal segment revenue, Elsevier data commands AI-licensing premiums. 9% compound growth, margin expansion to 37%. Year 5 owner earnings ~290p/share. Apply 25x multiple. Terminal value ~7,250p. Discounted at 8% = ~4,930p intrinsic value. This is not far from the May 2025 peak price.

**Multiples in Context**

On 2025 adjusted EPS of 128.5p, RELX trades at ~20.2x — at the low end of the 5-year range and well below the 30x+ it commanded in 2024. On EV/EBITDA (net debt ~£7.2bn, market cap at 2,594p x ~1.8bn shares ≈ £46.7bn, EV ~£53.9bn, EBITDA £3,846m), EV/EBITDA is roughly 14x — below the 10-year median of ~17x. Peer comparison: Wolters Kluwer trades at approximately 20–22x EV/EBITDA; Thomson Reuters at 25–30x. RELX is now the cheapest of the three major information services companies on nearly every multiple, despite generating more operating profit than its two largest peers combined.

**What Must Be True for Today's Price to Be Justified**

To justify 2,594p on a DCF with an 8% discount rate requires roughly 3% annual owner earnings growth in perpetuity after a five-year transition period — essentially a scenario where AI causes stagnation or mild decline. This is not impossible, but it requires believing that LexisNexis, Elsevier, and the Risk division's proprietary data networks all become substantially less valuable over the next five years. Management reported no signs of churn or AI-driven disruption in the Q4 2025 earnings call, and Lexis+ AI has hundreds of thousands of active users — evidence of adoption rather than substitution. The price appears to assume a disruption scenario that has not yet materialized in reported numbers.

**Market Pricing: Perfection or Disaster?**

The stock has de-rated dramatically — from pricing in perfection (30–32x earnings in early 2025) to pricing in near-disaster (20x, at the low of 1,991p the implied multiple dropped below 16x). The fundamental business delivered its strongest-ever adjusted cash flow in 2025. The de-rating appears driven by sentiment and sector rotation rather than earnings deterioration. That creates an interesting opportunity if the AI disruption thesis is wrong, and a real risk if it is right. The current price appears to lean toward pricing in too much fear.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** AI proves additive to RELX's data moat, growth reaccelerates to 8–9%, and the stock re-rates to historical multiples, yielding ~90% upside to ~4,900p.
- **Bear case:** AI meaningfully cannibalises legal research and academic publishing workflows, growth stalls at 2–3%, and the stock grinds sideways or declines to ~2,000–2,200p.
- **Confidence:** Medium — the business fundamentals are clear and strong, but the AI disruption risk introduces genuine uncertainty about the durability of the moat in the 3–5 year horizon; a binary narrative makes DCF estimates unusually wide.

## Red Flags

- The stock's 38% decline from peak despite strong reported results signals the market sees a structural threat that fundamentals have not yet reflected — this is either a major opportunity or an early warning of genuine disruption.
- Adjusted EPS (128.5p) meaningfully exceeds reported EPS (112.6p), partly due to large acquired intangible amortisation; investors must judge whether these are truly non-cash or represent real economic decay of acquired assets.
- Capex (£525m) almost entirely comprises capitalised development costs — if this investment does not generate returns in an AI-disrupted world, it becomes wasted spending, not a value driver.
- Net debt of £7.2bn (2.0x EBITDA) is manageable but limits flexibility if the business environment deteriorates faster than expected.

## Score: 6 / 10

The business is modestly undervalued on a base-case DCF (current price near or slightly above the bear case, ~30–35% below the base case), but the genuine uncertainty around AI disruption makes it impossible to assign the high conviction warranted for a 7–8 score; the stock is reasonable value if the moat holds, and a potential value trap if it does not.
