# Compact Checklist — TSM

**Date:** 2026-03-07

1. **Business**: TSMC manufactures chips designed by others -- charging $3,200 to $30,000+ per wafer depending on node complexity -- for 465 customers including NVIDIA (19% of revenue) and Apple (17%), who cannot switch foundries without spending $500M+ and 12-18 months redesigning each chip.

2. **Moat**: TSMC holds 72% of global foundry share and over 90% at leading-edge nodes, with 2nm yields at ~70% versus Samsung's ~40% and Intel's ~55%, a gap that has widened at every node transition for the past decade and allows TSMC to impose 3-10% annual price increases that customers absorb without defection.

3. **Economics**: ROIC surged to 49% in 2025 on 60% gross margins and $31B in free cash flow, extraordinary for the most capital-intensive manufacturer in tech, though FCF conversion of only 58% of net income reflects the $41B capex required to maintain the technology lead.

4. **Management**: CEO C.C. Wei publicly admitted being "very nervous" about an AI bubble and spent months personally verifying customer demand before committing to $52-56B in 2026 capex, while maintaining near-zero share dilution, no empire-building M&A, and a 28% dividend increase -- though minimal insider ownership means management's personal wealth is not deeply at risk.

5. **Debt risk**: With $95B in cash against $31B in total debt (0.34x debt-to-EBITDA), AA-/Aa3 credit ratings, and interest coverage above 100x, TSMC could survive a two-year 30% revenue decline by cutting capex without threatening solvency.

6. **Price**: At ~$339/ADR and 34x trailing earnings (53% above the 10-year average P/E of 22x), the stock requires roughly 20% annual earnings growth through 2030 -- meaning AI-driven demand must sustain near current levels and margins must hold above 40% -- just to deliver a 10% annualized return.

7. **Margin of safety**: The business itself is the margin of safety -- 72% market share, no viable alternative at leading edge, fortress balance sheet -- but the price offers none, with base-case DCF around $290/ADR and roughly 1:1 upside-to-downside asymmetry that falls short of the 2-3x ratio a conservative framework demands.

8. **Thesis breaker**: Sell if a Taiwan Strait crisis moves from theoretical to operational (military mobilization, blockade, or sustained disruption to shipping lanes), or if Samsung or Intel closes the yield gap at leading-edge nodes to within 10 percentage points of TSMC, eliminating the manufacturing monopoly that underpins every other aspect of this thesis.
