## Detailed Analysis

**Return on Capital: Exceptional and Accelerating.** Intuitive Surgical generates returns on invested capital that place it in the top decile of its industry. The TTM ROIC stands at approximately 27%, up sharply from a 5-year average of ~18%. This improvement reflects the operating leverage inherent in the model: once a da Vinci or da Vinci 5 system is placed, the recurring instrument and service revenue flows at high incremental margins. The company's ROE of ~17% appears lower than ROIC because the balance sheet carries substantial excess cash and investments (~$5.9B) that depress the equity return calculation. Adjusting for excess cash, the underlying business economics are even more impressive.

**Margin Structure: Best-in-Class for Medtech.** The FY2025 GAAP gross margin of 66.0% (non-GAAP 67.6%) reflects a temporary ~150 bps headwind from tariffs and the da Vinci 5 manufacturing ramp. Historically, gross margins have ranged from 65-69%, demonstrating remarkable stability for a hardware-plus-consumables business. GAAP operating margin was 29.3%, while the pro forma operating margin reached 37%, improving 70 bps year-over-year despite cost headwinds. Net profit margin of 28.4% is extraordinary for a company at this scale. The margin profile has been resilient through COVID (2020 revenue dipped only 2.7% before rebounding 31% in 2021), confirming the non-discretionary nature of surgical procedures.

**Cash Generation: Step-Change in FY2025.** Free cash flow surged to $2.5B in FY2025, nearly doubling from $1.3B in FY2024 and more than tripling from $0.75B in FY2023. This dramatic improvement reflects two factors: (1) operating income scaling with 21% revenue growth, and (2) capital expenditure normalizing toward the $625-675M guided range after a peak investment cycle of ~$1.1B annually in 2023-2024 for new manufacturing facilities. FCF conversion (FCF/net income) is approximately 86%, which is strong and improving. Owner earnings are essentially equivalent to reported FCF given the minimal maintenance capex nature of the business once facilities are built.

**Revenue Quality: Razor/Razorblade Moat.** The revenue mix is a Buffett-style dream. Instruments and accessories (the "razorblades") generated ~60% of FY2024 revenue and grew 19% in FY2025. Services added another ~16%. Combined, recurring revenue represents approximately 75-80% of total revenue, anchored by an installed base of 11,106 da Vinci systems as of December 31, 2025 (up 15% YoY). Each system drives approximately $2,000+ in instrument/accessory revenue per procedure, and procedures grew 18% in FY2025. System placements (the "razors") represent the remaining ~24% of revenue and serve as the growth engine that expands the recurring revenue base. This flywheel is self-reinforcing: more systems mean more procedures mean more instrument sales.

**Operating Leverage and Capital Intensity.** Intuitive is transitioning from a heavy investment phase to a harvesting phase. Capex peaked at $1.1B in 2023-2024 for facility construction and da Vinci 5 manufacturing ramp, but the FY2025 guided range of $625-675M represents a significant normalization. The business is fundamentally asset-light in its steady state: the incremental cost of selling instruments to an existing installed base is modest. Operating leverage is significant, as evidenced by the 70 bps pro forma operating margin expansion on 21% revenue growth. With procedure volumes projected to grow 13-15% in 2026 on top of a larger base, the operating leverage should continue compounding.

**Long-Term Revenue Trajectory.** Revenue has compounded from $3.7B (2018) to $10.1B (2025), a CAGR of approximately 15%. The growth has been remarkably consistent outside of the mild COVID dip. Three consecutive years of 20%+ growth underscore the secular tailwind of robotic surgery adoption, which remains well below 10% penetration in most procedure categories globally. The Ion platform (bronchoscopy) grew procedures 44% in Q4 2025, providing an additional growth vector beyond general surgery.

## Signal Summary
- **Bull case:** The razor/razorblade model with 75-80% recurring revenue, 27% ROIC, and $2.5B FCF creates a compounding machine with decades of global procedure adoption runway.
- **Bear case:** Gross margin pressure from da Vinci 5 ramp and tariffs could persist, and the 2026 procedure growth guide of 13-15% (vs 18% in 2025) may signal a deceleration trend.
- **Confidence:** High — Financial data is well-documented from public filings; the business model economics are transparent and have proven durable across cycles.

## Red Flags
- Gross margin declined ~150 bps in FY2025 due to tariffs and da Vinci 5 manufacturing costs; unclear how long this pressure persists
- 2026 procedure growth guidance of 13-15% is a step-down from 18% in 2025, though partially reflects a larger base
- Heavy capex cycle ($1.1B/year in 2023-2024) reduced FCF temporarily; any return to elevated spending would compress returns
- Stock-based compensation is significant (difference between GAAP 29.3% and pro forma 37% operating margin partly reflects SBC adjustments)

## Score: 9 / 10
Intuitive Surgical exhibits elite business economics: 27% ROIC, 66%+ gross margins, 75-80% recurring revenue, and $2.5B in free cash flow on a razor/razorblade model with a massive and growing installed base; the only deduction is for temporary gross margin pressure and the gap between GAAP and non-GAAP profitability metrics.
