# Compact Checklist — AAPL

**Date:** 2026-03-09

1. **Business**: Apple sells premium consumer hardware (iPhone at 50% of revenue) that converts buyers into a captive 2.35-billion-device installed base, then monetizes that base through a $107B/year services layer at 75% gross margins.

2. **Moat**: A layered pyramid of switching costs — iCloud data lock-in, app purchase investment, cross-device workflows, and iMessage social pressure — produces 92% iPhone retention and sustains ROIC above 37% despite being the world's most profitable consumer electronics company for over a decade.

3. **Economics**: Apple generates $100–123B in annual free cash flow at a 46.9% gross margin with only $12B in capex, producing ROIC of 37–48% and growing net income 19.5% in FY2025 on just 6.4% revenue growth — Services mix-shift operating leverage is real and expanding.

4. **Management**: Tim Cook has returned over $704B to shareholders since 2012 with disciplined small-bolt-on M&A, but has consistently executed buybacks at 30–35x earnings rather than at intrinsic value, and communicates in polished marketing language rather than the owner-operator candor of a Buffett or Singleton.

5. **Debt risk**: Apple holds negative net debt (cash exceeds total debt), generates $100B+ in annual FCF, carries AA+ credit ratings, has interest coverage of 33–44x, and could survive a 30% revenue shock for two years without equity issuance — the balance sheet is a fortress.

6. **Price**: For $257/share to be fair value, Apple must sustain 12–14% Services growth for five-plus years to $170B+, maintain iPhone pricing and installed-base size against China competition, and suffer no material loss from the DOJ antitrust case threatening the $18–20B/year Google search deal — all four conditions simultaneously.

7. **Margin of safety**: There is no price margin of safety — the stock trades at or above base-case intrinsic value (~$215), the bear case implies 40% downside, and the upside/downside asymmetry is approximately 1:3 against the buyer; the only protection is the underlying business quality, not the purchase price.

8. **Thesis breaker**: Sell if the DOJ forces termination of the Google search revenue-sharing agreement (a ~20% EPS hit not priced into the multiple) or if Services revenue growth decelerates to below 8% for two consecutive quarters, signaling structural regulatory damage to the App Store flywheel.
