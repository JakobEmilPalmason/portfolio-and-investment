# Compact Checklist — CDNS

**Date:** 2026-03-12

1. **Business**: Cadence sells the mission-critical chip design software (EDA) that every semiconductor company needs, with 91% recurring revenue and a $7.8B backlog locking in two-thirds of next year's sales.
2. **Moat**: A 30-year duopoly with Synopsys, near-100% customer retention, and switching costs so high that no engineer will risk a catastrophic chip error to save less than 2% of R&D budget on tools.
3. **Economics**: Gross margins above 85%, free cash flow of $1.5B at 135% of net income, and only $140M in capex confirm an asset-light franchise generating real cash well above reported earnings.
4. **Management**: CEO Devgan has grown revenue from $3B to $5.3B since 2021 with disciplined tuck-in M&A, 90%+ variable compensation, and a flat share count — solid but not owner-operator level at 196K shares.
5. **Debt risk**: Net cash of $451M, 18x interest coverage, and $1.5B in annual FCF mean Cadence could retire its entire $2.5B debt in under two years and would remain profitable even in a 30% revenue decline.
6. **Price**: At ~50x owner earnings, today's price requires 18% annual earnings growth for five years just to deliver a 10% return — near-perfection with no room for disappointment.
7. **Margin of safety**: The business fortress is real (duopoly, recurring revenue, near-zero churn), but the price offers negative margin of safety, trading 17% above base-case intrinsic value with 2:1 unfavorable downside/upside asymmetry.
8. **Thesis breaker**: Sell if revenue growth falls below 8% for two consecutive years, if open-source or AI-native EDA tools gain adoption at major foundries, or if management makes a large value-destructive acquisition.
