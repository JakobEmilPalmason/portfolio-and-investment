# Compact Checklist — ADBE

**Date:** 2026-03-09

1. **Business**: Adobe sells subscription creative software (Photoshop, Illustrator, Premiere Pro, Acrobat) that 41 million professionals and 98% of Fortune 500 companies rely on daily, generating $23.8B in revenue with 96% recurring — customers keep paying because their entire workflows, file libraries, and muscle memory are built around Adobe tools.

2. **Moat**: Proprietary file formats (PSD, PDF, AI, INDD) embedded in decades of professional workflows create switching costs that no competitor can shortcut, though Canva (260M users with free Affinity) and Figma ($57B IPO) are winning the prosumer and UI/UX flanks where Adobe has no credible answer.

3. **Economics**: Adobe earns 40% ROIC, 89% gross margins, and converts net income into $9.85B of free cash flow at a 138% conversion rate on just $172M in capex — this is one of the most capital-efficient businesses in the world.

4. **Management**: Capital allocation is mixed — buybacks have genuinely shrunk shares by 9.4% over three years, but the failed $20B Figma bid at 50x ARR (costing $1B in breakup fees), $1.94B in annual SBC (8% of revenue), and CEO Narayen's net selling of $92.5M in stock reveal a team that is competent but not owner-minded.

5. **Debt risk**: With $5.4B cash, $6.2B debt (all fixed-rate, no maturities before 2027), net debt/EBITDA of 0.1x, interest coverage of 33x, and A+/A2 credit ratings, Adobe could survive a 30% revenue decline for two consecutive years without issuing equity.

6. **Price**: At ~$284 and 12x forward earnings — 60% below its 5-year average P/E — the market prices Adobe as a no-growth business facing existential AI disruption, meaning you only need modest growth continuation (not acceleration) for the stock to work.

7. **Margin of safety**: The 2-3x upside/downside ratio ($450-500 bull vs. $200-220 bear), $9.85B in annual free cash flow, and $25B buyback program provide a floor, but the margin of safety is thin enough that you need Adobe's core professional moat to hold — this is not a fat pitch.

8. **Thesis breaker**: Sell if Creative Cloud ARR growth falls below 5% for two consecutive quarters, confirming that AI-native tools are genuinely displacing Adobe from professional workflows rather than just winning prosumers.
