## Detailed Analysis

**Debt Levels.** As of December 31, 2024, Constellation Software carried total debt of approximately $5.0B against total shareholder equity of approximately $4.1B, yielding a debt-to-equity ratio of roughly 122%. Total assets were approximately $15.3B against total liabilities of $11.2B. The debt composition is notable for its diversity: a $345M (C$495M) principal Series 1 Debenture tranche maturing March 31, 2040; a $500M promissory note to ICE at 7% maturing in 2063; a $1.085B undrawn revolving credit facility; and subsidiary-level debt from Topicus, Lumine, and other operating segments. Net debt (total debt less $2.8B cash) is approximately $2.2B. On a net debt/FCFA2S basis (~$2.2B / $1.47B), leverage is approximately 1.5x — well within the comfortable range. On gross debt/EBITDA, the figure depends on one's EBITDA proxy, but operating cash flow of $2.196B in FY2024 puts gross leverage at roughly 2.3x — moderate.

**Interest Coverage.** With EBIT of approximately $1.8B in FY2024 and an interest coverage ratio of 8.4x as reported by multiple sources, CSI is comfortably above the 5x threshold typically considered safe. This coverage is supported by consistently growing operating cash flows: FY2025 operating cash flow grew ~28% in the first nine months of 2025, further strengthening the coverage cushion. The Series 1 Debentures reset to 10.4% in March 2024 — elevated given inflation — but this affects only ~$345M of principal, which is modest relative to the total cash generation.

**Liquidity.** The liquidity position is solid. Cash and short-term investments of approximately $2.8B as of year-end 2024 covers more than 18 months of operating expenditures. The $1.085B revolving credit facility (amended January 2024 to increase the limit) was entirely undrawn with only $13M in letters of credit issued. Short-term assets ($5.8B) were reported as roughly equal to short-term liabilities ($5.7B), indicating adequate working capital with no near-term liquidity stress. CSI also generates over $2B in operating cash per year, providing substantial ongoing self-funding capacity.

**Refinancing Risk.** The primary near-term refinancing event is the revolving credit facility, which matures in November 2026 — approximately eight months from the analysis date. This is a standard renewal rather than a structural risk; CSI is a well-known investment grade-quality borrower with strong bank relationships and has routinely amended and extended this facility. The Series 1 Debentures do not mature until 2040, and the ICE promissory note matures in 2063. Subsidiary-level debt at Topicus and Lumine adds some complexity but those entities are publicly listed and separately financed. Overall refinancing risk is low but the 2026 revolving facility maturity deserves monitoring.

**Off-Balance-Sheet and Other Obligations.** Operating leases for office space and equipment exist, though CSI is predominantly a people-and-software business with minimal physical infrastructure. Pension obligations are not material for a software acquirer of this profile. Contingent consideration on acquisitions is routinely disclosed in the financial statements and is factored into FCFA2S metrics. The Series 1 Debentures are unique in structure — they are essentially inflation-indexed bonds distributed to shareholders through a rights offering, aligning the obligation with CSI's revenue growth. The floating rate (CPI + 6.5%) resets annually and could increase in a high-inflation environment, though $345M principal remains a contained exposure.

**Stress Test.** If revenue declined 30% for two years (from $11.6B to approximately $8.1B in year one and further to approximately $5.7B in year two — an extreme scenario), CSI would likely still survive. The recurring maintenance revenue (~73% of total) is the most durable component and would likely decline far less than total revenue in a downturn (software maintenance is among the last things customers cancel). Operating cash flow would compress significantly, but the $2.8B cash buffer, $1.085B undrawn credit facility, and largely long-dated debt maturities would provide substantial runway. The company's decentralized structure and minimal fixed capex requirements mean cash burn in a downturn would be manageable. The only acute pressure point would be the November 2026 revolving facility renewal if markets were simultaneously disrupted — a low-probability but non-zero risk.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** A net-debt-to-FCF ratio of ~1.5x, 8.4x interest coverage, $2.8B of cash, and a fully undrawn $1.1B credit line make this one of the better-capitalized large acquirers in software, with debt structure well-suited to long-duration compounding.
- **Bear case:** Total gross debt of ~$5B (D/E of 122%) and a floating-rate CPI-linked debenture at 10.4% represent real obligations; the revolving facility matures in November 2026 and must be renewed; in a credit crisis scenario combined with a revenue downturn, the balance sheet is adequate but not a fortress.
- **Confidence:** Medium-High — Q4 2024 data is from audited filings; the November 2026 credit facility maturity and FY2025 updated debt structure were not confirmed from an audited source.

## Red Flags

- Revolving credit facility matures November 2026 — eight months from analysis date. Must be renewed under prevailing market conditions.
- Series 1 Debentures reset to 10.4% effective March 2024, a relatively high floating rate that will adjust again in March 2025 and annually thereafter. A sustained high-inflation environment would increase this cost.
- Debt structure is complex across parent and multiple publicly-listed subsidiaries (Topicus, Lumine); consolidated balance sheet analysis requires careful disaggregation.
- FY2025 net income declined 30% — if this reflects a structural step-down in earnings quality rather than a one-time deal surge, interest coverage could erode over time.

## Score: 7 / 10

Constellation Software carries moderate-but-manageable leverage with strong interest coverage (8.4x), substantial liquidity ($2.8B cash + $1.1B undrawn facility), and well-laddered long-dated debt maturities, but the complex multi-entity capital structure, near-term revolving facility renewal, and floating-rate CPI-linked debenture at 10.4% prevent a fortress-balance-sheet score.
