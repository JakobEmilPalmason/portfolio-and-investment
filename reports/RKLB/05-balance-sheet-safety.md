## Detailed Analysis

**Debt Levels and Structure**

As of Q3 2025 (Sep 30, 2025), Rocket Lab's debt consisted primarily of two instruments. First, convertible senior notes with a net carrying value of approximately $347M (face value ~$355M, with unamortized discount and issuance costs of ~$8M). These notes carry an initial conversion price of approximately $5.13 per share and, from November 2028 onward, holders may convert at any time until maturity. The maturity date appears to be 2029 based on the conversion terms disclosed. Second, a Trinity Loan Agreement with approximately $68M outstanding as of Q3 2025. Total gross debt was therefore approximately $415-490M (estimates vary by source; one aggregate figure of $488M has been cited). Against a cash position of approximately $1.1B at year-end 2025, net cash is approximately $600-700M — a meaningfully positive net cash position. Debt/EBITDA is not calculable in a traditional sense since EBITDA is negative (Adjusted EBITDA loss of $101M for FY2025). However, the absence of EBITDA does not mean the balance sheet is distressed; the company has sufficient liquidity to service its current debt obligations.

**Interest Coverage**

Interest coverage by conventional EBIT/interest expense metrics is deeply negative. EBIT for FY2025 was a large negative number (operating loss well over $200M on a GAAP basis). Interest expense for the nine months ending September 2025 was approximately $21.9M ($7.3M in Q3 alone), implying full-year interest expense in the range of $28-30M. EBIT is far below zero, so this ratio is not a meaningful indicator of near-term default risk — what matters is cash coverage, and with $1.1B in cash, the company can service debt payments for many years at the current interest expense level. The convertible notes may carry a relatively low coupon (typical of convertible structures issued in 2020-2021 low-rate environment), reducing cash interest burden. However, the interest coverage ratio cannot be reported as "comfortable" by any standard framework.

**Liquidity**

Liquidity is the strongest element of Rocket Lab's balance sheet. The company ended Q4 2025 with approximately $1.1B in cash, cash equivalents, restricted cash, and marketable securities. This was elevated from Q3 2025 levels by the $280.6M ATM equity raise in Q4. At the current cash burn rate (approximately $321M FCF outflow in FY2025, trending toward $400M+ annualized given Q4 2025's -$114M non-GAAP FCF in a single quarter), the cash runway is approximately 2.5 to 3.5 years. Simply Wall St analysis corroborates a 3+ year runway estimate. The current ratio is not explicitly reported in search results but with $1.1B cash and total liabilities of approximately $603M, the company is clearly able to meet short-term obligations. The company can fund 12-24 months of operations with zero revenue given current cash holdings — 24 months is achievable, 36 months is possible if burn moderates.

**Refinancing Risk**

Refinancing risk is the most important vulnerability in this balance sheet analysis. Rocket Lab has been consistently reliant on capital markets to fund operations and development. In Q4 2025 alone, it raised $280.6M via ATM equity offering — described by management as intended for acquisitions (Mynaric acquisition in progress) and general corporate purposes. The company has used equity issuance repeatedly to extend its runway. The convertible notes mature in 2029 — before that date, the company needs either to have reached cash flow self-sufficiency (unlikely without Neutron revenue) or to refinance/convert the notes. If equity markets are closed (e.g., a broad risk-off environment or a Neutron program failure that destroys investor confidence), refinancing becomes difficult. The company's ability to survive is materially dependent on continued access to public equity and debt markets. This is a meaningful risk for a company that has never generated positive FCF.

**Off-Balance-Sheet Obligations**

Rocket Lab has operating leases (manufacturing facilities, launch site leases) that create off-balance-sheet-like obligations under old accounting standards, though they are now on-balance-sheet under ASC 842. Specific operating lease commitments were not available from search results but are expected to be material given the company's multiple global facilities (New Zealand launch site, Long Beach manufacturing, Virginia Neutron launch complex, etc.). Pension obligations are not known to be material for a U.S.-incorporated aerospace company of this size. Contingent liabilities include potential cost overruns on fixed-price government contracts, though the SDA contracts are structured to allow for some cost adjustment. The Mynaric acquisition (announced but not yet consummated as of Q4 2025 earnings) will add additional obligations and integration costs.

**Stress Test: 30% Revenue Decline for Two Years**

A 30% revenue decline from $602M would reduce annual revenue to approximately $421M. At current gross margin (~35%), that yields ~$147M in gross profit. Against operating expenses (guidance of ~$120-126M/quarter = $480-504M annually), the company would have a deeply negative operating result — far worse than the current trajectory. Cash burn would accelerate materially. With $1.1B in starting cash, the company could likely survive two years of this scenario but would need to raise equity capital to avoid insolvency before the convertible notes mature in 2029. There would be no covenant-driven default (convertible notes typically do not have maintenance covenants), but survival would require accessing capital markets while under extreme financial stress. The verdict: survival is likely but not certain, and it would require significant dilution. The company would not face immediate default but would be operationally distressed.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** With $1.1B in cash, no near-term debt maturities, and convertible notes structured for conversion (not cash repayment) at relatively low coupon rates, Rocket Lab has sufficient runway to reach Neutron's first flight and the beginning of commercial operations without a near-term liquidity crisis.
- **Bear case:** The company burns $300-400M per year in FCF, is dependent on continuous equity dilution to maintain liquidity, has negative interest coverage, and faces a 2029 convertible note maturity that will require either operational FCF or another capital market transaction — creating compounding refinancing risk.
- **Confidence:** Medium — The cash balance is clearly reported ($1.1B) and the debt structure ($415-490M, 2029 maturity) is disclosed in SEC filings. The uncertainty lies in future cash burn trajectory, which depends heavily on Neutron development execution.

## Red Flags

- FCF burn accelerated to approximately -$321M in FY2025 and reached -$114M in a single quarter (Q4 2025) — cash consumption is not moderating.
- The $1.1B cash position includes $280.6M raised via ATM equity in a single quarter, indicating the company cannot self-fund from operations.
- Interest coverage by EBIT/interest is deeply negative and has been negative throughout the company's public history.
- Neutron program has already overrun its budget (~$360M actual vs. $250-300M planned) and the first launch has slipped to Q4 2026 — further delays mean additional cash consumption.
- Refinancing risk: the company is entirely dependent on capital markets to fund operations and retire the 2029 convertible notes.
- The Mynaric acquisition and other M&A pipeline items will consume additional cash, further pressuring liquidity.
- ATM equity program creates a structural dilution overhang for existing shareholders — not a balance sheet default risk, but economically harmful.
- Zero history of generating positive operating cash flow; this is a critical structural fact, not a temporary cyclical issue.

## Score: 5 / 10

Rocket Lab's balance sheet scores in the middle range — the $1.1B cash position and 2029 debt maturity provide genuine near-term safety and avoid an acute liquidity crisis, but the persistent and accelerating FCF burn, dependence on equity markets for survival, negative interest coverage, and Neutron-driven execution risk collectively prevent a higher score; this is a balance sheet that requires successful execution of a difficult multi-year program to remain solvent beyond 2028-2029.
