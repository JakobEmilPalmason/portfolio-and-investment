## Detailed Analysis

**Owner Earnings Today.** For full-year 2025, IQVIA reported operating cash flow of $2,654M and free cash flow of $2,051M (after ~$603M capex implied). Adjusted net income was $2,068M. Because IQVIA's adjusted FCF conversion runs at ~99% of adjusted net income, $2,051M is a reliable proxy for owner earnings. GAAP net income is significantly lower due to amortization of acquisition intangibles (primarily the 2016 IMS Health merger and subsequent bolt-ons), but this amortization is mostly non-cash and does not represent real economic cost to maintaing the business — owner earnings therefore cluster around the $2.0–2.1B range for 2025.

**Enterprise Value and Current Multiples.** At $171.60/share with a market cap of ~$29.1B, and net debt of $13.7B, the enterprise value is approximately $42.8B. This yields:
- Trailing EV/EBITDA: $42.8B / $3.79B = **11.3x** (vs. 13-year median ~18.75x)
- Trailing P/FCF: $29.1B / $2.05B = **14.2x**
- Forward P/E (2026 midpoint $12.70 EPS): **13.5x**
- Forward EV/EBITDA (2026 guided ~$4.0B EBITDA): **10.7x**

These multiples sit at the low end of IQVIA's historical range (trough: ~10.75x EV/EBITDA; peak: ~29.89x). The stock has de-rated sharply from its 2021 peak (~$280+) on elevated CRO cancellations, biotech funding stress, and AI disruption fears.

**Scenario Analysis (5-year horizon, discount rate 9%).**

*Bear Case:* Revenue growth stagnates at 2–3%/year (cancellations persist, biopharma cuts R&D). EBITDA margins compress 100bps to ~22%. Owner earnings in Year 5: ~$1.85B. Apply a 10x EV/EBITDA exit (~$41B EV), subtract $12B net debt (modest deleveraging assumed), equity value ~$29B, ~$173/share. Discounted back at 9%: ~$112/share. This bear case is ~35% below today's price.

*Base Case:* Revenue grows at ~5–6%/year per company guidance, EBITDA margins stable to +50bps (AI efficiency offsets pricing pressure). Owner earnings in Year 5: ~$2.6–2.7B. Apply a 13x EV/EBITDA exit (~$54B EV), subtract $10B net debt (steady buybacks and amortization), equity value ~$44B, ~$260/share. Discounted back at 9%: ~$169/share. This implies roughly fair value at today's price.

*Bull Case:* Revenue growth re-accelerates to 7–8% (new obesity/GLP-1 pipeline boost, AI-enhanced win rates), margins expand 150bps. Owner earnings in Year 5: ~$3.1B. Apply a 15x EV/EBITDA exit (~$63B EV), subtract $9B net debt, equity value ~$54B, ~$320/share. Discounted back at 9%: ~$208/share. This is ~21% above today's price.

**Multiples in Context.** IQVIA's current forward P/E of ~13.5x compares to: ICON plc (~16x), Charles River (~18x), and Medpace (~30x). The S&P 500 trades at ~21x forward earnings. IQVIA's discount appears excessive given its market leadership, data moat, and recurring revenue mix. The discount reflects CRO sector-wide re-rating and is arguably a combination of cycle trough concerns and AI uncertainty overhang. At the 13-year median multiple of 18.75x EV/EBITDA on 2026 forward EBITDA of ~$4.0B, intrinsic EV would be ~$75B; subtract $13B debt → equity value ~$62B, ~$370/share. Even at a 20% discount to that, fair value is ~$295.

**What Must Be True to Justify Today's Price.** The market's current $171.60 is essentially pricing in the base case returning to near fair value only after several years of moderate growth, with no multiple re-rating. The implied assumption is that EV/EBITDA stays depressed around 10–11x indefinitely, revenue growth stays ~4–5%, and no margin expansion occurs. This is a credible but pessimistic scenario — it prices in permanent structural impairment of the CRO model without giving credit for IQVIA's data and analytics differentiation.

**Is the Market Pricing Perfection or Disaster?** Clearly, the market is pricing something closer to a stressed scenario — not quite disaster, but meaningful structural concern. The 52-week high was $247 just last year; the stock has fallen ~31% from that peak on macro and sector headwinds. Given that the business generated $2B+ in free cash flow in 2025 and is guiding for growth in 2026, the current price appears to over-discount the risks.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** Multiple re-rates from 11x to 15x EV/EBITDA as CRO cancellations stabilize and biopharma R&D spending recovers, delivering ~$200–$210 intrinsic value discounted back, or ~$300+ on a forward basis.
- **Bear case:** Persistent cancellation headwinds compress owner earnings to $1.8B and the multiple stays depressed near 10x, implying ~$112/share on a DCF basis — ~35% downside from current price.
- **Confidence:** Medium — IQVIA's FCF generation and competitive position are clear, but the trajectory of CRO cancellations and the AI impact on pricing/margins introduces meaningful uncertainty in the Year 3–5 earnings range.

---

## Red Flags

- Net debt of $13.7B (3.63x EBITDA) creates meaningful downside amplification if earnings disappoint
- FCF conversion, while excellent, is partly a function of deferred revenue dynamics in backlog — quality of $32B backlog matters
- Bear case DCF (~$112) implies real downside if sector conditions deteriorate further
- Amortization from acquisitions masks GAAP earnings; GAAP P/E (~22x trailing) overstates cheapness relative to adjusted metrics

---

## Score: 6 / 10

IQVIA trades at historically cheap multiples (11.3x trailing EV/EBITDA vs. 18.75x median) and generates $2B+ in owner earnings, but heavy leverage and genuine CRO cycle uncertainty make this fairly valued at best on a risk-adjusted basis — closer to a 7 if cancellation trends stabilize, a 5 if they persist.
