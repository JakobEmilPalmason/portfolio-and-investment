## Detailed Analysis

**Owner Earnings Estimate**

Owner earnings = Net income + D&A − maintenance capex. For FY 2025: net income $132.2B + depreciation $21.1B − estimated maintenance capex (roughly 40–50% of total capex, or ~$37–46B) = approximately $107–116B in owner earnings. Using a midpoint of ~$110B and the current market cap of approximately $3.8 trillion (at $308.45/share × ~12.3B diluted shares), the owner earnings yield is roughly 2.9%. This is not a bargain by any traditional Buffett-style metric.

The key distortion is that the 2025 capex of $91.4B was itself already elevated, and management has guided to $175–185B for 2026 — a near-doubling. If only 40% of that 2026 figure is "maintenance" (a generous assumption given much is AI infrastructure growth spending), maintenance capex rises to $70–74B, compressing owner earnings to roughly $79–83B at 2026 depreciation run rates. This puts forward owner earnings yield below 2.2% at current prices. The capex surge is the single most important near-term valuation variable.

**Scenario Analysis — Year 5 (FY 2030)**

Bear Case: Search market share erodes modestly to ~85% globally under AI-native competition; operating margins compress from 32% to 26% due to sustained AI infrastructure investment; revenue grows at 8% CAGR to ~$591B; net income ~$154B; apply 20x multiple = $3.08T enterprise value. Discounted back 5 years at 10% = ~$1.91T. Per share: ~$155. That represents ~50% downside from current price.

Base Case: Search holds 88–90% share with AI Overviews successfully defending monetization; Cloud continues ~25–30% growth; operating margin stabilizes at 30%; revenue grows at 13% CAGR to ~$742B; net income ~$222B; apply 23x multiple = $5.1T. Discounted at 10% = ~$3.17T. Per share: ~$258. Still below today's price of $308, indicating the current price already prices in solid execution.

Bull Case: Gemini monetization accelerates; Cloud achieves $150B+ run rate by 2030; margin expansion to 35%+; revenue grows 17% CAGR to ~$885B; net income ~$310B; apply 25x = $7.75T. Discounted at 10% = ~$4.81T. Per share: ~$391. This offers ~27% upside from today but requires near-flawless execution across Search, Cloud, and AI for five straight years.

**Current Multiples vs. History and Peers**

Trailing P/E of 28.1x is at the low end of GOOG's own 5-year range (which peaked above 35x in 2021) and represents a discount to the S&P 500 tech sector average. Forward P/E of approximately 26.5x is more attractive. EV/EBITDA is approximately 19–20x (estimated). P/FCF at ~51x is elevated, though this is heavily distorted by the capex surge — on a normalized maintenance-capex basis, P/owner earnings is closer to 34–38x. Peers: Microsoft trades at ~32x forward earnings; Meta at ~22x. GOOG is in a reasonable band versus peers but not cheap.

**What Must Be True for Today's Price to Be Justified**

At $308/share, the market is effectively pricing in: (1) successful AI monetization of Search through Gemini AI Overviews, sustaining ad revenue growth through the disruption; (2) Google Cloud growing to a $120–150B+ run rate by 2030; (3) the $175–185B 2026 capex program delivering durable returns rather than sunk cost; (4) antitrust remedies remaining manageable, with no forced structural divestiture; and (5) operating margins returning to 32–35% after the capex investment phase ends (2027–2028). None of these assumptions are unreasonable, but all five must hold simultaneously.

**Market Pricing: Perfection or Pessimism?**

The market is pricing in a reasonably optimistic but not extreme scenario. The stock is down from its February 2026 all-time high of $349 (~12% off peak), which reflects some investor anxiety about the $180B capex announcement and its FCF implications. The 28x P/E implies neither panic nor exuberance. However, from a pure value investor standpoint, the FCF yield of 1.93% and a base-case DCF that prices the stock below current levels suggest the margin of safety is thin. The "fair value" range from a Buffett-style lens is approximately $220–345, with the current price in the upper half of that range.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** Cloud acceleration, Gemini monetization, and eventual capex normalization deliver 25–30%+ upside by 2028–2030 as the AI investment cycle pays off.
- **Bear case:** The $180B capex bet on AI infrastructure fails to generate commensurate returns, FCF remains compressed for 3+ years, and AI-native search competitors erode the core advertising business.
- **Confidence:** Medium — Alphabet's earnings power is real and well-documented, but the capex surge creates genuine valuation uncertainty for the next 12–24 months; base case DCF suggests modest overvaluation at current price.

## Red Flags

- 2026 capex of $175–185B (vs. $91.4B in 2025) will suppress free cash flow for at least 2 years; FCF may decline significantly in 2026
- FCF yield of 1.93% is historically low and leaves limited margin of safety on a cash generation basis
- Base-case DCF (5-year, 10% discount rate) implies ~$258/share — 16% below current price
- Revenue from AI generative products grew 400% YoY in Q4 2025, but from a small base; absolute dollar contribution to earnings is still modest relative to Search ad revenue

## Score: 5 / 10

At ~$308, GOOG is fairly valued to modestly overvalued: the underlying business quality is exceptional but the near-term capex surge compresses FCF, the base-case DCF implies a lower intrinsic value, and the current price requires near-flawless execution of the AI transition to justify.
