## Detailed Analysis

**What Reddit sells, and to whom.** Reddit is a social media platform organized around roughly 100,000+ active topic communities called "subreddits." Users post text, images, links, and video; other users vote content up or down and comment. This creates self-organizing communities around virtually every human interest — finance, gaming, medicine, parenting, niche hobbies. Reddit sells two things: (1) advertising space around this content to brands and marketers, and (2) access to its corpus of human-generated text to AI companies that need training data. The primary customer paying real money is the advertiser. The secondary customer is the AI developer. End users pay nothing and in fact are the product — their attention and their words are what Reddit monetizes.

**How Reddit actually makes money.** In 2025, advertising accounted for approximately $2.06B (94%) of the $2.203B in total revenue. Ad revenue grew 74% for the full year, driven by new ad formats (Reddit Max, launched late 2025), improved automation and targeting, and expansion into new verticals. The remaining ~$140M (6%) came from "Other" revenue — primarily data licensing deals with AI companies. Reddit has disclosed more than $200M in cumulative contract value from licensing arrangements with Google (~$60M/year) and OpenAI (~$70M/year), plus others. There is also a small Reddit Premium subscription business, but it is not reported separately and is immaterial. The gross margin for the full business is 91.2% — typical for a software/platform model where infrastructure costs are modest relative to revenue.

**Key revenue drivers.** There are four variables that matter most. First, daily and weekly active user growth — more users means more inventory to sell to advertisers. Second, ARPU (average revenue per user), especially in the US, where logged-in users monetize at dramatically higher rates than international users. Third, advertiser demand and CPMs — Reddit is still early in penetrating the large brand advertising budget pool and was growing ad revenue at 74% in 2025, suggesting it is taking share. Fourth, data licensing contract renewals — the existing AI deals with Google and OpenAI are in renegotiation as of early 2026, with Reddit pushing for "dynamic pricing" models that scale with AI usage. This stream could become more significant over time.

**Revenue predictability.** Advertising revenue is moderately predictable but not highly recurring. It is cyclical (softens in recessions), seasonal (Q4 is the strongest quarter by far due to holiday ad spend), and event-driven. There is some stickiness as advertisers who see results re-up budgets, but there are no long-term contracts locking in spend. The data licensing piece is more like a B2B SaaS contract — higher visibility, multi-year in some cases — though the dollar amounts are smaller and the renewal terms are uncertain in 2026. Top 10 advertisers account for 25–26% of revenue, which is a notable concentration but not unusual for digital ad platforms.

**Customer concentration and composition.** On the advertiser side, Reddit's customer base spans e-commerce, financial services, gaming, consumer tech, and healthcare brands. No single advertiser is disclosed as dominant, but the top 10 customers represent roughly a quarter of revenue. The AI licensing customer list is even more concentrated — essentially two or three companies. On the user side, Reddit's audience is distinctive: skews male, educated, English-speaking, and affluent, making it attractive to certain categories of advertisers. International users now make up ~57% of weekly active users but generate only about 20% of the US monetization rate, creating a structural gap as the platform globalizes.

**Can you explain this in 2 minutes?** Yes, easily. Reddit is a website where millions of communities discuss every topic imaginable. People vote content up or down, which surfaces the best discussions. Advertisers pay to show ads alongside these conversations because the audience is engaged, topic-specific, and hard to reach elsewhere. AI companies pay Reddit separately for the right to use its 20-year archive of human conversations to train their models. Reddit keeps almost all of the revenue as profit because running an internet forum doesn't cost that much. The main risks are advertiser budget cycles (if the economy slows, ad spend drops) and whether Reddit can monetize international users as well as it monetizes US users. The business model is easy to understand.

## Signal Summary
- **Bull case:** Reddit is a structurally unique content platform monetizing at an early stage, with 69% revenue growth in 2025 and a new buyback signaling confidence — still early innings on advertising penetration.
- **Bear case:** Revenue is ~94% advertising, which is cyclical and dependent on user attention that competes with every other platform; US user growth is slowing, and international monetization lags badly.
- **Confidence:** High — Reddit's business model is simple, the financials are freshly reported (Q4 2025, Feb 5 2026), and the revenue drivers are well-documented.

---

## Red Flags
- US logged-in DAU growth has slowed for six consecutive quarters — the most monetizable segment is maturing
- International WAU now makes up ~57% of total users but monetizes at ~20% of US rates — global growth is partly hollow
- ~94% revenue concentration in advertising makes the income statement highly sensitive to macro ad budget cycles
- Top 10 advertisers represent ~25% of revenue — loss of one large advertiser would be noticeable
- Q1 2026 guidance implies growth decelerating from 69% to 52–54% YoY — whether this is a one-quarter blip or the start of a fade is unclear

## Score: 8 / 10
Reddit's business model is clear, direct, and easy to explain — sell ad space around authentic human community content — with a bonus AI licensing stream that adds optionality; the main complexity is the two-tier user monetization gap (US vs. international) and the single-revenue-source concentration in advertising.
