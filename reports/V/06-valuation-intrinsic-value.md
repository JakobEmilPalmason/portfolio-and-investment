## Detailed Analysis

**Owner Earnings Today**

Visa's FY2025 financials provide a clear picture of economic earnings power. Net income (GAAP) was approximately $20.0B; adding back D&A (estimated ~$800M based on historical run rates) and subtracting maintenance capex (roughly $1.0–1.2B of the $1.5B total capex, with the remainder growth-oriented) yields owner earnings of approximately **$20–21B**. On a per-share basis, using roughly 1.95B diluted shares outstanding as of early FY2026 (reflecting aggressive buybacks — Visa repurchased ~54M shares at $335/share in FY2025), owner earnings per share are approximately **$10.75–$11.00**. This aligns closely with non-GAAP EPS of $11.47 reported for FY2025.

**Scenario Analysis — Year 5 Owner Earnings (FY2030)**

*Bear case:* Payment volume growth slows to 4–5% (macro headwinds, fintech share loss, DOJ remedy), client incentive growth outpaces revenue, margins compress 200bps. Owner earnings grow at ~5% annually, reaching ~$26B (~$14/share at 1.85B shares). Apply 20x multiple → $280/share. Discount at 9% → ~$182/share. This scenario requires a genuine structural disruption or antitrust penalty affecting core debit economics.

*Base case:* 9–10% revenue growth (consistent with recent 11–15% quarters), FCF margins hold near 50–54%, buybacks continue reducing share count ~3%/year. Owner earnings grow 10% annually to ~$34B (~$18.50/share). Apply 22x multiple → $407/share. Discount at 9% → ~$264/share on a pure DCF basis. However, Visa historically commands a premium because of its asset-light model — a 25x multiple yields ~$465 undiscounted, or ~$302 discounted.

*Bull case:* Value-Added Services (VAS, up 28% YoY in Q1 FY2026) accelerates as a margin-enhancing layer; new flows (B2B, government, real-time payments) add incremental growth. Owner earnings grow 13–14% to ~$38B (~$21/share). Apply 28x multiple → $588/share undiscounted, ~$382 discounted at 9%.

**Multiples in Context**

The current trailing P/E of ~30x compares to Visa's 10-year average of approximately 33–35x and a 5-year forward average of ~28x. At ~24x forward (on FY2026 consensus of ~$12.83–$13.10), the stock is trading at or slightly below its long-run average forward multiple — unusual for a business with this quality profile. Compared to Mastercard (MA), which typically commands a similar or slight premium multiple, Visa appears modestly cheaper. Compared to the S&P 500 at ~22x forward, Visa's premium is approximately 10%, which is far below its historical spread.

EV/EBITDA of ~21x reflects the same modestly de-rated picture. In 2021, this ratio exceeded 40x. The compression reflects both share price underperformance and strong EBITDA growth, suggesting either a structural re-rating lower or a genuine opportunity for multiple re-expansion.

**What Must Be True for Today's Price to Be Justified?**

At $314/share and approximately $11 in trailing non-GAAP EPS, the stock requires roughly 8–10% annual EPS growth over 10 years with a terminal multiple of 20–22x to produce a 9–10% IRR. Visa has grown EPS at 13–15% compounded over the prior decade. The bar today is meaningfully lower than what Visa has historically delivered, which is why the stock does not appear to be pricing in perfection. It is pricing in a modest slowdown from recent trends — a reasonable expectation but one the company has repeatedly surprised to the upside.

**Is the Market Pricing Perfection or Disaster?**

Neither. The current valuation is in a "fair to modestly undervalued" zone. The de-rating from 2021 peak multiples is largely justified by higher interest rates raising discount rates; at the current price level, the embedded growth assumption is achievable and does not require heroic assumptions. The DOJ antitrust case (see Umbrella 7) introduces genuine tail risk that partly explains the multiple discount versus history.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** Re-rating to historical 27–30x forward P/E as DOJ risk resolves and VAS growth accelerates drives the stock to $380–$420 within 24 months.
- **Bear case:** DOJ remedy structurally impairs debit economics, client incentives continue rising faster than revenue, and the stock re-rates to 20x forward, implying $260–$270.
- **Confidence:** Medium — The quantitative picture is clear and consistent across sources; uncertainty around the DOJ outcome and its economic impact introduces a meaningful range of fair values (roughly $260–$420).

---

## Red Flags

- EV/EBITDA and P/E are still above market averages despite de-rating — not cheap in absolute terms
- Base-case DCF at 9% discount rate produces intrinsic value below current price; a positive IRR at $314 requires either growth outperformance or multiple stability
- DOJ case not yet reflected in consensus estimates; if a structural remedy is imposed on debit routing, the bear case valuation drops materially
- Management has been aggressive on buybacks at $335/share average in FY2025 — buying above intrinsic value on some scenarios

## Score: 5 / 10

Visa is fairly valued at current prices — not cheap enough for a classic margin-of-safety buy, but not egregiously overpriced; the embedded growth assumption (~9% annual EPS growth) is below historical delivery but the DOJ overhang and rate environment justify a discount to prior peak multiples.
