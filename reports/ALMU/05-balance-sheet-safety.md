## Detailed Analysis

**Debt Levels and Structure**

Aeluma carries virtually no financial debt. The debt/equity ratio for the quarter ended June 30, 2025 was reported at 0.00, and total debt is estimated at approximately $975K — likely operating leases or small equipment financing rather than any meaningful financial leverage. There is no term loan, revolving credit facility, or significant bond/note outstanding. The company resolved its only notable debt instrument in March 2025, when $3.1M in convertible notes were converted to equity at $3.50 per share (issuing 898,573 shares). This conversion eliminated the debt but added dilution. Total debt/EBITDA is not a meaningful ratio here — EBITDA is negative, and debt is immaterial. By any conventional leverage metric, Aeluma is unleveraged.

**Interest Coverage**

Interest coverage (EBIT/interest expense) is not a meaningful metric for Aeluma. With effectively zero financial debt, interest expense is negligible. EBIT is deeply negative (approximately -$1.3M to -$2M per quarter), but that reflects operating losses from investments in R&D and personnel, not debt service costs. There is no interest burden to cover. This is one of the few unambiguously positive balance sheet characteristics for an early-stage company — it is not exposed to rising interest rates and has no refinancing risk.

**Liquidity**

Aeluma's liquidity position is exceptionally strong relative to its size. Cash and cash equivalents (including a certificate of deposit) totaled $38.6M at December 31, 2025. This compares to a quarterly revenue run rate of approximately $1.3–1.4M and quarterly cash burn of approximately $0.8–1.9M. At $1.5M average quarterly cash outflow, the company has approximately 25+ quarters (~6 years) of runway. The current ratio is approximately 24–33x (reported at 24.59 for one quarter and 32.76 for another), indicating current assets overwhelmingly exceed current liabilities. The only caveat is that the cash is not self-funded — the $38.6M balance reflects primarily the proceeds of a $22.1M public equity offering priced at $13/share in September 2025 and a prior $25M raise. The company has a shelf registration (Form S-3) allowing up to $100M in future securities issuances, which is both a liquidity backstop and a dilution risk.

**Debt Maturity Schedule**

No material debt maturities exist. The convertible notes (the only significant debt instrument the company held) were fully converted to equity in March 2025. The residual ~$975K in total debt is presumed to be short-term or operating in nature. There is no refinancing risk. The company does not disclose a formal debt maturity ladder, which is consistent with having essentially no financial debt.

**Off-Balance-Sheet Obligations**

Aeluma is likely subject to operating lease obligations for its Goleta, California facility, but these are modest relative to the cash balance. No pension obligations are indicated (the company is approximately 4 years old as a public entity). No significant contingent liabilities have been disclosed in available press releases. The company does have government R&D contract commitments (deliverables tied to DARPA, NASA, and Navy contracts), which carry performance obligations but not financial debt-like claims. In sum, off-balance-sheet obligations appear immaterial.

**Stress Test: 30% Revenue Drop for 2 Years**

Aeluma's revenue in FY2026 is guided at $4–6M. A 30% haircut would reduce revenue to approximately $2.8–4.2M annually. At current operating expense levels (~$12M/year), the company would increase its annual cash burn from approximately $7–8M to approximately $8–10M. Starting from $38.6M in cash (December 31, 2025), the company would still have $18–25M of cash remaining after 2 years of this stress scenario — comfortably above zero and without needing to issue equity. This is an unusually strong stress-test outcome for any company, especially a small-cap pre-profitability technology firm. The company would survive this scenario without any equity issuance. The only scenario that produces a liquidity crisis would be a catastrophic, multi-year collapse in revenue combined with a significant expansion of operating costs — neither of which is the base case.

**Assessment of Balance Sheet Quality**

The balance sheet is genuinely strong in structure, though the origin of that strength matters: it is entirely the product of dilutive equity raises rather than retained earnings or operational cash generation. The company has diluted existing shareholders by approximately 50% over the past year to fund this cash position. That said, for the purpose of balance sheet safety analysis, the fact is that Aeluma has more than $38M in cash, effectively zero debt, and minimal off-balance-sheet obligations. A creditor or stress-test perspective finds nothing concerning here. The risk is not solvency — it is whether the company will ever stop burning cash and start generating it.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** Fortress-level cash position ($38.6M) with zero debt gives Aeluma 5–6 years of runway to achieve commercial scale without existential financial risk; no refinancing overhang.
- **Bear case:** The strong cash balance masks an ongoing equity dilution machine — the company has grown shares outstanding by ~50% in one year and holds a $100M shelf offering; continued dilution erodes per-share value even if the business survives.
- **Confidence:** High — Balance sheet structure is straightforward and well-documented in press releases; cash, debt, and liquidity figures are recent (December 2025) and consistent across sources.

## Red Flags

- Cash position is entirely equity-raise funded, not operationally generated — the balance sheet is strong but the underlying business is cash-consumptive
- Shares outstanding grew approximately 50% over the past year, indicating ongoing dilution as the primary funding mechanism
- $100M shelf registration (Form S-3) signals willingness to continue diluting shareholders as needed
- No disclosed credit facility — liquidity is entirely cash on hand with no committed backup facility identified
- Convertible note conversion at $3.50/share (vs. current price ~$17–18) was significantly dilutive when converted

## Score: 7 / 10

Aeluma's balance sheet is objectively conservative and presents no near-term solvency risk — zero meaningful debt, $38.6M cash, 25+ quarters of runway, and a clean maturity schedule — but the score is tempered by the fact that this safety is manufactured through serial equity dilution rather than earned through profitable operations, and the shelf registration signals continued dilution risk ahead.
