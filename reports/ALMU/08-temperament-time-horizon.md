## Detailed Analysis

**Holding Period Suitability.** Aeluma does not fit the profile of a 5–10 year business compounder in the Buffett sense. A 10-year compounder has: (1) a durable, profitable business today; (2) reinvestment opportunities at high returns; (3) pricing power that compounds owner earnings over time. Aeluma has none of these. Instead, the holding thesis is binary: either commercial photonics revenue materializes within 3–5 years — in which case the stock could be worth multiples of today's price — or it does not, in which case the stock drifts toward zero via dilution. This is a venture capital time horizon dressed up in a public market structure. The relevant holding period is 2–4 years to see whether the first commercial customer anchor emerges, not 10 years of compounding owner earnings.

**Volatility Tolerance.** The stock's behavior is incompatible with low-volatility portfolio temperaments. The 52-week range spans $5.79 to $25.88 — a 347% spread between low and high. The current price of $15.02 is already down ~42% from the July 2025 all-time high of $24.04. During the FY2026 period, the stock has exhibited daily volatility of up to 10.74% and intraday swings of over $1.50. For context, a 10% daily move on the position means a $100,000 investment can swing $10,000 in a single session. An investor in ALMU must psychologically and financially accept 50–70% drawdowns without being forced to sell — because if commercial revenue fails to materialize on schedule, that is a realistic scenario. Recovery time from drawdowns is unknowable, since it depends on external contract and customer events, not operational momentum.

**Catalysts — What, When, Within Company's Control.** The key catalysts that could change the risk/reward are:
- *New commercial customer announcement* (e.g., a signed development agreement or volume order from a hyperscaler, defense prime, or mobile OEM) — management has noted increasing price quotation requests; first commercial order close could come in H2 FY2026 (late calendar 2026). Partially within company's control (quality of sales process) but ultimately customer-driven.
- *DARPA contract renewal or new government award* — management targets 3–7 new development contracts in FY2026. Within partial company control (must win competitive bids). Timeline: FY2026 (June 2026 fiscal year end).
- *Nvidia / hyperscaler photonics supply chain formalization* — Nvidia reportedly committed ~$4B to photonics supply chain; whether Aeluma is a formal beneficiary remains unconfirmed. Not within company's control; timeline: uncertain.
- *Revenue ramp confirmation* — if H2 FY2026 (Q3 + Q4 results, reported September and November 2026) show meaningful commercial revenue uptick, it de-risks the thesis materially. Timeline: 6–9 months.

The absence of near-term catalysts that are firmly within management's control is a key concern. The stock's fate is largely determined by external parties (government agencies, large customers) making decisions on their own schedules.

**Position Sizing Logic.** At current risk/reward, ALMU should not be sized as a core holding. The appropriate framing is a *small speculative allocation* — if used at all — where total loss of the position would not materially damage portfolio outcomes. For a diversified portfolio, this means no more than 0.5–1.5% of total capital. A "starter" position of this size allows participation in a bull-case outcome (>50% upside) without catastrophic downside (>90%) damaging the overall portfolio. No case exists for a core position (>3% of portfolio) at current price and development stage.

**Correlation and Portfolio Overlap.** ALMU's exposure overlaps with: (1) semiconductor holdings (any NVDA, AVGO, or photonics-adjacent position is a correlated AI infrastructure bet); (2) defense/government contractor exposure (any DoD R&D contractor is correlated to government budget cycles); (3) small-cap speculative growth (any early-stage technology company with similar binary outcomes). An investor holding NVDA, LITE (Lumentum), or POET alongside ALMU has multiple overlapping bets on the same AI photonics theme. That concentration reduces diversification benefit.

**Process Discipline — Entry, Exit, Review.** For a disciplined investor choosing to hold a small speculative position:
- *Entry rule:* Only enter below $8 per share (closer to base-case intrinsic value of $6.20, with a small optionality premium). At $15.02, the entry price offers no margin.
- *Sell rules:* (1) Mandatory review if commercial revenue has not reached $1M annualized run-rate by end of Q4 FY2026 (November 2026); (2) Sell on any further dilutive equity raise above 20M total shares without corresponding commercial revenue progress; (3) Sell if CEO or other insiders accelerate selling beyond disclosed volumes; (4) Consider trimming if stock re-tests $22–25 range, as that prices in bull-case outcomes prematurely.
- *Review cadence:* Quarterly earnings releases (company fiscal year ends June 30, so Q3 = February, Q4 = May, full year = September).

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** If a commercial customer anchor is announced within 6–9 months, the stock de-risks materially and a larger position size becomes defensible.
- **Bear case:** If FY2026 closes (June 2026) with <$5M in total revenue and no commercial customer announcement, the thesis has failed to advance and the position should be exited regardless of price.
- **Confidence:** High — the volatility profile and catalyst dependency are clearly observable from public data; the position sizing and discipline conclusions follow directly from those facts.
