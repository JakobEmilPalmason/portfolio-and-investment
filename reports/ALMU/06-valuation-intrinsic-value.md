## Detailed Analysis

**Owner Earnings Assessment.** The owner earnings framework (net income + D&A − maintenance capex) cannot be applied to Aeluma in any meaningful way. Net loss for FY2025 was $3.0M on $4.7M in revenue, and Q2 FY2026 showed a $1.9M net loss on just $1.3M in revenue — losses that exceed the revenue generated. Adjusted EBITDA swung to a loss of $917K in Q2 FY2026 after brief near-breakeven in FY2025. There is no cash generation from operations; the business is entirely funded by equity issuances and government contracts. Maintenance capex is not separately disclosed but given the capital-light fab model, depreciation is likely modest. Owner earnings are approximately −$7M to −$8M on an annualized basis.

**Intrinsic Value Scenario Analysis.** Any intrinsic value estimate for ALMU requires making explicit assumptions about a revenue ramp that has not yet begun. The company's entire commercial revenue in Q2 FY2026 was $41,000 — 97% of all revenue came from government R&D contracts. The following scenarios assume Aeluma's fiscal year ends June 30.

*Bear Case (probability: 40%):* Government contracts do not renew at full value after FY2026, commercial revenue remains sub-$2M by FY2030, and the company requires two more equity raises totaling $40M in additional dilution. Year 5 (FY2030) revenue: $8M. Applying a 5x revenue multiple (distressed-but-alive pre-revenue tech): equity value ~$40M. Discounted back 5 years at 15%: ~$20M total equity value. With ~20M+ shares outstanding (post-dilution), intrinsic value per share: ~$1.00.

*Base Case (probability: 40%):* Aeluma successfully converts 2–3 commercial customers from its photonics platform (defense/AI datacenters), grows revenue to ~$25M by FY2030 with improving gross margins as government contract mix normalizes. Net loss narrows to ~$3M by FY2030. Applying a 10x revenue multiple: equity value ~$250M. Discounted back 5 years at 15%: ~$124M. Divided by ~20M shares: ~$6.20 per share.

*Bull Case (probability: 20%):* Nvidia and other hyperscaler photonics investment materializes into real commercial orders; Aeluma captures 1–2% of the $2.86B silicon photonics market by FY2030, reaching $50M+ in revenue and approaching operating breakeven. Applying a 15x revenue multiple to $55M: ~$825M equity value. Discounted back 5 years at 15%: ~$410M. Divided by ~18M shares: ~$22.80 per share.

**Probability-Weighted Intrinsic Value:** (0.40 × $1.00) + (0.40 × $6.20) + (0.20 × $22.80) = $0.40 + $2.48 + $4.56 = **~$7.44 per share.**

**Current Price vs Intrinsic Value.** At $15.02, the stock trades at approximately **2x the probability-weighted intrinsic value estimate**. This implies the market is pricing in a significantly higher probability of the bull case — or a more aggressive bull case revenue ramp — than the evidence currently supports.

**Multiple Context.** At ~$283M market cap and ~$245M enterprise value (subtracting $38.6M cash), ALMU trades at ~52x EV/FY2025 revenue of $4.7M. Comparable early-stage photonics names like POET Technologies trade at 1,200x+ and Lightwave Logic at 5,500x+ revenue, suggesting ALMU is "cheap" relative to the most speculative peers but expensive relative to any fundamental anchor. Against profitable semiconductor companies (AVGO, MCHP, TXN), ALMU's multiple is not comparable — it is purely a pre-revenue option on technology commercialization. The Morningstar fair value of $22.20 likely embeds the base-to-bull case; InvestingPro marks it overvalued, consistent with a bear-to-base weighting.

**What Must Be True for $15.02 to Be Fair Value.** At today's price, the market is implicitly assigning ~50%+ probability to a bull-case scenario where Aeluma captures meaningful commercial photonics revenue by FY2030. This requires: (1) at least one large commercial customer (hyperscaler, defense prime, or mobile OEM) committing to volume orders within 18–24 months; (2) no further material dilution above ~19M shares; (3) the $11.7M DARPA contract milestone being met on schedule; and (4) the company reaching at least $30M+ in revenue by FY2030. None of these conditions has been confirmed. The market is pricing a lottery ticket at mid-range odds when the ticket is, at best, early-stage.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** If Nvidia's photonics supply chain investment translates into real Aeluma commercial orders, and the company hits $50M+ revenue by FY2030, the stock could be worth $20–25+ from today's price.
- **Bear case:** If commercial revenue fails to materialize and contract renewals stall, repeated dilutive equity raises will compress per-share value toward $1–3.
- **Confidence:** Low — because 97% of revenue is government R&D contracts with lumpy timing, no commercial revenue base exists to anchor any DCF, and the entire valuation rests on outcomes 4–7 years away.

## Red Flags

- No commercial revenue to speak of — $41K in commercial product revenue in Q2 FY2026 against a $283M market cap
- Sustained insider selling with zero insider purchases (CEO sold 150K shares August 2025; major holder sold serially through early 2026)
- Revenue guidance for FY2026 ($4–6M) implies flat-to-decline from FY2025 ($4.7M), not acceleration
- Q2 FY2026 Adjusted EBITDA swung from +$647K to −$917K year-over-year — deteriorating economics
- Share count has grown ~50% in one year, from ~12M to ~18M shares, with more dilution likely

## Score: 2 / 10

At $15.02, ALMU is priced for an outcome that has less than a 20% probability of materializing on the timeline the market requires; the probability-weighted intrinsic value is approximately $7–8 per share, implying the stock is roughly 2x overvalued relative to a fair expected-value calculation.
