## Detailed Analysis

**ROIC and ROE.** Moody's trailing ROIC stands at approximately 29.5%, with a five-year average near 23–24%. Return on equity is 62%, which is inflated by the debt-funded balance sheet (D/E of ~1.75x), so ROIC is the more meaningful metric. Even on the more conservative invested-capital base, the business earns returns roughly 3–5x its cost of capital — a clear signal of durable pricing power. The 2022 trough did not meaningfully dent ROIC: even in that down-cycle year Moody's generated $1.37B in net income on revenues of $5.47B, implying a net margin of 25%. The business does not lose money in bad years; it merely earns less.

**Segment margin structure.** Moody's operates two segments with fundamentally different margin profiles. MIS (credit ratings) generated $4.12B in revenue in FY2025 at an adjusted operating margin of 63.6% — up from 60.1% in FY2024 and 54.5% in FY2023. Revenue in this segment is driven by global debt issuance activity; in a strong issuance year, incremental revenue flows almost entirely to the operating line because the cost structure is largely fixed (analyst headcount, technology, regulatory compliance). MA (analytics and data) generated $3.60B at a 33.1% adjusted margin — lower than MIS but growing steadily (30.5% in 2023, 30.7% in 2024, 33.1% in 2025). MA's subscription-heavy mix (95% recurring revenue) means it compounds predictably even when MIS is in a trough. The consolidated adjusted operating margin reached 51.1% in FY2025, up from 48.1% in FY2024 and 43.9% in FY2023.

**FCF and owner earnings.** Operating cash flow reached $2.90B in FY2025 and free cash flow (after $326M capex) was $2.58B — against GAAP net income of $2.46B. FCF conversion to net income is approximately 105%, which is exceptional. The business carries substantial D&A from goodwill amortization on past acquisitions, so owner earnings (FCF) are a cleaner measure than GAAP earnings. Over the five years from FY2021 to FY2025, cumulative FCF was approximately $10.6B against cumulative net income of $9.7B — confirming consistent over-conversion. Moody's returned $2.4B to shareholders in FY2025 ($1.71B buybacks + $0.70B dividends), funded comfortably from FCF.

**Capital intensity and operating leverage.** Capex runs at 4–5% of revenue and is almost entirely software development and technology infrastructure. There are no factories, no inventory, no heavy equipment. The primary asset is intellectual capital, brand, and regulatory moat. This light capital structure means incremental revenue largely converts to cash. The operating leverage is visible in the margin progression: from FY2023 to FY2025, revenue grew from $5.92B to $7.72B (+30%), while operating income grew from $2.14B to $3.35B (+57%), demonstrating substantial fixed-cost leverage.

**Revenue quality.** MIS revenue (~53% of total) is transactional — it spikes with bond issuance volumes and dips in credit market freezes. This was painfully visible in 2022, when rising rates crushed issuance and revenue fell 12%. MA revenue (~47% of total) is subscription-based and Annualized Recurring Revenue (ARR) grew 8% to $3.50B in FY2025 — providing predictable income regardless of issuance conditions. Management has deliberately grown MA to reduce MIS cyclicality, and the current ~53/47 split is the most balanced it has ever been. Over time MA's share is likely to increase further, making consolidated earnings progressively less volatile.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** The ratings franchise is an oligopoly with unassailable regulatory entrenchment; MIS margins will expand further as issuance volumes normalize at high levels, while MA's SaaS-like ARR compounds at 8–10% annually.
- **Bear case:** A severe multi-year credit market freeze (2022-type but prolonged) could cut MIS revenues by 20–30%, pressuring consolidated margins and FCF below the level needed to service debt comfortably.
- **Confidence:** High — the economics of the ratings business are well-documented and consistent across multiple cycles; margin and FCF data are from company-reported financials.

## Red Flags

- MIS is structurally cyclical: a 12% total revenue decline happened in a single year (2022) with no fundamental change to the business.
- Very high ROE (62%) reflects leverage, not pure earnings power; investors should anchor to ROIC (~29%), not ROE.
- MA margins (33%) trail MIS by 30 percentage points, which limits blended margin expansion as MA grows its share.

## Score: 9 / 10

A near-perfect economics profile — 29%+ ROIC, 50%+ adjusted operating margins, 100%+ FCF conversion, and minimal capital intensity — with one point deducted for the meaningful cyclicality of the dominant MIS segment.
