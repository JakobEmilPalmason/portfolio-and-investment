## Detailed Analysis

**Debt levels.** As of the end of FY2025, Moody's carried $6.99B in long-term debt and approximately $7.35B in total debt (including short-term portions). This is the product of deliberate financial engineering: management has used cheap debt to fund acquisitions (Bureau van Dijk in 2017 for $3.3B; RMS in 2021 for $2.0B) and to fund aggressive share buybacks. The debt-to-equity ratio is ~1.75x — elevated relative to most industrial companies but not extraordinary for a financial services firm with extremely stable cash flows. Net debt is ~$5.0B (total debt minus $2.38B cash). Debt-to-EBITDA is approximately 1.9x, which is conservative for Moody's credit profile. Critically, total debt has declined from $7.97B in FY2021 to $7.35B in FY2025, demonstrating gradual deleveraging while simultaneously returning capital.

**Interest coverage.** Moody's EBIT in FY2025 was approximately $3.35B (operating income per the income statement). Net interest expense runs approximately $215–225M annually. This implies interest coverage of roughly 15–17x on an EBIT basis. Even in the 2022 trough, operating income was $1.88B against a similar interest burden — implying coverage of ~8–9x, still perfectly safe. The company maintains an investment-grade credit rating on its own debt, which is notable given the business: it would be deeply reputationally damaging for a ratings agency to have its own debt downgraded to non-investment-grade.

**Liquidity.** The company held $2.38B in cash and short-term investments at year-end FY2025. In addition, an undrawn revolving credit facility of $1.25B provides a further liquidity backstop. Short-term assets ($5.2B) exceed short-term liabilities ($3.0B) by a healthy margin, and the current ratio is approximately 1.74x. The company also has access to its commercial paper program (approximately $1B capacity). Total immediate liquidity exceeds $3.6B. This is more than adequate for operations, near-term debt service, and opportunistic capital allocation.

**Refinancing risk.** The specific maturity schedule from the most recent 10-K was not accessible for this analysis, but available data indicates Moody's does not face any concentrated near-term maturities. The company's average coupon on its outstanding senior notes is estimated in the low-to-mid 3% range (based on ~$215–225M interest expense on ~$7B of debt), which is well below current investment-grade issuance rates of ~5–6%. Over time, as notes mature and are refinanced at higher rates, interest expense will drift upward — but even a 150-basis-point increase on the entire debt stack would add only ~$105M to annual interest, easily absorbed by FCF.

**Off-balance-sheet obligations.** Moody's has operating lease obligations (primarily office space globally) which are carried on-balance-sheet under ASC 842. No material off-balance-sheet financing vehicles, special purpose entities, or contingent obligations were identified in available disclosures. The primary contingent liability is ongoing litigation, including the lingering tail of legacy structured finance rating disputes; these have historically been settled for amounts that, while significant, did not impair the franchise.

**Stress test: severe debt-issuance drought.** In a scenario resembling 2022 but more severe — say, a 25% decline in MIS revenue sustained for two years — MIS would generate approximately $3.1B at 63.6% margins, producing ~$2.0B of adjusted operating income from that segment. Consolidated operating income might fall to approximately $1.8–2.0B. After interest (~$220M) and taxes (~25%), net income would be approximately $1.2–1.4B. FCF (near net income given low capex) would cover: interest ($220M), dividends ($700M at current rate), and leave $280–480M residual. Buybacks would stop. Debt service would be uninterrupted. The company would not violate any reasonable debt covenant. This is not a fragile balance sheet.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** Debt-to-EBITDA below 2x, 15–17x interest coverage, $3.6B liquidity cushion, and self-funding buyback capacity make this one of the most credit-safe businesses of its size.
- **Bear case:** A multi-year issuance freeze coinciding with forced debt refinancing at significantly higher rates could compress FCF margins meaningfully, though solvency risk remains remote.
- **Confidence:** High — balance sheet data sourced directly from company filings via multiple aggregators; key ratios are consistent across sources.

## Red Flags

- Goodwill/intangibles of ~$6.4B (~40% of total assets) represent an intangible asset base that would face scrutiny in a severe business downturn; a large impairment would hurt book equity but not cash flows.
- Exact debt maturity schedule not confirmed from primary source; refinancing risk assessment is based on estimated weighted average coupon rather than specific note-by-note data.
- Debt-to-equity of ~1.75x is elevated vs. non-financial peers, though appropriate for the business quality and cash flow stability.

## Score: 8 / 10

Moody's carries meaningful leverage (deliberate, well-structured) but enormous cash flow generation ensures coverage remains robust even under stress; the only deductions are for the elevated gross debt load and the goodwill concentration on the balance sheet.
