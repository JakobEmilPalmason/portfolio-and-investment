# Compact Checklist — QCOM

**Date:** 2026-03-10

1. **Business**: Qualcomm sells Snapdragon chips to smartphone and automotive OEMs (87% of revenue) and collects FRAND royalties on every CDMA/4G/5G device sold globally (13% of revenue at 74% operating margins), with Apple, Samsung, and Xiaomi each exceeding 10% of FY2025's $44.3B in consolidated revenue.

2. **Moat**: The QTL patent moat — 140,000+ patents ranked #2 globally in 5G SEPs — is a structural toll-road on global wireless communication that no competitor can replicate retroactively; the QCT chip moat is real but eroding, particularly as Apple's C1 modem ships and Samsung reduces Snapdragon share.

3. **Economics**: Qualcomm generated $12.8B in free cash flow on $44.3B in revenue (29% FCF margin) in FY2025, with capex of only $1.2B (2.7% of revenue) due to the fabless model, producing ROE of ~42–54% and owner earnings of roughly $11.5–$12.5B annually.

4. **Management**: CEO Cristiano Amon is executing a credible and trackable diversification strategy (auto + IoT combined grew 27% YoY in FY2025, targeting $22B by FY2029) and returned approximately 100% of FY2025 FCF to shareholders, but took a 15% pay raise in a year when GAAP net income fell 45%, and insider ownership is low enough that management's personal wealth is not materially at risk alongside shareholders.

5. **Debt risk**: With $14.8B in debt against $14.0B EBITDA (1.06x leverage), $13.8B in cash, 18.6x interest coverage, and A/A2 credit ratings, Qualcomm survives a 30% revenue decline for two consecutive years without needing to issue equity.

6. **Price**: At ~$136 (12.9x forward earnings, 8.6% FCF yield, EV/EBITDA of 10.7x versus a 13-year median of 12.8x), today's price is approximately fairly valued in a base case and requires only that Qualcomm holds earnings roughly flat while executing automotive and IoT diversification at a modest pace — it does not require growth to justify the price.

7. **Margin of safety**: The QTL licensing business alone — a structural royalty on the entire global handset industry generating ~$5–6B annually at 70%+ margins — survives the Apple chip exit intact and sets a durable earnings floor, but China at 46% of total revenue is a binary risk that could produce 30–35% downside if a trade escalation hits before diversification is complete.

8. **Thesis breaker**: Sell if China revenue declines more than 25% over any rolling 12-month period without offsetting growth elsewhere, or if QTL faces a successful legal or regulatory challenge that materially reduces its patent royalty rates — either event would destroy the earnings floor that makes the current valuation defensible.
