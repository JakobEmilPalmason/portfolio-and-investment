## Detailed Analysis

**Owner Earnings Estimate**

Copart's reported FY2025 financials: revenue $4.647B (+9.7% YoY), net income attributable to Copart $1.55B, diluted EPS $1.59, free cash flow $1.26B. Total capex was $569M, while D&A was approximately $200M (quarterly D&A of ~$50M × 4). The company's heavy capex is largely growth-oriented — acquiring and permitting new yards, expanding existing facilities. A reasonable estimate of maintenance capex (keeping existing yards operational without growth) is $150–200M per year, since the physical infrastructure is durable and slowly depreciating. Owner earnings = net income ($1.55B) + D&A ($200M) - maintenance capex (~$175M) = approximately **$1.575B**, or ~$1.61 per diluted share. This is a more accurate measure of the cash the business generates for owners than FCF, which is suppressed by substantial reinvestment.

However, in fiscal H1 2026 (August 2025–January 2026), revenue fell 1.4% YoY and Q2 revenue was down 3.6%, so trailing owner earnings have softened somewhat from the FY2025 peak. On an LTM (last twelve months through January 2026) basis, owner earnings are estimated at approximately $1.40–1.50B.

**Scenario Analysis — Year 5 (FY2031)**

*Bear Case:* Volume growth stalls at 2–3% per year as accident frequency declines, IAA/RB Global takes share, and insurance company behavior shifts. Net income margins compress from ~33% to ~28% due to pricing pressure. Revenue grows from $4.65B to ~$5.2B. Net income reaches ~$1.45B; owner earnings ~$1.5B. Apply a 20x P/E multiple (de-rating for lower growth). Market cap = $29B. Discount back 5 years at 10%: PV ≈ **$18B**, vs current market cap of $35B. Bear case implies significant downside — stock appears overvalued in this scenario.

*Base Case:* Revenue resumes 7–9% annual growth after current cyclical soft patch (accident frequency and severity normalize). Net income margins hold near 33%. Revenue grows to ~$6.5B by FY2031. Net income reaches ~$2.1B; owner earnings ~$2.2B. Apply 25x P/E multiple (consistent with historical average). Market cap = $52B. Discount at 10% over 5 years: PV ≈ **$32B**, close to current price. This suggests the stock is approximately fairly to modestly undervalued at current price in the base case.

*Bull Case:* International expansion (UK, Germany, Middle East, Canada) accelerates. Total-loss rates continue rising (were ~20% historically; now ~23–24%). Digital platform network effects attract more buyers globally, pushing average selling prices higher. Revenue grows to $8B by FY2031. Net income margins expand to 35–36%. Net income ~$2.8B; owner earnings ~$3.0B. Apply 30x multiple (premium compounder). Market cap = $85B. Discount at 10%: PV ≈ **$53B**. Bull case implies ~50% upside from current prices.

**Multiples in Context**

Current trailing P/E is ~23x, versus 10-year historical average of ~29x. The stock's premium multiple is historically justified by CPRT's durable moat, asset-light economics, and consistent double-digit earnings growth. At ~23x, the stock trades at a notable discount to its own history. EV/EBITDA is ~16–17x, down from recent highs above 30x at peak in 2023–2024. P/FCF (price-to-reported FCF) is approximately 28x given current market cap of $35B and $1.26B FCF — but as noted, reported FCF is depressed by growth capex. On an owner earnings basis, P/OE is approximately 22–23x, which is reasonable for a business of this quality.

Versus peers: Copart's P/E of ~23x compares favorably to the commercial services industry average of ~26x and is well below RB Global / IAA's implied multiples. The market appears to be pricing in a secular slowdown rather than a cyclical trough.

**Reverse-Engineering the Current Price**

At $36/share ($35B market cap), what must be true? Assuming a 10% required return over 10 years, the market cap must reach ~$91B in 10 years. At 25x earnings, that requires net income of ~$3.6B — roughly 8–9% annual earnings growth from FY2025's $1.55B base. That is not an unreasonable assumption for a business with a dominant network-effect moat, growing total-loss rates, and international expansion runway. The stock is pricing in steady compounding — not perfection, but not disaster either. The current decline reflects a near-term earnings soft patch being extrapolated as secular deterioration, which appears an overreaction.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** Current weakness is cyclical, not structural; 30–50% upside to intrinsic value as earnings resume growth and the market re-rates toward historical multiples.
- **Bear case:** Earnings decline is secular (accident frequency declining due to AVs and better road safety), and the business never returns to prior growth trajectory; stock fair at $20–25.
- **Confidence:** Medium — core valuation math is supportable, but significant uncertainty around the growth rate assumption and the degree to which current revenue softness is cyclical vs. structural.

---

## Red Flags

- Q2 FY2026 revenue declined 3.6% and EPS fell 10% — two consecutive quarters of decline is unusual for Copart and warrants monitoring.
- Reported FCF of $1.26B significantly overstates true owner earnings if growth capex is miscategorized as maintenance; conversely, if all $569M capex were maintenance, owner earnings would be far lower (~$880M).
- Total capex of $569M vs D&A of ~$200M — the capex-to-D&A ratio of ~2.8x is high; understanding the split between growth and maintenance capex requires management disclosure that is not fully transparent.
- At $35B market cap with $1.5B LTM owner earnings, the business is priced at ~23x owner earnings — not cheap in absolute terms.
