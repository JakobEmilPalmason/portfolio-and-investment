# Compact Checklist — AMZN

**Date:** 2026-03-12

1. **Business**: Amazon is three interconnected businesses — AWS ($128.7B revenue, ~35% margin), advertising ($68.5B, ~40-50% margin), and retail (~$588B, ~7% margin) — where AWS and advertising generate ~75-80% of the $80B total operating income on only ~28% of revenue.
2. **Moat**: AWS has a genuine wide moat from enterprise switching costs so high that migrating off proprietary services (Lambda, DynamoDB, Bedrock) takes years and carries execution risk; the retail flywheel, $300-500B logistics network, and first-party purchase-intent ad data add three additional moat sources.
3. **Economics**: Operating income expanded 555% from 2022-2025 ($12.2B to $80B) on only 39% revenue growth, proving real fixed-cost leverage; normalized owner earnings are approximately $78-88B once growth capex (~$100B of $131.8B) is stripped and $22B SBC is deducted.
4. **Management**: Jassy's Efficiency Era (2022-2023 layoffs, 15% management-layer reduction) and 50% YoY operating income growth in 2025 demonstrate operational discipline, but $22B/year in SBC with no meaningful buyback program and a historically unprecedented $200B capex commitment are real capital allocation concerns.
5. **Debt risk**: Net financial debt is essentially zero ($123B cash/ST investments vs. $65.6B financial debt), interest coverage is 35x ($80B EBIT / $2.3B interest expense), and $139.5B in operating cash flow self-funds the entire $200B 2026 capex without any external financing required.
6. **Price**: At ~$210/share, AMZN trades at ~24x owner earnings and ~15.8x EV/EBITDA — near 5-year lows on both metrics — which is roughly fair value in the base case with a DCF range of $130-145 (bear) to $310-360 (bull) and zero price margin of safety.
7. **Margin of safety**: The price margin of safety is negative — stock is ~45-60% above the bear-case DCF of $130-145, and a Buffett-framework entry requiring a 25-40% discount to intrinsic value would imply waiting for $135-175; the business quality is exceptional but the price offers no cushion for error.
8. **Thesis breaker**: AWS growth rate decelerating below 15% for two consecutive quarters while the $200B 2026 capex cycle produces returns below cost of capital would permanently impair the thesis and could drive a 40-50% de-rating to $105-130.
