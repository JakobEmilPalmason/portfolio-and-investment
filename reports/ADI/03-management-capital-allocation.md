## Detailed Analysis

**Insider ownership and skin in the game.** Vincent Roche has served as CEO since 2013 and holds approximately 155,688 shares directly, plus ~23,515 shares in a Grantor Retained Annuity Trust, for a total of roughly 179,000 shares valued at approximately $37M at recent prices. This is not trivial wealth but represents roughly 0.02% of a ~$240B market cap company — a common reality for executives of large-cap companies where their equity stake is meaningful in personal wealth terms but diluted by institutional ownership. Total insider ownership across all executives and directors is estimated below 5%, with institutional investors controlling over 70% of outstanding shares. The ownership is not "owner-operator" level, but Roche's long tenure (13+ years as CEO) and equity-heavy compensation structure create meaningful alignment.

**Capital allocation track record: dividends.** ADI's dividend record is outstanding by any measure. Twenty-two consecutive years of dividend increases through February 2026 — including an 11% raise in Q1 FY2026 — demonstrates both consistency and confidence in the earnings trajectory. The quarterly dividend is now $1.10/share, or $4.40/year annualized. FY2025 dividends totaled approximately $1.9B. The payout ratio on adjusted earnings is moderate, and the dividend is well-covered by free cash flow ($4.3B in FY2025, $4.56B trailing-twelve-month as of Q1 FY2026).

**Capital allocation track record: buybacks.** ADI repurchased $2.2B of stock in FY2025. The board has authorized ~$11.5B in remaining repurchase authority. The share count has declined modestly from the post-Maxim peak: diluted shares outstanding were ~497M in 2024 and ~491M by late 2025 — roughly 1% annual reduction. This is a measured rather than aggressive buyback pace, which is reasonable given that the Maxim deal was all-stock and the company is still deleveraging. The allocation between dividends (40-60% of FCF) and buybacks (remainder) follows the stated long-term framework of returning 100% of FCF to shareholders while maintaining investment grade credit. Buyback timing quality is hard to assess precisely without price vs. buyback data by quarter, but the pace appears rational rather than heroic.

**Capital allocation track record: M&A.** The two transformative acquisitions define ADI's capital allocation story. Linear Technology (acquired 2017, ~$15.8B) was a premium-quality analog company with exceptional margins and power management IP — widely regarded as one of the best-valued acquisitions in semiconductor history. The deal was immediately accretive in strategic terms and strengthened ADI's position in the industrial and communications markets. Maxim Integrated (completed August 2021, ~$21B all-stock) was more contested — some analysts called it "underwhelming" at the time, noting Maxim had been underperforming peers. However, the strategic rationale was sound: Maxim's power management and automotive portfolios complemented ADI's converter strength, and Roche stated a $275M/year synergy target within 24 months. Post-integration, ADI's combined revenue scale ($9B+ trailing at deal close) created a platform that is now generating $11B+ in annual revenue. The all-stock structure, while dilutive, preserved balance sheet flexibility. Both deals passed the test: they expanded the moat without changing the business model.

**Compensation structure.** Roche received total compensation of approximately $27.5M in FY2025 (up ~18% from FY2024). The structure is roughly 4% base salary, 96% equity and performance-based components. This is a relatively investor-friendly structure — executives with heavily equity-weighted pay are motivated to protect per-share value rather than grow revenue for its own sake. SBC as a percentage of revenue is not directly available from public sources reviewed here, but for a company of ADI's scale ($11B revenue), SBC in the range of $400-600M/year (3-5% of revenue) would be typical for a large-cap semiconductor; this figure should be confirmed from the 10-K. It would not be a red flag at these levels but warrants monitoring.

**Candor and consistency.** Roche's public statements consistently emphasize R&D, customer relationships, and long-term value creation over short-term guidance games. ADI's guidance has been directionally honest — they gave investors fair warning of the FY2024 down-cycle. The Q1 FY2026 earnings call commentary ("relentless innovation," "new high watermark for ADI") is aspirational but backed by Q2 FY2026 guidance of $3.5B revenue (+30% YoY), which would be a record quarter if achieved. The 22-year dividend growth streak is itself a form of candor — management does not cut dividends when convenient and does not overpromise on growth to juice the stock.

**Governance.** The board has a majority of independent directors with relevant semiconductor and industrial technology expertise. The Chair/CEO dual role (Roche holds both titles) is a mild governance flag — independent lead director structures or split Chair/CEO would be preferable. No material governance scandals have been identified in public sources reviewed.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** A 22-year dividend growth streak, disciplined acquisition strategy that has consistently widened the moat, and equity-heavy executive compensation create strong alignment between management and long-term shareholders.
- **Bear case:** Low direct insider ownership, dual Chair/CEO structure, and large acquisition premiums (Maxim was a 22% premium at announcement) mean shareholders are trusting management rather than being protected by structural checks.
- **Confidence:** Medium-High — the long tenured CEO has a demonstrably strong capital allocation track record, but the limited insider ownership means this is faith in character and culture rather than aligned incentive economics.

## Red Flags

- Dual Chair/CEO role reduces independent board oversight — a governance structure that investors should monitor.
- Total insider ownership below 5% means executives are stewards of other people's capital more than they are co-investors.
- SBC as % of revenue is not directly confirmed in sources reviewed — should be verified from the 10-K; at peers it typically runs 3-6% of revenue for large-cap semis.
- The Maxim acquisition was all-stock, which diluted existing shareholders; the jury on final value creation is still partially out given integration was recent.

## Score: 7 / 10

Roche has been an above-average steward — two moat-widening acquisitions, 22 consecutive dividend raises, and disciplined FCF return — but the low direct insider ownership, dual Chair/CEO governance structure, and the complexity of assessing Maxim's final value creation prevent a higher rating; this is a well-run company with good (not exceptional) owner alignment.
