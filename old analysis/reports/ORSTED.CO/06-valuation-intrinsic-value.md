## Detailed Analysis

**Owner Earnings Today**

Owner earnings are approximated as net income + D&A - maintenance capex. Orsted reported 2025 net profit of DKK 3.2 billion and underlying EBITDA of DKK 25.1 billion. Total gross capital investment guidance for 2025 was DKK 50-54 billion (actual came in at DKK 55 billion), but the overwhelming majority of this is growth capex — building new wind farms, not maintaining existing ones. Maintenance capex for an operating offshore wind fleet is estimated at roughly 15-20% of D&A. D&A is not separately disclosed in the search results found, but at a typical EBITDA-to-EBIT spread for capital-intensive utilities, D&A likely runs DKK 8-12 billion annually. Assuming D&A of ~DKK 10 billion and maintenance capex of ~DKK 2-3 billion, owner earnings are roughly DKK 3.2B (net income) + DKK 10B (D&A) - DKK 2.5B (maintenance capex) = approximately DKK 10-11 billion. On 1.32 billion shares, that is roughly DKK 8 per share in owner earnings today. This is a rough estimate — the true figure awaits full annual report disclosure. In 3-5 years, as the 8.1 GW construction pipeline reaches operations (adding ~DKK 10-15B in incremental EBITDA), owner earnings could plausibly reach DKK 18-25 billion, or DKK 14-19 per share.

**Scenario Analysis (Year 5, discounted back at 8%)**

Bear case: US wind remains politically toxic, European auction returns disappoint, interest rates stay elevated at 4.5%+. Owner earnings grow only modestly to DKK 12 billion. Apply a 10x multiple (utility-like, limited growth). Intrinsic value = DKK 120 billion, or DKK 91 per share. Discounted at 8% for 5 years = ~DKK 62 per share. At DKK 139 today, this is a loss scenario.

Base case: US political headwinds stabilize, construction pipeline completes on schedule, European markets absorb new capacity. Owner earnings reach DKK 20 billion by Year 5. Apply 12x multiple. Intrinsic value = DKK 240 billion / 1.32B shares = DKK 182 per share. Discounted at 8% = ~DKK 124 per share. Roughly in line with current price — neither cheap nor expensive on this basis.

Bull case: Rates decline, global offshore wind accelerates, Orsted wins major new allocations and restores ROCE toward 12-14%. Owner earnings reach DKK 28 billion. Apply 14x multiple. Intrinsic value = DKK 392 billion / 1.32B shares = DKK 297 per share. Discounted at 8% = ~DKK 202 per share. This implies ~45% upside from today's price.

**Multiples in Context**

At DKK 139, the trailing P/E of ~14x and EV/EBITDA of ~9.1x are near the low end of Orsted's historical range (the stock traded at EV/EBITDA of 20-25x at its 2021 peak). Versus peers, comparable offshore wind and utility-scale renewable developers trade at EV/EBITDA of 8-14x depending on growth expectations. Orsted at 9x is on the cheaper end but not drastically below peers — it is no longer pricing in the premium it once commanded as the global market leader. The P/FCF multiple is harder to calculate given the massive growth capex program, which makes reported FCF negative for several more years.

**What Must Be True for Today's Price to Be Justified?**

At DKK 139 per share on 1.32 billion shares, the market cap is ~DKK 184 billion. Adding net debt of DKK 19 billion gives an enterprise value of ~DKK 203 billion. For this to be the right price, the market is essentially saying: Orsted will generate roughly DKK 25-28 billion in normalized EBITDA indefinitely with minimal growth, or grow EBITDA modestly but face persistent capital allocation uncertainty. The implied EV/EBITDA of ~7-8x on 2026 consensus (DKK 28.5B) tells you the market is applying a below-average multiple to an above-average EBITDA growth forecast — a sign of distrust, not of a fairly valued business.

**Implied Expectations vs Likely Reality**

The market appears to be pricing in: (1) continued US political risk shutting Orsted out of its most lucrative growth market for years; (2) ongoing dilution risk beyond the rights issue; (3) potential for further impairments on the remaining construction portfolio. All three are real risks. However, the current valuation does not appear to price in much recovery. Morningstar's fair value of DKK 160 (most recent confirmed estimate as of February 2026) implies modest upside. The higher estimate of DKK 382 reflects a scenario where Orsted's US exposure normalizes. At DKK 139, you are arguably buying the European business at close to fair value and getting the option on US recovery nearly for free — but the quality of that option depends heavily on geopolitics outside Orsted's control.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** Construction pipeline completes on budget, European markets deliver strong auction returns, and a normalization of US offshore wind policy unlocks DKK 200+ intrinsic value within 3-5 years.
- **Bear case:** Prolonged US hostility to offshore wind, further construction delays and impairments in the remaining 8.1 GW portfolio, and elevated interest rates compress multiples, pushing intrinsic value below DKK 100 per share.
- **Confidence:** Low — the Morningstar fair value range of DKK 160-382 illustrates the enormous uncertainty; the answer to whether this is cheap or fairly valued depends almost entirely on how you resolve the US political risk and construction execution risk.

## Red Flags

- Rights issue at DKK 66.60/share (October 2025) was massively dilutive, issued at a ~67% discount to then-prevailing price, resetting per-share economics dramatically and signaling the company was under severe financial stress.
- EV/EBITDA of ~9x on 2026 EBITDA is below the sector average but not dramatically cheap — no large margin of safety baked into multiples alone.
- Gross investments of DKK 55 billion in 2025 against EBITDA of DKK 25.1 billion means the company is cash-flow negative before financing for years to come — the owner earnings concept only works if you trust the construction pipeline to deliver.
- Morningstar's fair value estimates range from DKK 160 to DKK 382 — an uncertainty band of more than 2x. This is not a company where you can have high conviction about intrinsic value.
- Net debt fell to DKK 19 billion post-rights issue, but gross investments of DKK 50-55 billion planned annually will rebuild leverage quickly if EBITDA doesn't grow as expected.

## Score: 5 / 10

At DKK 139, Orsted is roughly fairly valued on a base-case scenario, with meaningful upside in the bull case but genuine downside in the bear case — the valuation is not cheap enough to provide a true margin of safety given the construction, regulatory, and geopolitical risks that remain.
