# Compact Checklist — CAT

**Date:** 2026-03-11

1. **Business**: Caterpillar sells heavy equipment, engines, and turbines to construction, mining, and energy customers globally, and once those customers buy Cat, they purchase parts and service from the local Cat dealer network for the 10-30 year life of the machine.
2. **Moat**: Competitors cannot replicate the 150+ dealer network spanning ~2,800 facilities built over a century, and the resulting 24/48-hour parts availability and operator familiarity create switching costs that justify premium pricing even as gross margins run 12+ points above the industry average.
3. **Economics**: ROIC expanded from 6.2% in 2020 to 18.2% in 2024, FCF reached $9.5B in 2025, and the services revenue stream ($24B, ~39% of ME&T revenue) growing from $14B in 2016 demonstrates that the business generates genuine cash well above its cost of capital.
4. **Management**: Management has reduced the share count by 14% over five years, raised the dividend for 30 consecutive years, and kept M&A bolt-on in character, but buybacks are executed mechanically regardless of price — including $7.7B repurchased in 2025 when FCF was only $7.5B.
5. **Debt risk**: The ME&T industrial entity carries approximately $5B in net cash with 22x interest coverage and $10B in enterprise cash, giving the manufacturing business the liquidity to survive a 30% two-year revenue decline without equity issuance, as it did in 2009.
6. **Price**: For $705/share to be justified, Caterpillar's owner earnings must grow at 10%+ annually for 10+ years with no cyclical interruption while the market sustains a 25-30x multiple — roughly twice the company's own historical norm — on a business whose earnings fell 15% in a year revenues grew 4%.
7. **Margin of safety**: There is no price margin of safety at $705 — every DCF scenario from bear ($151) to bull ($377) produces an intrinsic value below the current price, meaning the only protection against loss is the business's resilience to survive a cycle downturn, not the price paid.
8. **Thesis breaker**: Sell if the order backlog begins declining quarter-over-quarter or if the Energy & Transportation segment's data center generator demand proves short-lived, as either fact would invalidate the secular re-rating that is the sole justification for a 31x forward P/E on a cyclical industrial.
