# Compact Checklist -- AVGO

**Date:** 2026-03-07

1. **Business**: Broadcom sells custom AI accelerator chips to hyperscalers (Google, Meta, OpenAI), dominates data center Ethernet switching with ~90% share, and harvests recurring cash from VMware's enterprise virtualization software -- customers stay because switching out co-designed silicon or migrating thousands of VMs is a multi-year, multi-billion-dollar ordeal.
2. **Moat**: A triple moat of switching costs (years of co-designed custom silicon tightly coupled to each hyperscaler's software stack), efficient scale (only Broadcom and Marvell ship 51.2 Tb/s+ networking ASICs), and 8,278 patents -- though VMware's moat is actively eroding as Broadcom's 200-1,200% price hikes push 98% of customers to evaluate alternatives.
3. **Economics**: Adjusted EBITDA margins of 68%, semiconductor gross margins of 93%, and $26.9B in free cash flow on $64B revenue (42% FCF margin) in FY2025 -- these are among the highest in the semiconductor industry and confirm real pricing power.
4. **Management**: CEO Hock Tan is a proven acquirer-operator whose new $205M compensation is almost entirely equity tied to achieving $90-120B in cumulative AI revenue by 2030, but stock-based compensation running at 20-23% of revenue is quietly diluting shareholders and there is no disclosed succession plan.
5. **Debt risk**: Total debt of $66B is manageable at 1.5-1.9x EBITDA with 8-9x interest coverage, $15B already repaid since the VMware acquisition, all fixed-rate with maturities laddered to 2056, and a stress test shows the company survives a 30% revenue decline without existential risk.
6. **Price**: At ~$330/share (68x trailing P/E, ~$1.56T market cap), the stock requires owner earnings to compound at 25-30% annually for five years -- meaning AI chip revenue must hit $100B+, VMware retention must hold, and the multiple must stay elevated -- essentially pricing in near-perfection.
7. **Margin of safety**: The business itself provides real protection (dominant market positions, $27B annual FCF, sticky customer relationships), but there is no price margin of safety -- conservative DCF estimates range from $95-$304, all below the current price, and even the bull case (~$287 discounted) falls short of $330.
8. **Thesis breaker**: Sell if any major hyperscaler customer (Google, Meta, or OpenAI) publicly announces it is bringing custom chip design fully in-house and ending the Broadcom co-design relationship, because that would confirm the "shrinking iceberg" bear case and collapse the AI revenue narrative that supports the entire premium valuation.
