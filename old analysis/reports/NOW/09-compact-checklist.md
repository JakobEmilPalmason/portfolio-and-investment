# Compact Checklist — NOW

**Date:** 2026-03-10

1. **Business**: ServiceNow sells multi-year subscription workflow automation software to large enterprises (85% of Fortune 500), acting as the process-routing backbone for IT, HR, security, and customer service — and customers stay because ripping it out requires a multi-month business transformation project, evidenced by a 98% renewal rate and $12.85B in contracted near-term revenue already on the books.

2. **Moat**: Switching costs compound with every workflow module a customer adds — the 44% increase in customers paying above $5M ACV (from 420 to 603 in two years) shows customers are going deeper, not shallower, and deeper means higher exit cost.

3. **Economics**: The business generated $4.58B in free cash flow in FY2025 at a 35% FCF margin on $13.28B revenue, growing 34% year-over-year, while net revenue retention of ~125% means existing customers alone would grow the base 25% annually even with zero new logos.

4. **Management**: CEO McDermott has delivered consistent beat-and-raise quarters since 2019 and signed a contract extension through 2030, but the abrupt shift to $12B in M&A commitments in 2025 — including the $7.75B Armis acquisition — introduces integration risk that has not yet been tested, and buybacks are a dilution-management treadmill, not Buffett-style capital allocation.

5. **Debt risk**: The company holds $10.1B in liquid assets against $1.5B in fixed-rate debt due 2030 at 1.40%, generating $5.4B in operating cash flow annually — even a 30% revenue decline sustained for two years would leave it with approximately $8B in net cash and no solvency risk.

6. **Price**: At ~$121/share (~$120B market cap) and 26x trailing FCF, today's price makes sense if FCF grows at just 10–12% annually for five years and the exit multiple holds above 20x — neither assumption requires heroic conditions, just rough continuity of what the business is already doing.

7. **Margin of safety**: The stock has fallen ~50% from its January 2025 all-time high despite FY2025 revenue growing 21% and FCF margins expanding — that multiple compression from 60–80x to 26x FCF is itself the margin of safety, as even the bear-case DCF (~10% growth, 28% FCF margin) approximates current market cap.

8. **Thesis breaker**: Sell if cRPO growth decelerates below 15% for two consecutive quarters OR net revenue retention falls below 110%, as either would confirm that AI disruption (from Microsoft bundling or open-source alternatives) is causing actual demand destruction rather than just investor fear.
