## Detailed Analysis

**Owner Earnings Today**

Using FY2025 financials: net income of $112.0B + D&A of $29.1B = $141.1B gross owner earnings. Apple's reported capex was $12.7B in FY2025, though a meaningful portion is likely growth capex (new data centers, manufacturing capacity). Applying a conservative maintenance capex estimate of $8–10B yields owner earnings in the range of $125–133B. At today's market cap of approximately $3.9T (257.46 × ~15.1B diluted shares), the price-to-owner-earnings multiple is approximately 29–31x. Free cash flow as reported was $98.8B in FY2025, but Q1 FY2026 annualized FCF runs higher — approximately $123B — given the strong quarter. Both measures confirm Apple is generating exceptional cash.

**Scenario Analysis — Owner Earnings in Year 5 (FY2030)**

*Bear case:* iPhone revenue stagnates as China share erodes, Services growth decelerates to 6–8% due to antitrust/DMA headwinds, and margins compress 200bp. Owner earnings grow at ~4% annually: ~$155B by FY2030. Apply a 22x multiple (reflects lower-growth mature tech): ~$3.41T equity value. Discounted at 9%: ~$2.22T. Per share (assuming 14.5B shares after buybacks): ~$153. That represents ~40% downside from current price.

*Base case:* iPhone grows modestly at 2–3% annually, Services grows 12% driven by Apple Intelligence monetization and subscription expansion, total owner earnings grow at 8–9% annually: ~$185B by FY2030. Apply a 26x multiple: ~$4.8T. Discounted at 9%: ~$3.12T. Per share: ~$215. That represents roughly 16% downside from current price — not a comfortable margin.

*Bull case:* Apple Intelligence triggers a major iPhone upgrade cycle, Services hits $140B+ annually (15%+ growth), owner earnings grow at 13%+ annually: ~$230B by FY2030. Apply a 28x multiple: ~$6.4T. Discounted at 9%: ~$4.2T. Per share: ~$290. That represents ~13% upside — modest for a bull case.

**Multiples in Context**

Apple's trailing P/E of 32.5x compares to its own 5-year average of ~28x, suggesting ~15% multiple compression risk. Its forward P/E of ~29.8x is elevated for a company expected to grow EPS at 10–12% annually — the PEG ratio sits around 2.5x, which is full to expensive. EV/EBITDA of 26.5x versus the 3-year average of 23.8x indicates a ~11% premium to recent history. Among mega-cap tech peers, Apple's multiple is lower than Microsoft (~32x EV/EBITDA) but higher than Alphabet (~18x), and lacks the hyper-growth profile that typically justifies such premiums. P/FCF is approximately 31x (using $123B annualized FCF vs. $3.9T market cap) — full but not extreme for a capital-light, recurring-revenue business of this quality.

**What Must Be True for Today's Price to Be Justified**

Reverse-engineering at $257/share (~$3.9T market cap): the market is implying that Apple will grow owner earnings at roughly 10–11% annually for the next decade and can be exited at a 22–24x multiple. That requires: (1) Services sustaining 12–14% growth for 5+ years and reaching $170B+ in revenue, (2) iPhone holding its installed base and pricing power against Android competition and China substitution, (3) no material antitrust or regulatory damage to the App Store or Google search deal (currently ~$20B/year in pure profit to Apple), and (4) continued aggressive buybacks reducing share count by 3–4% annually. Each of these is individually plausible; all four together represents optimistic but not outlandish expectations.

**Is the Market Pricing in Perfection?**

Not outright perfection, but the market is clearly pricing in execution. The current price offers thin margin of safety against even a modest growth disappointment. The wide dispersion in DCF estimates ($97 to $376) reflects genuine uncertainty about the durability of Services margins under regulatory pressure and the magnitude of AI-driven hardware refresh. The stock is not a screaming buy or an obvious short — it is a high-quality business priced for continued excellence.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** Apple Intelligence triggers a hardware super-cycle and Services reaches $150B+ annually by FY2028, driving EPS well above consensus and a re-rating to 35x.
- **Bear case:** The DOJ antitrust ruling forces App Store restructuring, the Google search deal collapses ($20B annual profit at risk), and China revenue continues softening — owner earnings stagnate and the multiple compresses to 22x.
- **Confidence:** Medium — Apple's financials are well-documented and the business model is well-understood; uncertainty centers on regulatory outcomes and AI monetization timeline, both of which are genuinely unknowable.

---

## Red Flags

- The base-case DCF produces ~16% downside from today's price — the stock is not cheap even on reasonable assumptions.
- The Google search revenue-sharing agreement ($18–20B/year, pure margin) is under DOJ antitrust scrutiny; if terminated, it would reduce EPS by roughly $1.30–$1.50 (roughly 20% of EPS), a risk the current multiple does not adequately reflect.
- Multiple DCF models from independent sources (Alpha Spread, GuruFocus) put fair value at $97–$142, implying 45–65% overvaluation under conservative assumptions. While these models may be too conservative, the gap is uncomfortably large.
- EV/EBITDA above its 3-year average suggests valuation has gotten ahead of fundamentals even by Apple's own history.

---

## Score: 3 / 10

Apple is an exceptional business priced for near-perfection — the base case implies downside, the bull case offers only modest upside, and multiple independent DCF models show significant overvaluation; the stock warrants a Pass at current prices.
