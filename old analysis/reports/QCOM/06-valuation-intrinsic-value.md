## Detailed Analysis

**Owner Earnings Estimate**

Qualcomm's FY2025 financials provide a solid baseline. Revenue came in at $44.3B (+13.7% YoY), operating income at $12.39B (27.9% margin), and EBITDA at $14.0B. Free cash flow was $12.8B, reflecting capex of just $1.2B — remarkably capital-light for a semiconductor company. Net income reported was $5.54B, but the gap between net income and FCF ($12.8B) is largely explained by significant non-cash charges (stock-based compensation, amortization of intangibles from acquisitions, deferred taxes). For owner earnings purposes, FCF is the better proxy: approximately $12.8B annually. Maintenance capex is likely well under $1.5B given the fabless model, so adjusted owner earnings are roughly $11.5–$12.5B. At the current ~$148B market cap (136 x ~1.09B shares), owner earnings yield is approximately 8–8.5%.

**Scenario Analysis — Year 5 (FY2031)**

Bear case: Apple modem revenues fully lost (already ~20% of iPhones remain, falling to zero by FY2027 per CEO guidance), China revenue contracts 20% due to geopolitical friction, handset market stagnates. Automotive and IoT grow but cannot fully offset. Owner earnings decline to ~$9–10B. Apply a 12x multiple (reflecting commodity-like perception): intrinsic value ~$108–$120B. Discounted at 10% over 5 years from today's $148B — this represents a loss. However, the bear case requires significant simultaneous headwinds.

Base case: Apple handset revenues decline to near-zero (already largely priced in), China modestly pressured (-10%), automotive achieves $4B target by FY2026 on path to $8B by FY2029, IoT grows toward $10B, AI/PC ramp contributes $2B+. Owner earnings stabilize then grow to ~$14–16B by FY2031. Apply 15x multiple: intrinsic value $210–$240B. Discounted at 10%: present value ~$130–$149B — roughly in line with current price. The base case is approximately fairly valued.

Bull case: China risk dissipates, diversification fully materializes (auto $8B, IoT $14B, AI/data center contribution), licensing revenues renegotiated with Huawei. Owner earnings reach $18–22B by FY2031. Apply 16x multiple: intrinsic value $288–$352B. Discounted at 10%: PV ~$179–$218B. Upside of 30–60% from current price.

**Multiples in Context**

Current forward P/E of ~12.9x compares favorably against the 3-year average of ~21x and the semiconductor peer median. Broadcom (AVGO) trades at ~25–30x forward earnings; Texas Instruments at ~20–22x; MediaTek at ~15–18x. QCOM's discount is substantial. EV/EBITDA of ~10.7x sits below the 13-year median of 12.8x and far below the peak of 38.6x. P/FCF is approximately 11.5x — one of the lowest readings in the company's public history outside of the 2022–2023 trough. The discount reflects legitimate concerns (Apple loss, China, ARM disruption) but appears to overshoot on pessimism.

**What Must Be True to Justify Today's Price**

At ~$136, the market appears to be pricing in: (1) complete loss of Apple modem revenue by FY2027 with no meaningful replacement, (2) China revenue declining 15–20% over the forecast period, (3) QTL licensing stagnation or modest decline, and (4) automotive/IoT growth that is real but not yet trusted. Essentially, the current price requires the company to merely hold steady on earnings while executing diversification at a modest pace. If any of the three growth vectors (auto, IoT, AI inference) significantly outperforms, the stock is materially undervalued.

**Market Sentiment**

The stock has declined from a 52-week high of ~$205 to ~$136 — a 34% drop. This is not a market pricing in perfection; it is a market pricing in meaningful fundamental deterioration. The question for the investor is whether that deterioration is already fully embedded or whether further downside surprises remain. The 8.6% FCF yield and sub-13x forward P/E suggest pessimism is well-represented in the price. However, the risks are real enough that a discount is warranted — the uncertainty is whether the current discount is sufficient.

## Signal Summary

- **Bull case:** QCOM is priced for stagnation but positioned for diversification across auto, IoT, and AI that could double earnings in 5 years, making today's price a significant bargain.
- **Bear case:** Apple revenue exit, persistent China geopolitical friction, and ARM/RISC-V disruption compress margins and earnings, making the current price merely fair rather than cheap.
- **Confidence:** Medium — financials are well-documented and current, but the Apple/China/licensing uncertainty creates wide outcome dispersion that limits valuation precision.

## Red Flags

- Net income of $5.54B vs FCF of $12.8B gap warrants scrutiny — verify this is accounting/amortization driven, not a cash earnings quality issue
- 52-week range of $120.80–$205.95 shows extreme volatility inconsistent with a stable "Owner Earnings" business
- Apple C1 modem launch (February 2025) has already begun eroding the modem chip supply relationship ahead of the 2027 deadline
- No confirmed renewal of Huawei licensing agreement; QTL growth guidance is zero for the near term

## Score: 6 / 10

The stock appears modestly undervalued in a base case scenario, trading at a historic discount to its own multiples and well below semiconductor peers, but the wide range of plausible outcomes (from deeply undervalued to fairly valued) prevents a higher conviction score.
