# Compact Checklist — ALAB

**Date:** 2026-03-06

1. **Business**: Astera Labs sells connectivity semiconductors (retimers, cables, fabric switches) that wire together GPU clusters in AI data centers, and customers keep buying because 12-18 month qualification cycles make ripping out their silicon painful — but over 70% of the $852.5M in FY2025 revenue came from a single customer (Amazon).
2. **Moat**: Design-win switching costs and the COSMOS software telemetry platform create real stickiness within each product cycle, but the advantage resets every 3-5 years with new PCIe/CXL standards, Broadcom and Marvell are launching direct competitors, and a 28-patent portfolio will not keep them out.
3. **Economics**: The fabless model delivers 75%+ gross margins, $282M in free cash flow on $853M revenue, and only 4% capex intensity — genuinely excellent unit economics, though $160M in annual stock-based compensation (19% of revenue) is a real cost that non-GAAP metrics obscure.
4. **Management**: Founder-CEO Mohan holds ~$900M in stock and speaks like an engineer building a business, but no insider has made a single open-market purchase since the IPO, the share count grew 27% in one year, and the CFO departed during a critical growth phase.
5. **Debt risk**: Zero debt, $1.19 billion in cash and securities, and a 10.2x current ratio — this company could operate for over two years at zero revenue without raising a dollar.
6. **Price**: At ~$120 per share (~86x forward P/E), the market is pricing in ~37% annual earnings growth for five years to reach $3.5-4.0B in revenue by 2030, while multiple DCF models place fair value at $80-93 — meaning the stock needs a near-perfect outcome just to justify today's price.
7. **Margin of safety**: There is none — 70%+ revenue from one customer with no contractual purchase obligation, zero recurring revenue, decelerating sequential growth, and a stock priced 30-45% above conservative intrinsic value estimates leave no cushion if anything goes wrong.
8. **Thesis breaker**: Amazon announcing in-house connectivity silicon, materially reducing orders, or shifting 30%+ of its connectivity spend to Broadcom — any of these would collapse the revenue base and the thesis simultaneously.
