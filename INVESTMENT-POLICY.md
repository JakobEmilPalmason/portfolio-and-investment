# Investment Policy Statement

> These rules are non-negotiable. Read this before every buy and sell decision.

---

## 1. Position Sizing

| Rule | Limit |
|------|-------|
| Initial buy | ≤ 3% of portfolio at cost |
| Maximum size | 7% at market value — trim if exceeded |
| Minimum size | 1% — no token positions |

**Why:** A position too small to matter is a position not worth owning. A position too large is a position not worth sleeping on. Start modest; let business quality earn the right to grow.

---

## 2. Sector Concentration

- **Hard cap:** 35% gross weight in any single GICS sector
- Exceptions are allowed but must be documented in writing with specific rationale before the trade is placed — not after

---

## 3. Minimum Requirements to Buy

All three conditions must be true simultaneously:

1. **Pipeline verdict: Own** — average umbrella score ≥ 7, no umbrella < 4, margin-of-safety score ≥ 6
2. **Margin of safety:** current price is ≥ 20% below your conservative intrinsic value estimate
3. **Conviction check:** you can explain the thesis clearly in 3 sentences to someone who doesn't follow markets

If any one of these is missing, the answer is no.

---

## 4. Sell Triggers

Sell when **any** of the following is true. These are business-driven rules, not price-driven:

| Trigger | Condition |
|---------|-----------|
| **Thesis broken** | A key assumption underlying the original thesis is now demonstrably false — not weakened, false |
| **Intrinsic value reached** | Price closes within 10% of your conservative IV estimate — no margin of safety remains |
| **Fundamental deterioration** | ROIC, FCF, or moat indicators decline meaningfully in two consecutive annual periods |
| **Better capital available** | A clearly superior Own-verdict position exists AND the current position is already at or near full size |
| **Position too large** | Market value exceeds 7% of portfolio — trim back toward 5%, not full exit |
| **3-year thesis check** | The core thesis has not started materializing after 3 years — re-underwrite from scratch: either produce a fresh Own verdict with current data or exit |

**What is never a sell trigger:** price decline alone. A lower price is only relevant if it reveals new information about business quality. If the business is intact, a lower price is a better entry, not a reason to leave.

---

## 5. New Cash

Follow this decision tree in order:

1. **Add to existing position** — does an Own-verdict position in the portfolio still have margin of safety and is below its full 3% allocation? Add there first.
2. **Deploy into new Own verdict** — is there a new Own-verdict name with adequate margin of safety that passes the 3-sentence conviction check? Buy at initial 3% size.
3. **Hold cash** — if neither condition is met, cash sits. Cash is a position. There is no obligation to be fully deployed.

**Never** buy a Watch or Pass verdict to reduce cash drag. Patience is not a cost — it is the strategy.

**Cooling-off rule:** wait at least 48 hours after a B2 triage result before acting on it. First impressions are not investment theses.

---

## Philosophy

These rules exist because discipline is hardest exactly when it matters most — when a position is down sharply, when cash is burning a hole, when a story sounds compelling before the analysis is done.

The pipeline exists to find quality businesses at fair prices. This policy exists to make sure the portfolio reflects only your best, clearest thinking — not your worst moments.

> "The most important thing is not to lose money." — Warren Buffett
